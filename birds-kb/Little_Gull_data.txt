<p>The <b>Little Gull</b>, <i>Hydrocoloeus minutus</i> or <i><b>Larus minutus</b></i>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a> which breeds in northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It also has small colonies in parts of southern <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering on coasts in western Europe, the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> and (in small numbers) the northeast <a href="/wiki/USA" title="USA" class="mw-redirect">USA</a>; in recent years non-breeding birds have summered in western Europe in increasing numbers. As is the case with many gulls, it has traditionally been placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Larus" title="Larus">Larus</a></i>. It is the only member of the genus <i><b>Hydrocoloeus</b></i>, although it has been suggested that <a href="/wiki/Ross%27s_Gull" title="Ross's Gull">Ross's Gull</a> also should be included in this genus.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Littlegull75.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Littlegull75.jpg/220px-Littlegull75.jpg" width="220" height="167" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Littlegull75.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Illustration, adult summer plumage</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This species breeds colonially on freshwater marshes, making a lined nest on the ground amongst vegetation. Normally, 2-6 eggs are laid.</p>
<p>This is the smallest gull species, with a length of 25–30 cm (9.8–12 in), a wingspan of 61–78 cm (24–31 in) and a weight of 68–162 g (2.4–5.7 oz).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_0-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is pale grey in breeding plumage with a black hood, dark underwings and often a pinkish flush on the breast. In winter, the head goes white apart from a darker cap and eye-spot. The bill is thin and black and the legs dark red. The flight on rounded wings is somewhat <a href="/wiki/Tern" title="Tern">tern-like</a>.</p>
<p>Young birds have black markings on the head and upperparts, and "W" pattern across the wings. They take three years to reach maturity.</p>
<p>These gulls pick food off the water surface, and will also catch insects in the air like a <a href="/wiki/Black_Tern" title="Black Tern">Black Tern</a>.</p>
