<p>The <b>Caspian Plover</b> (<i>Charadrius asiaticus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a> family of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>.</p>
<p>It breeds on open <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> in central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, mainly to the north and east of the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a>. This bird breeds in loose colonies, with three eggs being laid in a ground nest. These birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> south in winter to east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, usually still on grassland or arable. This plover is a very rare vagrant in western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It is also a rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.</p>
<p>It feeds in a similar way to other plovers picking <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other small prey mainly from grassland or arable.</p>
<p>This attractive plover is slightly larger than <a href="/wiki/Ringed_Plover" title="Ringed Plover" class="mw-redirect">Ringed Plover</a>, and it recalls <a href="/wiki/Greater_Sandplover" title="Greater Sandplover" class="mw-redirect">Greater Sandplover</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Sandplover" title="Lesser Sandplover" class="mw-redirect">Lesser Sandplover</a> in appearance. It is slimmer and longer-legged than the sandplovers, and has a much stronger white supercilium, and a long thin <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a>. It also lacks white tail sides and a weak wing bar.</p>
<p>Summer males have grey-brown backs and a white face and belly. The breast is chestnut, bordered black below. Other <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a> have a grey-brown breast band, although the summer female may show a hint of chestnut. The call is a sharp <i>chip</i>.</p>
<p>The Caspian Plover is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
