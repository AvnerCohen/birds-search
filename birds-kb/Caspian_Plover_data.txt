<p>The <b>Caspian plover</b> (<i>Charadrius asiaticus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a> family of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. The genus name <i>Charadrius</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">Late Latin</a> word for a yellowish bird mentioned in the fourth-century <a href="/wiki/Vulgate" title="Vulgate">Vulgate</a>. It derives from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>kharadrios</i> a bird found in ravines and river valleys (<i>kharadra</i>, "ravine"). The specific <i>asiaticus</i> is  <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and means "Asian", although in binomials it usually means the <a href="/wiki/Type_(biology)#type_locality" title="Type (biology)">type locality</a> was <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Caspian_Plover.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Caspian_Plover.jpg/220px-Caspian_Plover.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Caspian_Plover.jpg/330px-Caspian_Plover.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Caspian_Plover.jpg/440px-Caspian_Plover.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1117" data-file-height="781" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Caspian_Plover.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Maasai_Mara" title="Maasai Mara">Maasai Mara</a> - <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a></div></div></div>
<p>This plover breeds in western and central Asia and migrates southward to eastern and southern Africa to escape the northern winter.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caspian_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This plover is slightly larger than <a href="/wiki/Ringed_plover" class="mw-redirect" title="Ringed plover">ringed plover</a>, and it recalls <a href="/wiki/Greater_sandplover" class="mw-redirect" title="Greater sandplover">greater sandplover</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lesser_sandplover" class="mw-redirect" title="Lesser sandplover">lesser sandplover</a> in appearance. It is slimmer and longer-legged than the sandplovers, and has a much stronger white supercilium, and a long thin <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a>. It also lacks white tail sides and a weak wing bar.
</p><p>Summer males have grey-brown backs and a white face and belly. The breast is chestnut, bordered black below. Other <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a> have a grey-brown breast band, although the summer female may show a hint of chestnut. The call is a sharp <i>chip</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caspian_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Caspian plover breeds in western Asia in the area of the Caspian Sea. Its range includes southern Russia, Turkey, Iran, Turkmenistan, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan and Afghanistan. Its habitat is steppes, desert verges, saltpans and saline soils amongst sparse shrubby vegetation up to an altitude of about 800&#160;m (2,625&#160;ft). After breeding, it <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> to eastern and southern Africa, going as far as the <a href="/wiki/Zambezi_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Zambezi River">Zambezi River</a>. Its winter quarters are usually dry grassland, coastal dunes, saltmarshes, dry floodplains and sometimes cultivated land.<sup id="cite_ref-BLI_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This plover is a very rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It is also a rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biology">Biology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Caspian_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Biology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds on open <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> in central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, mainly to the north and east of the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a>. It breeds in loose colonies, with three eggs being laid in a nest on the bare ground or among short vegetation.
</p><p>It feeds in a similar way to other plovers picking <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Termite" title="Termite">termites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ant" title="Ant">ants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grasshopper" title="Grasshopper">grasshoppers</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">snails</a> and other small prey mainly from the ground. It sometimes eats the seeds of grasses.<sup id="cite_ref-BLI_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The Caspian plover has a very wide range. The number of birds may be declining slowly due to degradation of its breeding range, especially in Europe, however the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> lists it as being of <i><a href="/wiki/Least_Concern" class="mw-redirect" title="Least Concern">Least Concern</a>" because it does not consider the rate of decline justifies putting it in a more threatened category.<sup id="cite_ref-BLI_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLI-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p><p>It is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
