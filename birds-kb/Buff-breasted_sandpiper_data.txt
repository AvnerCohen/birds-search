<p>The <b>buff-breasted sandpiper</b> (<i>Calidris subruficollis</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">shorebird</a>. The species name <i>subruficollis</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>subrufus</i>, "reddish" (from <i>sub</i>, "somewhat", and <i>rufus</i>, "rufous") and <i>collis</i>, "-necked/-throated" (from <i>collum</i>, "neck").<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is a <a href="/wiki/Calidrid" class="mw-redirect" title="Calidrid">calidrid</a> <a href="/wiki/Sandpiper" title="Sandpiper">sandpiper</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buff-breasted_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is brown above, and has a buff face and underparts in all plumages. It has a short bill and yellow legs. Males are larger than females. Juveniles resemble the adults, but may be paler on the rear underparts.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buff-breasted_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Grasläufer.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg/220px-Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg/330px-Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg/440px-Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg 2x" data-file-width="534" data-file-height="800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grasl%C3%A4ufer.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p><i>C. subruficollis</i> breeds in the open arctic <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and is a very long-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, spending the non-breeding season mainly in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.
</p><p>It <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> mainly through central North America, and is uncommon on the <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coasts</a>. It occurs as a regular wanderer to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and is not classed as rare in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, where small flocks have occurred. Only the <a href="/wiki/Pectoral_sandpiper" title="Pectoral sandpiper">pectoral sandpiper</a> is a more common American shorebird visitor to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p><p>This species nests as far north as Canada including Alaska<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> on the ground, laying four <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>. The male has a display which includes raising the wings to display the white undersides, which is also given on migration, sometimes when no other buff-breasted sandpipers are present. Outside the breeding season, this bird is normally found on short-grass <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> such as airfields or golf-courses, rather than near water.
</p><p>These birds pick up food by sight, mainly eating <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>. The buff-breasted sandpipers are known to prey on <i><a href="/wiki/Bombus_polaris" title="Bombus polaris">Bombus polaris</a></i>, a species of bumblebee found within the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Circle" title="Arctic Circle">Arctic Circle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fs_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fs-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They will either eat the bees or feed them to their young.<sup id="cite_ref-fs_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fs-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They are often very tame.
</p><p>Buff-breasted sandpipers are <a href="/wiki/Hybridisation_in_shorebirds#Other_hybrid_scolopacids" title="Hybridisation in shorebirds">suspected</a> to have <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridized</a> with the <a href="/wiki/White-rumped_sandpiper" title="White-rumped sandpiper">white-rumped</a> or <a href="/wiki/Baird%27s_sandpiper" title="Baird&#39;s sandpiper">Baird's sandpiper</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_South_Asia_and_Australia">In South Asia and Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Buff-breasted_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In South Asia and Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This species has been sighted in South Asia on at least three occasions. It is believed that instead of going to Argentina, this bird might have been wind-blown from the Great Plains Flyway of North America and landed up in South Asia. In November 2011, this species was sighted by Dr.Jayan Thomas and P.C. Rajeevan near <a href="/wiki/Kannur" title="Kannur">Kannur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">Kerala</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_India" title="South India">South India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-migrantwatch_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-migrantwatch-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and photographed by Dr. Jayan Thomas. The buff-breasted sandpiper has also been recorded in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> on at least eight occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-tonypalliser_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tonypalliser-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1978, Phillips recorded the species from <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>, and after that very few sightings were recorded. In 2001 a single bird was also recorded in Marievale, South Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
