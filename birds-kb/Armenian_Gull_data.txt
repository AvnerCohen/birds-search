<p>The <b>Armenian Gull</b> (<i>Larus armenicus</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>. It was formerly classified as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/European_Herring_Gull" title="European Herring Gull">Herring Gull</a> (<i>L. argentatus</i>) but is now generally considered to be a separate species although <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a> lumps it with the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-legged_Gull" title="Yellow-legged Gull">Yellow-legged Gull</a> (<i>L. michahellis</i>).</p>
<p>The Armenian Gull is a fairly large gull species, though is on average the smallest of the "<a href="/wiki/Herring_Gull" title="Herring Gull">Herring Gull</a>" complex. It can range from 52 to 62 cm (20 to 24 in), from 120 to 145 cm (47 to 57 in) across the wings and weighs from 600 to 960 g (1.3 to 2.1 lb). Among standard measurements, its <a href="/wiki/Wing_chord_(biology)" title="Wing chord (biology)">wing chord</a> is 38.5 to 45.8 cm (15.2 to 18.0 in), its <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is 4.1 to 5.6 cm (1.6 to 2.2 in) and its <a href="/wiki/Tarsus_(skeleton)" title="Tarsus (skeleton)">tarsus</a> is 5.7 to 6.4 cm (2.2 to 2.5 in).<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> They superficially similar to Yellow-legged Gulls but are slightly smaller with a slightly darker grey back and dark eyes. The area of black on the wingtips is more extensive with smaller white spots. The bill is short with a distinctive black band just before the tip. First-winter birds are mainly brown. They have a whitish rump, pale inner <a href="/wiki/Remiges" title="Remiges" class="mw-redirect">primary feathers</a> and a narrow, sharply-defined black band on the tail.</p>
<p>The Armenian Gull nests beside mountain lakes in <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. The largest colonies are at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Sevan" title="Lake Sevan">Lake Sevan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Arpi" title="Lake Arpi">Lake Arpi</a> in Armenia. It is a partial <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> with many birds wintering on the coasts of Turkey, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>. Smaller numbers reach <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>.</p>
<p>The nest is a mound of vegetation built on the ground on an island or the lakeshore. Three eggs are laid, mainly in late April. The nesting colonies are very dense with nests close together and territorial conflicts common.</p>
