<p>The <b>pale</b> or <b>pallid harrier</b> (<i>Circus macrourus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Harrier_(bird)" title="Harrier (bird)">harrier</a> family. The scientific name is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>. <i>Circus</i> is from <i>kirkos</i>, referring to a bird of prey named for its circling flight (<i>kirkos</i>, "circle"), probably the <a href="/wiki/Hen_harrier" title="Hen harrier">hen harrier</a> and <i>macrourus</i> is "long-tailed", from <i>makros</i>, "long" and <i>-ouros</i> "-tailed".<sup id="cite_ref-Jobling_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jobling-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds in southern parts of eastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">central Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and winters mainly in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and southeast Asia. It is a very rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and western Europe, although remarkably a juvenile wintered in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> in the winter of 2002/2003. In 2017 a pair of pallid harriers nested in a barley field in the Netherlands; they raised four chicks.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">raptor</a> breeds on open plains, <a href="/wiki/Bog" title="Bog">bogs</a> and heathland. In winter it is a bird of open country.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_harrier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Circus_macrourus.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Circus macrourus.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Circus_macrourus.jpg/220px-Circus_macrourus.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="312" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Circus_macrourus.jpg/330px-Circus_macrourus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Circus_macrourus.jpg/440px-Circus_macrourus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1102" data-file-height="1563" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Circus_macrourus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div></div></div></div>
<p>This is a typical harrier, with long wings held in a shallow V in its low flight. It also resembles other harriers in having distinct male and female <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a>. Adults measure 40–48&#160;cm (16–19&#160;in) long with a wingspan of 95–120&#160;cm (37–47&#160;in). Males weigh 315&#160;g (11.1&#160;oz) while the slightly larger females weigh 445&#160;g (15.7&#160;oz). The male is whitish grey above and white below, with narrow black wingtips. It differs from the <a href="/wiki/Hen_harrier" title="Hen harrier">hen harrier</a> in its smaller size, narrower wings, paler colour, and different wing tip pattern. The female is brown above with white upper tail coverts, hence females and the similar juveniles are often called "ringtails". Her underparts are buff streaked with brown. It is best distinguished from the female <a href="/wiki/Hen_harrier" title="Hen harrier">hen harrier</a> on structure. It is very similar to the female <a href="/wiki/Montagu%27s_harrier" title="Montagu&#39;s harrier">Montagu's harrier</a>, but has darker and more uniform secondaries from below.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_harrier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pallid harriers hunt small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, surprising them as they drift low over fields and <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moors</a>.
</p><p>The nest of this species is on the ground. Four to six whitish eggs are laid.
</p>
