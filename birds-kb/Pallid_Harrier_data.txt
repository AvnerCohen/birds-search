<p>The <b>Pale</b> or <b>Pallid Harrier</b> (<i>Circus macrourus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Harrier_(bird)" title="Harrier (bird)">harrier</a> family. It breeds in southern parts of eastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and winters mainly in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and southeast Asia. It is a very rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and western Europe, although remarkably a juvenile wintered in <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> in the winter of 2002/3.</p>
<p>This medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">raptor</a> breeds on open plains, <a href="/wiki/Bog" title="Bog">bogs</a> and heathland. In winter it is a bird of open country.</p>
<p>This is a typical harrier, with long wings held in a shallow V in its low flight. It also resembles other harriers in having distinct male and female <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a>. Adults measure 40–48&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 95–120&#160;cm. Males weigh 315&#160;g while the slightly larger females weigh 445&#160;g.</p>
<p>The male is whitish grey above and white below, with narrow black wingtips. It differs from the <a href="/wiki/Hen_Harrier" title="Hen Harrier">Hen Harrier</a> in its smaller size, narrower wings, pale colour different wing tip pattern.</p>
<p>The female is brown above with white upper tail coverts, hence females and the similar juveniles are often called "ringtails". Her underparts are buff streaked with brown. It is best distinguished from the female <a href="/wiki/Hen_Harrier" title="Hen Harrier">Hen Harrier</a> on structure. It is very similar to the female <a href="/wiki/Montagu%27s_Harrier" title="Montagu's Harrier">Montagu's Harrier</a>, but has darker and more uniform secondaries from below.</p>
<p>Pallid Harriers hunt small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, surprising them as they drift low over fields and <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moors</a>.</p>
<p>The nest of this species is on the ground. Four to six whitish eggs are laid.</p>
