<p>The <b>White-headed Duck</b> (<i>Oxyura leucocephala</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Stiff-tailed_duck" title="Stiff-tailed duck">stiff-tailed duck</a>.</p>
<p>Adult males have a grey and reddish body, a blue bill and a largely white head with a black cap and neck. Adult females have a grey-brown body with a white face and a darker bill, cap and a cheek stripe. Length is 43–48 cm (17–19 in) and weight is 580–750 g (1.3–1.7 lb).</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:White-headed_Duck.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/White-headed_Duck.jpg/220px-White-headed_Duck.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:White-headed_Duck.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Male in winter</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This duck breeds in Spain and <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, with a larger population in western and central Asia. Their breeding <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" title="Habitat (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">habitat</a> is large tracts of open water with dense stands of aquatic plants to provide cover and nesting sites. Individuals are fairly frequently reported well north of their breeding range, but as with many <a href="/wiki/Wildfowl" title="Wildfowl" class="mw-redirect">wildfowl</a> the status of these extra-limital records is clouded by the possibility of escapes from collections.</p>
<p>These <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> dive and swim underwater. They are <a href="/wiki/Omnivorous" title="Omnivorous" class="mw-redirect">omnivorous</a>, with vegetable matter predominating. They are reluctant to fly, preferring to swim for cover.</p>
<p>This duck is considered <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> due to a large reduction in populations in the last ten years. Most of this decline is due to habitat loss and hunting, but interbreeding of the Spanish population with the introduced <a href="/wiki/Ruddy_Duck" title="Ruddy Duck">Ruddy Duck</a> (<i>Oxyura jamaicensis</i>) is a more recent threat. This has led to the attempted eradication of the American species from western Europe.</p>
<p>The White-headed Duck is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
