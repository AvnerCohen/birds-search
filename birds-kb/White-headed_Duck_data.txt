<p>The <b>white-headed duck</b> (<i>Oxyura leucocephala</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Stiff-tailed_duck" title="Stiff-tailed duck">stiff-tailed duck</a> some 45&#160;cm (18&#160;in) long. The male has a white head with black crown, a blue bill, and reddish-grey plumage. The female has a dark bill and rather duller colouring. Its breeding habitat is lakes with open water and dense vegetation at the margin. It dives under water and feeds on aquatic vegetation as well as some animal matter. It is more likely to swim away from a perceived threat than to fly. This duck is known from Spain, North Africa, <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. Populations are declining, mostly due to <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">loss of habitat</a> and pollution, and the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has rated the bird's status as "<a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a>".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-headed_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adult males have a grey and reddish body, a blue bill and a largely white head with a black cap and neck. Adult females have a grey-brown body with a white face and a darker bill, cap, and cheek stripe. Length is 43–48&#160;cm (17–19&#160;in) and weight is 580–750&#160;g (1.28–1.65&#160;lb).
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:White-headed_Duck.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/White-headed_Duck.jpg/220px-White-headed_Duck.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/White-headed_Duck.jpg/330px-White-headed_Duck.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/White-headed_Duck.jpg/440px-White-headed_Duck.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2547" data-file-height="1910" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:White-headed_Duck.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Male in winter</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-headed_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG/220px-Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="152" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG/330px-Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG/440px-Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3371" data-file-height="2332" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Oxyura_leucocephala_MWNH_1036.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This duck breeds in Spain and <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, with a larger population in western and central Asia. Their breeding <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat (ecology)">habitat</a> is large tracts of open water, such as lakes and ponds including artificial water bodies, with dense stands of aquatic plants to provide shelter and nesting sites.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Individuals are fairly frequently reported well north of their breeding range, but as with many <a href="/wiki/Wildfowl" class="mw-redirect" title="Wildfowl">wildfowl</a>, the status of these extralimital records is clouded by the possibility of escapes from collections.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-headed_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These birds dive and swim under water. They are <a href="/wiki/Omnivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Omnivorous">omnivorous</a>, with vegetable matter predominating. They are reluctant to fly, preferring to swim for cover.
</p><p>This duck is considered <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> due to a large reduction in populations in the last 10 years. Most of this decline is due to habitat loss and hunting, but interbreeding of the Spanish population with the introduced <a href="/wiki/Ruddy_duck" title="Ruddy duck">ruddy duck</a> (<i><a href="/wiki/Oxyura_jamaicensis" class="mw-redirect" title="Oxyura jamaicensis">Oxyura jamaicensis</a></i>) is a more recent threat. This has led to the attempted eradication of the American species from western Europe.
</p><p>The white-headed duck is one of the species to which the Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies, and the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has rated the bird's conservation status as being "<a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
