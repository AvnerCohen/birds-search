<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Common_rock_thrush.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Common_rock_thrush.jpg/220px-Common_rock_thrush.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="274" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Common_rock_thrush.jpg/330px-Common_rock_thrush.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Common_rock_thrush.jpg/440px-Common_rock_thrush.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1029" data-file-height="1280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Common_rock_thrush.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Boulmane de Dades, Morocco</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>common rock thrush</b> (<i>Monticola saxatilis</i>),<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> also known as <b>rufous-tailed rock thrush</b> or simply <b>rock thrush</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chat</a> belonging to the family <a href="/wiki/Muscicapidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Muscicapidae">Muscicapidae</a>. It was formerly placed in the family <a href="/wiki/Turdidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Turdidae">Turdidae</a>. The scientific name is from  <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. <i>Monticola</i> is from  <i>mons, montis</i> "mountain", and <i>colere</i>, "to dwell", and <i>saxatilis</i> means "rock-frequenting", from <i>saxum</i>, "stone" .<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> across  <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. This species is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, all populations wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> south of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. It is an uncommon visitor to northern Europe. Its range has contracted somewhat at the periphery in recent decades due to <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a>. For example, in the early 20th century it bred in the <a href="/wiki/Jura_Krakowsko-Cz%C4%99stochowska" class="mw-redirect" title="Jura Krakowsko-Częstochowska">Jura Krakowsko-Częstochowska</a> (<a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>) where none occur today,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> but it is not considered globally <a href="/wiki/Endangered" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered">endangered</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_rock_thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a medium-sized but stocky thrush 17–20&#160;cm in length. The summer male is unmistakable, with a blue-grey head, orange underparts and outer tail feathers, dark brown wings and white back. Females and immatures are much less striking, with dark brown scaly upperparts, and paler brown scaly underparts. The outer tail feathers are reddish, like the male.<sup id="cite_ref-Clement_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clement-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_rock_thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG/220px-Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="272" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG/330px-Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG/440px-Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2848" data-file-height="3526" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Monticola_saxatilis_MWNH_1830.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This species breeds in open dry hilly areas, usually above 1500&#160;m. It nests in rock cavities, laying 4–5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>. It is omnivorous, eating a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Insects" class="mw-redirect" title="Insects">insects</a>, berries and small reptiles. The male common rock thrush has a clear and tuneful song.<sup id="cite_ref-Clement_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clement-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
