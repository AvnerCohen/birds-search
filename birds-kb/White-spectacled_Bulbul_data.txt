<p>The <b>white-spectacled bulbul</b> (<i>Pycnonotus xanthopygos</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Bulbul" title="Bulbul">bulbul</a> family. It is 20–25&#160;cm (7.9–9.8&#160;in) in length with a wingspan of 20–25&#160;cm (7.9–9.8&#160;in). These birds live in fruit plantations, gardens, and cities. It is the most common member of the bulbul family in Israel and Lebanon.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In Turkey, it is mainly found in the coastal Mediterranean region, but its range extends from Patara/Gelemiş near Kaş in the west to Türkoğlu in the east. Breeding populations are found from Central and Southern Turkey to Western Syria, Lebanon, Western Jordan, Israel, Sinai and western, central and southern Arabia.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both sexes are similar; juveniles have a browner hood and less obvious eye-rings than the adults. The nest – a small cup of thin twigs, grass stems, leaves and moss – is generally located in bushes and lined with hair, shredded bark and small roots.<sup id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-spectacled_bulbul&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The white-spectacled bulbul was originally described in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Ixos" title="Ixos">Ixos</a></i>. The white-spectacled bulbul is considered to belong to a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a> along with the <a href="/wiki/Himalayan_bulbul" title="Himalayan bulbul">Himalayan bulbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/White-eared_bulbul" title="White-eared bulbul">white-eared bulbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_red-eyed_bulbul" title="African red-eyed bulbul">African red-eyed bulbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_bulbul" title="Cape bulbul">Cape bulbul</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Common_bulbul" title="Common bulbul">common bulbul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Alternate names for the white-spectacled bulbul include the  several names used for other species (black-capped, dark-capped, spectacled and yellow-vented bulbul) and one name (<b>white-eyed bulbul</b>) shared with the <a href="/wiki/Cream-vented_bulbul" title="Cream-vented bulbul">cream-vented bulbul</a>.
</p>
