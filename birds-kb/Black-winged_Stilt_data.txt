<p>The <b>Black-winged Stilt</b>, <b>Common Stilt</b>, or <b>Pied Stilt</b> (<i>Himantopus himantopus</i>) is a widely distributed very long-legged <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Avocet" title="Avocet">avocet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stilt" title="Stilt">stilt</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> (<a href="/wiki/Recurvirostridae" title="Recurvirostridae">Recurvirostridae</a>). Opinions differ as to whether the birds treated under the <a href="/wiki/Scientific_name" title="Scientific name" class="mw-redirect">scientific name</a> <i>H. himantopus</i> ought to be treated as a single <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> and if not, how many species to recognize. Most sources today accept 2–4 species.<sup id="cite_ref-2008a_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008a-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2008b_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008b-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2008c_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008c-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-winged_Stilt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg/220px-Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg/330px-Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg/440px-Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Himantopus_himantopus_leucocephalus_-_Lake_Joondalup.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Adult White-headed Stilt wading</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Adults are 33–36&#160;cm long. They have long pink legs, a long thin black bill and are blackish above and white below, with a white head and neck with a varying amount of black. Males have a black back, often with greenish gloss. Females' backs have a brown hue, contrasting with the black <a href="/wiki/Remiges" title="Remiges" class="mw-redirect">remiges</a>. In the populations that have the top of the head normally white at least in winter, females tend to have less black on head and neck all year round, while males often have much black, particularly in summer. This difference is not clear-cut, however, and males usually get all-white heads in winter.</p>
<p>Immature birds are grey instead of black and have a markedly sandy hue on the wings, with light feather fringes appearing as a whitish line in flight.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-winged_Stilt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy" title="Taxonomy">taxonomy</a> of this bird is still somewhat contentious. Some believe that there are as many as five distinct <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>; others consider some or all of these to be <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>. In addition, two dubious subspecies are also sometimes listed, but not as independent species.</p>
<p>In the most extensive circumscription, with one species and 5–7 subspecies, this bird is often called <b>Common Stilt</b>. The name Black-winged Stilt on the other hand can specifically refer to the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a> <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" title="Nominate subspecies" class="mw-redirect">nominate subspecies</a>. The commonly accepted <a href="/wiki/Taxa" title="Taxa" class="mw-redirect">taxa</a> are:</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Black-winged Stilt</b> proper (<i>Himantopus himantopus himantopus</i> or <i>H. himantopus</i>) <small>(<a href="/wiki/Linnaeus" title="Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a>, 1758)</small> – including proposed subspecies <i>meridionalis</i> (S <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>) and <i>ceylonensis</i> (<a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>)</li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>W <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> region to <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>; localized breeder in <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>) but more widespread during winter; has become a regular migrant to the <a href="/wiki/Marianas" title="Marianas" class="mw-redirect">Marianas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">Saipan</a> and sometimes is seen on other islands in western <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Koror" title="Koror">Koror</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ngeriungs_Islet" title="Ngeriungs Islet" class="mw-redirect">Ngeriungs Islet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peleliu" title="Peleliu">Peleliu</a> of <a href="/wiki/Palau" title="Palau">Palau</a>) since the late 20th-century.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> NW populations migrate south to Africa in winter.</dd>
<dd>Head and neck vary from all-white to white with all-black cap and hindneck, usually with white band across upper back. Sometimes vestigial open black chest band.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Black-necked_Stilt" title="Black-necked Stilt">Black-necked Stilt</a></b> (<i>Himantopus himantopus mexicanus</i>, <i>Himantopus mexicanus mexicanus</i> or <i>H. mexicanus</i>) <small>(<a href="/wiki/Philipp_Ludwig_Statius_M%C3%BCller" title="Philipp Ludwig Statius Müller">P.L.S.Müller</a>, 1776)</small></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd>Southern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> through <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> to N <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a> and NE <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. Northernmost populations migrate south in winter. Intergrades with White-backed Stilt in C Brazil.</dd>
<dd>Head and neck always white with black cap down to the eyeline, white spot above eye, black hindneck. Usually no white band across upper back. Often vestigial open black chest band.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b>White-backed Stilt</b> (<i>Himantopus himantopus melanurus</i>, <i>Himantopus mexicanus melanurus</i> or <i>H. melanurus</i>) <small>(<a href="/wiki/Louis_Jean_Pierre_Vieillot" title="Louis Jean Pierre Vieillot">Vieillot</a>, 1817)</small></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd><a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> from C Peru and N <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> to SE Brazil and south to SC <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>. Intergrades with Black-necked Stilt in C Brazil.</dd>
<dd>Head and neck usually white with black hindneck and a black line from the nape to the eye. Usually has open black chest band and a white band across upper back.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/White-headed_Stilt" title="White-headed Stilt">White-headed Stilt</a></b>, <b>Pied Stilt</b>, or (<a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>) <i><b>Poaka</b></i> (<i>Himantopus himantopus leucocephalus</i> or <i>H. leucocephalus</i>) <small>(<a href="/wiki/John_Gould" title="John Gould">Gould</a>, 1837)</small></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Java" title="Java">Java</a> to <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and New Zealand. Southern population winter in the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> region.</dd>
<dd>Head usually all-white, neck white, black behind and with open black chest band. Usually a white band across upper back.</dd>
</dl>
<ul>
<li><b>Hawaiian Stilt</b> or <i><b>āe<span class="unicode">ʻ</span>o</b></i> (<i>Himantopus himantopus knudseni</i>, <i>Himantopus mexicanus knudseni</i> or <i>H. knudseni</i>) <small>(<a href="/wiki/Leonhard_Hess_Stejneger" title="Leonhard Hess Stejneger">Stejneger</a>, 1887)</small></li>
</ul>
<dl>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Hawaiian_Islands" title="Hawaiian Islands">Hawaiian Islands</a>, where it is the only breeding shorebird</dd>
<dd>Generally similar to Black-necked Stilt, but black on head and neck more extensive, usually extending below the eye.</dd>
</dl>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-winged_Stilt&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology and status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology_and_status">Ecology and status</span></h2>
<p>The breeding <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> of all these stilts is <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshes</a>, shallow <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pond" title="Pond">ponds</a>. Some populations are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and move to the ocean coasts in winter; those in warmer regions are generally resident or short-range <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" title="Vagrancy in birds" class="mw-redirect">vagrants</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the Black-winged Stilt is a regular spring overshoot vagrants north of its normal range, occasionally remaining to breed in northern European countries, for example in Britain in 1987.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>These birds pick up their food from sand or water. They mainly eat insects and crustaceans.</p>
<p>The nest site is a bare spot on the ground near water. These birds often nest in small groups, sometimes with <a href="/wiki/Avocet" title="Avocet">Avocets</a>.</p>
<p>The Hawaiian population is endangered due to habitat loss and probably also introduced predators. The <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a> recognizes 3 species at present, merging the Hawaiian and South American birds with the Black-necked Stilt; consequently, none of the three is listed as <a href="/wiki/Threatened_species" title="Threatened species">threatened species</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2008a_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008a-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2008b_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008b-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2008c_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008c-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The Black-winged Stilt is one of the species to which the <i><a href="/wiki/Agreement_on_the_Conservation_of_African-Eurasian_Migratory_Waterbirds" title="Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds">Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</a></i> applies.</p>
