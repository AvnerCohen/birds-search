<p>The <b>pygmy cormorant</b> (<i>Microcarbo pygmaeus</i>) is a member of the Phalacrocoracidae (<a href="/wiki/Cormorant" title="Cormorant">cormorant</a>) family of <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabirds</a>. It breeds in south-eastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and south-western <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Asia</a>. It is partially <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, with northern populations wintering further south, mostly within its breeding range. It is a rare migrant to <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pygmy_cormorant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The pygmy cormorant occupies an area from the south-east of Europe (east of Italy) and the south-west of temperate Asia, east to <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCNMap_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCNMap-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The largest distribution is in south-east Europe, <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, the Balkan countries, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> (namely the <a href="/wiki/Tigris%E2%80%93Euphrates_river_system" title="Tigris–Euphrates river system">Tigris–Euphrates river system</a>), <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>.
</p><p>In Romania, according to studies of Czech ornithologist Robert Ritter von Dombrowski,<sup id="cite_ref-zobodat_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-zobodat-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> at the end of the 19th century, the pygmy cormorant was present in large colonies in the <a href="/wiki/Danube_Delta" title="Danube Delta">Danube Delta</a>, Brăila and Ialomița Pond, on Vederoasa Lake (<a href="/wiki/Constan%C8%9Ba_County" title="Constanța County">Constanța County</a>), on certain pools and ponds with reeds and willows in <a href="/wiki/Muntenia" title="Muntenia">Muntenia</a>. The number of pygmy cormorant pairs was 10,000 without taking in account the breeding population of the Danube Delta. Populations of pygmy cormorant in Romania have dramatically declined, especially in 1960 when, due to the communist agricultural policies, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Br%C4%83ila_Island" title="Great Brăila Island">Great Brăila Island</a> and important parts of Ialomiţa Pond were drained in order to practice agriculture, so the habitats of a great number of aquatic birds were destroyed. The pygmy cormorant can be found in the Danube Delta, Jijia Largă Pond (<a href="/wiki/Iasi_County" class="mw-redirect" title="Iasi County">Iasi County</a>), probably on Maţa, Rădeanu, Vădeni Ponds (in <a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_County" title="Galați County">Galați County</a>), Cârja Pond (<a href="/wiki/Vaslui_County" title="Vaslui County">Vaslui County</a>), at Vlădeşti on Prut River (<a href="/wiki/Gala%C8%9Bi_County" title="Galați County">Galați County</a>), on Calinovăţ Island from <a href="/wiki/Cara%C8%99-Severin_County" title="Caraș-Severin County">Caraș-Severin County</a>, on Small Brăila Island, on Dunăreni Pond (Mârleanu, Constanța County), in Danubian Plain on Parches Pond-Somova (<a href="/wiki/Tulcea_County" title="Tulcea County">Tulcea County</a>).
</p><p>At global scale, it was estimated that the entire population of pygmy cormorants is 85,000-180,000 individuals (a study effectuated by Wetlands International in 2006) and 74-94% of total population lives in Europe. The biggest colony is in the Danube Delta, numbering 4,000 pairs, but this seems certain to plunge due to a massive canalization scheme, which despite the protected status of the delta, commenced in May 2004. According to a study made by BirdLife International in 2004,<sup id="cite_ref-BirdLIfe_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLIfe-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> it was estimated that population of pygmy cormorant in <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> was 11,500-14,000 pairs and during the winter 1,500-4,000 pairs.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_ecology">Habitat and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pygmy_cormorant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg/220px-BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg/330px-BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg/440px-BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3932" data-file-height="2365" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BirdsVeniceLagoon-pjt.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Pygmy cormorants and <a href="/wiki/Little_egret" title="Little egret">little egret</a> (<i>Egretta garzetta</i>) in the <a href="/wiki/Venetian_Lagoon" title="Venetian Lagoon">Venetian Lagoon</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG/220px-Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG/330px-Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG/440px-Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2767" data-file-height="1656" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phalacrocorax_pygmeus_MWNH_0533.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>Pygmy cormorants like pools with plenty of vegetation, lakes and river deltas. They avoid mountainous and cold and dry areas. They love rice fields or other flooded areas where trees and shrubs can be found. During winter they also go to waters with higher salinity, in estuaries or on barrier lakes. These are birds who can live alone or in groups and they have adapted to human presence. They build <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nests</a> from sticks and reeds in dense vegetation, in trees, shrubs, willows but occasionally in reeds on small floating islets. At the end of May, beginning of June, both parents incubate for 27–30 days, and nestlings become independent after 70 days. The young are fed by their parents with small fish and other aquatic animals.<sup id="cite_ref-Kiss2002_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kiss2002-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This bird frequently shares the same type of habitat with egrets, herons and spoonbills.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pygmy_cormorant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The pygmy cormorant is a species with <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> strongly affected by human actions. Threats include the drainage and serious degradation of wetlands and their associated woodland, water pollution, disturbance, and poaching as well as drowning in fishing nets.
</p><p>Being a great fish consumer and destroyer of fishing nets, it is often persecuted by fishermen. In Romania, pond drainage located on the inferior course of the Danube for agricultural purposes, the accidental death of birds captive in improvised fishing nets, nest destruction by professional fishermen in order to protect fish resources decreased of the number of individuals which put the species on the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> (IUCN) <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">Red List</a> as a vulnerable species. It is also hunted in leisure activities, and in Iran the pygmy cormorant is commercialized for cooking.
</p><p>This species is distinguished from the great cormorant and the common shag by its much smaller size, lighter build, and long tail.
</p><p>It feeds mainly on fish, often hunting in groups, and perches in trees between fishing expeditions.
</p><p>The pygmy cormorant is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.<sup id="cite_ref-aewa_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aewa-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
