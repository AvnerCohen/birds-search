<p>The <b>broad-billed roller</b> (<i>Eurystomus glaucurus</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Coraciidae" title="Coraciidae">roller</a> family of birds which breeds across tropical Africa and Madagascar in all but the driest regions. It is a wet season breeder, which <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> from the northern and southern areas of its range towards the moister equatorial belt in the dry season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_roller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The broad-billed roller was originally described in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Coracias" title="Coracias">Coracias</a></i>. Formerly, some authorities have considered the broad-billed roller to have been a subspecies of the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_dollarbird" title="Oriental dollarbird">oriental dollarbird</a>. Alternate names for the broad-billed roller include the <b>African broad-billed roller</b> and <b>cinnamon roller</b>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_roller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li><i>E. g. aethiopicus</i> <small><a href="/wiki/Oscar_Neumann" title="Oscar Neumann">Neumann</a>, 1905</small>, the Nile Valley broad-billed roller or Abyssinian broad-billed roller is found in <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a></li>
<li><i>E. g. afer</i> <small>(<a href="/wiki/John_Latham_(ornithologist)" title="John Latham (ornithologist)">Latham</a>, 1790)</small>, originally described as a separate species in the genus <i>Coracias</i>, is found from <a href="/wiki/Senegambia" title="Senegambia">Senegambia</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Republic of Congo">Democratic Republic of Congo</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Sudan" title="South Sudan">South Sudan</a></li>
<li><i>E. g. suahelicus</i> <small>Neumann, 1905</small>, the East African broad-billed roller, is found in from <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Zambia" title="Zambia">Zambia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mozambique" title="Mozambique">Mozambique</a> and north-eastern <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a></li>
<li><i>E. g. glaucurus</i> <small>(<a href="/wiki/Statius_Muller" class="mw-redirect" title="Statius Muller">Statius Muller</a>, 1776)</small>, the Madagascar roller or Madagascar broad-billed roller, breeds in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> but ranges to mainland Africa in winter</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_roller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The broad-billed roller is 29â30&#160;cm in length. It has a warm back and head, lilac foreneck and breast, with the rest of the plumage mainly brown. The broad bill is bright yellow. Sexes are similar, but the juvenile is a drabber version of the adult, with a pale breast. 
The broad-billed roller is striking in its strong direct flight, with the brilliant blues of the wings and tail contrasting with the brown back.
</p><p>The call of the broad-billed roller is a snarling <i>k-k-k-k-k-r-r-r-r-r</i> sound.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_roller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a species of open woodland with some tall trees, preferably near water. These rollers often perch prominently on trees, posts or overhead wires, like giant <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrikes</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_roller&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They are inactive for most of the day, apart from chasing intruders, but in late afternoon they hunt for the swarming ants and termites on which they feed, sometimes in groups of 100 or more rollers. They drink like <a href="/wiki/Hirundinidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Hirundinidae">swallows</a>, dipping their bills into water in flight.
</p><p>This bird nests in an unlined hole in a tree cavity, laying 2-3 <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>.
</p>
