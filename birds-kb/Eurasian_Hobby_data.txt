<p>The <b>Eurasian Hobby</b> (<i>Falco subbuteo</i>), or just simply <b>Hobby</b>, is a small slim <a href="/wiki/Falcon" title="Falcon">falcon</a>. It belongs to a rather close-knit group of similar falcons often considered a <a href="/wiki/Subgenus" title="Subgenus">subgenus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hypotriorchis" title="Hypotriorchis" class="mw-redirect">Hypotriorchis</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>Adults are slate-grey above with a dark crown and 2 short black moustachial stripes. The throat is unstreaked white, thighs and undertail coverts are unstreaked rufous and rest of the underparts are whitish with black streaks. Close views enable the red "trousers" and vent to be seen. Sexes are similar. Juveniles are generally much browner, with scaled upper parts and streaked buffy thighs and undertail coverts.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Hobby has a distinct first-summer plumage.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span></h2>
<p>This species was first described by <a href="/wiki/Carolus_Linnaeus" title="Carolus Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a> in his <i>Systema naturae</i> in 1758 as <i>Falco subbuteo</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Currently two subspecies are recognized:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>subbuteo</i> – the nominate race is resident in Africa, Europe and Central and East Asia, winters in Central and South Africa and South Asia</li>
<li><i>streichi</i>, described by Hartert and Neumann in 1907, is smaller in size and is found further east of <i>subbuteo'</i>s distribution range</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_status">Distribution and status</span></h2>
<p>This species breeds across <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is a long-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span></h2>
<p>It is a bird of open country such as farmland, marshes, taiga and savannah. They are widespread in lowlands with scattered small woods. It is an elegant <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>, appearing sickle-like in flight with its long pointed wings and square tail, often resembling a swift when gliding with folded wings. It flies powerfully and fast. It will take large insects, such as <a href="/wiki/Dragonfly" title="Dragonfly">dragonflies</a>, which it transfers from talons to beak and eats while soaring slowly in circles.<sup id="cite_ref-strayf3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strayf3-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> It also captures small bats and small birds like swallows, swifts, pipits etc. in flight. Its speed and aerobatic skills enable it to take <a href="/wiki/Hirundinidae" title="Hirundinidae" class="mw-redirect">swallows</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Swift" title="Swift">swifts</a> on the wing, and <a href="/wiki/Barn_Swallow" title="Barn Swallow">Barn Swallows</a> or <a href="/wiki/House_Martin" title="House Martin" class="mw-redirect">House Martins</a> have a characteristic "hobby" alarm call. It is known to harass swallows while they are roosting and dispersing from roosts.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> When not breeding, it is crepuscular, hawking principally in the mornings and evenings. While on migration, they may move in small groups.</p>
<p>Hobbies nest in old nests of crows and other birds. The tree selected is most often one in a hedge or on the extreme edge of a spinney, whence the bird can observe intruders from a considerable distance. It lays 2–4 eggs. Incubation is said to take 28 days and both parents share in this duty, though the female does the greater part.<sup id="cite_ref-fbi5_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fbi5-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It is a very bold and courageous bird and was used in falconry, trained to hawk birds like quails, larks, hoopoes, drongos etc.<sup id="cite_ref-jerdon_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jerdon-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
