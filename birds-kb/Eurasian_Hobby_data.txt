<p>The <b>Eurasian hobby</b> (<i>Falco subbuteo</i>) or just simply <b>hobby</b>, is a small, slim <a href="/wiki/Falcon" title="Falcon">falcon</a>. It belongs to a rather close-knit group of similar falcons often considered a <a href="/wiki/Subgenus" title="Subgenus">subgenus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Hypotriorchis" class="mw-redirect" title="Hypotriorchis">Hypotriorchis</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Helbig1994_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Helbig1994-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Wink1998_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wink1998-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Nittinger2005_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nittinger2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">formal description</a> of the Eurasian hobby was by the Swedish naturalist <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> in 1758 in the <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">tenth edition</a> of his <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_Naturae" title="Systema Naturae">Systema Naturae</a></i> under the present <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">binomial name</a> <i>Falco subbuteo</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Linnaeus1758_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Linnaeus1758-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The genus name <i>falco</i> derives from Late Latin <i>falx</i>, <i>falcis</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Sickle" title="Sickle">sickle</a>, referring to the claws of the bird.<sup id="cite_ref-SOED_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOED-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The species name <i>subbuteo</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>sub</i>, "near to" and <i>buteo</i>, " buzzard" .<sup id="cite_ref-job_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The species' English name comes from <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>hobé</i> or <i>hobet</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-OED_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OED-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It became the <a href="/wiki/Trademark" title="Trademark">trademark</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Subbuteo" title="Subbuteo">Subbuteo</a> games company after its creator, who was an ornithologist, was refused permission to register "Hobby".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Currently two subspecies are recognized:
</p>
<ul><li><i>F. s. subbuteo</i>: the <a href="/wiki/Nominate_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate race">nominate race</a> is resident in Africa, Europe and Central and East Asia, winters in Central and South Africa and South Asia</li>
<li><i>F. s. streichi</i>: described by Hartert and Neumann in 1907, is smaller in size and is found further east of <i>F. s. subbuteo</i><span style="padding-left:0.15em;">&#39;</span>s distribution range</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adults are slate-grey above with a dark crown and two short black moustachial stripes. The throat is unstreaked white, thighs and undertail coverts are unstreaked rufous and rest of the underparts are whitish with black streaks. Close views enable the red "trousers" and vent to be seen. Sexes are similar. Juveniles are generally much browner, with scaled upper parts and streaked buffy thighs and undertail coverts.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The hobby has a distinct first-summer plumage.<sup id="cite_ref-small1992_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-small1992-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This falcon is 29–36&#160;cm (11–14&#160;in) in length with a wingspan of 74–84&#160;cm (29–33&#160;in) and a weight of 175–285&#160;g (6.2–10.1&#160;oz).<sup id="cite_ref-europeanraptors_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europeanraptors-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_status">Distribution and status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species breeds across <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Palearctc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Palearctc (page does not exist)">Asia</a>. It is a long-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and Asia.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_hobby&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG/220px-Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="285" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG/330px-Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG/440px-Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2227" data-file-height="2882" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Falco_subbuteo_MWNH_2030.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Museum Wiesbaden, Germany">Museum Wiesbaden, Germany</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif/lossy-page1-220px-Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif/lossy-page1-330px-Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif/lossy-page1-440px-Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3840" data-file-height="2383" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Boomvalk_Falco_subbuteo_Jos_Zwarts_2.tif" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Drawing by Jos Zwarts</div></div></div>
<p>It is a bird of open country such as <a href="/wiki/Farmland" class="mw-redirect" title="Farmland">farmland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Savannah" class="mw-redirect" title="Savannah">savannah</a>. They are widespread in lowlands with scattered small woods. It is an elegant <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>, appearing sickle-like in flight with its long pointed wings and square tail, often resembling a swift when gliding with folded wings. It is fast and powerful in flight and will take large insects, such as <a href="/wiki/Dragonfly" title="Dragonfly">dragonflies</a>, which it transfers from talons to beak and eats while soaring slowly in circles.<sup id="cite_ref-strayf3_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strayf3-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It also captures small <a href="/wiki/Bat" title="Bat">bats</a><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and small <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> in flight. Its speed and aerobatic skills enable it to take <a href="/wiki/Hirundinidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Hirundinidae">swallows</a> and even <a href="/wiki/Swift" title="Swift">swifts</a> on the wing, and <a href="/wiki/Barn_swallow" title="Barn swallow">barn swallows</a> or <a href="/wiki/Common_house_martin" title="Common house martin">house martins</a> have a characteristic "hobby" alarm call. It is known to harass swallows while they are roosting and dispersing from roosts.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> When not breeding, it is <a href="/wiki/Crepuscular" class="mw-redirect" title="Crepuscular">crepuscular</a>, hawking principally in the mornings and evenings. While on migration, they may move in small groups.
</p><p>Hobbies nest in old nests of <a href="/wiki/Crow" title="Crow">crows</a> and other birds. The tree selected is most often one in a hedge or on the extreme edge of a <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/spinney" class="extiw" title="wikt:spinney">spinney</a>, from where the bird can observe intruders from a considerable distance. It lays 2–4 eggs. Incubation is said to take 28 days and both parents share in this duty, though the female does the greater part.<sup id="cite_ref-fbi5_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fbi5-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is a very bold and courageous bird and was used in falconry, trained to hawk birds like <a href="/wiki/Quail" title="Quail">quails</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">larks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hoopoe" title="Hoopoe">hoopoes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drongo" title="Drongo">drongos</a>, etc.<sup id="cite_ref-jerdon_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jerdon-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
