<p>The <b>Black-headed Plover</b> or <b>Black-headed Lapwing</b> (<i>Vanellus tectus</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Lapwing" title="Lapwing">lapwing</a>, a group of largish <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Charadriidae" title="Charadriidae">Charadriidae</a>. It is a resident breeder across sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> from <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, although it has seasonal movements. It lays two or three eggs on a ground scrape.</p>
<p>These are conspicuous and unmistakable <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. They are medium-large waders with a black head other than a white forehead, lower face and bands across the rear head and nape. There is a wispy black crest like <a href="/wiki/Northern_Lapwing" title="Northern Lapwing">Northern Lapwing</a> and the bill and legs are red. The tail is white, tipped black.</p>
<p>In flight, Black-headed Plover's upperwings have black flight feathers and brown coverts separated by a white bar. The underwings are white with black flight feathers.</p>
<p>This species is a common breeder in wet lowland habitats close to water. It often feeds in drier habitats, such as golf courses and grassy scrub, picking <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other invertebrates from the ground.</p>
<p>Black-headed Plover has a metallic <i>tink-tink</i> call.</p>
