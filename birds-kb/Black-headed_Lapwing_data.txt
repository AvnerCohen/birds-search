<p>The <b>black-headed lapwing</b> or <b>black-headed plover</b> (<i>Vanellus tectus</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Lapwing" title="Lapwing">lapwing</a>, a group of largish <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Charadriidae" title="Charadriidae">Charadriidae</a>. It is a resident breeder across sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> from <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, although it has seasonal movements. It lays two or three eggs on a ground scrape.
</p><p>These are conspicuous and unmistakable <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. They are medium-large waders with a black head other than a white forehead, lower face and bands across the rear head and nape. There is a wispy black crest like <a href="/wiki/Northern_lapwing" title="Northern lapwing">northern lapwing</a> and the bill and legs are red. The tail is white, tipped black.
</p><p>In flight, the black-headed lapwing's upperwings have black flight feathers and brown coverts separated by a white bar. The underwings are white with black flight feathers.
</p><p>This species is a common breeder in wet lowland habitats close to water. It often feeds in drier habitats, such as golf courses and grassy scrub, picking <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other invertebrates from the ground.
</p><p>The black-headed lapwing has a metallic <i>tink-tink</i> call.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-headed_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The black-headed lapwing was described by the French polymath <a href="/wiki/Georges-Louis_Leclerc,_Comte_de_Buffon" title="Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon">Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon</a> in 1781 in his <i>Histoire Naturelle des Oiseaux</i> from a specimen collected in Senegal.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The bird was also illustrated in a hand-coloured plate engraved by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois-Nicolas_Martinet" title="François-Nicolas Martinet">François-Nicolas Martinet</a> in the <i>Planches Enluminées D'Histoire Naturelle</i> which was produced under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Edme-Louis_Daubenton" title="Edme-Louis Daubenton">Edme-Louis Daubenton</a> to accompany Buffon's text.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Neither the plate caption nor Buffon's description included a scientific name but in 1783 the Dutch naturalist <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Boddaert" title="Pieter Boddaert">Pieter Boddaert</a> coined the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Charadrius tectus</i> in his catalogue of the <i>Planches Enluminées</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The black-headed lapwing is now placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Vanellus" title="Vanellus">Vanellus </a></i> that was erected by the French zoologist <a href="/wiki/Mathurin_Jacques_Brisson" title="Mathurin Jacques Brisson">Mathurin Jacques Brisson</a> in 1760.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ioc_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The generic name <i>Vanellus</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Medieval_Latin" title="Medieval Latin">Medieval Latin</a> for a "lapwing". It is a diminutive of the Latin <i>vanus</i> meaning "winnowing" or "fan". The <a href="/wiki/Specific_epithet" class="mw-redirect" title="Specific epithet">specific epithet</a> <i>tectus</i> is Latin for "covered" (i.e. blackcrowned).<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognised:<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>V. t. tectus</i> (<a href="/wiki/Pieter_Boddaert" title="Pieter Boddaert">Boddaert</a>, 1783) – southwest Mauritania to Ethiopia south to Ivory Coast and Uganda</li>
<li><i>V. t. latifrons</i> (<a href="/wiki/Anton_Reichenow" title="Anton Reichenow">Reichenow</a>, 1881) – south Somalia to east Kenya</li></ul>
