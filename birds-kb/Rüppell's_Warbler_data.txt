<p>The <b>Rüppell's Warbler</b> (<i>Sylvia rueppelli</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> of the genus <i>Sylvia</i>. It breeds in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and neighbouring islands. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in north east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. This is a rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. The name is occasionally cited as "Rueppell's Warbler".</p>
<p>It is a typical "sylvia" warbler, similar in size but slimmer than <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_Warbler" title="Sardinian Warbler">Sardinian Warbler</a>. The adults have a plain grey back and paler grey underparts. The bill is fine and pointed, with brown legs and red eyes. The striking male has a black head and, usually, a black throat, separated by a white <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> streak ("moustache"). Females have a pale throat, and the head is grey rather than black. Their grey back has a brownish tinge. The song is a slower, deeper rattle than that of Sardinian Warbler.</p>
<p>Together with the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Warbler" title="Cyprus Warbler">Cyprus Warbler</a> it forms a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a> with dark throats, white <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> streaks and light <a href="/wiki/Remiges" title="Remiges" class="mw-redirect">remiges</a> fringes. This in turn is related to the species of Mediterranean and <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> <i>Sylvia</i> warblers that have a naked eye-ring, namely the <a href="/wiki/Subalpine_Warbler" title="Subalpine Warbler">Subalpine Warbler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_Warbler" title="Sardinian Warbler">Sardinian Warbler</a> and <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9n%C3%A9tries%27s_Warbler" title="Ménétries's Warbler" class="mw-redirect">Ménétries's Warbler</a>. Both groups have a white malar area, but this may not form a clear streak in the latter group; above the white, the heads of males are uniformly dark.(Helbig 2001, Jønsson &amp; Fjeldså 2006)</p>
<p>These small insectivorous <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> are found in thick thorny shrubs where they build their nests and lay four to six eggs.</p>
<p>The species is named after the German zoologist and explorer <a href="/wiki/Eduard_R%C3%BCppell" title="Eduard Rüppell">Eduard Rüppell</a> (1794–1884).</p>
