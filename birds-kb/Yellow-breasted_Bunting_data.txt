<p>The <b>Yellow-breasted Bunting</b> (<i>Emberiza aureola</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasian</a> <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> (Emberizidae).</p>
<p>This bird is similar in size to a <a href="/wiki/Reed_Bunting" title="Reed Bunting">Reed Bunting</a>, but longer-billed. The breeding male has bright yellow <a href="/w/index.php?title=Underparts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Underparts (page does not exist)">underparts</a> with black flank streaks, brown <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upperparts&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Upperparts (page does not exist)">upperparts</a>, black face and throat <a href="/wiki/Bar" title="Bar">bar</a><sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//toolserver.org/%7Edispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Yellow-breasted_Bunting&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn"><span title="Link needs disambiguation from October 2012">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>, and a pink lower mandible. The female has a heavily streaked grey-brown back, and less intensely yellow underparts. She has a whitish face with dark crown, eye and cheek stripes. The juvenile is similar, but the background colour of the underparts and face is buff.</p>
<p>The call is a distinctive <i>zick</i>, and the song is a clear <i>tru-tru, tri-tri</i>.</p>
<p>It breeds in northeastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and across northern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in southeast Asia, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, and southern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. It is a rare but regular wanderer to western Europe.</p>
<p>The Yellow-breasted Bunting breeds in open scrubby areas, often near water, and it is very common in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. It lays 4-6 eggs in a nest on the ground. Its food consists of insects when feeding young, and otherwise seeds.</p>
<p>It was formerly classified as a <a href="/wiki/Near_Threatened" title="Near Threatened">Near Threatened</a> species by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> But new research has shown it to be rarer than it was believed. Consequently, it is uplisted to <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">Vulnerable</a> status in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN2012_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN2012-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
