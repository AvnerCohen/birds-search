<p>The <b>marbled duck</b>, or <b>marbled teal</b> (<i>Marmaronetta angustirostris</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Threatened_species" title="Threatened species">threatened</a>, medium-sized species of <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">duck</a> from southern Europe, northern Africa, and western and central <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Asia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The scientific name, <i>Marmaronetta angustirostris</i>, comes from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a> <i>marmaros</i>, marbled and <i>netta</i>, a duck, and <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>angustus</i>, narrow or small and <i>-rostris</i> <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">billed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DSBN_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSBN-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span id="Distribution.2C_habitat_and_breeding"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution,_habitat_and_breeding">Distribution, habitat and breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marbled_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution, habitat and breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG/220px-Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG/330px-Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG/440px-Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4097" data-file-height="2673" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marmaronetta_angustirostris_MWNH_1005.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This duck formerly bred in large numbers in the Mediterranean region, but is now restricted to a few sites in southern Spain, southern Italy, northwest Africa and the broader <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a>. Further east it survives in the <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamian_Marshes" title="Mesopotamian Marshes">Mesopotamian marshland</a> in southern Iraq and in Iran (<a href="/wiki/Shadegan" title="Shadegan">Shadegan</a> Marshes - the world's most important site), as well as isolated pockets in <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, South <a href="/wiki/European_Russia" title="European Russia">European Russia</a>, western India and western China. In general the species has nomadic tendencies. In some areas birds disperse from the breeding grounds, and have been encountered in the winter period in the <a href="/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a> zone, south of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Its preferred breeding habitat is temporary and shallow fresh, <a href="/wiki/Brackish" class="mw-redirect" title="Brackish">brackish</a> or <a href="/wiki/Alkaline" class="mw-redirect" title="Alkaline">alkaline</a> waters with densely vegetated shores in regions that otherwise are fairly dry. It may also breed in coastal lagoons, along slow rivers or man-made waters like reservoirs. The on average <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr> 12 eggs are placed in a nest covered by dense vegetation at the waters edge. It is usually on the ground, but occasionally higher among reeds or on huts made from reeds.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They are common in captive collections but are a nervous and flighty bird.
</p><p>These are gregarious birds, at times even when nesting. Outside the breeding season flocks are often small, although large wintering flocks have been reported in some areas. The largest winter concentration known is in <a href="/wiki/Kh%C5%ABzest%C4%81n_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Khūzestān Province">Khuzestan</a>, Iran.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011, a group of Iraqi ornithologists counted a single flock of the rare marbled teal on the lakes of the <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamian_Marshes" title="Mesopotamian Marshes">Iraqi marshes</a>, numbering at least 40,000 birds.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description_and_diet">Description and diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marbled_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description and diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg/220px-Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg/330px-Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg/440px-Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3298" data-file-height="2638" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marmaronetta_angustirostris_portrait.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Close-up of head</div></div></div>
<p>The marbled duck is approximately 39–42&#160;cm (15–17&#160;in) long. Adults are a pale sandy-brown colour, diffusely blotched off-white, with a dark eye-patch and shaggy head. The female averages smaller than the male, but otherwise the sexes are alike. Juveniles are similar but with more off-white blotches. In flight, the wings look pale without a marked pattern, and no <a href="/wiki/Speculum_feathers" title="Speculum feathers">speculum</a> on the secondaries.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>These birds feed mainly in shallow water by dabbling or up-ending, occasionally diving.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Adults feed mostly on seeds (for example, from <i><a href="/wiki/Scirpus" title="Scirpus">Scirpus</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Ruppia" title="Ruppia">Ruppia</a></i>), but also take significant quantities of invertebrates (especially aquatic insect larvae and pupae, tiny crustaceans, and—highly unusual for a duck—<a href="/wiki/Ant" title="Ant">ants</a>) and green plants (for example, <i><a href="/wiki/Potamogeton" title="Potamogeton">Potamogeton</a></i>). Their <a href="/wiki/Gizzard" title="Gizzard">gizzard</a> allows them to break down seeds and the <a href="/wiki/Pecten_(biology)" title="Pecten (biology)">lamellae</a> in their beak allow them to <a href="/wiki/Filter_feed" class="mw-redirect" title="Filter feed">filter feed</a> on <a href="/wiki/Zooplankton" title="Zooplankton">zooplanktonic</a> organisms. Young marbled ducks feed mostly on invertebrates. Although they may take tiny seeds, they lack the large gizzard necessary to break down the larger seeds commonly consumed by adults.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Marbled_duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird is considered <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">vulnerable</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> due to a reduction in population caused by <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a> and hunting. It is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
