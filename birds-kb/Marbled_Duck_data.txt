<p>The <b>Marbled Duck</b>, or <b>Marbled Teal</b> (<i>Marmaronetta angustirostris</i>), is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">duck</a>. It used to be included among the <a href="/wiki/Dabbling_duck" title="Dabbling duck" class="mw-redirect">dabbling ducks</a>, but is now classed as a <a href="/wiki/Diving_duck" title="Diving duck">diving duck</a>.</p>
<p>This duck formerly bred in large numbers in the Mediterranean region, but is now restricted to a few sites in southern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, northwest <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and in Israel. In the east it survives in the <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamian_Marshes" title="Mesopotamian Marshes">Mesopotamian marshland</a> in southern <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> (<a href="/wiki/Shadegan" title="Shadegan">Shadegan</a> Marshes - the world's most important site), as well as isolated pockets in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and further to the east in western <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and western <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_0-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Its breeding habitat is lowland where they lay their eggs in long grass or in high trees. They may lay 7 to 10 cream eggs. They are common in private collections but are a nervous and flighty bird. In some areas birds disperse from the breeding grounds, and have been encountered in the winter period in the <a href="/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a> zone, south of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>.</p>
<p>The Marbled Duck is approximately 39–42 centimetres (15–17 in) long. Adults are a pale sandy-brown colour, diffusely blotched off-white, with a dark eye-patch and shaggy head. Juveniles are similar but with more off-white blotches. In flight, the wings look pale without a marked pattern, and no <a href="/wiki/Speculum_feathers" title="Speculum feathers">speculum</a> on the secondaries.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>These are gregarious birds, at times even when nesting. Outside the breeding season flocks are often small, although large wintering flocks have been reported in some areas. The largest winter concentration known is in <a href="/wiki/Kh%C5%ABzest%C4%81n_Province" title="Khūzestān Province" class="mw-redirect">Khuzestan</a>, Iran.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In 2011, a group of Iraqi ornithologists counted a single flock of the rare marbled teal on the lakes of the <a href="/wiki/Mesopotamian_Marshes" title="Mesopotamian Marshes">Iraqi marshes</a>, numbering at least 40,000 birds.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>These birds feed mainly in shallow water by dabbling or up-ending, occasionally diving. Little is known of their diet.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>This bird is considered vulnerable due to a reduction in population caused by habitat destruction and hunting. It is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The scientific name, <i>Marmaronetta angustirostris</i>, comes from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Greek</a> <i>marmaros</i>, marbled and <i>netta</i>, a duck, and <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>angustus</i>, narrow or small and <i>rostris</i> <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">billed</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DSBN_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSBN-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
