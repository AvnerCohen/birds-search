<p>The <b>Barolo shearwater</b> (<i>Puffinus baroli</i>), also known as the <b>North Atlantic little shearwater</b> or <b>Macaronesian shearwater</b>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Shearwater" title="Shearwater">shearwater</a> which breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canaries</a> of <a href="/wiki/Macaronesia" title="Macaronesia">Macaronesia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Ocean" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic Ocean">North Atlantic Ocean</a>. <i>Puffinus</i> is a <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> <a href="/wiki/Loanword" title="Loanword">loanword</a> based on the English "puffin" and its variants, such as  poffin, pophyn and puffing,<sup id="cite_ref-oedpuffin_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oedpuffin-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> that referred to the cured carcass of the fat nestling of the <a href="/wiki/Manx_shearwater" title="Manx shearwater">Manx shearwater</a>, a former delicacy. The specific <i>baroli</i> refers to Carlo Tencredi Falletti, marquis of <a href="/wiki/Barolo,_Piedmont" title="Barolo, Piedmont">Barolo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job90_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job90-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barolo_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was previously considered <a href="/wiki/Conspecific" class="mw-redirect" title="Conspecific">conspecific</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Little_shearwater" title="Little shearwater">little shearwater</a> (<i>Puffinus assimilis</i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mitochondrial_DNA" title="Mitochondrial DNA">Mitochondrial DNA</a> <a href="/wiki/Cytochrome_b" title="Cytochrome b">cytochrome <i>b</i></a> <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" class="mw-redirect" title="DNA sequence">sequence</a> analysis (Austin <i>et al.</i>, 2004) indicates, however, that <i>baroli</i> and <i>boydi</i> are very close to the <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate subspecies</a> of <a href="/wiki/Audubon%27s_shearwater" title="Audubon&#39;s shearwater">Audubon's shearwater</a>. Whether the morphological distinctness and the non-overlapping ranges, or the genetic similarity<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> are considered to be more significant is a matter of opinion, and the <a href="/wiki/Taxonomy_(biology)" title="Taxonomy (biology)">taxonomic</a> status of these birds remains equivocal.  Although some institutions (such as <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a>) retain the forms <i>baroli</i> and <i>boydi</i> within little shearwater, this is as a result of inaccurate <a href="/wiki/Splitters_and_lumpers" class="mw-redirect" title="Splitters and lumpers">lumping</a> in the past, and is not supported by modern evidence.   The <a href="/wiki/British_Ornithologists%27_Union" title="British Ornithologists&#39; Union">British Ornithologists' Union</a> has accepted <i>P. baroli</i> as a distinct species (Sangster et al. 2005), as has <i><a href="/wiki/Clements_Checklist" class="mw-redirect" title="Clements Checklist">Clements Checklist</a></i>.  The <a href="/wiki/American_Ornithologists%27_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="American Ornithologists&#39; Union">American Ornithologists' Union</a> followed in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Birdwatch_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birdwatch-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barolo_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Features that distinguish the Barolo shearwater from the <a href="/wiki/Manx_shearwater" title="Manx shearwater">Manx shearwater</a> and other North Atlantic <i><a href="/wiki/Puffinus" title="Puffinus">Puffinus</a></i> species include the pale face, silvery panel in the upperwings, shorter more rounded wings, and blue feet.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As well as the pale face with the darkly contrasting eye.<sup id="cite_ref-WindBirds_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WindBirds-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barolo_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Barolo shearwater occurs in the <a href="/wiki/Macaronesia" title="Macaronesia">Macaronesian</a> Islands and breeds on the <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desertas" class="mw-redirect" title="Desertas">Desertas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savage_Islands" title="Savage Islands">Selvagens</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canary_islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Canary islands">Canary islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Avibase_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Avibase-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The largest colony, of 1400 pairs, occurs on the Selvagen Islands.<sup id="cite_ref-Birds_&amp;_Company_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Birds_&amp;_Company-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barolo_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Barolo shearwater feeds in the upper 15m of the water column, which is similar to the closely related <a href="/wiki/Audubon%27s_shearwater" title="Audubon&#39;s shearwater">Audubon's shearwater</a> <i>Puffinus lherminieri</i> of the western Atlantic and <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean Sea</a>. Barolo shearwaters do not have a preferred time of day to forage or rest and they may hunt for food during either day or night, although they seem to be more ready to fly in the daylight hours. They feed mainly on fish and <a href="/wiki/Cephalopod" title="Cephalopod">cephalopods</a>, with <i><a href="/wiki/Argonauta_argo" title="Argonauta argo">Argonauta argo</a></i> being the most common cephalopod taken in the Azores but also being part of a diverse selection of cephalopod prey, while the fish taken were almost exclusively <i><a href="/wiki/Phycis" title="Phycis">Phycis</a> spp</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Neves_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Neves-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barolo_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like other Procellariforms, introduced predators (rats and cats) must be their main threats at breeding colonies. In addition, fledglings are attracted to artificial lights at night during their maiden flights from nests to the sea.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On Tenerife, Canary Islands, a decline on the number of birds attracted to lights have been reported, suggesting a population decline on the island.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
