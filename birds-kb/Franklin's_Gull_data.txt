<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_Gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>It breeds in central provinces of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and adjacent states of the northern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory bird</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.</p>
<p>The summer adult's body is white and its back and wings are much darker grey than all other gulls of similar size except the larger <a href="/wiki/Laughing_Gull" title="Laughing Gull">Laughing Gull</a>. The wings have black tips with an adjacent white band. The bill and legs are red. The black hood of the breeding adult is mostly lost in winter.</p>
<p>Young birds are similar to the adult but have less developed hoods and lack the white wing band. They take three years to reach maturity.</p>
<p>Although the bird is uncommon on the coasts of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, it occurs as a rare vagrant to northwest <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, south and west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, with a single record from <a href="/wiki/Eilat" title="Eilat">Eilat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, in 2002 (Smith 2002).</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_Gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h3>
<p>They are <a href="/wiki/Omnivores" title="Omnivores" class="mw-redirect">omnivores</a> like most gulls, and they will scavenge as well as seeking suitable small prey.</p>
<h4><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_Gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span></h4>
<p>The birds breed in colonies near prairie lakes with the nest constructed on the ground, or sometimes floating. The two or three eggs are incubated for about three weeks.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_Gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span></h2>
