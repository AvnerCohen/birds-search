<p><b>Franklin's gull</b> (<i>Leucophaeus pipixcan</i>) is a small (length 12.6–14.2 in, 32–36&#160;cm) <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The genus name <i>Leucophaeus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>leukos</i>, "white", and <i>phaios</i>, "dusky". The specific <i>pipixcan</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Nahuatl" title="Nahuatl">Nahuatl</a> name for a type of gull.<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gray_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gray-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds in central provinces of <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and adjacent states of the northern United States. It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory bird</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">Peru</a>.
</p><p>The summer adult's body is white and its back and wings are much darker grey than all other gulls of similar size except the larger <a href="/wiki/Laughing_gull" title="Laughing gull">laughing gull</a>. The wings have black tips with an adjacent white band. The bill and legs are red. The black hood of the breeding adult is mostly lost in winter.
</p><p>Young birds are similar to the adult but have less developed hoods and lack the white wing band. They take three years to reach maturity.
</p><p><br />
<b>Measurements</b><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Length</b>: 12.6-14.2 in (32-36 cm)</li>
<li><b>Weight</b>: 8.1-10.6 oz (230-300 g)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan</b>: 33.5-37.4 in (85-95 cm)</li></ul>
<p>Although the bird is uncommon on the coasts of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, it occurs as a rare vagrant to northwest <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, south and west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, with a single record from <a href="/wiki/Eilat" title="Eilat">Eilat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, in 2011 (Smith 2011), and a single record from <a href="/wiki/Larnaca" title="Larnaca">Larnaca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, July 2006. At the beginning of 2017 has been observed also in Southern <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, southeast <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>They are <a href="/wiki/Omnivores" class="mw-redirect" title="Omnivores">omnivores</a> like most gulls, and they will scavenge as well as seeking suitable small prey. In the spring, on rivers such as the Bow River large groups will float with the current, sipping the emerging insect hatch. The behaviour includes floating through a particular stretch and returning repeatedly to the same section.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg/220px-Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg/330px-Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg/440px-Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3307" data-file-height="3307" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Leucophaeus_pipixcan_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.125.19.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Leucophaeus pipixcan</i> - <a href="/wiki/MHNT" class="mw-redirect" title="MHNT">MHNT</a></div></div></div>
<p>The birds breed in colonies near prairie lakes with the nest constructed on the ground, or sometimes floating. The two or three eggs are incubated for about three weeks.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Franklin%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bird was named after the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a> explorer Sir <a href="/wiki/John_Franklin" title="John Franklin">John Franklin</a>, who led an <a href="/wiki/John_Franklin#1823:_Marriage_and_third_Arctic_expedition" title="John Franklin">1823 expedition</a> in which the first specimen of Franklin's gull was taken.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
