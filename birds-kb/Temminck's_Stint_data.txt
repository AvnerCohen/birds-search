<p><b>Temminck's stint</b> (<i>Calidris temminckii</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a>. This bird's common name and Latin binomial commemorate the Dutch naturalist <a href="/wiki/Coenraad_Jacob_Temminck" title="Coenraad Jacob Temminck">Coenraad Jacob Temminck</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Beolens2003_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beolens2003-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The genus name is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>kalidris</i> or <i>skalidris</i>, a term used by <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a> for some grey-coloured waterside birds.<sup id="cite_ref-job_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Temminck's stint is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temminck%27s_stint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These birds are very small waders, at 13.5–15&#160;cm (5.3–5.9&#160;in) length. They are similar in size to the <a href="/wiki/Little_stint" title="Little stint">little stint</a> (<i>Calidris minuta</i>) but shorter legged and longer winged. The legs are yellow and the outer tail feathers white, in contrast to little stint's dark legs and grey outer tail feathers.
</p><p>This is a rather drab wader, with mainly plain brown upperparts and head, and underparts white apart from a darker breast. The breeding adult has some brighter rufous mantle feathers to relieve the generally undistinguished appearance. In winter plumage, the general appearance recalls a tiny version of <a href="/wiki/Common_sandpiper" title="Common sandpiper">common sandpiper</a>.
</p><p>The call is a loud trill.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temminck%27s_stint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Stint" title="Stint">stint</a>'s breeding habitat is bogs and marshes in the <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> of Arctic northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Asia</a>. It will breed in southern <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> and occasionally <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. It has a distinctive hovering display flight. It nests in a scrape on the ground, laying 3–4 eggs. Temminck's stint is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering at freshwater sites in tropical <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Subcontinent">Indian Subcontinent</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>.
</p><p>Temminck's stints have an intriguing breeding and parental care system in which males and female parents incubate separate clutches, typically in different locations. Males establish small territories and mate with a female who lays a first clutch of eggs. She then moves to a second territory and mate, and lays a second clutch that she incubates herself. Concurrently, her first male may mate with an incoming second female, who lays her second clutch on his territory. The male thereafter incubates his first mate's first clutch alone.
</p><p>An apparent <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybrid</a> between this species and the <a href="/wiki/Little_stint" title="Little stint">little stint</a> has been reported from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jonsson1996_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jonsson1996-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Temminck%27s_stint&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> forage in soft mud with some vegetation, mainly picking up food by sight. They have a distinctive <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mouse-like</a> feeding behaviour, creeping steadily along the edges of pools. They mostly eat insects and other small invertebrates. They not as gregarious as other <i>Calidris</i> waders, and rarely form large flocks.
</p>
