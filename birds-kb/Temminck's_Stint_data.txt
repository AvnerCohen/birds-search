<p><b>Temminck's Stint</b>, <i>Calidris</i> or <i>Erolia temminckii</i>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Timminck%27s_Stint_(Breeding_plumage)_at_Hodal_I_IMG_9618.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Timminck%27s_Stint_%28Breeding_plumage%29_at_Hodal_I_IMG_9618.jpg/220px-Timminck%27s_Stint_%28Breeding_plumage%29_at_Hodal_I_IMG_9618.jpg" width="220" height="153" class="thumbimage" /></a>

In Breeding plumage near <a href="/wiki/Hodal" title="Hodal">Hodal</a> in <a href="/wiki/Faridabad" title="Faridabad">Faridabad</a> District of <a href="/wiki/Haryana" title="Haryana">Haryana</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</div>

<p>This <a href="/wiki/Stint" title="Stint">stint's</a> breeding habitat is bogs and marshes in the <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> of Arctic northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It will breed in southern <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> and occasionally <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. It has a distinctive hovering display flight. It nests in a scrape on the ground, laying 3–4 eggs. Temminck's Stint is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering at freshwater sites in tropical <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect">Indian Subcontinent</a> and parts of <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>.</p>
<p>These <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> forage in soft mud with some vegetation, mainly picking up food by sight. They have a distinctive <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mouse-like</a> feeding behaviour, creeping steadily along the edges of pools. They mostly eat insects and other small invertebrates. They not as gregarious as other <i>Calidris</i> waders, and rarely form large flocks.</p>
<p>These birds are very small waders, at 13.5–15&#160;cm length similar in size to <a href="/wiki/Little_Stint" title="Little Stint">Little Stint</a>, <i>Calidris</i>/<i>Erolia minuta</i>. They are shorter legged and longer winged than Little Stint. The legs are yellow, and the outer tail feathers white, in contrast to Little Stint's dark legs and grey outer tail feathers.</p>
<p>This is a rather drab wader, with mainly plain brown upperparts and head, and underparts white apart from a darker breast. The breeding adult has some brighter rufous mantle feathers to relieve the generally still undistinguished appearance. In winter plumage, the general appearance recalls a tiny version of <a href="/wiki/Common_Sandpiper" title="Common Sandpiper">Common Sandpiper</a>. The call is a loud trill.</p>
<p>Temminck's Stints have an intriguing breeding and parental care system in which males and female parents incubate separate clutches, typically in different locations. Males establish small territories and mate with a female who lays a first clutch of eggs. She then moves to a second territory and mate, and lays a second clutch that she incubates herself. Concurrently, her first male may mate with an incoming second female, who lays her second clutch on his territory. The male thereafter incubates his first mate's first clutch alone.</p>
<p>This bird was named after <a href="/wiki/Coenraad_Jacob_Temminck" title="Coenraad Jacob Temminck">Coenraad Jacob Temminck</a>, a Dutch naturalist.</p>
<p>Temminck's Stint is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
<p>An apparent <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybrid</a> between this species and the <a href="/wiki/Little_Stint" title="Little Stint">Little Stint</a> has been reported from the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
