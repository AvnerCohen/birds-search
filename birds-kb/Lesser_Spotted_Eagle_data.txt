<p>The <b>lesser spotted eagle</b> (<i>Clanga pomarina</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern European</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>. Like all typical <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagles</a>, it belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>. The typical eagles are often united with the <a href="/wiki/Buteo" title="Buteo">buteos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sea_eagle" title="Sea eagle">sea eagles</a>, and other more heavy-set <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>, but more recently it appears as if they are less distinct from the more slender <a href="/wiki/Accipitrinae" title="Accipitrinae">accipitrine</a> hawks than believed.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_spotted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesserspoteagle.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Lesserspoteagle.JPG/220px-Lesserspoteagle.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/7b/Lesserspoteagle.JPG 1.5x" data-file-width="250" data-file-height="226" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesserspoteagle.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A drawing of a lesser spotted eagle</div></div></div>
<p>This is a medium-sized eagle, about 60&#160;cm (24&#160;in) in length and with a wingspan of 150&#160;cm (59&#160;in). Its head and wing coverts are pale brown and contrast with the generally dark plumage. The head and bill are small for an eagle. Usually, a white patch occurs on the upper wings, and even adults retain a clearly marked white "V" on the rump; the wing markings are absent and the white "V" is not well-defined in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_spotted_eagle" title="Greater spotted eagle">greater spotted eagle</a>.
</p><p>The juvenile has less contrast in the wings, but the <a href="/wiki/Remiges" class="mw-redirect" title="Remiges">remiges</a> bear prominent white spots. It differs from greater spotted eagle juveniles by a lack of <a href="/wiki/Wing_covert" class="mw-redirect" title="Wing covert">wing covert</a> spotting and the presence of a cream-colored neck patch.
</p><p>The call is a dog-like <i>yip</i>.
</p>
<h2><span id="Systematics.2C_taxonomy_and_evolution"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Systematics,_taxonomy_and_evolution">Systematics, taxonomy and evolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_spotted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Systematics, taxonomy and evolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The birds formerly considered to be the resident <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are now considered a separate <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_spotted_eagle" title="Indian spotted eagle">Indian spotted eagle</a> (<i>Clanga hastata</i>), quite distinct and readily separable by <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological</a>, <a href="/wiki/Behavior" title="Behavior">behavioral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecological</a>, and <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" class="mw-redirect" title="DNA sequence">DNA sequence</a> data. The European <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> is actually closer to the greater spotted eagle; their common ancestor seems to have diverged around the middle <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a>, perhaps some 3.6 million years ago,<sup id="cite_ref-ValiNote_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ValiNote-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> from the ancestors of the Indian birds. The "proto-spotted eagle" probably lived in the general region of <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, being split into a northern and a southern lineage when both <a href="/wiki/Glaciers" class="mw-redirect" title="Glaciers">glaciers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a> advanced in Central Asia as the <a href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" class="mw-redirect" title="Last glacial period">last ice age</a> began. The northern lineage subsequently separated into the eastern (greater) and western (lesser) species of today, probably around the Pliocene-<a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> boundary not quite 2 million years ago.<sup id="cite_ref-Parry2002_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parry2002-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Rasmussen2005_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rasmussen2005-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Vali2006_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vali2006-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The spotted eagles as a group are quite distinct from the typical members of <i><a href="/wiki/Aquila_(genus)" title="Aquila (genus)">Aquila</a></i>, the "true eagles".
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg/220px-Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="140" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg/330px-Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg/440px-Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg 2x" data-file-width="533" data-file-height="340" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A two-egg <a href="/wiki/Clutch_(eggs)" title="Clutch (eggs)">clutch</a> in nest</div></div></div>
<p>The present species <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridizes</a> occasionally with the greater spotted eagle. Hybrid birds are almost impossible to identify if not seen up close.<sup id="cite_ref-Vali2004_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vali2004-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_ecology">Distribution and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_spotted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG/220px-Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="215" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG/330px-Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG/440px-Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2387" data-file-height="2332" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aquila_pomarina_MWNH_0818.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, collection of <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>The lesser spotted eagle breeds in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> and southeastward to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-ABCC_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABCC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">winters</a> in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. This is a very wary species of open or lightly wooded country, in which it hunts small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a> and similar terrestrial prey. This eagle lays one to three white, buff-spotted <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a tree nest. As usual for eagles, only in breeding seasons with very abundant prey does more than one young <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a>, but the female starts <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" class="mw-redirect" title="Avian incubation">incubating</a> when the first egg has been laid,  thus the first young to hatch usually outgrows its clutch mate(s) and will kill and even eat them sooner or later.
</p><p>The territories of Lesser Spotted Eagles are spaced regularly and consistently between years in the north-western part of their distribution. The distances between territories vary across the region, although this was not found to be related to breeding success. Synchronous variation in breeding success across the region indicates that the eagles are instead influenced by large-scale factors such as fluctuations in climate or prey availability.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
