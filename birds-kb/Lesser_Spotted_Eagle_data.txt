<p>The <b>Lesser Spotted Eagle</b> (<i>Aquila pomarina</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern European</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>. Like all typical <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagles</a>, it belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>. The typical eagles are often united with the <a href="/wiki/Buteo" title="Buteo">buteos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sea_eagle" title="Sea eagle">sea eagles</a> and other more heavy-set <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>, but more recently it appears as if they are less distinct from the more slender <a href="/wiki/Accipitrinae" title="Accipitrinae">accipitrine</a> hawks than believed.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Spotted_Eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesserspoteagle.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Lesserspoteagle.JPG/220px-Lesserspoteagle.JPG" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesserspoteagle.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A drawing of a lesser spotted eagle.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This is a medium-sized eagle, about 60&#160;cm in length and with a wingspan of 150&#160;cm. Its head and wing coverts are pale brown and contrast with the generally dark plumage. The head and bill are small for an eagle. There is usually a white patch on the upperwings, and even adults retain a clear-marked white V on the rump; the wing markings are absent and the white V is not well-defined in the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Spotted_Eagle" title="Greater Spotted Eagle">Greater Spotted Eagle</a>.</p>
<p>The juvenile has less contrast in the wings, but the <a href="/wiki/Remiges" title="Remiges" class="mw-redirect">remiges</a> bear prominent white spots. It differs from Greater Spotted Eagle juveniles by a lack of <a href="/wiki/Wing_covert" title="Wing covert" class="mw-redirect">wing covert</a> spotting and the presence of a cream-colored neck patch.</p>
<p>The call is a dog-like <i>yip</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Spotted_Eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Systematics, taxonomy and evolution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Systematics.2C_taxonomy_and_evolution">Systematics, taxonomy and evolution</span></h2>
<p>The birds formerly considered to be the resident <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are now considered a separate <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Spotted_Eagle" title="Indian Spotted Eagle">Indian Spotted Eagle</a> (<i>Aquila hastata</i>), quite distinct and readily separable by <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological</a>, <a href="/wiki/Behavior" title="Behavior">behavioral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ecology" title="Ecology">ecological</a> and <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" title="DNA sequence" class="mw-redirect">DNA sequence</a> data. The European <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> is actually closer to the Greater Spotted Eagle; their common ancestor seems to have diverged around the middle <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a>, perhaps some 3.6 million years ago (mya),<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> from the ancestors of the Indian birds. The "proto-Spotted Eagle" probably lived in the general region of <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, being split into a northern and a southern lineage when both <a href="/wiki/Glaciers" title="Glaciers" class="mw-redirect">glaciers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a> advanced in Central Asia as the <a href="/wiki/Last_ice_age" title="Last ice age" class="mw-redirect">last ice age</a> began. The northern lineage subsequently separated into the eastern (Greater) and western (Lesser) species of today, probably around the Pliocene-<a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a> boundary not quite 2 mya.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The spotted eagles as a group are quite distinct from the typical members of <i><a href="/wiki/Aquila_(genus)" title="Aquila (genus)">Aquila</a></i>, the "true eagles".</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg/220px-Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg" width="220" height="140" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aquila_pomarina_nest_with_eggs.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
2-egg <a href="/wiki/Clutch_(eggs)" title="Clutch (eggs)">clutch</a> in nest</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The present species <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridizes</a> occasionally with the Greater Spotted Eagle. Hybrid birds are almost impossible to identify if not seen from up close.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Spotted_Eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and ecology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_ecology">Distribution and ecology</span></h2>
<p>The Lesser Spotted Eagle breeds in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Europe" title="Eastern Europe">Eastern Europe</a> and southeastward to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">winters</a> in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. This is a very wary species of open or lightly wooded country, in which it hunts small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a> and similar terrestrial prey. This eagle lays 1–3 white buff-spotted <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a tree nest. As usual for eagles, only in breeding seasons with very abundant prey does more than one young <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a> however: the female starts <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" title="Avian incubation" class="mw-redirect">incubating</a> when the first egg has been laid, and thus the first young to hatch usually outgrows its clutchmate(s) and will kill and even eat them sooner or later.</p>
