<p>The <b>hooded wheatear</b> (<i>Oenanthe monacha</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wheatear" title="Wheatear">wheatear</a>, a small insectivorous <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> that was formerly classed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae, but is now more generally considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a>, Muscicapidae.
</p><p>This 15.5–17&#160;cm long <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a resident breeder in unvegetated desert from eastern <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabian peninsula">Arabian peninsula</a> used to be in <a href="/wiki/UAE" class="mw-redirect" title="UAE">UAE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a> a scarce breeder in Hajar mountains to <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It occurs annually in <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> on passage. The nest is built in a rock crevice, and 3-6 eggs is the normal clutch.
</p><p>In summer the male hooded wheatear is a white and black bird. The white crown and belly contrast with the black face, back and throat. The tail and rump are white with black central tail <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>.
</p><p>The female is brown, becoming somewhat paler below. The tail pattern is similar to the male's, but the ground colour is buff rather than white.
</p><p>Hooded wheatear feeds on insects, often taken in the air. Its call is a whistled <i>vit</i>, and the song is a harsh chattering.
</p>
