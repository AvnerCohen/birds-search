<p>The <b>Oriental Skylark</b> (<i>Alauda gulgula</i>), also known as the <b>Oriental Lark</b> or <b>Small Skylark</b>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Skylark" title="Skylark" class="mw-redirect">skylark</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect">Indian Subcontinent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. Like other skylarks, it is found in open <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> - often near <a href="/wiki/Waterbodies" title="Waterbodies" class="mw-redirect">waterbodies</a> - where it feeds on <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>.</p>
<p>These skylarks frequently rocket up into the sky, fluttering and singing before descending down to earth. Male Oriental Skylarks may also hover in the air and <a href="/wiki/Birdcall" title="Birdcall" class="mw-redirect">sing</a>, in order to attract a mate.</p>
<p>Oriental Skylarks are about 16&#160;cm long. They have streaked, yellow-brown upper <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a>, with white outer tail <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a> and a short <a href="/wiki/Crest_(feathers)" title="Crest (feathers)">crest</a>. Both sexes are similar.</p>
