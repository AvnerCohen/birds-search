<p>The <b>Oriental skylark</b> (<i>Alauda gulgula</i>), also known as the <b>small skylark</b>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Skylark" class="mw-redirect" title="Skylark">skylark</a> found in the southern, central and eastern <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> . Like other skylarks, it is found in open <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a> where it feeds on <a href="/wiki/Seed_predation" title="Seed predation">seeds</a> and insects.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_skylark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Oriental skylark was <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">described</a> by the English soldier and naturalist <a href="/wiki/James_Franklin_(naturalist)" title="James Franklin (naturalist)">James Franklin</a> in 1831 and given the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Alauda gulgula</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The meaning of the <a href="/wiki/Specific_name_(zoology)" title="Specific name (zoology)">specific epthet</a> <i>gulgula</i> is uncertain but is perhaps a reference to the song as <i>gula</i> is Latin for "throat".<sup id="cite_ref-hbwkey_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbwkey-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other alternate names for the Oriental skylark include <b>eastern skylark</b>, <b>Indian skylark</b> and <b>lesser skylark</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_skylark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thirteen <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li><b>Kashmir skylark</b> (<i>A. g. lhamarum</i>) or <b>Kashmir small skylark</b> - <small><a href="/wiki/Richard_Meinertzhagen" title="Richard Meinertzhagen">Meinertzhagen, R</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Annie_Meinertzhagen" title="Annie Meinertzhagen">Meinertzhagen, A</a>, 1926</small>: Found in the <a href="/wiki/Pamir_Mountains" title="Pamir Mountains">Pamir Mountains</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a></li>
<li><i>A. g. inopinata</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Valentin_Bianchi" title="Valentin Bianchi">Bianchi</a>, 1905</small>: Found on the <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau" title="Tibetan Plateau">Tibetan Plateau</a> and north-western China</li>
<li><i>A. g. vernayi</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Ernst_Mayr" title="Ernst Mayr">Mayr</a>, 1941</small>: Found in the eastern Himalayas and south-western China</li>
<li><b>Western Oriental skylark</b> (<i>A. g. inconspicua</i>) or <b>Turkestan small skylark</b> - <small><a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Severtzov" title="Nikolai Severtzov">Severtsov</a>, 1873</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found from southern Kazakhstan to eastern Iran, Pakistan and north-western India</li>
<li><i>A. g. gulgula</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/James_Franklin_(naturalist)" title="James Franklin (naturalist)">Franklin</a>, 1831</small>: Found from north-central India to Sri Lanka and east to northern Indochina</li>
<li><i>A. g. dharmakumarsinhjii</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Humayun_Abdulali" title="Humayun Abdulali">Abdulali</a>, 1976</small>: Found in west-central India</li>
<li><i>A. g. australis</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/William_Edwin_Brooks" title="William Edwin Brooks">Brooks, WE</a>, 1873</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in south-western India</li>
<li><i>A. g. weigoldi</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hartert" title="Ernst Hartert">Hartert</a>, 1922</small>: Found in central and eastern China</li>
<li><i>A. g. coelivox</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Robert_Swinhoe" title="Robert Swinhoe">R. Swinhoe</a>, 1859</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in southern and south-eastern China, northern Vietnam</li>
<li><i>A. g. sala</i> - <small>R. Swinhoe, 1870</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found on <a href="/wiki/Hainan" title="Hainan">Hainan Island</a> (off south-eastern China)</li>
<li><i>A. g. herberti</i> - <small>Hartert, 1923</small>: Found from central and eastern Thailand to southern Vietnam</li>
<li><i>A. g. wattersi</i> - <small>R. Swinhoe, 1871</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in Taiwan</li>
<li><i>A. g. wolfei</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Masauji_Hachisuka" title="Masauji Hachisuka">Hachisuka</a>, 1930</small>: Found on Luzon (northern Philippines)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_skylark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oriental skylarks are about 16&#160;cm long. They have streaked, yellow-brown upper <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a>, with white outer tail <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a> and a short <a href="/wiki/Crest_(feathers)" title="Crest (feathers)">crest</a>. Both sexes are similar.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Oriental_skylark.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Oriental_skylark.jpg/220px-Oriental_skylark.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Oriental_skylark.jpg/330px-Oriental_skylark.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Oriental_skylark.jpg/440px-Oriental_skylark.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2179" data-file-height="1449" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Oriental_skylark.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>in Bangladesh</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_skylark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These skylarks frequently rocket up into the sky, fluttering and singing before descending down to earth. Male Oriental skylarks may also hover in the air and <a href="/wiki/Birdcall" class="mw-redirect" title="Birdcall">sing</a>, in order to attract a mate.
</p>
