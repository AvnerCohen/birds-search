<p>The <b>Blackstart</b>, <i>Cercomela melanura</i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chat</a> found in <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> regions in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>.</p>
<p>It is a 14–16 cm long bird named for its black tail, which is frequently fanned; the rest of its plumage is bluish-grey or grey-brown (North African races being browner, Middle Eastern races bluer). The sexes are similar, but the male on average has blacker lores. The song is a clear melancholy whistle: <i>CHURlee...TRUloo...CHURlee...TRUlur...</i>, with short phrases from the song used as a call.</p>
<p>It is a species of rocky deserts and mountain slopes which nests in a rock crevice where it lays its 3–5 eggs. It feeds on <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, taken mainly on the ground.</p>
<p>The Blackstart is a confident species, unafraid of man.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackstart&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Detailed distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Detailed_distribution">Detailed distribution</span></h2>
<p>The Blackstart is <a href="/wiki/Resident_bird" title="Resident bird" class="mw-redirect">resident</a> throughout its <a href="/wiki/Range_(biology)" title="Range (biology)">range</a>.</p>
<ul>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, the Blackstart is common in the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_peninsula" title="Sinai peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Sinai peninsula</a></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> the species is primarily found in the <a href="/wiki/Negev_Desert" title="Negev Desert" class="mw-redirect">Negev Desert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arava_Valley" title="Arava Valley" class="mw-redirect">Arava Valley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dead_Sea" title="Dead Sea">Dead Sea</a> areas; it is present in areas of the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_River" title="Jordan River">Jordan River</a> valley further north, but is scarcer there.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, the species is only found in western parts of the country, in areas from the Jordan River valley south through the Dead Sea region to the Arava Valley and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aqaba_Mountains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aqaba Mountains (page does not exist)">Aqaba Mountains</a>.</li>
</ul>
