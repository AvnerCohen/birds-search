<p>The <b>Oriental reed warbler</b> (<i>Acrocephalus orientalis</i>)  is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of eastern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> belonging to the reed warbler genus <i><a href="/wiki/Acrocephalus_(bird)" title="Acrocephalus (bird)">Acrocephalus</a></i>. It was formerly classified as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Great_reed_warbler" title="Great reed warbler">great reed warbler</a> (<i>A. arundinaceus</i>) of the western <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a large warbler, 18–20&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 23–26&#160;cm. The <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> is brown above with a paler rump and whitish tips to the tail feathers. The underparts are whitish below, browner on the flanks and undertail-coverts. There are narrow greyish streaks on the throat and breast. It has a dark eyestripe and a whitish stripe above the eye. The fairly long, heavy <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is brownish above and pink below with a bright orange <a href="/wiki/Gape" class="mw-redirect" title="Gape">gape</a>. The feet are grey.
</p><p>It is slightly smaller, slenderer and shorter-winged than the great reed warbler. Its tail is shorter and more square-ended than that of the <a href="/wiki/Clamorous_reed_warbler" title="Clamorous reed warbler">clamorous reed warbler</a> (<i>A. stentoreus</i>) and it has a slightly longer <a href="/wiki/Flight_feathers#Primaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Flight feathers">primary</a> projection and a slightly shorter and thicker bill. The pale tip to the tail separates it from both species.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" class="mw-redirect" title="Bird song">song</a> is a mixture of warbling phrases and guttural, croaking notes. It is given from a prominent perch such as the top of a reed stem or bush. The bird also has a loud, harsh <i>chack</i> call.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The breeding range covers southern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, northern, central and eastern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It winters in north-east <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and across <a href="/wiki/South-east_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="South-east Asia">South-east Asia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, occasionally reaching <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. It has occurred as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Vagrancy in birds">vagrant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>. It breeds mainly in <a href="/wiki/Reedbed" class="mw-redirect" title="Reedbed">reedbeds</a> and can also be found in marshes, <a href="/wiki/Paddy_field" title="Paddy field">paddy fields</a>, grassland and scrub where it forages for <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Oriental reed warblers have small breeding territories and can achieve high population densities. The <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nest</a> is built 1–1.5 metres above the ground among reed stems. Two to six <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid and are <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" class="mw-redirect" title="Avian incubation">incubated</a> for 12 to 14 days. The young birds <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a> after 10 to 15 days. Major <a href="/wiki/Predator" class="mw-redirect" title="Predator">predators</a> at the nest include the <a href="/wiki/Siberian_weasel" title="Siberian weasel">Siberian weasel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a> of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Elaphe" title="Elaphe">Elaphe</a></i>.
</p>
