<p>The <b>Eyebrowed Thrush</b> (<i>Turdus obscurus</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae.</p>
<p>It breeds in dense <a href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer" class="mw-redirect">coniferous</a> forest and <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> eastwards from Siberia. It is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering south to southeast <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. It is a rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>It nests in trees, laying 4-6 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a neat nest. Migrating birds and wintering birds often form small flocks. It is omnivorous, eating a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earthworm" title="Earthworm">earthworms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Berry" title="Berry">berries</a>.</p>
<p>This is an attractive thrush, with a grey back and head, the latter having a black eyeline, bordered white above and below. The breast and flanks are orange, and the belly white. The sexes are fairly similar, but immatures have a browner back.</p>
<p>The male has a simple whistling song, similar to the related <a href="/wiki/Mistle_Thrush" title="Mistle Thrush">Mistle Thrush</a>.</p>
<p>In 2007 an Eyebrowed Thrush was sighted at the <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem_Bird_Observatory" title="Jerusalem Bird Observatory">Jerusalem Bird Observatory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> This is the second recorded sighting in Israel, the first was at <a href="/wiki/Eilat" title="Eilat">Eilat</a>, October 1996.</p>
<p>In 2011, an Eyebrowed Thrush was sighted in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Malanda" title="Malanda" class="mw-redirect">Malanda</a> in <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> This is possibly the first confirmed sighting of the species on the Australian mainland.</p>
