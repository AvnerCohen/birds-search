<p>The <b>Red-breasted Goose</b> (<i>Branta ruficollis</i>) is a brightly marked, <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered</a> species of <a href="/wiki/Goose" title="Goose">goose</a> in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Branta" title="Branta">Branta</a></i> from <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. It is sometimes separated in <i>Rufibrenta</i> but appears close enough to the <a href="/wiki/Brent_Goose" title="Brent Goose" class="mw-redirect">Brent Goose</a> (<i>Branta bernicla</i>) to make this unnecessary, despite its distinct appearance.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Goose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>

<p>All the <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Branta" title="Branta">Branta</a></i> genus are distinguished by their dark sooty colour, relieved by white, and as a distinction from the grey geese of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Anser_(genus)" title="Anser (genus)" class="mw-redirect">Anser</a></i>. Among the species from these two genera, the Red-breasted Goose is the smallest at 53–56 centimetres (21–22 in) in length.<sup id="cite_ref-Ogilvie_0-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ogilvie-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> This brightly marked species is unmistakable, but can be surprisingly difficult to find amongst Brent Geese. At long distances, the red of the breast tends to look dark, and the broad white stripe on the flank is more evident.<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Goose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p>The Red-breasted Goose breeds in Arctic <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, mainly on the <a href="/wiki/Taymyr_Peninsula" title="Taymyr Peninsula">Taymyr Peninsula</a>, with smaller populations in the <a href="/wiki/Gydan_Peninsula" title="Gydan Peninsula">Gydan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yamal_Peninsula" title="Yamal Peninsula">Yamal peninsulas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BLIfactsheet_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLIfactsheet-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Most winter along the northwestern shores of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> (occasionally moving further southwest to <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>), but some winter in <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BLIfactsheet_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLIfactsheet-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is a rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and other western European areas, where it is sometimes found with flocks of Brent or <a href="/wiki/Barnacle_Goose" title="Barnacle Goose">Barnacle Geese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> However, since it is common in captive wildfowl collections, escapees outside its usual range are fairly frequent.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Goose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>

<p>The Red-breasted Goose often nests close to nests of <a href="/wiki/Birds_of_prey" title="Birds of prey" class="mw-redirect">birds of prey</a>, such as <a href="/wiki/Snowy_Owl" title="Snowy Owl">Snowy Owls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_Falcon" title="Peregrine Falcon">Peregrine Falcons</a>, which helps to protect this small goose from <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammalian</a> predators such as the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Fox" title="Arctic Fox" class="mw-redirect">Arctic Fox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Ogilvie_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ogilvie-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Collins_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>While wintering, the Red-breasted Goose feeds on grasses, leafs and seeds.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Goose&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span></h2>
<p>The Red-breasted Goose is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies. It was considered a Vulnerable species by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a>. Over 80% of the population roost during the winter at just five sites, with nearby feeding areas threatened by changes in land use. In addition, there has been a strong decline in numbers in the last decades. As it is not clear to what extent the known population fluctuates in this species – as in other Arctic geese – and given the worsening outlook for the species as a whole, the Red-breasted Goose was uplisted from a species of Least Concern to Endangered status in the 2007 <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
