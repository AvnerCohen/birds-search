<p>The <b>Eastern Olivaceous Warbler</b> (<i>Hippolais pallida</i>) is a "warbler", formerly placed in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warblers</a> when these were a <a href="/wiki/Paraphyletic" title="Paraphyletic" class="mw-redirect">paraphyletic</a> <a href="/wiki/Wastebin_taxon" title="Wastebin taxon" class="mw-redirect">wastebin taxon</a>. It is now considered a member of the acrocephaline warbelrs, <a href="/wiki/Acrocephalidae" title="Acrocephalidae">Acrocephalidae</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> genus <i>Hippolais</i> . It was formerly regarded as part of a wider "Olivaceous Warbler" species, but as a result of modern taxonomic developments, this species is now usually considered distinct from the <a href="/wiki/Western_Olivaceous_Warbler" title="Western Olivaceous Warbler">Western Olivaceous Warbler</a>, <i>Hippolais opaca</i>.</p>
<p>It is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>, found in dry open country, including cultivation, with bushes or some trees. 2-3 eggs are laid in a nest in low in undergrowth or a bush. Like most warblers, Eastern Olivaceous is insectivorous.</p>
<p>It is a medium-sized warbler, more like a very pale <a href="/wiki/Reed_Warbler" title="Reed Warbler" class="mw-redirect">Reed Warbler</a> than its relative the <a href="/wiki/Melodious_Warbler" title="Melodious Warbler">Melodious Warbler</a>. The adult has a plain pale brown back and whitish underparts. The bill is strong and pointed and the legs grey. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers, but young birds are more buff on the belly. It has a characteristic downward tail flick.</p>
<p>Eastern Olivaceous Warbler breeds in south east <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the Middle East. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> or <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>. It is a rare vagrant to northern Europe.</p>
<p>Western Olivaceous Warbler differs from this species in being larger and having a browner tinge to the upperparts; it also has a larger bill. Eastern Olivaceous Warbler sometimes has a greenish tinge to its upperparts, and can be very difficult to separate from <a href="/wiki/Sykes%27s_Warbler" title="Sykes's Warbler">Sykes's Warbler</a>, <i>Hippolais rama</i>. The song is a fast nasal babbling.</p>
