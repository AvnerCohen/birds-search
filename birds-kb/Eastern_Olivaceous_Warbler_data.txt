<p>The <b>eastern olivaceous warbler</b> (<i>Iduna pallida</i>) is a small passerine bird with drab plumage tones, that is native to the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>. For the most part it breeds in the northern <a href="/wiki/Afrotropics" class="mw-redirect" title="Afrotropics">Afrotropics</a> and winters in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">western Asia</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_olivaceous_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>eastern olivaceous warbler</b> (<i>Iduna pallida</i>) is a "warbler", formerly placed in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warblers</a> when these were a <a href="/wiki/Paraphyletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Paraphyletic">paraphyletic</a> <a href="/wiki/Wastebin_taxon" class="mw-redirect" title="Wastebin taxon">wastebin taxon</a>. It is now considered a member of the acrocephaline warblers, <a href="/wiki/Acrocephalidae" title="Acrocephalidae">Acrocephalidae</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> genus <i>Iduna</i>. It was formerly regarded as part of a wider "olivaceous warbler" species, but as a result of modern taxonomic developments, this species is now usually considered distinct from the <a href="/wiki/Western_olivaceous_warbler" title="Western olivaceous warbler">western olivaceous warbler</a>, <i>Iduna opaca</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_olivaceous_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Keyserling" class="mw-redirect" title="Alexander Keyserling">Keyserling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johann_Heinrich_Blasius" title="Johann Heinrich Blasius">Blasius</a> gave no explanation of the genus name <i>Iduna</i>. The specific <i>pallida</i>  is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "pale".<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_olivaceous_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is found in dry open country, including cultivation, with bushes or some trees. Like most warblers it is insectivorous.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_olivaceous_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> or <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>. It is a rare vagrant to northern Europe.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_olivaceous_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg/220px-Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg/330px-Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg/440px-Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3891" data-file-height="3473" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Iduna_pallida_elaeica_MHNT225_Gr%C3%A8ce.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs of <i>Iduna pallida elaeica</i> <a href="/wiki/MHNT" class="mw-redirect" title="MHNT">MHNT</a></div></div></div>
<p>Eastern olivaceous warbler breeds from southeastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, and the subspecies <i>reiseri</i> is thought to be locally common as a breeding species in southeast Morocco.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 2 to 3 eggs are laid in a nest which is placed low in a bush or in undergrowth.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_olivaceous_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a medium-sized warbler, more like a very pale <a href="/wiki/Reed_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Reed warbler">reed warbler</a> than its relative the <a href="/wiki/Melodious_warbler" title="Melodious warbler">melodious warbler</a>. The adult has a plain pale brown back and whitish underparts. The bill is strong and pointed and the legs grey. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers, but young birds are more buff on the belly. It has a characteristic downward tail flick.
</p><p>Western olivaceous warbler differs from this species in being larger and having a browner tinge to the upperparts; it also has a larger bill. Eastern olivaceous warbler sometimes has a greenish tinge to its upperparts, and can be very difficult to separate from <a href="/wiki/Sykes%27s_warbler" title="Sykes&#39;s warbler">Sykes's warbler</a>, <i>Iduna rama</i>. The song is a fast nasal babbling.
</p>
