<p>The <b>Eurasian Jay</b> (<i>Garrulus glandarius</i>) is a species of bird occurring over a vast region from Western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and north-west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect">Indian Subcontinent</a> and further to the eastern seaboard of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and down into south-east Asia. Across its vast <a href="/wiki/Range_(biology)" title="Range (biology)">range</a>, several very distinct racial forms have evolved to look very different from each other, especially when forms at the extremes of its range are compared.</p>
<p>The bird is called <b>jay</b>, without any <a href="/wiki/Epithet" title="Epithet">epithets</a>, by English speakers in Britain and Ireland.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Jay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>The Eurasian Jay was one of the many species originally described by <a href="/wiki/Carolus_Linnaeus" title="Carolus Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a> in his 18th century work <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_Naturae" title="Systema Naturae">Systema Naturae</a></i>. He recognised its affinity with other corvids, naming it <i>Corvus glandarius</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Eight racial groups (33 <a href="/wiki/Subspecies_(biology)" title="Subspecies (biology)" class="mw-redirect">subspecies</a> in total) are recognised by Madge &amp; Burn (1994):<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<ul>
<li>the nominate group (nine <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> races), with a streaked <a href="/w/index.php?title=Crown_(plumage)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Crown (plumage) (page does not exist)">crown</a>.</li>
<li>the <i>cervicalis</i> group (three races in <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>), with a rufous <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nape_(plumage)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Nape (plumage) (page does not exist)">nape</a>, grey <a href="/w/index.php?title=Mantle_(plumage)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Mantle (plumage) (page does not exist)">mantle</a>, very pale head sides, and a streaked or black crown.</li>
<li>the <i>atricapillus</i> group (four races in <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crimea" title="Crimea">Crimea</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>), with a uniform mantle &amp; nape, black crown and very pale face.</li>
<li>the race <i>hyrcanus</i> (<a href="/wiki/Caspian_Hyrcanian_mixed_forests" title="Caspian Hyrcanian mixed forests">Caspian Hyrcanian mixed forests</a> of <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>), small with black forecrown and broadly-streaked hindcrown.</li>
<li>the <i>brandtii</i> group (four races in <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>), with a streaked crown, reddish head, dark <a href="/wiki/Iris_(anatomy)" title="Iris (anatomy)">iris</a> and grey mantle.</li>
<li>the <i>leucotis</i> group (two races in <a href="/wiki/South-east_Asia" title="South-east Asia" class="mw-redirect">south-east Asia</a>), with no white in the wing, a white forecrown, black hindcrown and much white on the sides of the head.</li>
<li>the <i>bispecularis</i> group (six races in the <a href="/wiki/Himalaya" title="Himalaya" class="mw-redirect">Himalayan</a> region), with an unstreaked rufous crown, and no white wing-patch.</li>
<li>the <i>japonicus</i> group (four races in the southern Japanese islands), with a large white wing-patch, blackish face and scaled crown.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Jay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>A member of the widespread <a href="/wiki/Jay" title="Jay">jay</a> group, and about the size of the <a href="/wiki/Jackdaw" title="Jackdaw" class="mw-redirect">Jackdaw</a>, it inhabits mixed <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a>, particularly with <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oaks</a>, and is an habitual <a href="/wiki/Acorn" title="Acorn">acorn</a> hoarder. In recent years, the bird has begun to migrate into urban areas, possibly as a result of continued erosion of its woodland habitat.</p>

<p>Its usual call is the alarm call which is a harsh, rasping screech and is used upon sighting various predatory animals, but the Jay is well known for its <a href="/wiki/Mimicry" title="Mimicry">mimicry</a>, often sounding so like a different species that it is virtually impossible to distinguish its true identity unless the Jay is seen. It will even imitate the sound of the bird it is attacking, such as a <a href="/wiki/Tawny_Owl" title="Tawny Owl">Tawny Owl</a>, which it does mercilessly if attacking during the day. However, the Jay is a potential prey item for <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owls</a> at night and other birds of prey such as <a href="/wiki/Northern_Goshawk" title="Northern Goshawk">Goshawks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peregrine_Falcon" title="Peregrine Falcon">Peregrines</a> during the day.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Jay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span></h3>
<p>Feeding in both trees and on the ground, it takes a wide range of <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a> including many <a href="/wiki/Pest_(organism)" title="Pest (organism)">pest</a> insects, acorns (<a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a> seeds, which it buries for use during winter),<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> mast and other <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruits</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Blackberry" title="Blackberry">blackberries</a> and rowan berries, young birds and eggs and small rodents.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Jay&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>It nests in trees or large shrubs laying usually 4–6 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> that hatch after 16–19 days and are fledged generally after 21–23 days. Both sexes typically feed the young.</p>
