<p>The <b>Wood Warbler</b> (<i>Phylloscopus sibilatrix</i>) is a common and widespread <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> which breeds throughout northern and temperate <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and just into the extreme west of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Ural_Mountains" title="Ural Mountains">Ural Mountains</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warbler</a> is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and the entire population winters in tropical <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Wood_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span></h2>
<p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of open but shady mature woodlands, such as <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sessile_oak" title="Sessile oak" class="mw-redirect">sessile oak</a>, with some sparse ground cover for nesting. The nest is built near the ground in low shrub. Like most <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a> <a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warblers</a>, this small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> is insectivorous.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Wood_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Wood Warbler is 11–12.5&#160;cm long, and a typical leaf warbler in appearance, green above and white below with a lemon-yellow breast. It can be distinguished from similar <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, like the <a href="/wiki/Common_Chiffchaff" title="Common Chiffchaff">Chiffchaff</a> <i>P. collybita</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Willow_Warbler" title="Willow Warbler">Willow Warbler</a>, <i>P. trochilus</i> by its yellow supercilium, throat and upper breast, pale tertial edges, longer primary projection, and by its shorter but broader tail.</p>
<p>It is a summer visitor to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, seen from April until August; only a few pairs breed in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Wood_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Songs">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Songs">Songs</span></h2>
<p>It has two song types, often (but not always) given alternately; a high-pitched fluid metallic trill of increasing tempo <span class="unicode"><a href="/wiki/File:Phylloscopus_sibilatrix.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" /></a>&#160;<a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/90/Phylloscopus_sibilatrix.ogg" class="internal" title="Phylloscopus sibilatrix.ogg"><i>pit-pit-pitpitpitpt-t-t-ttt</i></a><small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Phylloscopus_sibilatrix.ogg" title="File:Phylloscopus sibilatrix.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> lasting 2–3 seconds, and a series of 3 to 5 descending piping notes of lower pitch <i>piüü-piüü-piüü</i>. The contact call is a soft piping note, similar to the second song type, but shorter and given singly, "<i>piü</i>".</p>
