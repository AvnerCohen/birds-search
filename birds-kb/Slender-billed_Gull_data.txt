<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Breeding_plumage.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Breeding_plumage.jpg/220px-Breeding_plumage.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Breeding_plumage.jpg/330px-Breeding_plumage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Breeding_plumage.jpg/440px-Breeding_plumage.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1131" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Breeding_plumage.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>in Breeding plumage at Kutch</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>slender-billed gull</b> (<i>Chroicocephalus genei</i>) is a mid-sized <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a> which breeds very locally around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> and the north of the western <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>) on islands and coastal lagoons. Most of the population is somewhat <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering further south to north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, and a few birds have wandered to western  <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Vagrant_bird" class="mw-redirect" title="Vagrant bird">vagrant</a> individual was reportedly seen on <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">Antigua</a>, April 24, 1976 (<a href="/wiki/American_Ornithologists%27_Union" class="mw-redirect" title="American Ornithologists&#39; Union">AOU</a>, 2000).
</p><p>The genus name <i> Chroicocephalus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>khroizo</i>, "to colour", and <i>kephale</i>, "head". The specific <i>genei</i> commemorates Italian naturalist <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Gen%C3%A9" title="Giuseppe Gené">Giuseppe Gené</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slender-billed_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is 37 to 40&#160;cm (14.6 to 15.7&#160;in) long with a 90 to 102&#160;cm (35.4 to 40.2&#160;in) wingspan. It is therefore slightly larger than the <a href="/wiki/Black-headed_gull" title="Black-headed gull">black-headed gull</a>, which it resembles, although it does not have a black hood in summer. It has a pale grey body, white head and breast and black tips to the primary wing feathers. The head and dark red bill have an elongated tapering appearance, and this bird also appears long-necked. The legs are dark red, and the iris is yellow. In summer, the breast has a faint pink colouration. This bird takes two years to reach maturity, as is usual in gulls. First year immature birds have a black terminal tail band, and dark areas on the wings.<sup id="cite_ref-Oiseaux_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oiseaux-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slender-billed_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The slender-billed gull breeds in lagoons and lakes around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea" title="Mediterranean Sea">Mediterranean Sea</a> and in similar locations in countries bordering the northwestern part of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>. In 2010 a successful colony was found breeding on an island in a mountain lake in Algeria. The lack of predators and disturbance allowed over 98% of the eggs to hatch.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It overwinters on the coast in estuaries and bays. It feeds in deltas, marshes and grassland. It is one of a number of species of gull to feed on landfill sites.<sup id="cite_ref-Oiseaux_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oiseaux-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slender-billed_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG/220px-Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="379" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG/330px-Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG/440px-Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2340" data-file-height="4036" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_genei_MWNH_0339.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>About half of the slender-billed gull's food is fish. It flies a few metres above the surface of the water and dives into the water when it sees suitable <a href="/wiki/Predation" title="Predation">prey</a>. It also probes in the mud with its beak to feed on marine invertebrates and catches insects in flight.<sup id="cite_ref-Oiseaux_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oiseaux-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This rather uncommon gull breeds in <a href="/wiki/Colony_(biology)" title="Colony (biology)">colonies</a>, nesting on the ground and laying up to three brown-spotted white eggs in a scrape sparsely lined with feathers and bits of vegetation. Incubation takes about 25 days and the young fly after another 25 days. Like most gulls, it is gregarious in winter, both when feeding and in evening roosts. It is not a pelagic species, and is rarely seen far from land.<sup id="cite_ref-Oiseaux_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Oiseaux-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Slender-billed_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The slender-billed gull is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
