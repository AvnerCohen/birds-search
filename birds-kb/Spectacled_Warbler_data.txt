<p>The <b>spectacled warbler</b> (<i>Curruca conspicillata</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> genus, <i>Curruca</i>. The specific <i>conspicillata</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>conspicillum</i>,  a place to look from, equivalent to "spectacled".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds in north west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, southwest <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> from <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and then further east on the eastern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> islands and coastal regions. It is mainly resident in Africa, but other populations <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> to winter in more widely in north and west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a rare vagrant to northern and western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p><p>It also occurs in some <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a> islands. The <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>orbitalis</i> has been proposed for those of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cape Verde Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a>. The presumed subspecies for the <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a> birds, <i>bella</i> is today usually included in this <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a>, as are the birds of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, where the species is quite common except on <a href="/wiki/El_Hierro" title="El Hierro">El Hierro</a> and known as <i>zarzalero y ratonero</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>These are very small "warblers" and are intermediate between <a href="/wiki/Whitethroat" class="mw-redirect" title="Whitethroat">whitethroats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tristram%27s_warbler" title="Tristram&#39;s warbler">Tristram's warbler</a> in coloration. Spectacled warblers are brown above and buff below, with chestnut wing patches and a white throat. Adult males have a grey head and the white eye ring which gives the species its name. Immature birds can be confused with both the whitethroat and the <a href="/wiki/Subalpine_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Subalpine warbler">subalpine warbler</a>, and identification is difficult in the field. The song is a fast high warble.
</p><p>About the precise relationships of this bird, not much can be said with certainty. It seems though as if its intermediate appearance, apart from the <a href="/wiki/Autapomorph" class="mw-redirect" title="Autapomorph">autapomorphic</a> white eye ring, indicates its relationships reasonably well. It is not the closest living relative of at least Tristram's warbler though.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>These small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> birds are found in dry open country with bushes. 3-6 eggs are laid in a nest in a bush.  Like their relatives, the spectacled warbler is insectivorous.
</p>
