<p>The <b>Sardinian warbler</b> (<i>Curruca melanocephala</i>) is a common and widespread <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> region. Like most <i>Curruca</i> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, it has distinct male and female <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a>. The adult male has a grey back, whitish underparts, black head, white throat and red eyes. Plumages are somewhat variable even in the same locality, with the intensity of a reddish hue on upper- and/or underside varies from absent to (in some <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>) pronounced. The female is mainly brown above and buff below, with a grey head. The Sardinian warbler's song is fast and rattling, and is very characteristic of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> areas where this bird breeds.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardinian_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">formal description</a> of the Sardinian warbler was by the German naturalist <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Gmelin" title="Johann Friedrich Gmelin">Johann Friedrich Gmelin</a> in 1789 in the 13th edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_naturae" class="mw-redirect" title="Systema naturae">Systema naturae</a></i>. He coined the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Motacilla melanocephala</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The current genus <i>Sylvia</i> was introduced in 1769 by the Italian naturalist <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Antonio_Scopoli" title="Giovanni Antonio Scopoli">Giovanni Antonio Scopoli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The genus name is from <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">Modern Latin</a> <i>silvia</i>, a woodland sprite, related to <i>silva</i>, a wood. The specific <i>melanocephala</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>melas</i>, "black", and <i>kephale</i>, "head".<sup id="cite_ref-job_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Together with <a href="/wiki/Menetries%27_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Menetries&#39; warbler">Menetries' warbler</a> the Sardinian warbler forms a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a>. Both have white <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> areas and light throats, and otherwise black heads in adult males, as well as a naked ring around the eye. The <a href="/wiki/Subalpine_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Subalpine warbler">subalpine warbler</a>, which seems the superspecies' closest relative, has a dark throat and breast and a dark gray upper head in males, but otherwise shares these characters. These three species are related to a dark-throated superspecies consisting of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%BCppell%27s_warbler" title="Rüppell&#39;s warbler">Rüppell's warbler</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_warbler" title="Cyprus warbler">Cyprus warbler</a>, which also share the white malar area with blackish above.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This bird may be considered a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a>, divided into the western <i>Curruca melanocephala</i> and <i><b>Curruca momus</b></i> from the more arid regions of the <a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a> and adjacent Africa.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardinian_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The geographical variation in the Sardinian warbler conforms to some extent with <a href="/wiki/Gloger%27s_rule" title="Gloger&#39;s rule">Gloger's rule</a>, though not as strongly as in some other typical warblers. The validity of <i>leucogastra</i> and <i>norissae</i> is not accepted by some authors, and <i>valverdei</i> has been described very recently. On the other hand, <i>leucogastra</i> might be more than one subspecies.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>Curruca melanocephala melanocephala</i> <small>(Gmelin, 1789)</small></li></ul>
<dl><dd><a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberia</a> across the northern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> to western <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. Extends into the <a href="/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> from Iberia, and into <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> from <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> via <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>. Migrates to the <a href="/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oases" class="mw-redirect" title="Oases">oases</a> in the Sahara in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd>Large, long wings, tail tip rather pointed. A dark form, usually lacking any reddish in males but flanks extensively grey. Females' uppersides vary between deep olive brown and greyish olive.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>Curruca melanocephala leucogastra</i> <small>(Ledru, 1810)</small> - often included in <i>melanocephala</i>; <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Phylogenetic">phylogenetic</a> status requires review<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<dl><dd><a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, resident, probably some vagrancy between eastern islands and Maghreb.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd>Medium size, short-winged and large-billed. <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Palma" title="La Palma">La Palma</a> (western) birds are most distinct, being dark above with some rusty/beige hue on the underside in males. Eastern birds (<a href="/wiki/Fuerteventura" title="Fuerteventura">Fuerteventura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lanzarote" title="Lanzarote">Lanzarote</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gran_Canaria" title="Gran Canaria">Gran Canaria</a>) are more like <i>melanocephala</i> and <i>momus</i> but differ in measurements.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>Curruca melanocephala momus</i> <small>(Hemprich &amp; Ehrenberg, 1833)</small></li></ul>
<dl><dd><a href="/wiki/Near_East" title="Near East">Near East</a>. Resident, some local movements.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd>Smallish, short-winged. Varies between brownish grey and rusty above; underside almost always has reddish hue. Females rusty to rusty olive.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>Curruca melanocephala norissae</i> <small>Nicoll, 1917</small>: <b>Fayyum warbler</b> - probably only a local <a href="/wiki/Morph_(zoology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Morph (zoology)">morph</a> of <i>momus</i><sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<dl><dd><a href="/wiki/Nile_Delta" title="Nile Delta">Nile Delta</a> region. <a href="/wiki/Extinct" class="mw-redirect" title="Extinct">Extinct</a> since around 1940.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd>Like <i>momus</i>, but tend to be very reddish.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>Curruca melanocephala valverdei</i> <small>Cabot &amp; Urdiales, 2005</small> - recently split from <i>melanocephala</i></li></ul>
<dl><dd>From <a href="/wiki/Tiznit" title="Tiznit">Tiznit</a> (<a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>) south to the <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">Tropic of Cancer</a>, inland to the edge of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. Resident, but some seasonal movements.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd>Medium-sized, tail tip quite square. A very pure-colored form, the palest subspecies. Undersides clean white. Matte black cap in males. Juveniles decidedly sandy.<sup id="cite_ref-cabot&amp;urdiales_8-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cabot&amp;urdiales-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardinian_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds in the southernmost areas of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and just into <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> in Turkey and the eastern end of the Mediterranean. This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>, unlike most "<a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warblers</a>", is not particularly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, but some birds winter in north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and it occurs as a vagrant well away from the breeding range, as far as <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sardinian_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg/220px-Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="172" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg/330px-Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg/440px-Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2929" data-file-height="2291" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sylvia_melanocephala_MHNT.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i> Curruca melanocephala </i> - <a href="/wiki/MHNT" class="mw-redirect" title="MHNT">MHNT</a></div></div></div>
<p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of open country and cultivation, with bushes for nesting. The nest is built in low shrub or brambles, and 3-6 eggs are laid. Like most "<a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warblers</a>", it is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a>, but will also take berries and other soft <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a>.
</p>
