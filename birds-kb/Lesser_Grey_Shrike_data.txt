<p>The <b>Lesser Grey Shrike</b> (<i>Lanius minor</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family <i>Laniidae</i>.</p>
<p>It is similar in appearance to the <a href="/wiki/Great_Grey_Shrike" title="Great Grey Shrike">Great Grey Shrike</a> <i>Lanius excubitor</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Grey_Shrike" title="Southern Grey Shrike">Southern Grey Shrike</a> <i>L. meridionalis</i>. It breeds in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and into <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.</p>
<p>This species prefers dry open lowlands, and is prominent on telephone wires.</p>
<p>It is slightly smaller than the Great Grey Shrike, and has a black forehead and relatively longer wings.</p>
<p>This medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> <a href="/wiki/Perching_bird" title="Perching bird" class="mw-redirect">passerine</a> eats large <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, small birds and <a href="/wiki/Lizards" title="Lizards" class="mw-redirect">lizards</a>. Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a "larder".</p>
<p>It is a scarce vagrant to western Europe, including <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, usually as a spring overshoot.</p>
