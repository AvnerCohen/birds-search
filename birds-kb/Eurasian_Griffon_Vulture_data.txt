<p>The <b>Griffon Vulture</b> (<i>Gyps fulvus</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Old_World_vulture" title="Old World vulture">Old World vulture</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> family <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>.</p>
<p>The Griffon Vulture is 93–122 cm (37–48 in) long with a 2.3–2.8 m (7.5–9.2 ft) wingspan. In the nominate race the males weigh 6.2 to 10.5 kg (14 to 23 lb) and females typically weigh 6.5 to 11.3 kg (14 to 25 lb), while in the Indian subspecies (<i>G. f. fulvescens</i>) the vultures average 7.1&#160;kg (16&#160;lb). Extreme adult weights have been reported from 4.5 to 15 kg (9.9 to 33 lb), the latter likely a weight attained in captivity.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Hatched naked, it is a typical Old World vulture in appearance, with a very white head, very broad wings and short tail feathers. It has a white neck ruff and yellow bill. The buff body and wing coverts contrast with the dark flight <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>.</p>
<p>Like other <a href="/wiki/Vulture" title="Vulture">vultures</a>, it is a <a href="/wiki/Scavenger" title="Scavenger">scavenger</a>, feeding mostly from carcasses of dead animals which it finds by soaring over open areas, often moving in flocks. It establishes nesting colonies in cliffs that are undisturbed by humans while coverage of open areas and availability of dead animals within dozens of kilometers of these cliffs is high.<sup id="cite_ref-Gavashelishvili5_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gavashelishvili5-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gavashelishvili6_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gavashelishvili6-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It grunts and hisses at roosts or when feeding on <a href="/wiki/Carrion" title="Carrion">carrion</a>.</p>
<p>The maximum lifespan recorded for the Griffon Vulture is 41.4 years, for a specimen in captivity.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It breeds on crags in mountains in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, laying one egg. Griffon Vultures may form loose colonies. The population is mostly resident. Juveniles and immature individuals may migrate far or embark on long-distance movements.<sup id="cite_ref-Gavashelishvili2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gavashelishvili2-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Gavashelishvili12_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gavashelishvili12-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Griffon_Vulture&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Status in Europe and Asia">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Status_in_Europe_and_Asia">Status in Europe and Asia</span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, the species survived only in <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, but was <a href="/wiki/Reintroduction" title="Reintroduction">re-introduced</a> in a few other areas of the peninsula. As a result, several specimens been spotted again in August 2006 on the <a href="/wiki/Gran_Sasso_d%27Italia" title="Gran Sasso d'Italia">Gran Sasso</a> massif (central Italy).</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a>, a colony of Griffon Vultures can be found near the town of <a href="/wiki/Beli_(Croatia)" title="Beli (Croatia)" class="mw-redirect">Beli</a> on the island of <a href="/wiki/Cres" title="Cres">Cres</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> There they breed at lower elevations, with some nests just 10 m above sea level. Therefore, contact with people is common. The population makes frequent incursions in the <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenian</a> territory, especially in the mountain <a href="/wiki/Stol_(mountain)" title="Stol (mountain)">Stol</a> above <a href="/wiki/Kobarid" title="Kobarid">Kobarid</a>.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, there is a colony at <a href="/wiki/Episkopi,_Limassol" title="Episkopi, Limassol">Episkopi</a>, in the south of the island.</li>
<li>Colonies of Griffon Vultures can be found in northern <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and in the <a href="/wiki/Golan_Heights" title="Golan Heights">Golan Heights</a>, where a large colony breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Mount_Carmel" title="Mount Carmel">Carmel Mountains</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Negev" title="Negev">Negev</a> desert and especially at <a href="/wiki/Gamla" title="Gamla">Gamla</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Reintroduction" title="Reintroduction">reintroduction</a> projects are being carried out at breeding centers in the <a href="/wiki/Carmel_Hai-Bar_Nature_Reserve" title="Carmel Hai-Bar Nature Reserve">Carmel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yotvata_Hai-Bar_Nature_Reserve" title="Yotvata Hai-Bar Nature Reserve">Negev</a>.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, there are nearly 1000 birds. On <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a> they can be found in most mountainous areas, sometimes in groups of up to 20.</li>
<li>Griffon Vultures have been <a href="/wiki/Reintroduction" title="Reintroduction">re-introduced</a> successfully into the <a href="/wiki/Massif_Central" title="Massif Central">Massif Central</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>; about 500 are now found there.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, around 100 birds were present in the summer of 2007. These were vagrants from the <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">Pyrenees</a> population (see below).<sup id="cite_ref-n-tv2007_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-n-tv2007-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, the species died out in the mid 18th century. Some 200 vagrant birds, probably from the Pyrenees, were sighted in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-handelsblatt2006_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-handelsblatt2006-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> and several dozen of the vagrants sighted in Belgium the following year crossed into Germany in search for food.<sup id="cite_ref-n-tv2007-2_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-n-tv2007-2-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> There are plans to reintroduce the species in the <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a>. In September 2008, pieces of a griffon vulture bone, about 35,000 years old, were excavated from Hohle Fels cave in southern Germany, which are believed to form a <a href="/wiki/Flute" title="Flute">flute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ap_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ap-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sc_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sc-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, there are around 60–65 pairs of Griffon Vultures in western parts of the country, around Zlatar mountain,also 35 birds in canyon of river Trešnjica<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> and they are under legal protection from hunting.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, there is a population of several dozen birds.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>, there is a remnant population around <a href="/wiki/Salzburg_Zoo" title="Salzburg Zoo">Salzburg Zoo</a>, and vagrants from the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a> are often seen.</li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, there are tens of thousands of birds, from a low of a few thousand around 1980.</li>
<li>The Pyrenees population has apparently been affected by an <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">EC</a> ruling that due to danger of <a href="/wiki/Bovine_spongiform_encephalopathy" title="Bovine spongiform encephalopathy">BSE</a> transmission, no carcasses must be left on the fields for the time being. This has critically lowered food availability, and consequently, <a href="/wiki/Carrying_capacity" title="Carrying capacity">carrying capacity</a>. Although the Griffon Vulture does not normally attack larger living prey, there are reports of Spanish Griffon Vultures killing weak, young or unhealthy living animals as they do not find enough carrion to eat.<sup id="cite_ref-newscientist2007_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-newscientist2007-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
