<p>The <b>Namaqua Dove</b> (<i>Oena capensis</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Columbidae" title="Columbidae">pigeon</a>. It is the <a href="/wiki/Monotypic_taxon" title="Monotypic taxon">only species</a> in the genus <i>Oena.</i></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Namaqua_Dove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Namaqua Dove is a tiny <a href="/wiki/Sparrow" title="Sparrow">sparrow</a>-sized pigeon, typically 22&#160;cm in length with a 28–33&#160;cm wingspan, and weighing 40g. It has a very long black tapered tail, and the size and shape have led to comparison with the <a href="/wiki/Budgerigar" title="Budgerigar">Budgerigar</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">plumage</a> is mostly grey apart from a white belly, and chestnut primary feathers which are visible in flight.</p>
<p>The adult male has a black face, throat and breast, and a yellow and red <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">beak</a>. The adult female lacks the black and has a red-based grey bill. Young birds are dark blotched on the wings and shoulders, and otherwise resemble the females.</p>
<p>The song is a quiet, short, double <i>hoo</i>, higher on the longer second note <i>kuh-whooo</i>, mournful and frequently repeated.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Namaqua_Dove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>The dove is a widespread resident breeding bird in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> with its range extending into the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> southern <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> and as far north as <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. It is found in near desert with <a href="/wiki/Acacia" title="Acacia">acacia</a> and bushes.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Namaqua_Dove&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>The dove is quite terrestrial, and usually forages on open ground and roadsides. The food is almost exclusively minute seeds, such as those of <a href="/wiki/Grass" title="Grass">grasses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyperaceae" title="Cyperaceae">sedges</a> and weeds. It is not gregarious, being encountered singly or in pairs, though they may form larger flocks at waterholes. The flight is fast with clipped beats and a tendency to stay low. It builds a stick <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> in a bush, and lays two white <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>, which are incubated for 16 days in typical pigeon fashion; the female at night and early morning and the male from mid morning till late afternoon.</p>
