<p>The <b>streaked scrub warbler</b> (<i>Scotocerca inquieta</i>), also known as simply the <b>scrub warbler</b>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird. It is the only member of the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <b>Scotocercidae</b>, although some taxonomic authorities expand the family to include the closely-related <a href="/wiki/Cettiidae" title="Cettiidae">Cettiidae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erythrocercidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Erythrocercidae">Erythrocercidae</a> as well.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  It is found in Africa and south-western Asia. It is a bird of desert fringes, frequenting scrubby areas, ravines and gorges, and is mainly resident, although local movements can occur outside the breeding season.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The streaked scrub warbler is a small, skulking desert warbler which cocks its tail over its back. The adults are grey brown above, finely streaked with dark brown. They have a broad pale supercilium and a thin black eyestripe. The underparts are whitish with reddish flanks and vent, the breast is finely streaked. The tail is graduated and dark brown with a white tip. Juveniles are duller.<sup id="cite_ref-B&amp;D_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B&amp;D-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The song of the streaked scrub warbler is distinctive and is rendered as "<i>zit-zit dweedle-doolredle-doleed"<sup id="cite_ref-B&amp;D_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B&amp;D-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The streaked scrub warbler is a bird of open desert with a sparse cover of scrub, especially wadi beds with a denser cover than the surrounding desert, as well as scree areas with bushes in ravines and gorges.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_subspecies">Distribution and subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are currently eight recognised subspecies of streaked scrub warbler:<sup id="cite_ref-ITIS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBW_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>Scotocerca inquieta theresae</i> <small>R. Meinertzhagen, 1939</small> – Southern <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_Sahara" title="Western Sahara">Western Sahara</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Adrar_Region" title="Adrar Region">Adrar Region</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta saharae</i> <small>(Loche, 1858)</small> – Eastern Morocco, <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta inquieta</i> <small>(Cretzschmar, 1830)</small> – <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> east to the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta grisea</i> <small>Bates, 1936</small> – Western <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta buryi</i> <small>Ogilvie-Grant, 1902</small> – Southern Saudi Arabia and western Yemen.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta striata</i> <small>(W. E. Brooks, 1872)</small> – South-central <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta montana</i> <small>Stepanyan, 1970</small> – The mountains of North-eastern Iran, southern <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, western <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> and northern Afghanistan.</li>
<li><i>Scotocerca inquieta platyura</i> <small>(Severtsov, 1873)</small> – The plains of northern Turkmenistan, southern <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a> and south-western Tajikistan.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The streaked scrub warbler nests in low scrub up to 1.5m above the ground, the nest is a domed structure made of grass and twigs and lined with feathers, fur and plant down. It has 1-2 side entrances, if there is a second it is used only as an exit.  The clutch size averages 3–5 but varies from 2–5, incubation is roughly two weeks with another two weeks before the young fledge. Its main food is insects but it will also eat seeds which may be very important in winter. It forages on the ground, fossicking through leaf litter and other debris under bushes, and into cavities but will also feed up in the vegetation at times.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The streaked scrub warbler was formerly placed in the family <a href="/wiki/Cisticolidae" title="Cisticolidae">Cisticolidae</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and then in its own family. D. Winkler et al. now place this species and those included by IOC in <a href="/wiki/Cettiidae" title="Cettiidae">Cettiidae</a> and <a href="/wiki/Erythrocercidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Erythrocercidae">Erythrocercidae</a> in an enlarged family Scotocercidae.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some authorities have split the streaked scrub warbler into two species, the Saharan scrub warbler (<i>Scotocerca saharae</i>, including subspecies <i>saharae</i> and <i>theresae</i>) and the Levant scrub warbler (<i>Scotocerca inquieta</i>, including all other subspecies), an approach which has been followed in the most recent version of the <a href="/wiki/Collins_Bird_Guide" title="Collins Bird Guide">Collins Bird Guide</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_scrub_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The streaked scrub warbler has a very wide range and is scarce in some places and common in others. No particular threats have been identified and the population is believed to be steady or declining slightly. The <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has rated its conservation status as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least-concern_species" title="Least-concern species">least concern</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
