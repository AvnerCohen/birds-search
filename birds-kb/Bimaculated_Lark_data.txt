<p>The <b>bimaculated lark</b> (<i>Melanocorypha bimaculata</i>) breeds in warm <a href="/wiki/Temperate_countries" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate countries">temperate countries</a> eastwards from Turkey into <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. It is the eastern counterpart of its relative, the <a href="/wiki/Calandra_lark" title="Calandra lark">calandra lark</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bimaculated_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bimaculated lark was originally placed in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>. The current genus name, <i>Melanocorypha</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>melas</i>, "black", and <i>koruphos</i> a term used by ancient writers for a now unknown bird, but here confused with <i>korudos</i>, "lark". "Bimaculate" and the specific <i>bimaculata</i> are from <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> <i>bimaculatus</i>, "two-spotted".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-OED_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OED-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The alternate name <b>calandra lark</b> should not be confused with the species of the same name. The alternate name <b>Eastern calandra lark</b> is also used for a subspecies of the calandra lark.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bimaculated_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a large, robust <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a>, 16–18&#160;cm in length. It is an undistinguished looking species on the ground, mainly streaked grey above and white below, and with two small black patches on the breast sides, which give this species its name. It has a white supercilium.
</p><p>In flight it shows short broad wings, which are grey-brown below, and a short tail with a white tip, but not white edges. The wing and tail patterns are distinctions from its more westerly relative.
</p><p>The song is like a harder version of that of calandra lark.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bimaculated_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bimaculated lark is found from west-central Turkey to southern Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, north-eastern Iran and northern Afghanistan. It is also found in northern Israel, Lebanon, western Syria and northern Iraq. It is mainly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in northeast Africa, and ranges widely throughout the greater Middle East to Pakistan, India and Tibet. It is a very rare vagrant to western Europe.
</p><p>This is a bird of stony semi-desert and higher altitude cultivation. Its nest is on the ground, with 3-4 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> being laid. Food is seeds and insects, the latter especially in the breeding season. It is gregarious in winter.
</p>
