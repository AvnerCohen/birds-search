<p>The <b>Greater Flamingo</b> (<i>Phoenicopterus roseus</i>) is the most widespread <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Flamingo" title="Flamingo">flamingo</a> family. It is found in parts of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, southern Asia (coastal regions of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>), and southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (including <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Albania" title="Albania">Albania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Camargue" title="Camargue">Camargue</a> region of <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>). Some populations are short distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrants</a>, and sightings north of the breeding range are relatively frequent; however, given the species' popularity in captivity, whether or not these are truly wild individuals is a matter of some debate. A single bird was seen on North Keeling Island (<a href="/wiki/Cocos_(Keeling)_Islands" title="Cocos (Keeling) Islands">Cocos (Keeling) Islands</a>) in 1988. The Greater Flamingo is the state bird of <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a>, India.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phoenicopterus_roseus-pjt1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Phoenicopterus_roseus-pjt1.jpg/220px-Phoenicopterus_roseus-pjt1.jpg" width="220" height="153" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phoenicopterus_roseus-pjt1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Aggressive behavior during territorial fight</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This is the largest species of flamingo, averaging 110–150&#160;cm (43–60&#160;in) tall and weighing 2–4&#160;kg (4.4–8.8&#160;lbs). The largest male flamingos have been recorded at up to 187&#160;cm (74&#160;in) tall and 4.5&#160;kg (10&#160;lbs).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/American_Flamingo" title="American Flamingo">American Flamingo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chilean_Flamingo" title="Chilean Flamingo">Chilean Flamingo</a>, with which it has sometimes been considered <a href="/wiki/Conspecificity" title="Conspecificity">conspecific</a>, but that treatment is now widely seen (e.g., by the American and British Ornithologists' Union) as incorrect and based on insufficient evidence.</p>
<p>Like all flamingos, this species lays a single chalky-white <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">egg</a> on a mud mound.</p>
<p>Most of the <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> is pinkish-white, but the wing coverts are red and the primary and secondary flight <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a> are black.</p>
<p>The bill is pink with a restricted black tip, and the legs are entirely pink. The call is a goose-like honking.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Rann_of_Kutch" title="Rann of Kutch">Rann of Kutch</a> salt marsh of India and Pakistan, Greater Flamingos are occasionally electrocuted when they sit on 1000 watt electric cables near their breeding areas. Recently 139 deaths were officially recorded in the region.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Lifespan">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Lifespan">Lifespan</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Extinctbirds1907_P31_Leguatia_gigantea0353.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Extinctbirds1907_P31_Leguatia_gigantea0353.png/170px-Extinctbirds1907_P31_Leguatia_gigantea0353.png" width="170" height="240" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Extinctbirds1907_P31_Leguatia_gigantea0353.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
"Leguat's Giant", a hypothetical giant rail based on <a href="/wiki/Francois_Leguat" title="Francois Leguat" class="mw-redirect">Francois Leguat</a>'s brief description.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It has been suggested that Legaut actually observed a greater flamingo.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The average lifespan in captivity, according to <a href="/wiki/Zoo_Basel" title="Zoo Basel">Zoo Basel</a>, is over 60 years.</p>
<p>The oldest known Greater Flamingo, a resident of the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Zoo" title="Adelaide Zoo">Adelaide Zoo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, is at least 77 years old. The bird's exact age is not known; however, he was already a mature adult when he arrived in Adelaide in 1933, and he was still there as of 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span></h2>
<p>The bird resides in mudflats and shallow coastal lagoons with salt water. Using its feet, the bird stirs up the mud, then sucks water through its bill and filters out small shrimp, seeds, blue-green algae, microscopic organisms and mollusks.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Captivity">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Captivity">Captivity</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Zoo_Basel_flamingos_breeding.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e7/Zoo_Basel_flamingos_breeding.JPG/220px-Zoo_Basel_flamingos_breeding.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Zoo_Basel_flamingos_breeding.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<a href="/wiki/Flamingo" title="Flamingo">Flamingo</a> colony at Zoo Basel</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The first recorded zoo hatch was in 1959 at <a href="/wiki/Zoo_Basel" title="Zoo Basel">Zoo Basel</a>. In Zoo Basel's <a href="/wiki/Breeding_programs_at_Zoo_Basel" title="Breeding programs at Zoo Basel">breeding program</a> over 400 birds have been hatched with an average of between 20 and 27 per year since 2000. <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Because of Zoo Basel's extraordinarily successful breeding program and a lack of room, most of the hatchlings are sent to zoos around the world. Given the history and the large number of birds hatched in Basel since 1959, it may be concluded that most of the Greater Flamingo zoo colonies around the world are related to the one at Basel.</p>
