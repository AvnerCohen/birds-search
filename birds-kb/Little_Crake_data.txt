<p>The <b>Little Crake</b> (<i>Porzana parva</i>) is a very small waterbird of the family <a href="/wiki/Rallidae" title="Rallidae">Rallidae</a>.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is <a href="/wiki/Reed_bed" title="Reed bed">reed beds</a> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, mainly in the east, and just into western <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. They nest in a dry location in reed vegetation, laying 4-7 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>. This species is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
<p>At 17–19&#160;cm length, they are slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Spotted_Crake" title="Spotted Crake">Spotted Crakes</a>, from which they are readily distinguished by the lack of dark barring and white spots on the flanks. They are more similar to <a href="/wiki/Baillon%27s_Crake" title="Baillon's Crake">Baillon's Crake</a>.</p>
<p>The Little Crake has a short straight bill, yellow with a red base. Adult males have mainly brown upperparts and blue-grey face and underparts. They have green legs with long toes, and a short tail which is barred black and white underneath. Females have buff underparts, and are grey only on the face. Immature Little Crakes are similar to the female but have a white face and breast. The downy chicks are black, as with all <a href="/wiki/Rallidae" title="Rallidae">rails</a>.</p>
<p>These birds probe with their bill in mud or shallow water, also picking up food by sight. They mainly eat <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and aquatic animals.</p>
<p>Little Crakes are very secretive in the breeding season, and are then mostly heard rather than seen. They are then noisy <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, with a yapping <i>kua</i> call. They can be easier to see on migration.</p>
<p>The Little Crake is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
