<p>The <b>Marsh Sandpiper</b> (<i>Tringa stagnatilis</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a>. It is a rather small <a href="/wiki/Tringa" title="Tringa">shank</a>, and breeds in open grassy <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> <a href="/wiki/Wetlands" title="Wetlands" class="mw-redirect">wetlands</a> from easternmost <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> to central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marsh_Sandpiper_(Tringa_stagnatilis)_in_Hyderabad,_AP_W_IMG_2400.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Marsh_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_stagnatilis%29_in_Hyderabad%2C_AP_W_IMG_2400.jpg/250px-Marsh_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_stagnatilis%29_in_Hyderabad%2C_AP_W_IMG_2400.jpg" width="250" height="223" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marsh_Sandpiper_(Tringa_stagnatilis)_in_Hyderabad,_AP_W_IMG_2400.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
in <a href="/wiki/Hyderabad,_India" title="Hyderabad, India">Hyderabad, India</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It resembles a small elegant <a href="/wiki/Greenshank" title="Greenshank" class="mw-redirect">Greenshank</a>, with a long fine <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> and very long yellowish legs. Like the Greenshank, it is greyish brown in breeding <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a>, paler in winter, and has a white wedge up its back that is visible in flight. However, it is more closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Common_Redshank" title="Common Redshank">Common Redshank</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Wood_Sandpiper" title="Wood Sandpiper">Wood Sandpiper</a> (Pereira &amp; Baker, 2005). Together, they form a group of smallish shanks which tend to have red or reddish legs, and in breeding plumage are generally a subdued, light brown above with some darker mottling, with a pattern of somewhat diffuse small brownish spots on the breast and neck. The length is 22–26 cm (8.7–10 in), wingspan is 55–59 cm (22–23 in) and weight is 45–120 g (1.6–4.2 oz).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> species, with majority of birds wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> with fewer migrating to Southeast <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. They prefer to winter on fresh water wetlands such as <a href="/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamps</a> and lakes and are usually seen singly or in small groups.</p>
<p>These birds forage by probing in shallow water or on wet mud. They mainly eat insects, and similar small <a href="/wiki/Prey" title="Prey" class="mw-redirect">prey</a>.</p>
<p>The Marsh Sandpiper is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
