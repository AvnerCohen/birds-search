<p>The <b>Olive-tree Warbler</b> (<i>Hippolais olivetorum</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> genus <i>Hippolais</i>. It breeds in southeast <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the near east. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in eastern and southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> south to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.</p>
<p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in open-canopy <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oakwoods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olive</a> groves, orchards and <a href="/wiki/Almond" title="Almond">almond</a> plantations. 3-4 eggs are laid in a nest in a low tree or a bush.</p>
<p>This is a medium-sized warbler, similar to in size to the <a href="/wiki/Barred_Warbler" title="Barred Warbler">Barred Warbler</a>, with a slightly longer bill and shorter tail. It is the largest <i>Hippolais</i> warbler, with a heavy bill, rather flat crown, long wings, and heavy legs. The adult has a dusty- or brownish-grey back and wings, and dusty-white underparts.</p>
<p>It feeds on <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>. Its song is a succession of loud creaks and squawks, lower in pitch than other <i>Hippolais</i> warblers, and slower in delivery.</p>
