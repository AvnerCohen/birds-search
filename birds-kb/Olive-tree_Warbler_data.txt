<p>The <b>olive-tree warbler</b> (<i>Hippolais olivetorum</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> genus <i> Hippolais</i>. It breeds in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Europe" title="Southeast Europe">southeast Europe</a> and the near east. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in eastern and southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> south to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>.
</p><p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in open-canopy <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oakwoods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olive" title="Olive">olive</a> groves, orchards and <a href="/wiki/Almond" title="Almond">almond</a> plantations. 3-4 eggs are laid in a nest in a low tree or a bush.
</p><p>This is a medium-sized warbler, similar to in size to the <a href="/wiki/Barred_warbler" title="Barred warbler">barred warbler</a>, with a slightly longer bill and shorter tail. It is the largest <i>Hippolais</i> warbler, with a heavy bill, rather flat crown, long wings, and heavy legs. The adult has a dusty- or brownish-grey back and wings, and dusty-white underparts.
</p><p>It feeds on <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>. Its song is a succession of loud creaks and squawks, lower in pitch than other <i>Hippolais</i> warblers, and slower in delivery.
</p>

<p>The genus name <i>Hippolais</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>hupolais</i>, as misspelt by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a>. It referred to a small bird mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a> and others and may be <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeic" class="mw-redirect" title="Onomatopoeic">onomatopoeic</a> or derived from <i>hupo</i>,"under", and <i>laas</i>, "stone". The specific <i>olivetorum</i>  is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "of the olive groves ".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
