<p>The <b>green sandpiper</b> (<i>Tringa ochropus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> (shorebird) of the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>. The genus name <i>Tringa</i> is the <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> name given to the green sandpiper by <a href="/wiki/Ulisse_Aldrovandi" title="Ulisse Aldrovandi">Aldrovandus</a> in 1599 based on <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>trungas</i>, a thrush-sized, white-rumped, tail-bobbing wading bird mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a>. The specific <i>ochropus</i> is from Ancient Greek <i>okhros</i>, "ochre", and <i>pous</i>, "foot".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The green sandpiper represents an ancient lineage of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Tringa" title="Tringa">Tringa</a></i>; its only close living relative is the <a href="/wiki/Solitary_sandpiper" title="Solitary sandpiper">solitary sandpiper</a> (<i>T. solitaria</i>). They both have brown wings with little light dots and a delicate but contrasting neck and chest pattern. In addition, both species nest in trees, unlike most other <a href="/wiki/Scolopacidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Scolopacidae">scolopacids</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pereira2005_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pereira2005-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Given its <a href="/wiki/Basal_(phylogenetics)" title="Basal (phylogenetics)">basal</a> position in <i>Tringa</i>, it is fairly unsurprising that suspected cases of <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridisation</a> between this species and the <a href="/wiki/Common_sandpiper" title="Common sandpiper">common sandpiper</a> (<i>A. hypoleucos</i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Sister_genus" class="mw-redirect" title="Sister genus">sister genus</a> <i>Actitis</i> have been <a href="/wiki/Hybridisation_in_shorebirds#Other_hybrid_scolopacids" title="Hybridisation in shorebirds">reported</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Green_Sandpiper_(Tringa_ochropus)_W_IMG_6454.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Green_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_ochropus%29_W_IMG_6454.jpg/220px-Green_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_ochropus%29_W_IMG_6454.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Green_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_ochropus%29_W_IMG_6454.jpg/330px-Green_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_ochropus%29_W_IMG_6454.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Green_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_ochropus%29_W_IMG_6454.jpg/440px-Green_Sandpiper_%28Tringa_ochropus%29_W_IMG_6454.jpg 2x" data-file-width="663" data-file-height="500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Green_Sandpiper_(Tringa_ochropus)_W_IMG_6454.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Wintering adult near <a href="/wiki/Hodal" title="Hodal">Hodal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Faridabad_district" title="Faridabad district">Faridabad district</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haryana" title="Haryana">Haryana</a>, (<a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>This species is a somewhat plump <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> with a dark greenish-brown back and wings, greyish head and breast and otherwise white underparts. The back is spotted white to varying extents, being maximal in the breeding adult, and less in winter and young birds. The legs and short bill are both dark green.
</p><p>It is conspicuous and characteristically patterned in flight, with the wings dark above and below and a brilliant white rump. The latter feature reliably distinguishes it from the slightly smaller but otherwise very similar <a href="/wiki/Solitary_sandpiper" title="Solitary sandpiper">solitary sandpiper</a> (<i>T. solitaria</i>) of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.
</p><p>In flight it has a characteristic three-note whistle.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_ecology">Distribution and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Green_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG/220px-Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG/330px-Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG/440px-Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4016" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tringa_ochropus_MWNH_0209.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>It breeds across subarctic <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and east across the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> and is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>, wintering in southern Europe, the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Subcontinent">Indian Subcontinent</a>, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>, and tropical <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Food is small invertebrate items picked off the mud as this species works steadily around the edges of its chosen pond.
</p><p>This is not a gregarious species, although sometimes small numbers congregate in suitable feeding areas. Green sandpiper is very much a bird of <a href="/wiki/Freshwater" class="mw-redirect" title="Freshwater">freshwater</a>, and is often found in sites too restricted for other waders, which tend to like a clear all-round view.
</p><p>It lays 2–4 eggs in an old tree nest of another species, such as a <a href="/wiki/Fieldfare" title="Fieldfare">fieldfare</a> (<i>Turdus pilaris</i>). The <a href="/wiki/Clutch_(eggs)" title="Clutch (eggs)">clutch</a> takes about three weeks to hatch.
</p><p>The green sandpiper is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies. Widely distributed and not uncommon, it is not considered a <a href="/wiki/Threatened_species" title="Threatened species">threatened species</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> on a global scale.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
