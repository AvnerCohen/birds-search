<p>The <b>Lesser Black-backed Gull</b> (<i>Larus fuscus</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a> that breeds on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> coasts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering from the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> south to West <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is a regular winter visitor to the east coast of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, probably from the breeding population in <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>.</p>
<p>This species breeds colonially on coasts and lakes, making a lined nest on the ground or a cliff. Normally, three eggs are laid. In some cities the species nests within the urban environment, often in association with <a href="/wiki/European_Herring_Gull" title="European Herring Gull">Herring Gulls</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>They are smaller than the Herring Gull. The taxonomy of the Herring Gull / Lesser Black-backed Gull complex is very complicated; different authorities recognise between two and eight species. This group has a <a href="/wiki/Ring_species" title="Ring species">ring distribution</a> around the northern hemisphere. Differences between adjacent forms in this ring are fairly small, but by the time the circuit is completed, the end members, Herring Gull and Lesser Black-backed Gull, are clearly different species. The Lesser Black-backed Gull measures 51–64 cm (20–25 in), 124–150 cm (49–59 in) across the wings and weighs 452–1,100 g (1.00–2.4 lb), with the nominate race averaging slightly smaller than the other two subspecies.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Males, at average weight of 824&#160;g (1.82&#160;lb), are slightly larger than females, at an average of 708&#160;g (1.56&#160;lb). Among standard measurements, the <a href="/wiki/Wing_chord_(biology)" title="Wing chord (biology)">wing chord</a> is 38.3 to 45 cm (15.1 to 18 in), the <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is 4.2 to 5.8 cm (1.7 to 2.3 in) and the <a href="/wiki/Tarsus_(skeleton)" title="Tarsus (skeleton)">tarsus</a> is 5.2 to 6.9 cm (2.0 to 2.7 in).<sup id="cite_ref-Olsen_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsen-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Harrison_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrison-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CRC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> A confusable species in Europe is <a href="/wiki/Great_Black-backed_Gull" title="Great Black-backed Gull">Great Black-backed Gull</a>. The Lesser is a much smaller bird, with slimmer build, yellow rather than pinkish legs, and smaller white "mirrors" at the wing tips. The adults have black or dark grey wings (depending on race) and back. The bill is yellow with a red spot which young peck at, inducing feeding (see <a href="/wiki/Fixed_action_pattern" title="Fixed action pattern">fixed action pattern</a>). The head is greyer in winter, unlike Great Black-backed.</p>
<p>Young birds have scaly black-brown upperparts and a neat wing pattern. They take four years to reach maturity. Identification from juvenile Herring Gulls is most readily done by the more solidly dark (unbarred) tertial feathers.</p>
<p>The call is a "laughing" cry like that of the Herring Gull (to which this species is closely related), but with a markedly deeper pitch.</p>
<p>They are omnivores like most <i>Larus</i> gulls, and they will eat fish, insects, crustaceans, worms, starfish, molluscs, seeds, berries, small mammals, eggs, small birds, chicks, scraps, offal, and carrion.</p>
<p>There are three races:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Larus fuscus fuscus</i> – <a href="/wiki/Baltic_Sea" title="Baltic Sea">Baltic Sea</a>, eastern <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>. Mantle jet black.</li>
<li><i>Larus fuscus intermedius</i> – <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>. Mantle sooty black.</li>
<li><i>Larus fuscus graellsii</i> – <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Mantle dark grey.</li>
</ul>
