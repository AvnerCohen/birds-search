<p>The <b>Bridled Tern</b> (<i>Onychoprion anaethetus</i>, formerly <i>Sterna anaethetus</i> - see Bridge <i>et al.</i>, 2005) is a <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Tern" title="Tern">tern</a> family Sternidae. It is a bird of the tropical oceans.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Bridled_Tern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bridledtern1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Bridledtern1.jpg/170px-Bridledtern1.jpg" width="170" height="198" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bridledtern1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
In non-breeding plumage</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This is a medium-sized tern, at 30–32&#160;cm in length and with a 77–81&#160;cm wingspan similar to the <a href="/wiki/Common_Tern" title="Common Tern">Common Tern</a> in size, but more heavily built. The wings and deeply forked tail are long, and it has dark grey upperparts and white underparts. The forehead and eyebrows are white, as is a striking collar on the hindneck. It has black legs and bill. Juvenile Bridled Terns are scaly grey above and pale below.</p>
<p>This species is unlikely to be confused with any tern apart from the similarly dark-backed <a href="/wiki/Sooty_Tern" title="Sooty Tern">Sooty Tern</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Spectacled_Tern" title="Spectacled Tern">Spectacled Tern</a> from the Tropical Pacific. It is paler-backed than that Sooty, (but not as pale as the Grey-backed) and has a narrower white forehead and a pale neck collar.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Bridled_Tern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and movements">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_movements">Distribution and movements</span></h2>
<p>This bird is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and dispersive, wintering more widely through the tropical oceans. It has markedly marine habits compared to most terns. The Atlantic <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>melanopters</i> breeds in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a> and west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>; other races occur around the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>, but the exact number of valid subspecies is disputed. It is a rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Bridled_Tern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h2>
<p>This species breeds in colonies on rocky islands. It nests in a ground scrape or hole and lays one egg. It feeds by plunge-diving for fish in marine environments, but will also pick from the surface like the <a href="/wiki/Black_Tern" title="Black Tern">Black Tern</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Gull-billed_Tern" title="Gull-billed Tern">Gull-billed Tern</a>. It usually dives directly, and not from the "stepped-hover" favoured by the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_Tern" title="Arctic Tern">Arctic Tern</a>. The offering of fish by the male to the female is part of the courtship display.</p>
