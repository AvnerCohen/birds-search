<p>The <b>Black-crowned Night Heron</b> (<i>Nycticorax nycticorax</i>), commonly abbreviated to just <b>Night Heron</b> in <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>, is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a> found throughout a large part of the world, except in the coldest regions and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a> (where it is replaced by the closely related <a href="/wiki/Rufous_Night_Heron" title="Rufous Night Heron" class="mw-redirect">Rufous Night Heron</a>, with which it has <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridized</a> in the area of contact).</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-crowned_Night_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>

<p>Adults are approximately 64&#160;cm (25&#160;in) long and weigh 800&#160;g (28&#160;oz). They have a black crown and back with the remainder of the body white or grey, red eyes, and short yellow legs. They have pale grey wings and white under parts. Two or three long white plumes, erected in greeting and courtship displays, extend from the back of the head. The sexes are similar in appearance although the males are slightly larger. Black-crowned Night Herons do not fit the typical body form of the heron family. They are relatively stocky with shorter bills, legs, and necks than their more familiar cousins, the egrets and "day" herons. Their resting posture is normally somewhat hunched but when hunting they extend their necks and look more like other wading birds.</p>
<p>Immature birds have dull grey-brown plumage on their heads, wings, and backs, with numerous pale spots. Their underparts are paler and streaked with brown. The young birds have orange eyes and duller yellowish-green legs. They are very noisy birds in their nesting colonies, with calls that are commonly transcribed as <i>quok</i> or <i>woc</i>, <i>woc</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-crowned_Night_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p>The breeding habitat is fresh and salt-water <a href="/wiki/Wetland" title="Wetland">wetlands</a> throughout much of the world. The subspecies <i>N. n. hoactli</i> breeds in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> from <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> as far south as northern <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, <i>N. n. obscurus</i> in southernmost South America, <i>N. n. falklandicus</i> in the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>, and the nominate race <i>N. n. nycticorax</i> in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Black-crowned Night Herons nest in <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonies</a> on platforms of sticks in a group of trees, or on the ground in protected locations such as islands or reedbeds. Three to eight <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid.</p>
<p>This heron is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> in the northernmost part of its range, but otherwise resident (even in the cold <a href="/wiki/Patagonia" title="Patagonia">Patagonia</a>). The North American population winters in <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, the southern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies" class="mw-redirect">West Indies</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a> birds winter in tropical Africa and southern Asia.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-crowned_Night_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>These birds stand still at the water's edge and wait to ambush prey, mainly at night or early morning. They primarily eat small <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a>, aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Mammals" title="Mammals" class="mw-redirect">mammals</a> and small birds. During the day they rest in trees or bushes. <i>N. n. hoactli</i> is more gregarious outside the breeding season than the nominate race.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Black-crowned_Night_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span></h2>
<p>The scientific name, <i>Nycticorax</i>, means "night raven", and refers to this species' nocturnal habits and harsh <a href="/wiki/Crow" title="Crow">crow</a>-like call.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Falkland_Islands" title="Falkland Islands">Falkland Islands</a>, the bird is called "quark", which is an <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeia" title="Onomatopoeia">onomatopoeia</a> similar to its name in many other languages, like "kwak" in <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a> and <a href="/wiki/West_Frisian_language" title="West Frisian language">Frisian</a>, "kvakoš noční" in <a href="/wiki/Czech_language" title="Czech language">Czech</a>, "квак" in <a href="/wiki/Ukrainian_language" title="Ukrainian language">Ukrainian</a>, "кваква" in <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>, "Vạc" in <a href="/wiki/Vietnamese_language" title="Vietnamese language">Vietnamese</a>, "Kowak-malam" in <a href="/wiki/Indonesian_language" title="Indonesian language">Indonesian</a>, and "Waqwa" in <a href="/wiki/Quechua_languages" title="Quechua languages">Quechua</a>.</p>
