<p>The <b>Baillon's Crake</b> (<i>Porzana pusilla</i>) is a very small waterbird of the family <i>Rallidae</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p>Their breeding habitat is <a href="/wiki/Cyperaceae" title="Cyperaceae">sedge</a> beds in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, mainly in the east, and across <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. They used to breed in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> up to the mid-19th century, but the western European population declined through drainage. They nest in a dry location in wet sedge bogs, laying 4-8 <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>. This species is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">south Asia</a>.</p>
<p>It is also a resident breeder in Africa and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>. There is a single <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> record of this species on <a href="/wiki/Attu_Island" title="Attu Island">Attu Island</a> in September 2000.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>They are 16–18&#160;cm in length, and are similar to the only slightly larger <a href="/wiki/Little_Crake" title="Little Crake">Little Crake</a>. Baillon's Crake has a short straight bill, yellow or green without a red base. Adults have mainly brown upperparts with some white markings, and a blue-grey face and underparts. The rear flanks are barred black and white. They have green legs with long toes, and a short tail which is barred underneath.</p>
<p>Immature Baillon's Crakes are similar to the adults, but have extensively barred underparts. The downy chicks are black, as with all rails.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>These birds probe with their bill in mud or shallow water, also picking up food by sight. They mainly eat <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and aquatic animals.</p>
<p>Baillon's Crakes are very secretive in the breeding season, and are then mostly heard rather than seen. They are then noisy <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, with a rattling call like that of the <a href="/wiki/Edible_Frog" title="Edible Frog">Edible Frog</a>, or perhaps <a href="/wiki/Garganey" title="Garganey">Garganey</a>. They can be easier to see on migration or when wintering.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and nomenclature">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_nomenclature">Taxonomy and nomenclature</span></h2>
<p>This bird is named after French naturalist <a href="/wiki/Louis_Antoine_Francois_Baillon" title="Louis Antoine Francois Baillon" class="mw-redirect">Louis Antoine Francois Baillon</a>. The names <b>Marsh Crake</b> and <b>Tiny Crake</b> have previously been used to refer to this species.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span></h3>
<p>The Baillon's Crake is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_Crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span></h3>
<p>Baillon's Crakes are not listed as threatened on the Australian <a href="/wiki/Environment_Protection_and_Biodiversity_Conservation_Act_1999" title="Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999">Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999</a>. However, their conservation status varies from state to state within Australia. For example:</p>
<ul>
<li>The Baillon's Crake is listed as <b>threatened</b> on the Victorian <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_Fauna_Guarantee_Act_(1988)" title="Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988)" class="mw-redirect">Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vic_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vic-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Under this Act, an <i>Action Statement</i> for the recovery and future management of this species has not yet been prepared.<sup id="cite_ref-vic_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vic-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On the 2007 advisory list of threatened vertebrate fauna in Victoria, the Baillon's Crake is listed as <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">vulnerable</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DSE200_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSE200-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
