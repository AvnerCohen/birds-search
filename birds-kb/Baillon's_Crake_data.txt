<p><b>Baillon's crake</b> (<i>Zapornia pusilla</i>) or the <b>marsh crake</b>, is a small waterbird of the family <a href="/wiki/Rallidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Rallidae">Rallidae</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Their breeding habitat is <a href="/wiki/Cyperaceae" title="Cyperaceae">sedge</a> beds in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, mainly in the east, and across the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>. They used to breed in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> up to the mid-19th century, but the western European population declined through drainage. There has been a recovery in north-western Europe in recent years, with the recolonisation of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and breeding suspected in Britain; an Irish record in 2012 was the first there since the 1850s.<sup id="cite_ref-IrishBirds_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IrishBirds-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They nest in a dry location in wet sedge bogs, laying 4–8 <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>. This species is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">south Asia</a>.
</p><p>It is also a resident breeder in Africa and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>. There is a single <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> record of this species on <a href="/wiki/Attu_Island" title="Attu Island">Attu Island</a> in September 2000.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They are 16–18&#160;cm (6.3–7.1&#160;in) in length, and are similar to the only slightly larger <a href="/wiki/Little_crake" title="Little crake">little crake</a>. Baillon's crake has a short straight bill, yellow or green without a red base. Adults have mainly brown upperparts with some white markings, and a blue-grey face and underparts. The rear flanks are barred black and white. They have green legs with long toes, and a short tail which is barred underneath.
</p><p>Immature Baillon's crakes are similar to the adults, but have extensively barred underparts. The downy chicks are black, as with all rails.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG/220px-Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="282" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG/330px-Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG/440px-Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2384" data-file-height="3056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Porzana_pusilla_MWNH_0060.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden,_Germany" class="mw-redirect" title="Museum Wiesbaden, Germany">Museum Wiesbaden, Germany</a></div></div></div>
<p>These birds probe with their bill in mud or shallow water, also picking up food by sight. They mainly eat <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and aquatic animals.
</p><p>Baillon's crakes are very secretive in the breeding season, and are then mostly heard rather than seen. They are then noisy <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, with a rattling call like that of the <a href="/wiki/Edible_frog" title="Edible frog">edible frog</a>, or perhaps <a href="/wiki/Garganey" title="Garganey">garganey</a>. They can be easier to see on migration or when wintering.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_nomenclature">Taxonomy and nomenclature</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and nomenclature">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird is named after French naturalist <a href="/wiki/Louis_Antoine_Francois_Baillon" class="mw-redirect" title="Louis Antoine Francois Baillon">Louis Antoine Francois Baillon</a>. The names <b>marsh crake</b> and <b>tiny crake</b> have previously been used to refer to this species. <i>pusillus</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "very small".<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International">International</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: International">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Baillon's crake is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baillon%27s_crake&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baillon's crakes are not listed as threatened on the Australian <a href="/wiki/Environment_Protection_and_Biodiversity_Conservation_Act_1999" title="Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999">Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999</a>. However, their conservation status varies from state to state within Australia. For example:
</p>
<ul><li>The Baillon's crake is listed as threatened on the Victorian <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_Fauna_Guarantee_Act_(1988)" class="mw-redirect" title="Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988)">Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Under this Act, an <i>Action Statement</i> for the recovery and future management of this species has not yet been prepared.<sup id="cite_ref-guarantee_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guarantee-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On the 2007 advisory list of threatened vertebrate fauna in Victoria, the Baillon's crake is listed as <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">vulnerable</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DSE200_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSE200-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
