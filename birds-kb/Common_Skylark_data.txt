<p><i><b>Alauda</b></i> is a <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">larks</a> with four species found across much of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and in the mountains of north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and one species (the <a href="/wiki/Raso_Lark" title="Raso Lark">Raso Lark</a>) <a href="/wiki/Endemic_(ecology)" title="Endemic (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">endemic</a> to the islet of <a href="/wiki/Ilh%C3%A9u_Raso" title="Ilhéu Raso">Raso</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands" class="mw-redirect">Cape Verde Islands</a>.</p>
<p>These <a href="/wiki/Birds" title="Birds" class="mw-redirect">birds</a> are 14–18&#160;cm long and live in <a href="/wiki/Tillage" title="Tillage">cultivation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heath_(habitat)" title="Heath (habitat)" class="mw-redirect">heath</a>, natural <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a> and other open habitats. Their characteristic songs are delivered in flight. They are fairly undistinguished: streaked brown above and pale below, with a short, blunt, erectile <a href="/wiki/Crest_(feathers)" title="Crest (feathers)">crest</a>. In flight, they display a short tail and short broad wings. The tail and the rear edge of the wings are edged with white.</p>
<p>Their diet consists of seeds, supplemented with insects in the breeding season. They nest on the ground in tufts of <a href="/wiki/Grass" title="Grass">grass</a>, with 3-6 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> per clutch. They form <a href="/wiki/Flock_(birds)" title="Flock (birds)">flocks</a> when not breeding.</p>
