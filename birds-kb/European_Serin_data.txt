<p>The <b>European Serin</b>, or just <b>Serin</b> (<i>Serinus serinus</i>) is the smallest European species of the family of <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finches</a> (Fringillidae) and is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canary" title="Atlantic Canary">Canary</a>. Its diet consists mainly of a combination of <a href="/wiki/Bud" title="Bud">buds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Serin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>It is a small short-tailed bird, 11–12&#160;cm in length. The upper parts are dark-streaked greyish green, with a yellow rump. The yellow breast and white belly are also heavily streaked. The male has a brighter yellow face and breast, yellow wing bars and yellow tail sides. The song of this bird is a buzzing trill, very familiar in <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> countries.</p>
<p>It breeds across southern and central <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. Southern and Atlantic coast populations are largely resident, but the northern breeders <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> further south in Europe for the winter. Open woodland and cultivation, often with some <a href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer" class="mw-redirect">conifers</a>, is favoured for breeding. It builds its nest in a shrub or tree, laying 3-5 eggs. It forms flocks outside the breeding season, sometimes mixed with other finches.</p>
<p>The food is mainly seeds, and, in the breeding season, insects. This small <a href="/wiki/Serin" title="Serin" class="mw-redirect">serin</a> is an active and often conspicuous bird.</p>
