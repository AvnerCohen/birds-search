<p>The <b>lesser kestrel</b> (<i>Falco naumanni</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Falcon" title="Falcon">falcon</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> breeds from the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> across <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and Central Asia, to China and <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>. It is a summer <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, wintering in Africa and <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and sometimes even to India and Iraq. It is rare north of its breeding range, and declining in its European range. The genus name derives from Late Latin <i>falx</i>, <i>falcis</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Sickle" title="Sickle">sickle</a>, referencing the claws of the bird,<sup id="cite_ref-SOED_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SOED-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the species name commemorates the German naturalist <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Naumann" title="Johann Friedrich Naumann">Johann Friedrich Naumann</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_kestrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg/220px-Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="162" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg/330px-Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg/440px-Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="471" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Female_Lesser_Kestrel_in_flight.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Female in flight showing whitish talons</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Falnau.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Falnau.jpg/220px-Falnau.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Falnau.jpg/330px-Falnau.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Falnau.jpg/440px-Falnau.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2521" data-file-height="1683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Falnau.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lesser kestrels mating</div></div></div>
<p>It is a small <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>, 27–33&#160;cm (11–13&#160;in) in length with a 63–72&#160;cm (25–28&#160;in) wingspan. It looks very much like the larger <a href="/wiki/Common_kestrel" title="Common kestrel">common kestrel</a> but has proportionally shorter wings and tail. It shares a brown back and barred grey underparts with the larger species. The male has a grey head and tail like male common kestrels, but lacks the dark spotting on the back, the black <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> stripe, and has grey patches in the wings.
</p><p>The female and young birds are slightly paler than their relative, but are so similar that call and structure are better guides than plumage. The call is a diagnostic harsh <i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://drive.google.com/open?id=1mdWluvoZoGLLGCGo3qCmhKWmEWgQu1Gg">chay-chay-chay</a></i>, unlike the common kestrel's <i>kee-kee-kee</i>. Neither sex has dark <a href="/wiki/Claw" title="Claw">talons</a> as is usual in falcons; those of this species are a peculiar whitish-horn color. This, however, is only conspicuous when birds are seen at very close range, e.g. in captivity.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_kestrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite its outward similarity, this species appears not to be closely related to the common kestrel. In fact, <a href="/wiki/MtDNA" class="mw-redirect" title="MtDNA">mtDNA</a> <a href="/wiki/Cytochrome_b" title="Cytochrome b">cytochrome <i>b</i></a> <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" class="mw-redirect" title="DNA sequence">sequence</a> analysis places it at a <a href="/wiki/Basal_(phylogenetics)" title="Basal (phylogenetics)">basal</a> position with regards to the other "true" kestrels (i.e., excluding the <a href="/wiki/American_kestrel" title="American kestrel">American kestrel</a> and probably the grey <a href="/wiki/Kestrel#Description" title="Kestrel">African kestrels</a> too).<sup id="cite_ref-Groombridge2002_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Groombridge2002-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Its divergence is tentatively placed to around the <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a>-<a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> boundary (<a href="/wiki/Messinian" title="Messinian">Messinian</a> to <a href="/wiki/Zanclean" title="Zanclean">Zanclean</a>, or about 7–3.5 <a href="/wiki/Mya_(unit)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mya (unit)">mya</a>). The <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological</a> similarity with the common kestrel is most puzzling, but still it appears to betray the present species' actual relationships: the lack of a <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> stripe seems ancestral for kestrels, and the grey wing colour unites the lesser kestrel with most other <i>Falco</i> species, but not the other true kestrels.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Kestrel.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Lesser_Kestrel.png/220px-Lesser_Kestrel.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Lesser_Kestrel.png/330px-Lesser_Kestrel.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Lesser_Kestrel.png/440px-Lesser_Kestrel.png 2x" data-file-width="1080" data-file-height="772" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Kestrel.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lesser kestrel&#160;– a very rare winter migrant to India</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_kestrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lesser kestrel is, as the name implies, a smaller and more delicate bird than the common kestrel, and it is entirely <a href="/wiki/Sympatric" class="mw-redirect" title="Sympatric">sympatric</a> in its breeding range with it; they compete to a limited extent. Thus, the possibility that there is some form of adaptive advantage to the similar coloration deserves study. Considering that the lesser kestrel would in fact have an advantage if some would-be <a href="/wiki/Predator" class="mw-redirect" title="Predator">predators</a> confused it with the larger species and consequently avoided it, it might be a case of <a href="/wiki/M%C3%BCllerian_mimicry" title="Müllerian mimicry">Müllerian mimicry</a>.
</p><p>The lesser kestrel eats <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, but also small birds, reptiles and rodents (especially mice),<sup id="cite_ref-ADW_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ADW-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> which are often taken on the ground. It nests colonially on buildings, cliffs, or in tree holes, laying up to 3–6 eggs. No nest structure is built, which is typical for falcons. On their wintering grounds in West Africa, lesser kestrels favor a "latitude belt"<sup id="cite_ref-birdsnews_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birdsnews-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> through Senegal where locusts and grasshoppers are plentiful. Surveys of lesser kestrels wintering in January 2007 by the <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ligue_pour_la_Protection_des_Oiseaux" class="extiw" title="fr:Ligue pour la Protection des Oiseaux">Ligue pour la Protection des Oiseaux</a> revealed them roosting communally. A roost in Senegal discovered during one of the surveys held 28,600 birds, together with 16,000 <a href="/wiki/Scissor-tailed_kite" title="Scissor-tailed kite">scissor-tailed kites</a> <i>Chelictinia riocourii</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-birdlife.news_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birdlife.news-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is widespread and plentiful on a global scale, and the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> have classed it as Least Concern.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Apart from possible <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a>, it appears that indiscriminate use of <a href="/wiki/Pesticide" title="Pesticide">pesticides</a> has a strong effect on this species due to its insectivorous habits.<sup id="cite_ref-birdlife_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birdlife-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
