<p>The <b>Eurasian Siskin</b> (<i>Carduelis spinus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird in the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> family Fringillidae. It is also called the <b>European Siskin</b>, <b>Common Siskin</b> or just <b>Siskin</b>. Other (archaic) names include <i>Black-headed Goldfinch</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>barley bird</i> and <i>aberdevine</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lockwood_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lockwood-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is very common throughout Europe and Asia. It is found in forested areas, both <a href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer" class="mw-redirect">coniferous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mixed_woodland" title="Mixed woodland" class="mw-redirect">mixed woodland</a> where it feeds on seeds of all kinds, especially of <a href="/wiki/Alder" title="Alder">alder</a> and conifers.</p>
<p>It can be distinguished from other similar finches by the color of the plumage. The upper parts are greyish green and the under parts grey-streaked white. Its wings are black with a conspicuous yellow wing bar, and the tail is black with yellow sides. The male has a mainly yellow face and breast, with a neat black cap. Female and young birds have a greyish green head and no cap. It is a trusting, sociable and active bird. The song of this bird is a pleasant mix of twitters and trills. For these reasons it is often raised in captivity.</p>
<p>These birds have an unusual <a href="/wiki/Migration_(birds)" title="Migration (birds)" class="mw-redirect">migration</a> pattern as every few years in winter they migrate southwards in large numbers. The reasons for this behavior are not known but may be related to climatic factors and above all the availability of food. In this way overwintering populations can thrive where food is abundant. This small finch is an acrobatic feeder, often hanging upside-down like a <a href="/wiki/Titmouse" title="Titmouse" class="mw-redirect">tit</a>. It will visit garden bird feeding stations.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Siskin is a small, short-tailed bird, 11–12.5 centimetres (4.3–4.9 in) in length<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Citation_style" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="Wikipedia:Citing_sources#Citation_style">full citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> with a wingspan that ranges from 20–23 centimetres (7.9–9.1 in).<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-isr_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isr-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fichero_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fichero-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> It weighs between 10–18 grams (0.35–0.63 oz).<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-isr_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isr-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hume_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hume-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The bird's appearance shows <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexual dimorphism</a>. The male has a greyish green back; yellow rump; the sides of the tail are yellow and the end is black; the wings are black with a distinctive yellow wing stripe; its breast is yellowish becoming whiter and striped towards the <a href="/wiki/Cloaca" title="Cloaca">cloaca</a>; it has a black bib (or chin patch) and on its head it has two yellow <a href="/wiki/Ear" title="Ear">auriculas</a> and a black cap.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The amount of black on the bib is very variable between males and the size of the bib has been related to dominance within a flock.<sup id="cite_ref-blackrib_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blackrib-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> of the female is more olive-coloured than the male. The cap and the auriculas are greenish with a white bib and a rump that is a slightly striped whitish yellow.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from October 2011">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> The young have a similar colouration to the females, with drab colours and a more subdued plumage.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The shape of the Siskin's <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">beak</a> is determined by its feeding habits. It is strong although it is also slender in order to pick up the seeds on which they feed. The legs and feet are dark brown and the eyes are black.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from October 2011">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>It has a rapid and bounding flight pattern that is similar to other finches.<sup id="cite_ref-hume_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hume-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Siskin is easy to recognize, but in some instances it can be confused with other finches such as the <a href="/wiki/Citril_Finch" title="Citril Finch">Citril Finch</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Greenfinch" title="Greenfinch">Greenfinch</a> or the <a href="/wiki/European_Serin" title="European Serin">European Serin</a>.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from October 2011">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Whereas the <a href="/wiki/European_Siskin" title="European Siskin" class="mw-redirect">European Siskin</a>, in many plumages, is a bright bird, the <a href="/wiki/Pine_Siskin" title="Pine Siskin">Pine Siskin</a> in its typical <a href="/wiki/Morphotype" title="Morphotype" class="mw-redirect">morph</a> is a drab by comparison. Adult male European Siskins are bright green and yellow with a black cap, and an unstreaked throat and breast; the Pine Siskin does not have a corresponding bright plumage. Adult female European Siskins also usually have green and yellow plumage tones: for example, yellow in the <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a> and on the sides of the breast, green tones in the mantle and yellow in the rump. Adult Pine Siskins of the typical morph do not have green and yellow tones, although juveniles can have a yellowish-buff wash on their underparts and buff-toned wingbars, for a short period prior to their autumn migration. The ground colour of the underparts of European Siskin is normally pure white, whereas on Pine Siskin it is usually a dirtier colour. In female and juvenile European Siskin, the centre of the belly and lower breast are often largely or entirely unstreaked, whereas in most Pine Siskins the streaking extends across the whole of the underparts. The wingbars of European Siskin are broad and yellow (with the tips white) whereas they are normally narrower and buffish-white in Pine Siskin, contrasting with the bright yellow flash at the base of the primaries. Pine Siskins have a longer bill, usually with a straight <a href="/wiki/Beak#Culmen" title="Beak">culmen</a>, compare with a short bill in European Siskin, with a decurved culmen. There is a green morph of Pine Siskin which is closer in appearance to European Siskin; these birds make up only 1% of the population. These are closer in appearance to female European Siskin, but differ in that they have a yellow-wash on the undertail-coverts (white on European Siskin), no yellow in the supercilium, reduced underparts streaking, and much yellow at the base of the tail and remiges; there may also be a difference in the extent of yellow in the underparts but this needs further study.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Song and call">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Song_and_call">Song and call</span></h3>
<p>This bird has two <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">calls</a>, both powerful but conflicting, one is descending and the other is ascending, their <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeic" title="Onomatopoeic" class="mw-redirect">onomatopoeic</a> sounds can be represented as <i>"tilu"</i> and <i>"tluih"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> On occasions they also issue a harsh rattling chirrup.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The song is similar to the other finches, a smooth and rapid twitter and trill with a long duration and which is occasionally interrupted by a stronger of shorter syllable. Siskins sing throughout the year and often in groups.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>The Siskin was first was described by <a href="/wiki/Linnaeus" title="Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a> in 1758 as <i>Fringilla spinus</i>, in the <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_naturae" title="10th edition of Systema naturae" class="mw-redirect">10th edition</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_naturae" title="Systema naturae" class="mw-redirect">Systema naturae</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> In 1760, <a href="/wiki/Mathurin_Jacques_Brisson" title="Mathurin Jacques Brisson">Brisson</a> described the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Carduelis" title="Carduelis">Carduelis</a></i>, where this species would finally be placed.</p>
<p>Despite being found across a wide area it is a monotypic species, that is, there are no distinct <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material in the vicinity of this tag failed verification of its source citation(s) from October 2011">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-Avi_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Avi-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> This could be explained by a number of factors, such as spatial variability of individuals in breeding areas between years, the large overwintering area which supposes a constant genetic interchange,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text in the vicinity of this tag needs clarification or removal of jargon from October 2011">clarification needed</span></a></i>]</sup> and females having a number of clutches of eggs in one breeding season, each in a different place.<sup id="cite_ref-payevski_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-payevski-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Phylogeny" title="Phylogeny" class="mw-redirect">phylogeny</a> has been obtained by Antonio Arnaiz-Villena et al.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Adult_male_siskin.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Adult_male_siskin.JPG/220px-Adult_male_siskin.JPG" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Adult_male_siskin.JPG/330px-Adult_male_siskin.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Adult_male_siskin.JPG/440px-Adult_male_siskin.JPG 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Adult_male_siskin.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Adult male at a bird table.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This species can be found across the greater part of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a> and the north of Africa. Its breeding area is separated into two zones, each side of the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" title="Palearctic" class="mw-redirect">Palearctic</a> <a href="/wiki/Ecozone" title="Ecozone" class="mw-redirect">ecozone</a>: the east coast of Asia and the central and northern part of Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-mma_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mma-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>These birds can be found throughout the year in Central Europe and some mountain ranges in the south of the continent. They are present in the north of <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and they over-winter in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> basin and the area around the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>. In China they breed in the <a href="/wiki/Khingan_Mountains" title="Khingan Mountains" class="mw-redirect">Khingan Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> and in <a href="/wiki/Jiangsu" title="Jiangsu">Jiangsu</a> province; they spend summer in <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a>, Taiwan, the valleys of the lower <a href="/wiki/Yangtse_River" title="Yangtse River" class="mw-redirect">Yangtse River</a> and the south east coast.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Eurasian Siskin is occasionally seen in North America.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> There is also a similar and closely related North America counterpart, the <a href="/wiki/Pine_Siskin" title="Pine Siskin">Pine Siskin</a>, <i>Carduelis pinus</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-blackrib_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blackrib-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Their seasonal distribution is also marked by the fact that they follow an anomalous <a href="/wiki/Animal_migration" title="Animal migration">migration</a> pattern. Every few years they migrate southwards in larger numbers and the overwintering populations in the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a> are greatly augmented.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> This event has been the object of diverse theories, one theory suggests that it occurs in the years when <a href="/wiki/Picea_abies" title="Picea abies">Norway Spruce</a> produces abundant fruit in the centre and north of Europe, causing populations to increase. An alternative theory is that greater migration occurs when the preferred food of <a href="/wiki/Alder" title="Alder">alder</a> or <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> seed fails. This species will form large flocks outside the breeding season, often mixed with <a href="/wiki/Redpoll" title="Redpoll">redpolls</a>.</p>
<p>It is a bird that does not remain for long in one area but which varies the areas it used for breeding, feeding, over-wintering from one year to the next.</p>
<p>Its habitat is forested areas at a particular altitude on a mountain side and they have a certain predilection for humid areas.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mma_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mma-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer" class="mw-redirect">Coniferous</a> woodland, especially <a href="/wiki/Spruce" title="Spruce">Spruce</a>, is favoured for breeding. It builds its <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> in a tree, laying 2–6 eggs. The British range of this once local breeder has expanded greatly due to an increase in commercial conifer plantations. The Siskin also breeds in <a href="/wiki/Temperate_broadleaf_forest" title="Temperate broadleaf forest" class="mw-redirect">mixed woodland</a>; while in winter they prefer <a href="/wiki/Crop_residue" title="Crop residue">stubble</a> and crops and areas containing trees with seeds.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Conservation status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_status">Conservation status</span></h2>
<p>The worldwide population of the Siskin is estimated as between 20 and 36 million.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> The European population is estimated as between 2.7 and 15 million pairs.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> There does not seem to be a major decline in population numbers and for this reason the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a> has listed their conservation status as <a href="/wiki/Least_Concern" title="Least Concern">Least Concern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN2012_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN2012-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> The Siskin appears in Annex II of the <a href="/wiki/Berne_Convention_(conservation)" title="Berne Convention (conservation)" class="mw-redirect">Berne Convention</a> as a protected bird species.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_(Marek_Szczepanek).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/240px-Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg" width="240" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/360px-Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/480px-Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carduelis_spinus_1_tom_(Marek_Szczepanek).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Perched male observing its next destination.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>They are very active and restless birds. They are also very social, forming small cohesive flocks especially in autumn and winter.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> They are fairly trusting of humans, it being possible to observe them from a short distance. During the breeding season, however, they are much more timid, solitary and difficult to observe. For this reason there is a <a href="/wiki/German_folklore" title="German folklore">German legend</a> which says that Siskins guard a magic stone in their nests that makes them invisible.<sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup> It is one of the few species which has been described as exhibiting "allofeeding"' behavior, this is where subordinates (of the same sex) regurgitate food for the dominant members of the group,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span>[</span>27<span>]</span></a></sup> which creates a strong cohesion in the flocks and implies a hierarchical structure within the group.<sup id="cite_ref-allo_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allo-27"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Carduelis_spinus_naumann.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Carduelis_spinus_naumann.jpg/220px-Carduelis_spinus_naumann.jpg" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/82/Carduelis_spinus_naumann.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/82/Carduelis_spinus_naumann.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Carduelis_spinus_naumann.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Pair (male above) feeding in an alder tree. Illustration by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Naumann" title="Johann Friedrich Naumann">Naumann</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Siskin is mainly a <a href="/wiki/Granivore" title="Granivore" class="mw-redirect">granivore</a> although it varies its diet depending on the season. It feeds in trees avoiding eating on the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-hume_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hume-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In autumn and winter its diet is based on the seeds of <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> trees such as <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> and above all <a href="/wiki/Alder" title="Alder">alder</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sven_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sven-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> They also visit cultivated areas and pasture where they join with other finches in eating the seeds of various <i><a href="/wiki/Compositae" title="Compositae" class="mw-redirect">Compositae</a></i> such as <a href="/wiki/Thistle" title="Thistle">thistles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taraxacum" title="Taraxacum">dandelions</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Artemisia_(genus)" title="Artemisia (genus)">Artemisia</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Centaurea" title="Centaurea">knapweeds</a> and other herbaceous plants such as <a href="/wiki/Hypericum_perforatum" title="Hypericum perforatum" class="mw-redirect">St. John's wort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ulmaria" title="Ulmaria" class="mw-redirect">meadowsweet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rumex" title="Rumex">sorrel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fichero_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fichero-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In spring, during the breeding season, they are found in coniferous forests. At this time their feeding is based on the seeds of these trees, especially on trees belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genera</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Abies" title="Abies" class="mw-redirect">Abies</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Picea" title="Picea" class="mw-redirect">Picea</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Larix" title="Larix" class="mw-redirect">Larix</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-terra_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terra-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> They also feed on <a href="/wiki/Elm" title="Elm">elms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Poplar" title="Poplar" class="mw-redirect">poplars</a>. When feeding the young they eat more insects, mainly <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetles</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/Protein" title="Protein">proteins</a> they contain help the chicks to grow. In summer their feeding is more varied, adding other herbaceous plants to their diet of conifer seeds: <a href="/wiki/Chenopodium" title="Chenopodium">goosefoots</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Compositae" title="Compositae" class="mw-redirect">Compositae</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-terra_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terra-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span></h3>
<p>Pairs are generally formed during the winter period before migration.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span>[</span>30<span>]</span></a></sup> The males compete aggressively for the females. As part of the courtship the male plumps up the feathers of the <a href="/wiki/Pileus" title="Pileus">pileus</a> and rump, making itself bigger, extending the tail and singing repeatedly.<sup id="cite_ref-terra_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terra-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span>[</span>31<span>]</span></a></sup> They also make mating flights from tree to tree, although they are not as eye-catching as the flights of the other finches.<sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> They construct a <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> that is generally located at the end of a relatively high branch in a conifer, such that the nest is reasonably hidden and difficult to see.<sup id="cite_ref-fichero_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fichero-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> On the Iberian Peninsula they make their nests in <i><a href="/wiki/Abies_alba" title="Abies alba">afirs</a>,</i> <a href="/wiki/Pinus_sylvestris" title="Pinus sylvestris" class="mw-redirect">Scotch Pine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pinus_nigra" title="Pinus nigra">Corsican Pine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span>[</span>32<span>]</span></a></sup> They form small colonies of up to six pairs with the nests located near to each other.<sup id="cite_ref-allo_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-allo-27"><span>[</span>28<span>]</span></a></sup> The nest is small and bowl-shaped. It is made from small twigs, dried grasses, <a href="/wiki/Moss" title="Moss">moss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichen</a> and lined with <a href="/wiki/Down_feather" title="Down feather">down</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-terra_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terra-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The first brood is born in mid-April.<sup id="cite_ref-terra_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terra-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> The female lays between 2 and 6 eggs.<sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-red_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-red-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> The eggs are white or light grey or light blue, with small brown spots<sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> and they are approximately 16.5mm by 12mm in size.<sup id="cite_ref-fichero_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fichero-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" title="Avian incubation" class="mw-redirect">Incubation</a> takes between 10 and 14 days and is carried out entirely by the female.<sup id="cite_ref-isr_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isr-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup> The chicks are <a href="/wiki/Altricial" title="Altricial">altricial</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nidicolous" title="Nidicolous">nidicolous</a>. They leave the nest after 15 days in a semi-feathered condition. They then remain close to the nest area for up to a month when, with their plumage now complete, they disperse.<sup id="cite_ref-terra_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terra-28"><span>[</span>29<span>]</span></a></sup> The Siskin usually has a second brood, from the middle of June up to the middle of July.<sup id="cite_ref-paj_10-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-paj-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Captivity">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Captivity">Captivity</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG/220px-Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG/330px-Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG/440px-Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sagra_dei_Osei_Carduelis_spinus.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Structure holding cages used for listening to Siskins at the singing contest of Sagra dei Osei, Italy.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Like many of the finches the Siskin is valued by <a href="/wiki/Aviculture" title="Aviculture">aviculturalists</a> as a domestic bird for its song and appearance. They do not require specific care and adapt well to captivity, although they do not breed well in captivity.<sup id="cite_ref-red_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-red-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> There are no specific diseases that affect the species, although they can show certain intestinal pathologies associated with a poor diet.<sup id="cite_ref-red_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-red-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> They live for between 11 and 14 years,<sup id="cite_ref-fr_24-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fr-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-red_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-red-32"><span>[</span>33<span>]</span></a></sup> in sharp contrast to the 2 or 3 years it is estimated they live in the wild.<sup id="cite_ref-hume_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hume-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>They form hybrids with some other finches (for example <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canary" title="Atlantic Canary">canaries</a>) giving rise to intermediate species.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span>[</span>34<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span>[</span>35<span>]</span></a></sup> Hybridization also occurs in nature without human intervention.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability"><span title="The material in the vicinity of this tag failed verification of its source citation(s) from October 2011">not in citation given</span></a></i>]</sup><sup id="cite_ref-Avi_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Avi-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> In some areas individuals that are found are the result of escapes or releases of captive birds.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span>[</span>36<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Siskin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Miscellany">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Miscellany">Miscellany</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>, Benin and <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a> have all issued postage stamps bearing the image of the Siskin.<sup id="cite_ref-isr_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-isr-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span>[</span>37<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Saint_Petersburg" title="Saint Petersburg">Saint Petersburg</a> there is a statue of a Siskin, as its colours are the same as the uniform worn by the students at an elite school in the city. These students have come to be known by the <a href="/wiki/Sobriquet" title="Sobriquet">sobriquet</a> <i>Siskin</i>, <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>: <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">Чиж</span>. This term was popularized in the Russian children's song <i><a href="/wiki/Chizhik-Pyzhik" title="Chizhik-Pyzhik">Chizhik-Pyzhik</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span>[</span>38<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
