<p>The <b>Oriental Reed Warbler</b> (<i>Acrocephalus orientalis</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of eastern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> belonging to the reed warbler genus <i><a href="/wiki/Acrocephalus" title="Acrocephalus">Acrocephalus</a></i>. It was formerly classified as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Reed_Warbler" title="Great Reed Warbler">Great Reed Warbler</a> (<i>A. arundinaceus</i>) of western <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_Reed_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>It is a large warbler, 18–20&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 23–26&#160;cm. The <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> is brown above with a paler rump and whitish tips to the tail feathers. The underparts are whitish below, browner on the flanks and undertail-coverts. There are narrow greyish streaks on the throat and breast. It has a dark eyestripe and a whitish stripe above the eye. The fairly long, heavy <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is brownish above and pink below with a bright orange <a href="/wiki/Gape" title="Gape">gape</a>. The feet are grey.</p>
<p>It is slightly smaller, slenderer and shorter-winged than the Great Reed-warbler. Its tail is shorter and more square-ended than that of the <a href="/wiki/Clamorous_Reed_Warbler" title="Clamorous Reed Warbler">Clamorous Reed Warbler</a> (<i>A. stentoreus</i>) and it has a slightly longer <a href="/wiki/Flight_feathers#Primaries" title="Flight feathers" class="mw-redirect">primary</a> projection and a slightly shorter and thicker bill. The pale tip to the tail separates it from both species.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> is a mixture of warbling phrases and guttural, croaking notes. It is given from a prominent perch such as the top of a reed stem or bush. The bird also has a loud, harsh <i>chack</i> call.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_Reed_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>The breeding range covers southern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, northern, central and eastern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It winters in north-east <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and across <a href="/wiki/South-east_Asia" title="South-east Asia" class="mw-redirect">South-east Asia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, occasionally reaching <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. It has occurred as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" title="Vagrancy in birds" class="mw-redirect">vagrant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>. It breeds mainly in <a href="/wiki/Reedbed" title="Reedbed" class="mw-redirect">reedbeds</a> and can also be found in marshes, <a href="/wiki/Paddy_field" title="Paddy field">paddy fields</a>, grassland and scrub where it forages for <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Oriental_Reed_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span></h2>
<p>Oriental Reed Warblers have small breeding territories and can achieve high population densities. The <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nest</a> is built 1-1.5 metres above the ground among reed stems. Two to six <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid and are <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" title="Avian incubation" class="mw-redirect">incubated</a> for 12 to 14 days. The young birds <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a> after 10 to 15 days. Major <a href="/wiki/Predator" title="Predator" class="mw-redirect">predators</a> at the nest include the <a href="/wiki/Siberian_Weasel" title="Siberian Weasel" class="mw-redirect">Siberian Weasel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a> of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Elaphe" title="Elaphe">Elaphe</a></i>.</p>
