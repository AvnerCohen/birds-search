<p>The <b>Whimbrel</b> (<i>Numenius phaeopus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the large family Scolopacidae. It is one of the most widespread of the <a href="/wiki/Curlew" title="Curlew">curlews</a>, breeding across much of <a href="/wiki/Subarctic" title="Subarctic">subarctic</a> <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> as far south as <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>.</p>
<p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> species wintering on coasts in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, south <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> into <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a> and southern North America. It is also a coastal bird during <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migration</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It is fairly gregarious outside the breeding season.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Whimbrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>This is a fairly large wader though mid-sized as a member of the curlew genus. It is 37–47 cm (15–19 in) in length, 75–90 cm (30–35 in) and 270–493 g (9.5–17.4 oz) in weight.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is mainly greyish brown, with a white back and rump (subspecies <i>N. p. phaeopus</i> and <i>N. p. alboaxillaris</i> only), and a long curved <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> (longest in the adult female) with a kink rather than a smooth curve. It is generally wary. The usual <a href="/wiki/Bird_call" title="Bird call" class="mw-redirect">call</a> is a rippling whistle, prolonged into a trill for the song.</p>
<p>The only similar common species over most of this bird's range are larger curlews. The Whimbrel is smaller, has a shorter, decurved bill and has a central crown stripe and strong supercilia.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Whimbrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span></h3>
<p>There are four subspecies:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Numenius phaeopus phaeopus</i> - northern Europe, northwestern Asia</li>
<li><i>Numenius phaeopus variegatus</i> - northeastern Asia</li>
<li><i>Numenius phaeopus alboaxillaris</i> - central Asia (rare, endangered)</li>
<li><i>Numenius phaeopus hudsonicus</i> (<b>Hudsonian Curlew</b>) - northern North America</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Whimbrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span></h2>
<p>This species feeds by probing soft mud for small <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a> and by picking small <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a> and similar prey off the surface. Prior to migration, berries become an important part of their diet. It also eats blue butterflies.</p>
<p>The nest is a bare scrape on <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> or Arctic moorland. Three to five eggs are laid. Adults are very defensive of nesting area and will even attack humans who come too close.</p>
<p>Near the end of the 19th century, hunting on their migration routes took a heavy toll on this bird's numbers; the population has since recovered.</p>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a> it breeds in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, particularly around <a href="/wiki/Shetland" title="Shetland">Shetland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Hebrides" title="Outer Hebrides">Outer Hebrides</a> as well as the mainland at <a href="/wiki/Sutherland" title="Sutherland">Sutherland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caithness" title="Caithness">Caithness</a>.</p>
<p>The Whimbrel is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
