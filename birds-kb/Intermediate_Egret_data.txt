<p>The <b>intermediate egret</b>, <b>median egret</b>, <b>smaller egret</b>, or <b>yellow-billed egret</b> (<i>Ardea intermedia</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a>.  Some taxonomists put the species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Egretta" title="Egretta">Egretta</a></i> or <i>Mesophoyx</i>.  It is a resident breeder from east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> across the <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">Indian subcontinent</a> to <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediate_egret&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some authorities classify the intermediate egret in its own <a href="/wiki/Monotypy" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypy">monotypic</a> genus, <i>Mesophoyx</i>, while others place it with the smaller egrets in <i><a href="/wiki/Egretta" title="Egretta">Egretta</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are three recognised subspecies,<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and these are sometimes raised in to species:<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>A. i. brachyrhyncha</i> <small><a href="/wiki/Alfred_Brehm" title="Alfred Brehm">Brehm</a>, 1854</small> "yellow-billed egret" - sub-Saharan Africa</li>
<li><i>A.i. intermedia</i> <small>Wagler, 1827</small> "intermediate egret" - Asia from the Russian Far East to Japan to India and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Sundas" class="mw-redirect" title="Greater Sundas">Greater Sundas</a></li>
<li><i>A. i. plumifera</i> <small><a href="/wiki/John_Gould" title="John Gould">Gould</a>, 1848</small> "plumed egret" - eastern Indonesia, <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and Australia.</li></ul>
<p><i>A.i. intermedia</i> differs from <i>A.i. brachyrhyncha</i> and <i>A. i. plumifera</i> by having a black bill when in breeding plumage, while <i>A.i. plumata</i> has a yellow-and-pink bill and <i>A. i. brachyrhyncha</i> has much yellower <a href="/wiki/Lore_(anatomy)" title="Lore (anatomy)">lores</a> and face. A further difference between the nominate subspecies and the "yellow-billed" subspecies are that the "intermediate" subspecies has black at the top of the legs compared to reddish in the yellow-billed egret.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediate_egret&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species, as its scientific name implies, is intermediate in size between the <a href="/wiki/Great_egret" title="Great egret">great egret</a> and smaller white egrets like the <a href="/wiki/Little_egret" title="Little egret">little egret</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cattle_egret" title="Cattle egret">cattle egret</a>, though nearer to little than great.  It is about 56–72&#160;cm (22–28&#160;in) long with a 105–115&#160;cm (41–45&#160;in) wingspan and weighs c. 400&#160;g (14&#160;oz),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> with all-white plumage, generally dark legs and a thickish yellow bill. Breeding birds may have a reddish or black bill, greenish yellow <a href="/wiki/Gape" class="mw-redirect" title="Gape">gape</a> skin, loose filamentous plumes on their breast and back, and dull yellow or pink on their upper legs (regional variations).  The sexes are similar.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_great_egret">Differences from great egret</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediate_egret&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Differences from great egret">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg/170px-Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg.png" decoding="async" width="170" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg/255px-Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg/340px-Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="744" data-file-height="1052" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Large_Intermediate_Egrets.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sketch comparing gapes of intermediate and great egrets</div></div></div>
<p>The non-breeding colours are similar, but the intermediate is smaller, with neck length a little less than body length, a slightly domed head, and a shorter, thicker bill. The <a href="/wiki/Great_egret" title="Great egret">great egret</a> has a noticeable kink near the middle of its neck, and the top of its longer bill nearly aligns with the flat top of its head.  Close up, great egret's gape line extends behind the eye, while the intermediate's is less pointed and ends below the eye.  The intermediate tends to stalk upright with neck extended forward. The great is more patient, often adopting a sideways-leaning "one-eyed" stance.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_from_little_egret">Differences from little egret</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediate_egret&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Differences from little egret">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Little_egret" title="Little egret">Little egrets</a> have yellow-soled feet and black bills. They often run after fish in shallow water. Breeding birds have long nuptial plumes on the back of their heads.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Intermediate_egret&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The intermediate egret stalks its prey methodically in shallow coastal or fresh water, including flooded fields.  It eats fish, frogs, crustaceans and insects.  It often nests in <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonies</a> with other herons, usually on platforms of sticks in trees or shrubs.  Two to five eggs are laid, the clutch size varying with region.
</p>
