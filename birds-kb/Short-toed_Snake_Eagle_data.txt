<p>The <b>short-toed snake eagle</b> (<i>Circaetus gallicus</i>), also known as the <b>short-toed eagle</b>, is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>, which also includes many other diurnal <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">raptors</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Kite_(bird)" title="Kite (bird)">kites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buzzard" title="Buzzard">buzzards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harrier_(bird)" title="Harrier (bird)">harriers</a>. The genus name <i>Circaetus</i> is from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>kirkos</i>, a type of hawk, and <i>aetos</i>, "eagle". The specific <i>gallicus</i> means "of <a href="/wiki/Gaul" title="Gaul">Gaul</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-Jobling_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jobling-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range_and_habitat">Range and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Short-toed_snake_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a> species found throughout the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> basin, into <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, and parts of <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Subcontinent">Indian Subcontinent</a> and also further east in some <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> islands.
</p><p>Those present on the northern edge of the Mediterranean and other parts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> mainly to <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a> north of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>, leaving in September/October and returning in April/May.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the Middle and Far East the populations are resident. In Europe, it is most numerous in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> where it is fairly common but elsewhere it is rare in many parts of its range. A bird on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a>, in October 1999 was the first confirmed record for that country.
</p><p>The short-toed snake eagle is found in open cultivated plains, arid stony deciduous scrub areas and foothills and semi-desert areas.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It requires trees for nesting and open habitats, such as cultivations and grasslands for foraging.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Short-toed_snake_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adults are 62–67&#160;cm (2&#160;ft 0&#160;in–2&#160;ft 2&#160;in) long with a 170–185&#160;cm (5&#160;ft 7&#160;in–6&#160;ft 1&#160;in) wingspan and weigh 1.2–2.3&#160;kg (2.6–5.1&#160;lb).<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> They can be recognised in the field by their predominantly white underside, the upper parts being greyish brown. The chin, throat and upper breast are a pale, earthy brown. The tail has 3 or 4 bars. Additional indications are an <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>-like rounded head, brightly yellow eyes and lightly barred under wing.
</p><p>The short-toed snake eagle spends more time on the wing than do most members of its genus. It favours soaring over hill slopes and hilltops on updraughts, and it does much of its hunting from this position at heights of up to 500&#160;m (1,600&#160;ft). When quartering open country it frequently hovers like a <a href="/wiki/Common_kestrel" title="Common kestrel">kestrel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> When it soars it does so on flattish wings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Short-toed_snake_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Its prey is mostly reptiles, mainly <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a>, but also some <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Sometimes they become entangled with larger snakes and battle on the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-Jerdon1862_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jerdon1862-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Occasionally, they prey on small mammals up to the size of a rabbit, and rarely birds and large insects.
</p><p>This eagle is generally very silent. On occasions, it emits a variety of musical whistling notes. When breeding, it lays only one egg. It can live up to 17 years.
</p><p>The short-toed snake eagle has suffered a steep decline in numbers and range in Europe and is now rare and still decreasing in several countries due to changes in agriculture and land use. It needs protection.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the middle and far eastern part of its range, this species is not yet threatened.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_material">Historical material</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Short-toed_snake_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Historical material">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In his description of the species, <a href="/wiki/Georges-Louis_Leclerc,_Comte_de_Buffon" title="Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon">Buffon</a> says that he kept one of these eagles in captivity and observed its behavior. The captive bird ate mice and frogs, and he states that the <i>Jean-de-blanc</i> was well known by French farmers for raiding poultry.<sup id="cite_ref-Buffon_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Buffon-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
