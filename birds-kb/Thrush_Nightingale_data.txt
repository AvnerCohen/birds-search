<p>The <b>Thrush Nightingale</b> (<i>Luscinia luscinia</i>) (formerly colloquially known as Sprosser), is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> that was formerly classed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae, but is now more generally considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a>, Muscicapidae. It, and similar small <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> species, are often called <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chats</a>.</p>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> insectivorous species breeding in forest in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. The distribution is more northerly than the very closely related <a href="/wiki/Common_Nightingale" title="Common Nightingale">Common Nightingale</a>, <i>Luscinia megarhynchos</i>. It nests low in dense bushes. It winters in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
<p>The Thrush Nightingale is similar in size to the <a href="/wiki/European_Robin" title="European Robin">European Robin</a>. It is plain grey brown above and grey to white below. Its greyer tones, giving a cloudy appearance to the underside, and lack of the Nightingale's obvious red tail side patches are the clearest plumage differences from that species. Sexes are similar.</p>
<p>The male's <span class="unicode"><a href="/wiki/File:Luscinia_luscinia.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" /></a>&#160;<a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/13/Luscinia_luscinia.ogg" class="internal" title="Luscinia luscinia.ogg">song</a><small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Luscinia_luscinia.ogg" title="File:Luscinia luscinia.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> is loud, with range of whistles, trills and clicks. It does not have the Nightingale's loud whistling crescendo.</p>
