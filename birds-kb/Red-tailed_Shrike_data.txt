<p>The <b>red-tailed shrike</b> or <b>Turkestan shrike</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (<i>Lanius phoenicuroides</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family (<a href="/wiki/Laniidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Laniidae">Laniidae</a>). It was formerly considered conspecific with the <a href="/wiki/Isabelline_shrike" title="Isabelline shrike">isabelline shrike</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red-backed_shrike" title="Red-backed shrike">red-backed shrike</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The plumage is a sandy colour. It has a red tail.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red-tailed shrike breeds in south <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> eats large insects, small birds, rodents and lizards. Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a larder. It breeds in open cultivated country, preferably with thorn bushes.
</p>
