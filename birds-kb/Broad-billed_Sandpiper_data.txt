<p>The <b>broad-billed sandpiper</b> (<i>Calidris falcinellus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wading</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>. The scientific name is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. The specific name  <i>falcinella</i> is from <i>falx, falcis</i>, "a  <a href="/wiki/Sickle" title="Sickle">sickle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Some research suggests that it should rather go into the genus <i>Philomachus</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Thomas2004_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thomas2004-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Limicola_falcinellus_Taiwan_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Limicola_falcinellus_Taiwan_cropped.jpg/220px-Limicola_falcinellus_Taiwan_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d9/Limicola_falcinellus_Taiwan_cropped.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="322" data-file-height="220" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Limicola_falcinellus_Taiwan_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Broad billed sandpiper</div></div></div>
<p>Broad-billed sandpipers are small waders, slightly smaller than the dunlin, but with a longer straighter <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a>, and shorter legs. The breeding adult has patterned dark grey upperparts and white underparts with blackish markings on the breast. It has a pale crown stripe and supercilia.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Boreal_ecosystem" title="Boreal ecosystem">boreal</a> winter, they are pale grey above and white below, like a winter dunlin, but retaining the head pattern. Juveniles have backs, similar to young dunlin, but the white flanks and belly and brown-streaked breast are distinctive.
</p><p>Contact call is a dry, whistling “dree-it, dree-it” and a clicking “dik dik”.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Broad-billed_sandpiper&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG/220px-Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG/330px-Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG/440px-Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3176" data-file-height="2392" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Limicola_falcinellus_MWNH_0154.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>The broad-billed sandpiper is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, spending the non-breeding season from easternmost <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, through south and south-east <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>. It is highly gregarious, and will form flocks with other calidrid waders, particularly <a href="/wiki/Dunlin" title="Dunlin">dunlins</a>. Despite its European breeding range, this species is rare on passage in western Europe, presumably because of the south-easterly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migration</a> route.
</p><p>This bird's breeding habitat is wet <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> bogs in Arctic northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. The male performs an aerial display during courtship. They nest in a ground scrape, laying 4 <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>.
</p><p>They forage in soft mud on marshes and the coast, mainly picking up food by sight. They mostly eat <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other small invertebrates.
</p><p>The broad-billed sandpiper is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
