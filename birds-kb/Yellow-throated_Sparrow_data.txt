<p>The <b>yellow-throated sparrow</b> or <b>chestnut-shouldered petronia</b> (<i>Gymnoris xanthocollis</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Old_World_sparrow" title="Old World sparrow">sparrow</a> found in southern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellow-throated_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia,_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia%2C_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg/170px-Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia%2C_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia%2C_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg/255px-Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia%2C_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia%2C_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg/340px-Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia%2C_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1413" data-file-height="1288" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Another_pose_of_Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia,_Nagpur_by_Dr._Tejinder_Singh_Rawal.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Showing the yellow throat</div></div></div>
<p>It has a finer bill than typical sparrows of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Passer" title="Passer">Passer</a></i> and unlike them has no streaks on the plumage. The white double wing bar on the shoulder is diagnostic on the otherwise dull grey-brown sparrow. Males have a chestnut shoulder patch which can sometimes be hard to see. They also have a pale yellow spot on the throat in fresh plumage. Females are duller and lack the chestnut shoulder patch.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The yellow spot is much reduced or lacking in females.
</p><p>This species is tree-loving although sometimes seen on wires and on the ground, where it has a hopping gait. The usual call is a <i>chirrup</i> but the song is distinctive and repetitive <i>chilp chalp cholp</i>. It has a bounding flight and dips deeply before rising up.<sup id="cite_ref-clement_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clement-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellow-throated_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg/220px-Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg/330px-Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg/440px-Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chestnut_Shouldered_Petronia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Chestnut Shouldered Petronia at <a href="/wiki/Gir_Forest_National_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Gir Forest National Park">Gir Forest National Park</a></div></div></div>
<p>This species has sometimes been <a href="/wiki/Lumpers_and_splitters" title="Lumpers and splitters">lumped</a> along with the yellow-spotted petronia (<i><a href="/wiki/Petronia_pyrgita" class="mw-redirect" title="Petronia pyrgita">Petronia pyrgita</a></i>) of Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Currently, two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>G. x. transfuga</i> described by <a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hartert" title="Ernst Hartert">Hartert</a> from the type locality of Bagu Kelat, eastern <a href="/wiki/Baluchistan_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Baluchistan (region)">Baluchistan</a>, a pale desert form found from south-eastern Turkey and south-eastern Iraq to Baluchistan, <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a>, and south-western Afghanistan.</li>
<li><i>G. x. xanthocollis</i> described from the <a href="/wiki/Type_locality_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Type locality (biology)">type locality</a> of Bengal near the Ganges by Major Edward Burton (1790–1867)<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and found in north-eastern Afghanistan, northern Pakistan and India.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellow-throated_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_(Petronia_xanthocollis)_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_%28Petronia_xanthocollis%29_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg/220px-Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_%28Petronia_xanthocollis%29_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="262" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_%28Petronia_xanthocollis%29_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg/330px-Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_%28Petronia_xanthocollis%29_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_%28Petronia_xanthocollis%29_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg/440px-Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_%28Petronia_xanthocollis%29_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg 2x" data-file-width="504" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chestnut-shouldered_Petronia_(Petronia_xanthocollis)_feeding_at_Bharatpur_I_IMG_5258.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Feeding, at Keoladeo National Park</div></div></div>
<p>The species breeds in tree hollows from April to July, often making use of the holes made by primary hole-nesting birds such as <a href="/wiki/Megalaimidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Megalaimidae">barbets</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woodpecker" title="Woodpecker">woodpeckers</a>. They may also make use of hollows on buildings. The nest is built mainly by the female, but males may sometimes assist.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The female alone incubates the eggs, sometimes leaving the nest during the hotter parts of the day.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The eggs hatch after about 12 to 14 days.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They roost communally in low bushes. Some populations are migratory, moving in response to rains.<sup id="cite_ref-clement_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clement-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hbk_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They feed mainly on grains but also on insects, nectar and berries. An unusual food item is the petals of flowers such as those of <i><a href="/wiki/Madhuca_indica" class="mw-redirect" title="Madhuca indica">Madhuca indica</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> When they visit flowers such as those of <i><a href="/wiki/Capparis" title="Capparis">Capparis</a></i>, <i><a href="/w/index.php?title=Salmalia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Salmalia (page does not exist)">Salmalia</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Erythrina" title="Erythrina">Erythrina</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Bassia" title="Bassia">Bassia</a></i>, their foreheads are covered with pollen.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellow-throated_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The chestnut-shouldered petronia is found from Turkey into Iran, Afghanistan, Pakistan, India, Bangladesh and as a vagrant in Sri Lanka<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and possibly parts of Myanmar.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> It is found in forest, gardens and open scrub habitats.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Inspiration">Inspiration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellow-throated_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Inspiration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species was noted by <a href="/wiki/Salim_Ali" title="Salim Ali">Salim Ali</a> (1896–1987) as key to his introduction into <a href="/wiki/Ornithology" title="Ornithology">ornithology</a>. As a young boy he shot a sparrow that looked different, and it was identified for him by <a href="/wiki/Walter_Samuel_Millard" title="Walter Samuel Millard">W. S. Millard</a>, then secretary of the <a href="/wiki/Bombay_Natural_History_Society" title="Bombay Natural History Society">Bombay Natural History Society</a> (BNHS), who also introduced him to the literature and collections at the museum there.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As a result, Salim Ali ultimately took up ornithology as a profession. In 2003 the BNHS published a tribute to him entitled <i>Petronia</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
