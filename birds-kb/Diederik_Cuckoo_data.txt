<p>The <b>diederik cuckoo</b> (<i>Chrysococcyx caprius</i>), formerly <b>dideric cuckoo</b> or  <b>didric cuckoo</b> is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Cuckoo" title="Cuckoo">cuckoo</a> order of birds, the Cuculiformes, which also includes the <a href="/wiki/Geococcyx" class="mw-redirect" title="Geococcyx">roadrunners</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ani_(bird)" title="Ani (bird)">anis</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diederik_cuckoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The diederik cuckoo was described by the French polymath <a href="/wiki/Georges-Louis_Leclerc,_Comte_de_Buffon" title="Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon">Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon</a> in 1780 in his <i>Histoire Naturelle des Oiseaux</i> from a specimen collected in the <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">Cape of Good Hope</a> region of South Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The bird was also illustrated in a hand-coloured plate engraved by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois-Nicolas_Martinet" title="François-Nicolas Martinet">François-Nicolas Martinet</a> in the <i>Planches Enluminées D'Histoire Naturelle</i> which was produced under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Edme-Louis_Daubenton" title="Edme-Louis Daubenton">Edme-Louis Daubenton</a> to accompany Buffon's text.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  Neither the plate caption nor Buffon's description included a scientific name but in 1783 the Dutch naturalist <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Boddaert" title="Pieter Boddaert">Pieter Boddaert</a> coined the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Cuculus caprius</i> in his catalogue of the <i>Planches Enluminées</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The diederik cuckoo is now placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Chrysococcyx" title="Chrysococcyx">Chrysococcyx</a></i> that was erected by the German zoologist <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Boie" title="Friedrich Boie">Friedrich Boie</a> in 1826.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ioc_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The species is <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The generic name <i>Chrysococcyx </i> combines the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>khrusos</i> meaning "gold" and <i>kokkux</i> meaning "cuckoo". The <a href="/wiki/Specific_epithet" class="mw-redirect" title="Specific epithet">specific epithet</a> <i>caprius</i> is from the Latin <i>cupreus</i> meaning "coppery".<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The common name "diederik" is from <a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a> "diedrik", an <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeic" class="mw-redirect" title="Onomatopoeic">onomatopoeic</a> rendition of the bird's call.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diederik_cuckoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Diederik_cuckoo,_Chrysococcyx_caprius_(male),_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve,_Gauteng,_South_Africa_(23525617212).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Diederik_cuckoo%2C_Chrysococcyx_caprius_%28male%29%2C_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve%2C_Gauteng%2C_South_Africa_%2823525617212%29.jpg/220px-Diederik_cuckoo%2C_Chrysococcyx_caprius_%28male%29%2C_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve%2C_Gauteng%2C_South_Africa_%2823525617212%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="278" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Diederik_cuckoo%2C_Chrysococcyx_caprius_%28male%29%2C_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve%2C_Gauteng%2C_South_Africa_%2823525617212%29.jpg/330px-Diederik_cuckoo%2C_Chrysococcyx_caprius_%28male%29%2C_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve%2C_Gauteng%2C_South_Africa_%2823525617212%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Diederik_cuckoo%2C_Chrysococcyx_caprius_%28male%29%2C_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve%2C_Gauteng%2C_South_Africa_%2823525617212%29.jpg/440px-Diederik_cuckoo%2C_Chrysococcyx_caprius_%28male%29%2C_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve%2C_Gauteng%2C_South_Africa_%2823525617212%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2598" data-file-height="3287" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Diederik_cuckoo,_Chrysococcyx_caprius_(male),_at_Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve,_Gauteng,_South_Africa_(23525617212).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>At <a href="/wiki/Rietvlei_Nature_Reserve" title="Rietvlei Nature Reserve">Rietvlei Nature Reserve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gauteng" title="Gauteng">Gauteng</a>, South Africa</div></div></div>
<p>The diederik cuckoo is a smallish cuckoo at 18 to 20&#160;cm. Adult males are glossy green above with copper-sheened areas on the back and whitish underparts. They have a broken white eye-stripe and a short, green <a href="/wiki/Malar_stripe" class="mw-redirect" title="Malar stripe">malar stripe</a>. All <a href="/wiki/Flight_feather" title="Flight feather">remiges</a> have three to four white spots on the inner vanes. The four green outer tail <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a> are tipped white, and the outermost pair are spotted white on both vanes.
</p><p>Females show more copper above, and have coppery barring on the flanks. The underparts are often washed brownish.
</p><p>Juveniles have a red bill, streaky throat and a white wing-bar. They are more copper-coloured above and browner below than the females, and the flank markings are brown blotches.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diederik_cuckoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diederik_cuckoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The diederik cuckoo is a brood parasite.<sup id="cite_ref-Davies_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davies-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It lays a single <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">egg</a> mostly in the nests of <a href="/wiki/Ploceidae" title="Ploceidae">weavers</a>, especially the <a href="/wiki/Village_weaver" title="Village weaver">village weaver</a> and the bishops in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Euplectes" title="Euplectes">Euplectes</a></i>. For example, it has been recorded in <a href="/wiki/Red-collared_widowbird" title="Red-collared widowbird">red-collared widowbirds</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diederik_cuckoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The diederik cuckoo takes a variety of insects and <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar" title="Caterpillar">caterpillars</a>. It is a noisy species, with the persistent and loud <i>deed-deed-deed-deed-er-ick</i> call from which it gets its name. Usually four clear, roughly identical, notes followed by a little twitter.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Diederik_cuckoo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a common resident breeder in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a> and the southern <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>. It has been recorded as far north as Cyprus (1982). It is a short-distance seasonal <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, moving with the rains. It is a solitary bird, found in open woodland, savanna and riverside bushes.
</p>
