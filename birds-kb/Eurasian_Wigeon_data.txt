<p>The <b>Eurasian wigeon</b>, also known as <b>widgeon</b> (<i>Mareca penelope</i>) is one of three species of <a href="/wiki/Wigeon" title="Wigeon">wigeon</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dabbling_duck" class="mw-redirect" title="Dabbling duck">dabbling duck</a> <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i>Mareca</i>. It is common and widespread within its <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> range.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_wigeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Eurasian wigeon was described by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> in his landmark 1758 <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">10th edition of <i>Systema Naturae</i></a> under the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">binomial name</a> <i>Anas penelope</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Linnaeus_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Linnaeus-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <i>Anas</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "duck", and <i>penelope</i> refers to a duck that was supposed to have rescued <a href="/wiki/Penelope" title="Penelope">Penelope</a> when she was thrown into the sea. Her name derives from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> πήνη <i>pene</i>, "braid" and ὤψ <i>ops</i> "appearance", from the ruse she used to deter suitors while her husband <a href="/wiki/Odysseus" title="Odysseus">Ulysses</a> was absent.<sup id="cite_ref-job90_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job90-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_wigeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Dabbling_duck" class="mw-redirect" title="Dabbling duck">dabbling duck</a> is 42–52&#160;cm (17–20&#160;in) long with a 71–80&#160;cm (28–31&#160;in) wingspan, and a weight of 500–1,073&#160;g (1.102–2.366&#160;lb).<sup id="cite_ref-Floyd_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Floyd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CRC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The breeding male has grey flanks and back, with a black rear end, a dark green <a href="/wiki/Speculum_feathers" title="Speculum feathers">speculum</a> and a brilliant white patch on upper wings, obvious in flight or at rest. It has a pink breast, white belly, and a chestnut head with a creamy <a href="/wiki/Crown_(anatomy)" title="Crown (anatomy)">crown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Dunn_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dunn-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In non-breeding (eclipse) plumage, the drake looks more like the female. The female is light brown, with <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> much like a female <a href="/wiki/American_wigeon" title="American wigeon">American wigeon</a>. It can be distinguished from most other ducks, apart from American wigeon, on shape. However, that species has a paler head and white axillaries on its underwing. The female can be a rufous <a href="/wiki/Polymorphism_(biology)" title="Polymorphism (biology)">morph</a> with a redder head, and a gray morph with a more gray head.<sup id="cite_ref-Dunn_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dunn-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_wigeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG/220px-Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="206" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG/330px-Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG/440px-Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2299" data-file-height="2156" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anas_penelope_MWNH_0970.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>It breeds in the northernmost areas of Europe and the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Clements_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clements-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It is the Old World counterpart of North America's <a href="/wiki/American_wigeon" title="American wigeon">American wigeon</a>. It is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and winters further south than its breeding range. It migrates to southern Asia and Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-Clements_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clements-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, the Eurasian wigeon is common as a winter visitor, but scarce as a breeding bird in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lake_District" title="Lake District">Lake District</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Pennines" title="Pennines">Pennines</a> and occasionally further south, with only a handful of breeding pairs in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. It can be found as an uncommon winter visitor in the United States on the <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" class="mw-redirect" title="Mid-Atlantic States">mid-Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific">Pacific</a> coasts. It is a rare visitor to the rest of the United States except for the <a href="/wiki/Four_Corners" title="Four Corners">Four Corners</a> and the southern <a href="/wiki/Appalachians" class="mw-redirect" title="Appalachians">Appalachians</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Floyd_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Floyd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dunn_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dunn-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_habitat">Behaviour and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_wigeon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg/220px-Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg/330px-Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg/440px-Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3200" data-file-height="2200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mareca_penelope_in_flight_s4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Two males and a female in flight</div></div></div>
<p>The Eurasian wigeon is a bird of open wetlands, such as wet grassland or marshes with some taller vegetation, and usually feeds by dabbling for plant food or grazing, which it does very readily. It nests on the ground, near water and under cover. It is highly gregarious outside of the breeding season and will form large flocks. They will join with flocks of the <a href="/wiki/American_wigeon" title="American wigeon">American wigeon</a> in the United States, and they also hybridize with them.<sup id="cite_ref-Floyd_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Floyd-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This is a noisy species. The male has a clear whistle that sounds like: "pjiew pjiew", whereas the female has a low growl: "rawr".
</p><p>The Eurasian wigeon is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies. Its conservation status is <a href="/wiki/Least_concern" class="mw-redirect" title="Least concern">least concern</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
