<p>The <b>Long-billed Dowitcher</b> (<i>Limnodromus scolopaceus</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Shorebird" title="Shorebird" class="mw-redirect">shorebird</a>.</p>
<p>Adults have yellowish legs and a long straight dark bill. The body is dark brown on top and reddish underneath with spotted throat and breast, bars on flanks. The tail has a black and white barred pattern. The winter plumage is largely grey.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is wet tundra in the far north of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and eastern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. They nest on the ground, usually near water.</p>
<p>They <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> to the southern <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and as far south as <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">Central America</a>. Long-billed Dowitcher is a rare but regular visitor to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, with some individuals staying for long periods.</p>
<p>These birds forage by probing in shallow water or on wet mud. They mainly eat insects, mollusks, crustaceans and marine worms, but also eat some plant material.</p>
<p>They are more likely to be seen near fresh water than the <a href="/wiki/Short-billed_Dowitcher" title="Short-billed Dowitcher">Short-billed Dowitcher</a>.</p>
