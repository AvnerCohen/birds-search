<p>The <b>Crab-plover</b> or <b>Crab Plover</b> (<i>Dromas ardeola)</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> related to the <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a>, but sufficiently distinctive to merit its <a href="/wiki/Monotypic_taxon" title="Monotypic taxon">own family</a> <b>Dromadidae</b>. Its relationship within the <a href="/wiki/Charadriiformes" title="Charadriiformes">Charadriiformes</a> is unclear, some have considered it to be closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Thick-knee" title="Thick-knee" class="mw-redirect">thick-knees</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Pratincole" title="Pratincole">pratincoles</a>, while others have considered it closer to the <a href="/wiki/Auk" title="Auk">auks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gulls</a>. It is the only member of the genus <i>Dromas</i> and is unique among <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a> in making use of ground warmth to aid incubation of the <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Crab-plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DromasLeMessurier.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/DromasLeMessurier.jpg/220px-DromasLeMessurier.jpg" width="220" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/DromasLeMessurier.jpg/330px-DromasLeMessurier.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/DromasLeMessurier.jpg/440px-DromasLeMessurier.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DromasLeMessurier.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
The feet are partially webbed</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This bird resembles a <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a>, but has very long grey legs and a strong heavy black bill similar to a <a href="/wiki/Tern" title="Tern">tern</a>. Its black-and-white plumage and long-necked upright posture with heavy bill makes it distinctive and unmistakable. Its bill is unique among waders, and specialised for eating crabs. It has partially webbed toes. The plumage is white except for black on its back and in the primary feathers of the wings. They are noisy birds, calling frequently on their breeding sites and in their wintering grounds. The usual call is a <i>ka</i> similar to that of the Bar-tailed Godwit but repeated rapidly. Flocks may produce a whinnying sound that rises and at in the breeding season produce whistling <i>kew-ki-ki</i> notes.<sup id="cite_ref-hayman_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hayman-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Males and females are not easily distinguished but males have a heavier and longer bill. Juveniles have the black on the mantle, greyish and remain in this plumage for a year. Flocks fly in lines or "V" formations.<sup id="cite_ref-hayman_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hayman-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Crab Plover is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Crab-plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span></h2>
<p>It is resident on the coasts and islands of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, where it feeds on <a href="/wiki/Crab" title="Crab">crabs</a> and other small animals. They are gregarious and will feed in large groups, at night and during dawn and dusk as well as during the day; this <a href="/wiki/Crepuscular" title="Crepuscular">crepuscular</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nocturnal" title="Nocturnal" class="mw-redirect">nocturnal</a> behaviour is more common during the breeding season. They breed around the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, Gulf of Oman and the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> in the months of April to July then disperse across the Indian Ocean in August as far as the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Islands" title="Andaman Islands">Andaman Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> in the east and <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-hayman_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hayman-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Crab-plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span></h2>
<p>The Crab Plover is unusual for waders in that it nests in <a href="/wiki/Burrow" title="Burrow">burrows</a> in sandy banks. In the Red Sea region, the breeding season begins around the middle of May.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonial</a> breeder, nesting in colonies as large 1500 pairs. It lays one white <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">egg</a>, occasionally two, which are large for its body size. The nest burrow temperature is optimal due to solar radiation and the parents are able to leave the nest unattended for as long as 58 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The chicks are also unique for the usually <a href="/wiki/Nidifugous" title="Nidifugous">nidifugous</a> waders in being <a href="/wiki/Altricial" title="Altricial">unable to walk</a> and remain in the nest for several days after hatching, having food brought to them. Even once they <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a> they have a long period of parental care afterwards. Both males and females take care of the young.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
