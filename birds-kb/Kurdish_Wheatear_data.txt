<p>The <b>Kurdish wheatear</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (<i>Oenanthe xanthoprymna</i>), also known as the <b>Kurdistan wheatear</b>, the <b>chestnut-rumped wheatear</b> or the <b>red-rumped wheatear</b>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Muscicapidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Muscicapidae">Muscicapidae</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Red-tailed_wheatear" title="Red-tailed wheatear">red-tailed wheatear</a> (<i>O. chrysopygia</i>) was formerly considered a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of this bird but is now often regarded as a separate species. The two may <a href="/wiki/Intergradation" title="Intergradation">intergrade</a> in Iran ("<i>O. x. cummingi</i>")  but it is also possible that the rather differently coloured <i>cummingi</i> is in fact a one-year-old <i>O. xanthoprymna</i> with intermediate plumage.<sup id="cite_ref-Chestnut_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chestnut-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurdish_wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Kurdish wheatear is about 14 centimetres (5.5&#160;in) in length. The top of the head and the nape of the male are pale grey, separated by a white eye stripe from the black face and throat. The back is brownish grey and the underparts white or buff. The wings are charcoal with no white streak, the flight feathers being edged with brown. The flanks and under-tail coverts are orange and the tail white at the base with a dark terminal band. The female has similar markings but the colours are more muted and the dark face and throat replaced by a grey eye patch. The orange rump and tail coverts are particularly noticeable in the winter when the male is in flight.<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Call">Call</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurdish_wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Call">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The call note is a harsh <i>chack-chack-chack</i>. The song is similar to that of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_wheatear" title="Northern wheatear">northern wheatear</a> (<i>Oenanthe oenanthe</i>) and is a rather squeaky warble.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurdish_wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Kurdish wheatear mainly eats ants and other small insects.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurdish_wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nest is built among rocks, in a crevice, in scree or under a boulder. It is basin-shaped and lined with wool, hair and dead grasses. Four or five eggs are laid. They are bluish-white with a few chestnut brown speckles.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kurdish_wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The breeding range of the Kurdish wheatear covers the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, south-east <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, western <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, parts of the former USSR and south east Asia. It moves southwards in winter to southern Iran, the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>, Egypt, Sudan, Eritrea and north-east <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a><sup id="cite_ref-Chestnut_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chestnut-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and occasionally to north west <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is unclear whether all populations migrate or whether some birds just move down to lower altitudes for the winter. Its preferred habitat is heathland, open hillsides and scrubby, semi-desert regions.<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It is listed as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least_concern" class="mw-redirect" title="Least concern">least concern</a>" in the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List_of_Threatened_Species" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN Red List of Threatened Species">IUCN Red List of Threatened Species</a> because of its very large range.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
