<p>The <b>Ruddy Shelduck</b>, <i>Tadorna ferruginea</i>, is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">duck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Goose" title="Goose">goose</a> and <a href="/wiki/Swan" title="Swan">swan</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Anatidae" title="Anatidae">Anatidae</a>. It is in the <a href="/wiki/Shelduck" title="Shelduck">shelduck</a> <a href="/wiki/Subfamily" title="Subfamily" class="mw-redirect">subfamily</a> <a href="/wiki/Tadorninae" title="Tadorninae">Tadorninae</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruddy_Shelduck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p>There are very small resident populations of this species in north west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, but the main breeding area of this species is from south east Europe across central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>. These <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> are mostly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect">Indian Subcontinent</a>.</p>
<p>Although becoming quite rare in southeast <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, the Ruddy Shelduck is still common across much of its Asian range. It may be this population which gives rise to vagrants as far west as <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>. However, since the European population is declining, it is likely that most occurrences in western Europe in recent decades are escapes or feral birds. Although this bird is observed in the wild from time to time in eastern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, no evidence of a genuine <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a> has been found.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruddy_Shelduck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Descriptions">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Descriptions">Descriptions</span></h2>
<p>This is a bird of open country, and it will breed on cliffs,<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> in burrows, tree holes or crevices distant from water, laying 6-16 creamy-white eggs, incubated for 30 days. The Ruddy Shelduck is usually found in pairs or small groups and rarely forms large flocks. However, moulting and wintering gatherings on chosen lakes or slow rivers can be very large.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg/220px-Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg/330px-Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg/440px-Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ruddy_shelduck_arp.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>

<p>The Ruddy Shelduck is a distinctive species, 58–70&#160;cm long with a 110–135&#160;cm wingspan. It has orange-brown body plumage and a paler head. The wings are white with black flight feathers. It swims well, and in flight looks heavy, more like a goose than a duck. The sexes of this striking species are similar, but the male has a black ring at the bottom of the neck in the <a href="/wiki/Breeding_season" title="Breeding season">breeding season</a> summer, and the female often has a white face patch. The call is a loud wild honking. Ruddy Shelduck (Tadorna ferruginea), also called Brahminy Duck, is a common winter visitor in India. This bird is found in large wetlands, rivers with mud flats and shingle banks. Found in large congregation on lakes and reservoirs. It breeds in high altitude lakes and swamps in Jammu &amp; Kashmir. Arrives in north India by October and departs by April. The genus name <i>Tadorna</i> comes from Celtic roots and means "pied waterfowl", essentially the same as the <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> "shelduck".<sup id="cite_ref-Kear_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kear-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p><br /></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruddy_Shelduck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span></h2>
<p>The Ruddy Shelduck is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
