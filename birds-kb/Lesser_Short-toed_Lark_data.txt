<p>The <b>lesser short-toed lark</b> (<i>Alaudala rufescens</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> found in the southern <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> ncluding North Africa.    It is a common bird with a very wide range and the <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has rated its conservation status as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least-concern_species" title="Least-concern species">least concern</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Confusingly, <a href="/wiki/Hume%27s_short-toed_lark" title="Hume&#39;s short-toed lark">Hume's short-toed lark</a> is also sometimes called the lesser short-toed lark.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Lesser short-toed lark was originally described as belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>. The genus name <i>Alaudala</i> is a diminutive of <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>, and the specific <i>rufescens</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "reddish", from <i>rufus</i> "red".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alternate names for the lesser short-toed lark include the common short-toed lark, grey lark, rufous short-toed lark and short-toed lark. Formerly or presently, some authorities considered the <a href="/wiki/Athi_short-toed_lark" title="Athi short-toed lark">Athi short-toed lark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asian_short-toed_lark" title="Asian short-toed lark">Asian short-toed lark</a> and/or the <a href="/wiki/Somali_short-toed_lark" title="Somali short-toed lark">Somali short-toed lark</a> to be subspecies of the lesser short-toed lark.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Molecular_phylogenetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Molecular phylogenetic">molecular phylogenetic</a> study published in 2020 compared the nuclear and mitochondrial DNA from the sand, Asian short-toed, and lesser short-toed larks. The study analysed samples from 130 individuals that represented 16 of the 18 recognised subspecies. The resulting <a href="/wiki/Phylogenetic_tree" title="Phylogenetic tree">phylogenetic tree</a> indicated that neither the Asian short-toed lark, nor the lesser short-toed lark as currently defined are <a href="/wiki/Monophyletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monophyletic">monophyletic</a>. Most of the subspecies were also found to be non-monophyletic. The authors refrained from proposing a revised taxonomy until additional studies had been completed comparing the vocalizations, sexual behaviour and ecology.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nine <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>A. r. rufescens</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Louis_Jean_Pierre_Vieillot" title="Louis Jean Pierre Vieillot">Vieillot</a>, 1819)</small>: Found on <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a> (west-central <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>)</li>
<li><i>A. r. polatzeki</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hartert" title="Ernst Hartert">Hartert</a>, 1904)</small>: Found in eastern Canary Islands</li>
<li><i>A. r. apetzii</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Alfred_Brehm" title="Alfred Brehm">Brehm, AE</a>, 1857)</small>: Found in eastern and southern <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberian Peninsula</a></li>
<li><i>A. r. minor</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Jean_Cabanis" title="Jean Cabanis">Cabanis</a>, 1851)</small>: Found from Morocco to north-western Egypt and southern Turkey to the Sinai Peninsula and eastern Iraq</li>
<li><i>A. r. nicolli</i> - <small>(Hartert, 1909)</small>: Found in the <a href="/wiki/Nile_Delta" title="Nile Delta">Nile Delta</a> (northern Egypt)</li>
<li><i>A. r. pseudobaetica</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Boris_K._Stegmann" title="Boris K. Stegmann">Stegmann</a>, 1932)</small>: Found in eastern Turkey, <a href="/wiki/Transcaucasia" title="Transcaucasia">Transcaucasia</a> and northern Iran</li>
<li><i>A. r. heinei</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Eugen_Ferdinand_von_Homeyer" title="Eugen Ferdinand von Homeyer">Homeyer</a>, 1873)</small>: Found from Ukraine to eastern Kazakhstan</li>
<li><i>A. r. aharonii</i> - <small>(Hartert, 1910)</small>: Found in central Turkey</li>
<li><i>A. r. persica</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Richard_Bowdler_Sharpe" title="Richard Bowdler Sharpe">Sharpe</a>, 1890</small>: Found in eastern and southern Iraq to southern and south-western Afghanistan</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lesser short-toed lark is similar in size and appearance to the <a href="/wiki/Greater_short-toed_lark" title="Greater short-toed lark">greater short-toed lark</a> but is generally a duller-looking bird with a more streaked breast. It grows to a length of from 13 to 14&#160;cm (5.1 to 5.5&#160;in) and the sexes are similar. As with the greater short-toed lark, the colour varies across the broad range and is not a good distinguishing feature. It is dark-streaked greyish-brown above, and white below. It has a pale <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, and a short stubby bill.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Care must be taken to distinguish this species from the <i>Calandrella</i> larks. This species lacks the dark neck patches of the greater, and has fine streaking across the breast. The bill and head shape also differ, this species having a shorter, less-conical bill and a more-rounded, smaller head. The song is richer, more varied and imitative than that of its relative.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lesser short-toed lark breeds in Spain, north Africa, also including Turkey eastwards across the semi-deserts of central Asia to Mongolia and China. Many populations, including the Spanish and African breeders, are sedentary (<a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">non-migratory</a>), but some Asian birds from the north of the breeding range migrate south in winter. This species is a very rare wanderer to northern and western Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a bird of dry open country, preferring even drier and barer soils than the <a href="/wiki/Greater_short-toed_lark" title="Greater short-toed lark">greater short-toed lark</a>. It nests on the ground, laying two or three <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>. Its food is <a href="/wiki/Seed_predation" title="Seed predation">seeds</a> and insects, the latter especially in the breeding season.<sup id="cite_ref-HBWA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBWA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
