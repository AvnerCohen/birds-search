<p>The <b>sand partridge</b> (<i>Ammoperdix heyi</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Gamebird" class="mw-redirect" title="Gamebird">gamebird</a> in the pheasant family <a href="/wiki/Phasianidae" title="Phasianidae">Phasianidae</a> of the order <a href="/wiki/Galliformes" title="Galliformes">Galliformes</a>, gallinaceous birds.
</p><p>This <a href="/wiki/Partridge" title="Partridge">partridge</a> has its main native range from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> east to south <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>. It is closely related and similar to its counterpart in southeast <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> and east to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, the <a href="/wiki/See-see_partridge" title="See-see partridge">see-see partridge</a>, <i>Ammoperdix griseogularis</i>.
</p><p>This 22–25&#160;cm <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a resident breeder in dry, open and often hilly country. It nests in a scantily lined ground scrape laying 5-7 eggs. The sand partridge takes a wide variety of seeds and some insect food.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sand_partridge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The sand partridge is a rotund bird, mainly sandy-brown with wavy white and brown flank stripes.  The male has a grey head with a white stripe in front of the eye and a white cheek patch. The neck sides are plain, and not speckled with white. The head pattern is the best distinction from see-see partridge.
</p><p>The female is a very washed-out version of the male, and is more difficult to distinguish from its relative due to the weak head pattern.
</p><p>When disturbed, sand partridge prefers to run rather than fly, but if necessary it flies a short distance on rounded wings. The song is a slurred <i>kwa-kwa-kwa</i>.
</p>
