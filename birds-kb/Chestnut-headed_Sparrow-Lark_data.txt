<p>The <b>chestnut-headed sparrow-lark</b> (<i>Eremopterix signatus</i>) or <b>chestnut-headed finch-lark</b> is a species of <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird in the family <a href="/wiki/Alaudidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Alaudidae">Alaudidae</a>. It is found in eastern and north-eastern Africa. Its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> are subtropical or tropical dry <a href="/wiki/Shrubland" title="Shrubland">shrubland</a>, subtropical or tropical dry lowland <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a>, and hot <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chestnut-headed_sparrow-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chestnut-headed_sparrow-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>E. s. harrisoni</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/William_Robert_Ogilvie-Grant" title="William Robert Ogilvie-Grant">Ogilvie-Grant</a>, 1900)</small>: Found in south-eastern Sudan and north-western Kenya</li>
<li><i>E. s. signatus</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Emile_Oustalet" class="mw-redirect" title="Emile Oustalet">Oustalet</a>, 1886)</small>: Found in southern and eastern Ethiopia, Somalia and eastern Kenya</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chestnut-headed_sparrow-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The male chestnut-headed sparrow-lark has a black collar and bib, white cheeks and a white circular area on the nape of the crown, surrounded by a chestnut border. This distinguishes it from <a href="/wiki/Fischer%27s_sparrow-lark" title="Fischer&#39;s sparrow-lark">Fischer's sparrow-lark</a> which lacks the white spot. The female has duller plumage.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Chestnut-headed_sparrow-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird is usually found in pairs or small flocks of up to forty birds, often around water holes. It flies low to the ground and may sing in flight or when standing on bare ground.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
