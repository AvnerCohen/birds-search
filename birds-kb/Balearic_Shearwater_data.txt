<p>The <b>Balearic shearwater</b> (<i>Puffinus mauretanicus</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Shearwater" title="Shearwater">shearwater</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> family <i>Procellariidae</i>. <i>Puffinus</i> is a <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> <a href="/wiki/Loanword" title="Loanword">loanword</a> based on the English "puffin" and its variants, that referred to the cured carcass of the fat nestling of the <a href="/wiki/Manx_shearwater" title="Manx shearwater">Manx shearwater</a>, a former delicacy.<sup id="cite_ref-OEDpuffin_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OEDpuffin-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The specific <i>mauretanicus</i> refers to <a href="/wiki/Mauretania" title="Mauretania">Mauretania</a>, an old name for an area of North Africa roughly corresponding to Morocco and Algeria.<sup id="cite_ref-Jobling_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jobling-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Balearic shearwater was long regarded a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Manx_shearwater" title="Manx shearwater">Manx shearwater</a>. Following an initial split, it was held to be a subspecies of the "Mediterranean shearwater" for nearly ten more years,<sup id="cite_ref-Sibley_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sibley-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> until it was resolved to be a distinct <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, separate from the <a href="/wiki/Yelkouan_shearwater" title="Yelkouan shearwater">yelkouan shearwater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Wink_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wink-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Heidrich_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidrich-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sangster_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sangster-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It is the last <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> of the <i>Puffinus</i> complex that was recognized as a separate entity.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>It appears to belong to a group of <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_basin" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean basin">Mediterranean</a> and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a> species which includes the <a href="/wiki/Yelkouan_shearwater" title="Yelkouan shearwater">yelkouan shearwater</a><sup id="cite_ref-Austin_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Austin-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and one to three <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistorically</a> <a href="/wiki/Extinct" class="mw-redirect" title="Extinct">extinct</a> taxa, <a href="/wiki/Hole%27s_shearwater" class="mw-redirect" title="Hole&#39;s shearwater">Hole's</a> and possibly also <a href="/wiki/Lava_shearwater" title="Lava shearwater">Olson's shearwater</a> and an undescribed form of unclear distinctness from <a href="/wiki/Menorca" title="Menorca">Menorca</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alcover_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alcover-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hole's shearwater may be the closest known relative of <i>P. mauretanicus</i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The two living Mediterranean lineages had probably separated before the end of the <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> (c. 2 <a href="/wiki/Mya_(unit)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mya (unit)">mya</a>), as indicated by molecular differences and the <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibizan</a> fossil <i><a href="/wiki/Puffinus_nestori" title="Puffinus nestori">Puffinus nestori</a></i> from the Late Pliocene or Early <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a>, which may have been the direct ancestor of the present species.<sup id="cite_ref-Heidrich_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidrich-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird is approximately 33&#160;cm (13&#160;in) long, with an 85–90&#160;cm (33–35&#160;in) wingspan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> It has the typically "shearing" flight of the genus, dipping from side to side on stiff wings with few wingbeats, the wingtips almost touching the water. This bird looks like a flying cross, with its wing held at right angles to the body, and it changes from dark brown to dirty white as the dark upperparts and paler undersides are alternately exposed as it travels low over the sea.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Apart from its less contrasting <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a>, this species is very similar to the Manx and yelkouan shearwaters found elsewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>. At least one mixed breeding colony of Balearic and yelkouan shearwaters exists on <a href="/wiki/Menorca" title="Menorca">Menorca</a>, and the species' winter ranges overlap in the Central Mediterranean; for scientific purposes at least, a combination of <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological</a> characteristics and <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" class="mw-redirect" title="DNA sequence">DNA sequence</a> data is suggested to identify the species.<sup id="cite_ref-Genovart_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Genovart-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_range">Habitat and range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat and range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species breeds on islands and coastal cliffs in the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Balearic islands">Balearic islands</a>. Most winter in that sea, but some <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">enter</a> the Atlantic in late summer, reaching north to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a gregarious species, which can be seen in large numbers from <a href="/wiki/Boat" title="Boat">boats</a> or headlands, especially in autumn. It is silent at sea, but at night the breeding colonies are alive with raucous cackling calls, higher pitched than the Manx shearwater's.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This species nests in <a href="/wiki/Burrow_nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Burrow nest">burrows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cave" title="Cave">caves</a> <sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which are visited only at night to avoid predation by large <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gulls</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Balearic shearwater feeds on <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" class="mw-redirect" title="Mollusc">molluscs</a>. It does not follow boats.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_and_threats">Conservation and threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Balearic_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation and threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Balearic shearwater is considered <a href="/wiki/Critically_endangered" title="Critically endangered">critically endangered</a> with extinction by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Recent models estimate a mean decrease of 7.4% per year and a mean extinction time of 40.4 years. This equates to an ongoing decline of more than 80% over the next three generations (54 years).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> It is under severe threat from the development of holiday resorts near its breeding sites. These can destroy or alter its natural breeding habitat by, for example, producing light pollution around nesting colonies.<sup id="cite_ref-Rodríguez_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rodríguez-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Predation from introduced animals such as <a href="/wiki/Feral_cat" title="Feral cat">cats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rats</a> also cause problems.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The discovery of yelkouan shearwaters in the Menorcan colony suggests that <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridization</a> may also pose a problem.<sup id="cite_ref-Genovart_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Genovart-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
