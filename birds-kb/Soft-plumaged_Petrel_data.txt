<p>The <b>soft-plumaged petrel</b> (<i>Pterodroma mollis</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Procellariidae" title="Procellariidae">Procellariidae</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soft-plumaged_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds on islands in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">Southern Hemisphere</a>, nesting on <a href="/wiki/Tristan_da_Cunha" title="Tristan da Cunha">Tristan da Cunha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gough_Island" title="Gough Island">Gough Island</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward_Islands" title="Prince Edward Islands">Prince Edward Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crozet_Islands" title="Crozet Islands">Crozet Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macquarie_Island" title="Macquarie Island">Macquarie Island</a>, and on the <a href="/wiki/Antipodes_Islands" title="Antipodes Islands">Antipodes Islands</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.  Small numbers breed in the <a href="/wiki/Maatsuyker_Islands" title="Maatsuyker Islands">Maatsuyker Island Group</a> of southern <a href="/wiki/Tasmania" title="Tasmania">Tasmania</a>.  It disperses outside the breeding season, reaching eastern <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> north to <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. It has occurred as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Vagrancy in birds">vagrant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soft-plumaged_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fea%27s_petrel" title="Fea&#39;s petrel">Fea's petrel</a> (<i>P. feae</i>), <a href="/wiki/Desertas_petrel" title="Desertas petrel">Desertas petrel</a> (<i>P. desertae</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Zino%27s_petrel" title="Zino&#39;s petrel">Zino's petrel</a> (<i>P. madeira</i>) of the North Atlantic were formerly treated as <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of this bird.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Soft-plumaged_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a medium-sized petrel. Plumage is dark grey and white. Wings are narrow and with a pointed tail distinct in flight. It has a dark grey head with prominent white feathers on the cheeks and throat. There is a white speckling on forehead along with a prominent dark eye patch. The underside is mostly white, which turned dark grey at underwings. The black bill is about 25–32&#160;mm long, has a sharp hook. Legs are pink with black on the outer webs and toes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both sexes are noisy, making medium-pitched moans and shrill squeaks.
</p>
