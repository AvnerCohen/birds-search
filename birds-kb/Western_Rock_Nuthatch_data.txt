<p>The <b>western rock nuthatch</b> (<i>Sitta neumayer</i>) (sometimes known simply as <b>rock nuthatch</b>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> which breeds from <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> east through <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> to <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Nuthatch" title="Nuthatch">nuthatch</a> is largely resident apart from some post-breeding dispersal. The <a href="/wiki/Eastern_rock_nuthatch" title="Eastern rock nuthatch">eastern rock nuthatch</a> <i>Sitta tephronota</i> is a separate species, which occurs further east in south-central Asia.
</p><p>The western rock nuthatch is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> associated with habitats with bare rocks, especially in mountainous areas. Those at the highest altitudes may move lower down in winter.
</p><p>It feeds on <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spider" title="Spider">spiders</a> in summer, supplemented with seeds and <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">snails</a> in winter. It feeds on the ground, and will wedge larger items in rock crevices while it hammers them open with its strong bill. It will also flycatch.
</p><p>The western rock nuthatch is 13.5&#160;cm long, slightly smaller than <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_nuthatch" title="Eurasian nuthatch">Eurasian nuthatch</a>, and has the typical nuthatch big head, short tail and powerful bill and feet. It is long-legged and long-billed compared to most of its relatives.
</p><p>The race <i>S. n. neumayer</i> of southeast <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> is dark grey above, and has a long strong black eyestripe. It has a white throat and underparts shading to buff on the belly. Sexes are similar, and young birds are slightly duller versions of the adults. Two other races in the west of its <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> range are similar but less well marked.
</p><p><i>S.n. tschitscherini</i> of western Iran is paler grey above and has a much weaker eyestripe. <i>S.n. plumbea</i> of southern Iran resembles <i>tschitscherini</i>, but has grey underparts.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG/220px-Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="92" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG/330px-Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG/440px-Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4096" data-file-height="1716" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sitta_neumayer_MWNH_1444.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This territorial species builds a flask-shaped nest from mud, dung and hair or feathers in a rock crevice, cave, or under an overhang on a rock face. Decorative items may be pushed into crevices and cracks near the entrance to the nest. The nest is lined with softer materials and the entrance is sealed with mud. 4-10 eggs are laid, and are white speckled with yellow.
</p><p>The western rock nuthatch has a <i>tsik</i> call and a trilled <i>tui tui tui </i> song. It is common in suitable habitat in most of its range.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny the Elder</a> believed that it was these birds that inspired man to build homes of earth in imitation of the western rock nuthatch's nests.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
