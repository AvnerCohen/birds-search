<p>The <b>Rostratulidae</b> (commonly known as <b>Painted-snipes</b>), form a taxonomic <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> of <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, composed of two <a href="/wiki/Genus_(biology)" title="Genus (biology)" class="mw-redirect">genera</a>: <i><a href="/wiki/Rostratula" title="Rostratula">Rostratula</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Nycticryphes" title="Nycticryphes" class="mw-redirect">Nycticryphes</a></i>.</p>
<p>At present two species, the South American and Greater Painted-snipes, are not considered <a href="/wiki/Threatened" title="Threatened" class="mw-redirect">threatened</a> by human activities; however, The Australian Painted-snipe has declined and is considered endangered in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lane_0-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lane-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Painted-snipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Phylogeny and evolution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Phylogeny_and_evolution">Phylogeny and evolution</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Painted_snipe_family.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/Painted_snipe_family.jpg/220px-Painted_snipe_family.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Painted_snipe_family.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<i>R. benghalensis</i> with chicks</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The family <a href="/wiki/Rostratulidae" title="Rostratulidae" class="mw-redirect">Rostratulidae</a> encompasses two genera and four species (one extinct). Painted-snipes superficially resemble true <a href="/wiki/Snipe" title="Snipe">Snipes</a> but the two taxa are not closely related. Instead the similarity can be attributed to <a href="/wiki/Convergent_evolution" title="Convergent evolution">convergent evolution</a> where both groups have been subjected to similar selection pressures, thus promoting the evolution of analogous features such as a long slender bill and legs, mottled <a href="/wiki/Crypsis" title="Crypsis">crypsis</a> plumage and particular body proportions. While less similar in morphology, the species that are considered most closely related to painted-snipes are other members of the suborder <i><a href="/wiki/Thinocori" title="Thinocori" class="mw-redirect">Thinocori</a></i>; <a href="/wiki/Jacana" title="Jacana">Jacanas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seedsnipe" title="Seedsnipe">Seedsnipes</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Plains_Wanderer" title="Plains Wanderer" class="mw-redirect">Plains Wanderer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The painted-snipe, †<i>Rostratula minator</i> was described in 1988 from deposits of the early <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> found in <a href="/wiki/Langebaanweg" title="Langebaanweg">Langebaanweg</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. This is the first valid fossil belonging to the family <i>Rostratulidae</i>. Comparisons of bone measurements with <i>R. minator</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Extant_taxon" title="Extant taxon">extant</a> species show that it was relatively intermediate in size, although this considerable difference indicates that it may only be an <a href="/wiki/Endemic" title="Endemic" class="mw-redirect">endemic</a> African species that has become extinct, rather than the direct ancestor of <i><a href="/wiki/Rostratula_benghalensis" title="Rostratula benghalensis" class="mw-redirect">R. benghalensis</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Rminator_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rminator-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Australian painted-snipe was described as <i>Rostratula australis</i> by <a href="/wiki/John_Gould" title="John Gould">John Gould</a> in 1838, although later lumped with the similar <a href="/wiki/Greater_Painted-snipe" title="Greater Painted-snipe">Greater Painted-snipe</a>, <i>R. benghalensis</i> as subspecies <i>R. b. australis</i>. However, morphological and genetic differences have resulted in the species being split in recent years.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Such a similarity between the two species can be explained by a recent evolutionary divergence, and is an example of <a href="/wiki/Allopatric_speciation" title="Allopatric speciation">allopatric speciation</a> where the prevention of <a href="/wiki/Gene_flow" title="Gene flow">gene flow</a> by geographical isolation has resulted in an accumulation of differences by <a href="/wiki/Genetic_drift" title="Genetic drift">genetic drift</a> and differing selection pressures.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Painted-snipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The painted-snipes are short-legged, long-billed <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> similar in shape to the true snipes, but their <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> is much more striking. There is <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexual dimorphism</a> in both size and plumage, with the males being duller overall and smaller. All three species have large forward pointing eyes.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Painted-snipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostratula_benghalensis_MHNT.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Rostratula_benghalensis_MHNT.jpg/220px-Rostratula_benghalensis_MHNT.jpg" width="220" height="213" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rostratula_benghalensis_MHNT.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A museum egg specimen of <i>R. benghalensis</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>All three species of painted-snipe generally inhabit reedy <a href="/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamps</a> and marshes, usually in lowlands. Outside of the breeding season painted-snipes are generally solitary in habits. Painted snipes are <a href="/wiki/Crepuscular" title="Crepuscular">crepuscular</a> or even slightly <a href="/wiki/Nocturnal_animal" title="Nocturnal animal" class="mw-redirect">nocturnal</a> in their habits.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Painted-snipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span></h3>
<p>Painted-snipes are <a href="/wiki/Omnivorous" title="Omnivorous" class="mw-redirect">omnivorous</a>, feeding on <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a> and seeds. Animal prey taken includes <a href="/wiki/Annelid" title="Annelid">annelid</a> worms, snails, aquatic and marsh <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, and crustaceans. The seeds of grasses such as millet and rice are also consumed, and may form a major part of the diet of some populations.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Painted-snipe&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>The breeding biology of the painted snipes varies according to genus; the <i>Rostratula</i> painted snipes are generally <a href="/wiki/Polyandrous" title="Polyandrous" class="mw-redirect">polyandrous</a> whereas the Lesser Painted Snipe is <a href="/wiki/Monogamy" title="Monogamy">monogamous</a>. The females of the genus <i>Rostratula</i> will bond with several males during a breeding season, but once the eggs are laid the males provide all the <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" title="Avian incubation" class="mw-redirect">incubation</a> and parental care. The <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> of both species is a shallow cup, often built on a platform of vegetation. Clutch sizes range from 2-4 eggs, which are incubated for 15-21 days.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
