<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg/220px-Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="172" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg/330px-Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg/440px-Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg 2x" data-file-width="981" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Desert_warbler_at_Kutch.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Desert warbler at <a href="/wiki/Kutch" class="mw-redirect" title="Kutch">Kutch</a></div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Asian desert warbler</b> (<i>Curruca nana</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> which breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a> of central and western <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Asia</a> and the extreme east of <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Europe</a> (<a href="/wiki/Volga_Delta" title="Volga Delta">Volga Delta</a> area east to western <a href="/wiki/Inner_Mongolia" title="Inner Mongolia">Inner Mongolia</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrating</a> to similar habitats in southwestern Asia (<a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> to northwestern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>) and the far northeast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> (<a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> coastal regions) in winter. Until recently it was considered <a href="/wiki/Conspecificity" class="mw-redirect" title="Conspecificity">conspecific</a> with the <a href="/wiki/African_desert_warbler" title="African desert warbler">African desert warbler</a> (and called just "desert warbler"),<sup id="cite_ref-DelHoyo_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DelHoyo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but is now given <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">specific</a> status.<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Svensson_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Svensson-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The two are still each other's closest living relatives, and their relationships to other typical warblers are not clear. They may be fairly close to the <a href="/wiki/Whitethroat" class="mw-redirect" title="Whitethroat">whitethroat</a>; particularly, female whitethroats look much like a richly coloured Asian desert warbler. But it seems that all these three <a href="/wiki/Taxa" class="mw-redirect" title="Taxa">taxa</a> are fairly <a href="/wiki/Basal_(phylogenetics)" title="Basal (phylogenetics)">basal</a> members of the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is a small bird (the second-smallest in the genus after African desert warbler), 11.5–12.5&#160;cm long. The sexes are almost identical in colour, pale grey-brown above with browner wings and tail, and whitish below; the bill and legs are yellowish, and the eye has a yellow iris. Like its relatives, it is insectivorous, but will also take small berries; unlike most warblers, it commonly feeds on the ground. The song is a distinctive jingle often given in an advertisement flight, with a mix of clear and harsher notes. It breeds in semi-desert and dry <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a> environments, as long as some bushes for nesting occur. The nest is built in low shrub, and 4–6 eggs are laid.<sup id="cite_ref-DelHoyo_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DelHoyo-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Svensson_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Svensson-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It has occurred as a rare vagrant as far west as <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The specific <i>nana</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "dwarf", from earlier  <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>nanos</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
