<p>The <b>dusky thrush</b> (<i>Turdus eunomus</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family which breeds eastwards from central <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Kamchatka" class="mw-redirect" title="Kamchatka">Kamchatka</a>  wintering to Japan, South China and Myanmar. It is closely related to the more southerly breeding <a href="/wiki/Naumann%27s_thrush" title="Naumann&#39;s thrush">Naumann's thrush</a> <i>T. naumanni</i>; the two have often been regarded as <a href="/wiki/Conspecific" class="mw-redirect" title="Conspecific">conspecific</a>. The scientific name comes from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>Turdus</i>, "thrush" and <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>eunomos</i>, "orderly".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This species breeds in open woodland areas, but unlike Naumann's thrush, the dusky thrush is more tolerant of mountainous and tundra-edge habitats. This species is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering south to southeast Asia, principally in China and neighbouring countries. It is a rare vagrant to western Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In December 2016 a sighting of one in the <a href="/wiki/Derbyshire" title="Derbyshire">Derbyshire</a> in the United Kingdom brought hundreds of birdwatchers to see it.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg/220px-Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg/330px-Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg/440px-Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1177" data-file-height="786" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Dusky_Thrush_Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary_Darjeeling_West_Bengal_India_02.01.2018.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>From <a href="/wiki/Senchal_Wildlife_Sanctuary" title="Senchal Wildlife Sanctuary">Senchal Wildlife Sanctuary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darjeeling" title="Darjeeling">Darjeeling</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>It nests in trees, laying 3-5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in an untidy but neatly lined nest. Migrating birds and wintering birds often form small flocks. It is omnivorous, eating a wide range of insects, especially mosquitoes,  earthworms and berries.
</p><p>This is a medium-sized but stocky thrush, reminiscent in structure of a small <a href="/wiki/Fieldfare" title="Fieldfare">fieldfare</a>. The underwing is reddish brown, and there is a pale <dfn id=""><a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_bird_terms#supercilium" title="Glossary of bird terms"><span title="See entry at: Glossary of bird terms § supercilium" style="color:inherit;" class="glossary-link">supercilium</span></a></dfn>.
</p><p>Dusky thrush has a dark brown back and rump; the face, breast, and flank spots rump are black and the belly and undertail are white. Naumann's thrush in comparison has a paler brown back and head; the face, breast, flank spots and rump are reddish, and the belly and undertail are white.
</p><p>The female is fairly similar to the male, but immatures have a weaker patterning.
</p><p>The male dusky thrush has a simple fluted or whistling song, similar to the <a href="/wiki/Redwing" title="Redwing">redwing</a>. There are suggestions that the songs of dusky and Naumann's thrush differ.
</p>
