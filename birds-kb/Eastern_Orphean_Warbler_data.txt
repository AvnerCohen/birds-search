<p>The <b>eastern Orphean warbler</b> (<i>Curruca crassirostris</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i>Curruca</i>. This <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> occurs in summer around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, through the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a> via <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> and surrounding regions to <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> Africa.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At 15–16&#160;cm length—somewhat larger than a <a href="/wiki/Blackcap" class="mw-redirect" title="Blackcap">blackcap</a>—this is one of the largest species of typical warblers. The adult males have a plain grey back. The bill is long and pointed and the legs black. The male has a dark grey head, black eye mask, and white throat. The iris is white. Females and immatures have a paler head and reddish underparts; their grey back has a brownish tinge. The iris is dark in young birds. The song is a series of warbling <i>liroo-liroo</i> and scolding notes. Song is more varied than the <a href="/wiki/Western_Orphean_warbler" title="Western Orphean warbler">western Orphean warbler</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> approaching <a href="/wiki/Nightingale" class="mw-redirect" title="Nightingale">Nightingale</a> in richness.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>These small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> birds<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> are found in open <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">deciduous</a> woodland. 4–6 eggs are laid in a nest in a bush or tree. Like most "warblers", The eastern Orphean warbler is an <a href="/wiki/Insectivore" title="Insectivore">insectivore</a>.
</p>
