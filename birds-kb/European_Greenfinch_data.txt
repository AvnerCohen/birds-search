<p>The <b>European Greenfinch</b>, or just <b>Greenfinch</b> <i>(Carduelis chloris)</i>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> family Fringillidae. The genus <i>Carduelis</i> might be split up and in this case, the greenfinches would be separated in their old genus <i>Chloris</i> again.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from May 2012">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<p>This bird is widespread throughout <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and south west <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is mainly resident, but some northernmost populations <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> further south. The Greenfinch has also been introduced into both <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</p>
<p>Woodland edges, farmland hedges and gardens with relatively thick vegetation are favoured for breeding. It nests in trees or bushes, laying 3-8 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>.</p>
<p>This species can form large flocks outside the breeding season, sometimes mixing with other finches and buntings. They feed largely on seeds, but also take berries and seeds.</p>
<p>The Greenfinch is 15&#160;cm in length with a wing span of 24.5-27.5&#160;cm and is similar in size and shape to a <a href="/wiki/House_Sparrow" title="House Sparrow">House Sparrow</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> but is mainly green, with yellow in the wings and tail. The female and young birds are duller and have brown tones on the back. The bill is thick and conical. The song contains wheezes and twitters, and the male has a "butterfly" display flight.</p>
