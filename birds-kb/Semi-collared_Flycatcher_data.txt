<p>The <b>semicollared flycatcher</b> (<i>Ficedula semitorquata</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> family, one of the four species of <a href="/wiki/Western_Palearctic_black-and-white_flycatchers" title="Western Palearctic black-and-white flycatchers">Western Palearctic black-and-white flycatchers</a>. It breeds in montane forests of the southeastern corner of Europe (<a href="/wiki/Balkan_Peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Balkan Peninsula">Balkan Peninsula</a>) to Northwest <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in Central and Eastern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is a rare vagrant in western Europe. Tracking studies using geolocators have revealed that weather conditions during spring migration can have a large impact on the timing of Semicollared flycatcher migration and survival of adult birds.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This is a 12–13.5&#160;cm long bird, intermediate in appearance between <a href="/wiki/Collared_flycatcher" title="Collared flycatcher">collared flycatcher</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_pied_flycatcher" title="European pied flycatcher">European pied flycatcher</a>. It has often been classed as a subspecies of collared flycatcher.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg/220px-SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="206" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg/330px-SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg/440px-SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg 2x" data-file-width="717" data-file-height="671" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:SemicollaredFlycatcher.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Struma River Valley - Bulgaria</div></div></div>
<p>The breeding male is mainly black above and white below, with a white half-collar, extending further back than in pied, large white wing patch, extensively white tail sides and a large white forehead patch. It has a pale grey rump. The bill is black and has the broad but pointed shape typical of aerial insectivores. It mainly takes insects in flight, rarely hunting <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar" title="Caterpillar">caterpillars</a> amongst the tree foliage like pied flycatcher.
</p><p>Non-breeding male, females and juvenile semi-collared flycatchers have the black replaced by a pale brown, and may be very difficult to distinguish from other <i> Ficedula</i> flycatchers, particularly the collared flycatcher. A distinction is that semicollared may show a white second wing bar, but many individuals are not separable in the field.
</p><p>Semicollared flycatchers are birds of belts of deciduous woodlands, especially <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">oak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hornbeam" title="Hornbeam">hornbeam</a>, in mountainous areas. They build an open nest in a native tree-holes and old nests of woodpeckers, also the species can occupy nest-boxes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The clutch size is 4-7 eggs. The song is again intermediate between collared and pied flycatchers, with slow strained whistles, but some rhythmic elements.
</p>
