<p>The <b>Semicollared Flycatcher</b> (<i>Ficedula semitorquata</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> family, one of the four species of <a href="/wiki/Western_Palearctic_black-and-white_flycatchers" title="Western Palearctic black-and-white flycatchers">Western Palearctic black-and-white flycatchers</a>. It breeds in the southeastern corner of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and southwestern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is a rare vagrant in western Europe.</p>
<p>This is a 12-13.5&#160;cm long bird, intermediate in appearance between <a href="/wiki/Collared_Flycatcher" title="Collared Flycatcher">Collared Flycatcher</a> and <a href="/wiki/European_Pied_Flycatcher" title="European Pied Flycatcher">European Pied Flycatcher</a>. It has often been classed as a subspecies of Collared Flycatcher.</p>
<p>The breeding male is mainly black above and white below, with a white half-collar, extending further back than in Pied, large white wing patch, extensively white tail sides and a large white forehead patch. It has a pale grey rump. The bill is black and has the broad but pointed shape typical of aerial insectivores. It mainly takes insects in flight, rarely hunting <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar" title="Caterpillar">caterpillars</a> amongst the tree foliage like Pied Flycatcher.</p>
<p>Non-breeding male, females and juvenile Semi-collared Flycatchers have the black replaced by a pale brown, and may be very difficult to distinguish from other <i>Fidecula</i> flycatchers, particularly the Collared Flycatcher. A distinction is that Semi-collared may show a white second wing bar, but many individuals are not separable in the field.</p>
<p>Semicollared Flycatchers are birds of belts of deciduous woodlands, especially <a href="/wiki/Oak" title="Oak">Oak</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hornbeam" title="Hornbeam">Hornbeam</a>, in mountainous areas. They build an open nest in a tree hole, and 4-7 eggs are laid. The song is again intermediate between Collared and Pied Flycatchers, with slow strained whistles, but some rhythmic elements.</p>
