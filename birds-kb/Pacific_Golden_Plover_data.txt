<p>The <b>Pacific Golden Plover</b> (<i>Pluvialis fulva</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a>.</p>
<p>The 23–26&#160;cm long breeding adult is spotted gold and black on the crown, back and wings. Its face and neck are black with a white border and it has a black breast and a dark rump. The legs are black. In winter, the black is lost and the plover then has a yellowish face and breast, and white underparts.</p>
<p>It is similar to two other golden plovers, Eurasian and American. Pacific Golden Plover is smaller, slimmer and relatively longer-legged than <a href="/wiki/European_Golden_Plover" title="European Golden Plover">European Golden Plover</a>, <i>Pluvialis apricaria</i>, which also has white axillary (armpit) feathers. It is more similar to <a href="/wiki/American_Golden_Plover" title="American Golden Plover">American Golden Plover</a>, <i>Pluvialis dominica</i>, with which it was once considered conspecific (as "Lesser Golden Plover", see Sangster <i>et al.</i>, 2002). The Pacific Golden Plover is slimmer than the American species, has a shorter primary projection, and longer legs, and is usually yellower on the back.</p>
<p>The breeding habitat of Pacific Golden Plover is Arctic tundra from northernmost <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> into western <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a>. It nests on the ground in a dry open area.</p>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and winters in south <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>. A few winter in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a>, <a href="/wiki/USA" title="USA" class="mw-redirect">USA</a>. In Hawaii, the bird is known as the <i>kolea</i>. It is a very rare <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a> to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>This bird forages for food on <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a>, fields, beaches and <a href="/wiki/Tidal_flats" title="Tidal flats" class="mw-redirect">tidal flats</a>, usually by sight. It eats insects and crustaceans and some berries.</p>
