<p>The <b>Paddyfield Warbler</b> (<i>Acrocephalus agricola</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of marsh warbler (<a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Acrocephalidae" title="Acrocephalidae">Acrocephalidae</a>). It was formerly included in the "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a>" assemblage. The <a href="/wiki/Manchurian_Reed_Warbler" title="Manchurian Reed Warbler">Manchurian Reed Warbler</a> (<i>A. tangorum</i>) was (and sometimes still is) included in <i>A. agricola</i> as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>.</p>
<p>It breeds in temperate central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. It is a rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> although there are small breeding populations along the western shores of the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> around the border between <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in low vegetation such as long <a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">grass</a>, reeds and <a href="/wiki/Rice" title="Rice">rice</a>. 4-5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid in a nest in grass.</p>
<p>At 13 centimetres (5.1&#160;in) long with a wingspan of 15–17.5 centimetres (5.9–6.9 in), Paddyfield is close in size to the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Reed_Warbler" title="Eurasian Reed Warbler">Eurasian Reed Warbler</a> but with shorter bill and wingspan. The adult has an unstreaked pale brown back and buff underparts, with a warm brown rump. There is a clearer whitish supercilium and the bill is short and pointed. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers, but young birds are richer buff below. Like most warblers, it is insectivorous.</p>
<p>The song is fast and similar to <a href="/wiki/Marsh_Warbler" title="Marsh Warbler">Marsh Warbler</a>, with much mimicry and typically acrocephaline whistles added. Its song is weaker and more rhythmic than that of its relative.</p>
