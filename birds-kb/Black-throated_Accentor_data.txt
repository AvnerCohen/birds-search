<p>The <b>black-throated accentor</b> (<i>Prunella atrogularis</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Ural_Mountains" title="Ural Mountains">Ural</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tian_Shan" title="Tian Shan">Tian Shan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altai_Mountains" title="Altai Mountains">Altai Mountains</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and neighboring countries. It is a rare vagrant in western  <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p><p>The black-throated accentor builds a neat nest low in <a href="/wiki/Spruce" title="Spruce">spruce</a> thickets, laying 3-5 unspotted blue eggs. It winters in scrub or cultivation.
</p><p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Dunnock" title="Dunnock">dunnock-sized</a> bird, 13.5–14&#160;cm in length. It has a streaked dark brown back, somewhat resembling a <a href="/wiki/House_sparrow" title="House sparrow">house sparrow</a>, but adults have a black crown, face patch and throat, and a white <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>. The breast is orange, and the belly white with orange stripes. Like other <a href="/wiki/Accentor" title="Accentor">accentors</a>, this species has an insectivore's fine pointed bill.
</p><p>Sexes are similar, but winter birds and juveniles are less contrasted. In particular, the dark throat may be almost absent in young birds.
</p><p>The call is a fine <i>ti-ti-ti</i>, and the song is similar to the dunnock's pleasant twittering.
</p>
