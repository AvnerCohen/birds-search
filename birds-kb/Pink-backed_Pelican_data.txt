<p>The <b>Pink-backed Pelican</b> (<i>Pelecanus rufescens</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Pelican" title="Pelican">pelican</a> family of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. It is a resident breeder in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> and apparently <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinct</a> in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a> in <a href="/wiki/Swamp" title="Swamp">swamps</a> and shallow lakes.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pink-backed_Pelican&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>This is a relatively small pelican though by no means a small bird. The size difference is apparent besides the sympatric <a href="/wiki/Great_White_Pelican" title="Great White Pelican">Great White Pelican</a> as well as its grayish as opposed to white plumage. Length is from 125 to 155 cm (49 to 61 in), wingspan is 2.15–2.9 m (7.1–9.5 ft) and body mass if from 4 to 7 kg (8.8 to 15 lb). The bill is 30 to 38 cm (12 to 15 in) in length.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The plumage is grey and white, with a pinkish hue on the back occasionally apparent (never in the deep pink of a <a href="/wiki/Flamingo" title="Flamingo">flamingo</a>). The top of the bill is yellow and the pouch is usually greyish. Breeding adults have long <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feather</a> plumes on the head.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pink-backed_Pelican&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat and breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_breeding">Habitat and breeding</span></h2>
<p>Found in a range of aquatic habitats, but prefers quiet backwaters with shallow water, avoiding steep, vegetated lake banks. It prefers for freshwater lakes, swamps, large slow-flowing rivers, and seasonal pools but also frequents reservoirs, seasonally flooded land3 and flood-plains near river mouths. It may occur on alkaline and saline lakes and lagoons, and can sometimes be found along the coast in bays1 and estuaries2, 3, 5 (although seldom on open seashore). The species tends to roost and breed in trees (e.g. mangroves), but will also roost on sandy islands, cliffs, coral reefs and sand-dunes.</p>
<p><br />
Nesting trees have many nests built close together, these nests are re-used every year until often the trees collapse although the birds will normally remain in the area. The species nests colonially in trees, reeds or low bushes along waterfronts as well as (less often) on the ground on sandy islands and in mangroves.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> is a large heap of sticks, into which two to three large white <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid. The chicks feed by plunging their heads deep into the adult’s pouch and taking the partially digested regurgitated <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, and may be 10-50 m above the ground.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pink-backed_Pelican&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span></h2>
<p>Food is usually <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> (of any size up to 450 g, usually in the 80-290 g range) and <a href="/wiki/Amphibia" title="Amphibia" class="mw-redirect">amphibians</a>, and is usually obtained by fishing in groups.</p>
<p>The consists of most cichlids (Haplochromis and <i><a href="/wiki/Tilapia" title="Tilapia">Tilapia</a></i>)</p>
