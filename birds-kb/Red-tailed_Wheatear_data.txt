<p>The <b>Red-tailed Wheatear</b> (<i>Oenanthe chrysopygia</i>), also known as the <b>Rusty-tailed Wheatear</b>, <b>Persian Wheatear</b> or <b>Afghan Wheatear</b>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> breeding in mountainous areas of south-west and central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Wheatear" title="Wheatear">wheatear</a> <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i>Oenanthe</i> which was formerly placed in the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae but is now in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> family Muscicapidae. The Red-tailed Wheatear used to be considered a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Kurdistan_Wheatear" title="Kurdistan Wheatear" class="mw-redirect">Kurdistan Wheatear</a> (<i>O. xanthoprymna</i>) but is now often regarded as a separate species.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_Wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>It is 14.5&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 26–27&#160;cm and a weight of about 20-27&#160;g.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> is fairly drab, mainly grey-brown above and greyish-white below. It has a faint pale stripe over the eye, a rufous tinge to the ear-<a href="/wiki/Covert_(feather)" title="Covert (feather)" class="mw-redirect">coverts</a> and silvery-white underwing-coverts.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The rump and bases of the outer tail-feathers are reddish while the rest of the tail is black forming a T-shaped pattern.<sup id="cite_ref-Harris_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> There is little difference between males and females but the male has black between the eye and bill.<sup id="cite_ref-Grimmett_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grimmett-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The female Kurdistan Wheatear can be very similar but usually has white bases to the outer tail-feathers. Adult males and some females of the Kurdistan Wheatear are quite different with a black face and throat and greyer upperparts.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> is a simple repeated whistle. The bird has several clicking, rasping and whistling calls.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_Wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>The species was described in 1863 by the Italian zoologist <a href="/wiki/Filippo_de_Filippi" title="Filippo de Filippi">Filippo de Filippi</a> who named it <i>Dromolaea chrysopgyia</i>. It was later treated as a subspecies of the Kurdistan Wheatear (<i>O. xanthoprymna</i>) after birds with an appearance intermediate between the two ("<i>O. x. cummingi</i>") were found, suggesting that they could <a href="/wiki/Interbreed" title="Interbreed" class="mw-redirect">interbreed</a>. However, these hybrid birds may in fact be first-winter Kurdistan Wheatears.<sup id="cite_ref-Roselaar_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roselaar-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Red-tailed Wheatears in the eastern part of the range are slightly paler and are sometimes regarded as a separate subspecies, <i>O. c. kingi</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_Wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>Its breeding range extends from north-east <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> eastwards through <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> to <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It migrates south to winter in the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a>, southern parts of <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and north-west <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. It has occurred as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" title="Vagrancy in birds" class="mw-redirect">vagrant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>.</p>
<p>It breeds in mountainous areas from 1,200���4,000&#160;m above sea-level. It nests on steep, barren, rocky ground with little vegeatation. It winters at lower levels occurring on rocky hills, steppe, scrubland and semi-desert.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-tailed_Wheatear&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>It usually forages on bare ground, feeding mainly on <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Ant" title="Ant">ants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar" title="Caterpillar">caterpillars</a>. It will pick food from vegetation, dig with its bill for beetle larvae and launch attacks from an exposed perch on a rock or stone.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It breeds from late March or April to June, raising two broods. The <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a> is a cup of grass and other plant material built in a hole in rocks, stones or a wall. Four to six <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid and are <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" title="Avian incubation" class="mw-redirect">incubated</a> for 13 days. The eggs are pale bluish-white, sometimes with reddish-brown spots.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
