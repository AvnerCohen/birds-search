<p>The <b>Water Pipit</b> (<i>Anthus spinoletta</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird which breeds in the mountains of southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and southern temperate <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> across to <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. It is a short-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> moving to wet open lowlands such as marshes and flooded fields in winter. Some birds migrate north to Britain for winter, taking advantage of the warm <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_climate" title="Oceanic climate">oceanic climate</a>.</p>
<p>Like most other <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipits</a>, this is an undistinguished looking species on the ground, mainly brown above and dark streaked buff below. It has dark legs, white outer tail feathers and a longish dark bill. In summer it has a distinctive breeding <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a>, with a pinkish breast, grey head and pale supercilium.<sup id="cite_ref-id_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-id-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Rock_Pipit" title="Eurasian Rock Pipit">Eurasian Rock Pipit</a>'s subspecies <i>littoralis</i> in summer plumage is very close in outward appearance to the Water Pipit however. They can be told apart by their song,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> and occupy different <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" title="Habitat (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">habitat</a> types even when they occur in the same general area.<sup id="cite_ref-Bijlsma_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bijlsma-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> The Water Pipit is also much less approachable than the Eurasian Rock Pipit, rising high and quickly leaving the vicinity when approached. Water and <a href="/wiki/Buff-bellied_Pipit" title="Buff-bellied Pipit">Buff-bellied Pipit</a> do not co-occur except in a small area in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>This species is insectivorous. Its call is an explosive "fit", like Eurasian Rock Pipit. Its song is similar, but it consisting of maybe 5 "blocks" of just about half a dozen notes each (the Eurasian Rock Pipit has fewer, but longer blocks); it ends either with no or with repeated trills.<sup id="cite_ref-Bijlsma_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bijlsma-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Formerly included in the Water Pipit were the subspecies now separated as Rock Pipit and <a href="/wiki/Buff-bellied_Pipit" title="Buff-bellied Pipit">Buff-bellied Pipit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The former is more closely related to the Water Pipit than the latter, as indicated by external<sup id="cite_ref-id_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-id-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> and molecular characteristics.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
