<p>The <b>Crimson-winged Finch</b> (<i>Rhodopechys sanguineus</i>), is a pale-colored thickset <a href="/wiki/Fringillidae" title="Fringillidae" class="mw-redirect">finch</a> with a heavy, dull yellowish bill. The nominate <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> is found from <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> to NE <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. Another <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhodopechys_alienus" title="Rhodopechys alienus" class="mw-redirect"><i>alienus</i></a>, is found in the <a href="/wiki/Atlas_Mountains" title="Atlas Mountains">Atlas Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, and may represent a separate species.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It has an average length of 13&#160;cm. and a wingspan of ca. 32&#160;cm. It is light brown overall, with a whitish mid-belly, a black cap and a pinkish pattern on the wings and tail. The female is slightly duller than the male.</p>
<p>This species lives on rocky mountainsides, often at high elevation. It can be found in barren landscapes with little vegetation, and sometimes nests in rock crevices. It feeds on seeds, and during the winter descends in flocks to agricultural fields to find food. The female lays and incubates 4 or 5 blue, lightly speckled eggs.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Crimson-winged_Finch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Morphology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Morphology">Morphology</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Crimson-winged_Finch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Differences between North African and Asian birds">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_between_North_African_and_Asian_birds">Differences between North African and Asian birds</span></h3>
<p>There are several differences between Asian nominate Crimson-winged Finches and North African birds of the race <i>alienus</i>:</p>
<ul>
<li>North African birds have a rosy-tinged grey-white central chin and throat, with a narrow brown breast-band below it, whereas this whole area is solidly tawny-brown on Asian birds</li>
<li>The brown breast and flank markings on Asian birds are more extensive than on North African birds</li>
<li>North African birds have less black on the crown than Asian birds (on males it often tends to be restricted to the forecrown)</li>
</ul>
<p>Male birds show the following additional differences</p>
<ul>
<li>Asian birds have extensive pink in their uppertail-coverts, which is lacking in North African birds (although the latter can show a vinous wash here in fresh plumage)</li>
<li>Asian birds often have black spotting on their breast-sides; North African birds always lack this</li>
<li>Asian birds can have distinct black markings on their mantle, but these are much less distinct on North African birds</li>
<li>Asian birds have on average more distinct black markings on their ear-coverts than North African birds</li>
<li>Asian birds typically have more extensive red in their face, often in the fore-supercilium (North African birds can show red here but it is not the norm)</li>
</ul>
