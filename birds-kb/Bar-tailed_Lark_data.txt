<p>The <b>bar-tailed lark</b>, or <b>bar-tailed desert lark</b>, (<i>Ammomanes cinctura</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Alaudidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Alaudidae">Alaudidae</a>. Two other species, the <a href="/wiki/Rufous-tailed_lark" title="Rufous-tailed lark">rufous-tailed lark</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cape_clapper_lark" title="Cape clapper lark">Cape clapper lark</a> are both also sometimes referred to using the name bar-tailed lark. It is found from Morocco to Pakistan. Its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> is hot <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a>.  This is in many places a common species, but elsewhere rather less common. It has a very wide distribution and faces no obvious threats, but surveys have shown that it is slowly decreasing in numbers. The <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has rated its conservation status as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least-concern_species" title="Least-concern species">least concern</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bar-tailed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bar-tailed lark was originally placed in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Melanocorypha" title="Melanocorypha">Melanocorypha</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alternate names for the bar-tailed lark include: <b>bar-tailed finch-lark</b>, <b>black-tailed desert lark</b>, <b>black-tailed lark</b>, and <b>black-tailed sand lark</b>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bar-tailed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li><i>A. c. cinctura</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/John_Gould" title="John Gould">Gould</a>, 1839)</small>: Found on <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cape Verde Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a></li>
<li><i>A. c. arenicolor</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Carl_Jakob_Sundevall" title="Carl Jakob Sundevall">Sundevall</a>, 1850)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>. Found in North African deserts to the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia">Arabia</a></li>
<li><b>Persian rufous-tailed finch lark</b> (<i>A. c. zarudnyi</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hartert" title="Ernst Hartert">Hartert</a>, 1902</small>: Found in eastern Iran, southern Afghanistan and southern Pakistan</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bar-tailed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bar-tailed lark is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Desert_lark" title="Desert lark">desert lark</a> in appearance, but at 14 to 15&#160;cm (5.5 to 5.9&#160;in), is slightly smaller, with a smaller, more domed head, a smaller beak, thinner legs and a shorter tail. The upper parts are sandy-buff washed with grey, while the underparts are whitish with little if any streaking, and the breast and flanks washed with buff. The rufous wings have dark trailing edges and the rufous tail has a terminal black band.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bar-tailed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bar-tailed lark has a large distribution across North Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, the Middle East and western Asia. Its range includes Afghanistan, Algeria, Bahrain, Cape Verde, Chad, Egypt, Iran, Iraq, Israel, Jordan, Kuwait, Libya, Mali, Mauritania, Morocco, Niger, Oman, Pakistan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Sudan, Syria, Tunisia, United Arab Emirates, Western Sahara and Yemen.<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its typical habitat is sandy or stony desert or semi desert, with low scrubby vegetation. It is generally shier than the desert lark and has a preference for level ground whereas the desert lark tends to frequent rocky slopes and hillsides.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
