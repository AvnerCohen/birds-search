<p>The <b>marsh harriers</b> are <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">birds of prey</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Harrier_(bird)" title="Harrier (bird)">harrier</a> subfamily. They are medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">raptors</a> and the largest and broadest-winged harriers. Most of them are associated with marshland and dense reedbeds. They are found almost worldwide, excluding only the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.</p>
<p>Until recently two species were generally recognized: the Marsh Harrier (<i>Circus aeruginosus</i>) and the <a href="/wiki/African_Marsh_Harrier" title="African Marsh Harrier">African Marsh Harrier</a> (<i>C. ranivorus</i>). The Marsh Harrier is now usually split into several species, sometimes as many as six. These are the <a href="/wiki/Western_Marsh_Harrier" title="Western Marsh Harrier">Western Marsh Harrier</a> (<i>C. aeruginosus</i>), <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Marsh_Harrier" title="Eastern Marsh Harrier">Eastern Marsh Harrier</a> (<i>C. spilonotus</i>), <a href="/wiki/Papuan_Harrier" title="Papuan Harrier">Papuan Harrier</a> (<i>C. spilonotus spilothorax</i> or <i>C. spilothorax</i>), <a href="/wiki/Swamp_Harrier" title="Swamp Harrier">Swamp Harrier</a> (<i>C. approximans</i>), <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union_Harrier" title="Réunion Harrier">Réunion Harrier</a> (<i>C. maillardi maillardi</i> or <i>C. maillardi</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar_Marsh_Harrier" title="Madagascar Marsh Harrier" class="mw-redirect">Madagascar Marsh Harrier</a> (<i>C. maillardi macrosceles</i> or <i>C. macrosceles</i>).</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Marsh_Harrier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Species">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Species">Species</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Western_Marsh_Harrier-_Bangalore,_India.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Western_Marsh_Harrier-_Bangalore%2C_India.jpg/220px-Western_Marsh_Harrier-_Bangalore%2C_India.jpg" width="220" height="308" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Western_Marsh_Harrier-_Bangalore,_India.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Western Marsh Harrier- Bangalore, India</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Western_Marsh_Harrier" title="Western Marsh Harrier">Western Marsh Harrier</a></b> (<i>Circus aeruginosus</i>), often simply called the <b>Marsh Harrier</b>, breeds widely across <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is migratory except in the mildest regions, and winters mainly in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It hunts small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, surprising them as it drifts low over fields and reedbeds.</p>
<p>The Western Marsh Harrier is a typical harrier, with long wings held in a shallow V in its low flight. It also resembles other harriers in having distinct male and female plumages, but its plumages are quite different from those of its relatives. The male has wings with grey and brown sections and black wingtips. Its head, tail and underparts are greyish, except for the chestnut belly. The female is mainly brown with a cream crown and cream leading edge to her wings.</p>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Eastern_Marsh_Harrier" title="Eastern Marsh Harrier">Eastern Marsh Harrier</a></b> (<i>C. spilonotus</i>) breeds in the grasslands and wetlands of southern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>, north-east <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manchuria" title="Manchuria">Manchuria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> for the northern winter to <a href="/wiki/South-east_Asia" title="South-east Asia" class="mw-redirect">South-east Asia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Borneo" title="Borneo">Borneo</a>.</p>
<p>Like all marsh harriers, it favours open, wet environments, and is frequently seen drifting low over ricefields, interspersing long, watchful circling glides with two or three slow, powerful wingbeats. Two subspecies are recognised: <i>C. s. spilonotus</i> of east Asia, and <i>C. s. spilothorax</i>, of <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a> and the islands nearby, which is sometimes thought to be a separate species and is often called the <b><a href="/wiki/Papuan_Harrier" title="Papuan Harrier">Papuan Harrier</a></b>.</p>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Swamp_Harrier" title="Swamp Harrier">Swamp Harrier</a></b> (<i>C. approximans</i>) at about 50 to 58 cm is slightly larger than <i>C. spilonotus</i>, often a little darker, and has less heavily barred wings and tail. It is commonly found in suitable habitat anywhere in <a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasia</a>, particularly in the higher rainfall areas to the east, south-east, and south-west, of <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and throughout <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, but also in the tropical north of Australia and the island groups of <a href="/wiki/New_Caledonia" title="New Caledonia">New Caledonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fiji" title="Fiji">Fiji</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tonga" title="Tonga">Tonga</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Society_Islands" title="Society Islands">Society Islands</a>. It is also known as the <b>Australasian Harrier</b> or <b>Pacific Marsh Harrier</b>.</p>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Madagascar_Marsh_Harrier" title="Madagascar Marsh Harrier" class="mw-redirect">Madagascar Marsh Harrier</a></b> or <b>Madagascar Harrier</b> (<i>C. maillardi</i>) is found on the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a> islands of <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Comoros" title="Comoros">Comoros</a>. It is sometimes split into two species: Madagascar Harrier (<i>Circus macrosceles</i>) and <b>Réunion Harrier</b> (<i>C. maillardi).</i></p>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/African_Marsh_Harrier" title="African Marsh Harrier">African Marsh Harrier</a></b> (<i>C. ranivorus</i>) is a distinct non-migratory species that has not usually been included as a subspecies of <i>C. aeruginosus</i>. It inhabits southern and eastern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
