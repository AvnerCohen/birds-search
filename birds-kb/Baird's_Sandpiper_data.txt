<p>The <b>Baird's Sandpiper</b> (<i>Calidris bairdii</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Shorebird" title="Shorebird" class="mw-redirect">shorebird</a>. It is among those <a href="/wiki/Calidrid" title="Calidrid">calidrids</a> sometimes separated in <i>Erolia</i>.</p>
<p>Adults have black legs and a short thin dark bill. They are dark brown on top and mainly white underneath with a black patch on the rump. The head and breast are light brown with dark streaks. In winter plumage, this species is paler brownish gray above. This bird can be difficult to distinguish from other similar tiny shorebirds; these are known collectively as "peeps" or "<a href="/wiki/Stint" title="Stint">stints</a>".</p>
<p>One of the best identification features is the long wings, which extend beyond the tail when the bird is on the ground. Only the <a href="/wiki/White-rumped_Sandpiper" title="White-rumped Sandpiper">White-rumped Sandpiper</a> also shows this, and that bird can be distinguished by the feature from which it gets its name.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is the northern tundra from eastern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> to western <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>. They nest on the ground, usually in dry locations with low vegetation.</p>

<p>They are a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">long distance migrant</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. This species is a rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>These birds forage by moving about mudflats, picking up food by sight. They mainly eat insects, also some small crustaceans.</p>
<p>This bird was named after <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Fullerton_Baird" title="Spencer Fullerton Baird">Spencer Fullerton Baird</a>, a 19th-century naturalist.</p>
<p>Baird's Sandpiper <a href="/wiki/Hybridisation_in_shorebirds#Other_hybrid_scolopacids" title="Hybridisation in shorebirds">might</a> have <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridized</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Buff-breasted_Sandpiper" title="Buff-breasted Sandpiper">Buff-breasted Sandpiper</a>.</p>
