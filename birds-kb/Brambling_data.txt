<p>The <b>brambling</b> (<i>Fringilla montifringilla</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird in the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> family Fringillidae.   It has also been called the <b>cock o' the north</b> and the <b>mountain finch</b>. It is widespread and migratory, often seen in very large flocks.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1758 <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a> included the species in the <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">10th edition</a> of his <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_Naturae" title="Systema Naturae">Systema Naturae</a></i> under its current <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">binomial name</a>, <i>Fringilla montifringilla</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-checklist_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-checklist-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  <i>Montifringilla</i> is from Latin <i>mons, montis</i> mountain and <i>fringilla</i> finch.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The English name is probably derived from Common West Germanic *<i>brâma</i>, meaning bramble or a thorny bush (compare Standard German <i>Brämling</i> with the same meaning).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The brambling is similar in size and shape to a <a href="/wiki/Common_chaffinch" title="Common chaffinch">common chaffinch</a>. Breeding-<a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumaged</a> male bramblings are very distinctive, with a black head, dark upperparts, orange breast and white belly. Females and younger birds are less distinct, and more similar in appearance to some chaffinches. In all plumages, however, bramblings differs from chaffinches in a number of features:
</p>
<ul><li>the brambling has a white <a href="/wiki/Rump_(animal)#Birds" title="Rump (animal)">rump</a>, whereas that of the common chaffinch is grey-green;</li>
<li>the breast is orange, contrasting with a white belly, on the brambling, whereas on the common chaffinch, the underparts are more uniformly coloured (pink or buff);</li>
<li>the brambling's <a href="/wiki/Scapular_feathers" class="mw-redirect" title="Scapular feathers">scapular feathers</a> are orange, whereas the common chaffinch's are grey or grey-brown;</li>
<li>the flanks are dark-spotted on the brambling, plain on the common chaffinch;</li>
<li>bramblings lack the white outer tail feathers of common chaffinches.</li></ul>
<p>An additional difference for all plumages except breeding-plumaged males is the bill colour - yellow in the brambling, dull pinkish in the common chaffinch (breeding-plumaged male bramblings have black bills, common chaffinches in the corresponding plumage have grey bills).<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Measurements</b>:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Length</b>: 16 cm<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Weight</b>: 23-29 g<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Wingspan</b>: 25-26 cm<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fringilla_montifringilla_2_(Marek_Szczepanek).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Fringilla_montifringilla_2_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/220px-Fringilla_montifringilla_2_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Fringilla_montifringilla_2_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/330px-Fringilla_montifringilla_2_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Fringilla_montifringilla_2_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/440px-Fringilla_montifringilla_2_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="433" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fringilla_montifringilla_2_(Marek_Szczepanek).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Male and female in Poland</div></div></div>
<p>This bird is widespread, in the breeding season, throughout the forests of northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and east across the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in southern Europe, North <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It regularly strays into <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> during migration and may continue as far south as the western <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The global population of bramblings is about 100 to 200 million, with a decreasing trend.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Open <a href="/wiki/Conifer" class="mw-redirect" title="Conifer">coniferous</a> or <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a> is favoured for breeding.<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>This species is almost entirely migratory. In Europe, it forms large flocks in the winter, sometimes with thousands or even millions of birds in a single flock. Such large gatherings occur especially if <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> mast is abundant. Bramblings do not require beech mast in the winter, but winter flocks of bramblings will move until they find it. This may be an adaptation to avoid competition with the common chaffinch.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Bramblings mostly eat seeds<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>in winter, but insects in summer.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It builds its nest in a tree fork,<sup id="cite_ref-Collins_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Collins-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and decorates the exterior with moss or <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichen</a> to make it less conspicuous. It lays 4–9 eggs.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
