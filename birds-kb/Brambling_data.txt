<p>The <b>Brambling</b> (<i>Fringilla montifringilla</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> family Fringillidae.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span></h2>
<p>The common English name is probably derived from Common West Germanic *<i>brâma</i>, meaning bramble or a thorny bush. (Compare Standard German <i>Brämling</i> with the same meaning.) It has also been called the Cock o' the North and the Mountain Finch.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Brambling is similar in size and shape to a <a href="/wiki/Chaffinch" title="Chaffinch" class="mw-redirect">Chaffinch</a>. Breeding-plumaged male Bramblings are very distinctive, with a black head, dark upperparts, orange breast and white belly. Females and younger birds are less distinct, and more similar in appearance to some Chaffinches. In all plumages, however, Bramblings differs from Chaffinches in a number of features:</p>
<ul>
<li>Brambling has a white rump whereas that of Chaffinch is grey-green;</li>
<li>the breast is orange, contrasting with a white belly on Brambling, whereas on Chaffinch the underparts of more uniformly coloured (pink or buff);</li>
<li>Brambling's scapulars are orange, whereas Chaffinch's are grey or grey-brown;</li>
<li>the flanks are dark-spotted on Brambling, plain on Chaffinch;</li>
<li>Bramblings lack the white outer tail feathers of Chaffinch.</li>
</ul>
<p>An additional difference for all plumages except breeding-plumaged males is the bill colour - yellow in Brambling, dull pinkish in Chaffinch (breeding-plumaged male Bramblings have black bills, Chaffinches in the corresponding plumage have grey bills).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fringilla_montifringilla_-Poland_-male-8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Fringilla_montifringilla_-Poland_-male-8.jpg/220px-Fringilla_montifringilla_-Poland_-male-8.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fringilla_montifringilla_-Poland_-male-8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Male in Poland</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This bird is widespread throughout the forests of northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in southern Europe, north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, north <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN2012_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN2012-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It regularly strays into <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> during migration and may continue as far south as the western <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. Open <a href="/wiki/Conifer" title="Conifer" class="mw-redirect">coniferous</a> or <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a> is favoured for breeding.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from December 2009">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>This species is almost entirely migratory. In Europe, it forms large flocks in the winter, sometimes with thousands or even millions of birds in a single flock. Such large gatherings occur especially if <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">beech</a> mast is abundant. Bramblings do not require beech mast in the winter, but winter flocks of Bramblings will move until they find it. This may be an adaptation to avoid competition with the Chaffinch.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span></h3>
<p>Bramblings mostly eat seeds, but unlike most finches, their young are fed largely on insects.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Brambling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>It builds its nest in a tree fork, and decorates the exterior with moss or <a href="/wiki/Lichen" title="Lichen">lichen</a> to make it less conspicuous. It lays 4-9 eggs.</p>
