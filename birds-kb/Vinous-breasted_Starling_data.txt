<p>The <b>vinous-breasted starling</b> (<i>Acridotheres burmannicus</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Starling" title="Starling">starling</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Sturnidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Sturnidae">Sturnidae</a>.  It is sometimes placed in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Sturnus" title="Sturnus">Sturnus</a></i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vinous-breasted_starling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is found in <a href="/wiki/Cambodia" title="Cambodia">Cambodia</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laos" title="Laos">Laos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burma" class="mw-redirect" title="Burma">Burma</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thailand" title="Thailand">Thailand</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Vietnam" title="Vietnam">Vietnam</a>.  In the early 2000s it has become an <a href="/wiki/Invasive_species" title="Invasive species">invasive species</a> in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, alongside the <a href="/wiki/Common_myna" title="Common myna">common myna</a> that invaded a few years earlier. Both populations seem to have started from individuals that escaped from a bird park in Tel Aviv.
</p>
