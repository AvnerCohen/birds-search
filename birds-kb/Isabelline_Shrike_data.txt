<p>The <b>isabelline shrike</b> or <b>Daurian shrike</b> (<i>Lanius isabellinus</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family (<a href="/wiki/Laniidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Laniidae">Laniidae</a>). It was previously considered <a href="/wiki/Conspecific" class="mw-redirect" title="Conspecific">conspecific</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Red-backed_shrike" title="Red-backed shrike">red-backed shrike</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red-tailed_shrike" title="Red-tailed shrike">red-tailed shrike</a>. It is found in an extensive area between the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> and north and central China southeast to the <a href="/wiki/Qaidam_Basin" title="Qaidam Basin">Qaidam Basin</a>. Overwinters in Africa and Arabia. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isabelline_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The genus name, <i><a href="/wiki/Lanius" title="Lanius">Lanius</a></i>, is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> word for "<a href="/wiki/Butcher" title="Butcher">butcher</a>", and some shrikes are also known as "butcher birds" because of their feeding habits. The common name is from the specific <i>isabellinus</i>, <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> for "greyish-yellow" probably named for <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella I of Castile</a>, said to have promised not to change her undergarments until Spain was freed from the Moors.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The common English name "shrike" is from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>scríc</i>, "shriek", referring to the shrill call.<sup id="cite_ref-OED_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OED-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isabelline_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> eats large insects, small birds, rodents and lizards. Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a larder. It breeds in open cultivated country, preferably with thorn bushes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Isabelline_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The plumage is <a href="/wiki/Isabelline_(colour)" title="Isabelline (colour)">isabelline</a>, the sandy colour which gives rise to its name. It has a red tail. Young birds can be distinguished from young red-backed shrikes by the much sparser vermiculations on the underparts.
</p>
