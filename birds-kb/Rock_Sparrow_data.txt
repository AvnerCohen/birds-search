<p>The <b>Rock Sparrow</b> (<i>Petronia petronia</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Sparrow" title="Sparrow">sparrow</a> breeds on barren rocky hills from the <a href="/wiki/Iberian_peninsula" title="Iberian peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Iberian peninsula</a> and western north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> across southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and through central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is largely resident in the west of its range, but Asian birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> to more southerly areas, or move down the mountains.</p>
<p>It is a rare vagrant north of its breeding range. There is just a single record from <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Cley_next_the_Sea" title="Cley next the Sea">Cley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a> on 14 June 1981.</p>
<p>This gregarious bird is also found in human settlements in suitable country. It nests in crevices in rocks or walls, laying 3–7 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>.</p>
<p>This species is a large stocky sparrow, 15–17 cm in length, with a strong whitish supercilium and weaker crown stripe. It has a patterned brown back and wings, streaked underparts, and a diagnostic, but hard-to-see, yellow throat spot.</p>
<p>The Rock Sparrow's food is mainly <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> with some <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>. This bird has a loud wheezy song.</p>
