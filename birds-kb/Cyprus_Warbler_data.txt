<p>The <b>Cyprus warbler</b> (<i>Curruca melanothorax</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> which <a href="/wiki/Endemic" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic">breeds only</a> on <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a short-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, and winters in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.
</p><p>Like most <i>Curruca</i> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, it has distinct male and female <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a>. The adult male is a small typical warbler with a grey back, black head, white malar streaks ("moustaches"), and, uniquely among typical warblers, underparts heavily streaked with black. The female is mainly grey above, with a greyer head, and whitish with only light spotting. The Cyprus warbler's song is fast and rattling, and is similar to that of the <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_warbler" title="Sardinian warbler">Sardinian warbler</a>.
</p><p>Together with <a href="/wiki/R%C3%BCppell%27s_warbler" title="Rüppell&#39;s warbler">Rüppell's warbler</a> it forms a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a> with dark throats, white malar streaks and light <a href="/wiki/Remiges" class="mw-redirect" title="Remiges">remiges</a> fringes. This in turn is related to the species of Mediterranean and <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> <i>Curruca</i> warblers that have a naked eye-ring, namely the <a href="/wiki/Subalpine_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Subalpine warbler">subalpine warbler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_warbler" title="Sardinian warbler">Sardinian warbler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Menetries%27_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Menetries&#39; warbler">Menetries' warbler</a>. Both groups have a white malar area, but this may not form a clear streak in the latter group; above the white, the heads of males are uniformly dark.(The Sylvia Monograph, A &amp; C Black, London; Jønsson &amp; Fjeldså 2006)
</p><p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of dry open country, often on hill slopes, with bushes for nesting. The nest is built in low shrub or gorse, and 3–5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid. Like most "<a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warblers</a>", it is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a>, but will also take berries.
</p>
