<p>The <b>Calandra Lark</b> (<i>Melanocorypha calandra</i>) breeds in warm <a href="/wiki/Temperate_countries" title="Temperate countries" class="mw-redirect">temperate countries</a> around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> and eastwards through <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> into northern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It is replaced further east by its relative, the <a href="/wiki/Bimaculated_Lark" title="Bimaculated Lark">Bimaculated Lark</a>.</p>
<p>It is mainly resident in the west of its range, but Russian populations of this <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> are more <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, moving further south in winter, as far as the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_peninsula" title="Arabian peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Arabian peninsula</a> and <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. It is a very rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>This is a bird of open cultivation and <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a>. Its nest is on the ground, with 4–5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> being laid. Food is seeds supplemented with <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> in the breeding season. It is gregarious outside the breeding season.</p>
<p>This is a large, robust <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a>, 17.5–20&#160;cm long. It is an undistinguished-looking species on the ground, mainly streaked greyish-brown above and white below, and with large black patches on the breast sides. It has a white supercilium.</p>
<p>In flight it shows short broad wings, which are dark below, and a short white-edged tail. The wing and tail patterns are distinctions from its more easterly relatives.</p>
<p>The song is like a slower version of that of the <a href="/wiki/Skylark" title="Skylark" class="mw-redirect">Skylark</a>, so musical to human ears that the Calandra Lark was formerly a popular <a href="/wiki/Aviculture" title="Aviculture">cagebird</a> in its range (Kikkawa 2003). It is mentioned in, for instance, the <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscan</a> <a href="/wiki/Proverb" title="Proverb">proverb</a> <i>"Canta come una calandra"</i>, he or she sings like a lark (Giusti 1853), and the <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Ballad" title="Ballad">ballad</a> <i>"Romance del prisionero"</i>, where its song is the only way the prisoner knows when day breaks (Applebaum 2004).</p>
