<p>The <b>Bonelli's Eagle</b> (<i>Aquila fasciata</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>. Like all <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagles</a>, it belongs to the family <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a>. It breeds in southern Europe, Africa both north and south of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert" class="mw-redirect">Sahara Desert</a> and across the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect">Indian Subcontinent</a> to <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. It is usually a resident breeder which lays 1-3 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a tree or crag nest.</p>
<p>The Bonelli's Eagle is a species of wooded, often hilly, country with some open areas. The African race prefers savannah, forest edges, cultivation, and scrub, provided there are some large trees; this is not a species of very open or densely forested habitats.</p>
<p>This is a small to medium - sized <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagle</a> at 55–65&#160;cm in length. The upperparts are dark brown, and the underside is white with dark streaks. The wings are relatively short and rounded. The long tail is grey on top and white below and has a single broad black terminal band. The feet and eyes are yellow.</p>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bonelli%27s_Eagle_(Aquila_fasciata)-_juvenile_in_Kawal_WS,_AP_W_IMG_2158.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Bonelli%27s_Eagle_%28Aquila_fasciata%29-_juvenile_in_Kawal_WS%2C_AP_W_IMG_2158.jpg/220px-Bonelli%27s_Eagle_%28Aquila_fasciata%29-_juvenile_in_Kawal_WS%2C_AP_W_IMG_2158.jpg" width="220" height="173" class="thumbimage" /></a>

<p>Immature birds have deep buff underparts and underwing coverts, and have fine barring on the tail without the terminal band.</p>
<p>The Bonelli's Eagle takes a wide range of live prey, all taken alive. It usually hunts from cover by a quick dash from inside a tree, but it will also catch prey by quartering hill slopes like other eagles, or make a stoop from a soaring position. Most prey is taken on the ground.</p>
<p>This eagle takes large prey items, usually <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a> or birds. Mammals up to the size of a <a href="/wiki/Hare" title="Hare">hare</a> are regularly taken, and birds up to <a href="/wiki/Guineafowl" title="Guineafowl">guineafowl</a> size. The Bonelli's Eagle is usually silent except in display and near the nest. Its fluted <i>klu-kluklu-kluee</i> call is less shrill than that of its near relatives.</p>
<p>Recent <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> research resulted in this species being moved to the genus <i>Aquila</i> from <i><a href="/wiki/Hieraaetus" title="Hieraaetus">Hieraaetus</a></i>.</p>

<p>The Bonelli's Eagle will foster orphaned chicks of same species in an empty nest but only if egg or chick loss has happened a few hours earlier so also they will foster chicks during the post-fledging dependence period and this conservation strategy may be applicable to other raptor species provide that <a href="/wiki/Siblicide" title="Siblicide">siblicide</a> is not common in the host species.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
