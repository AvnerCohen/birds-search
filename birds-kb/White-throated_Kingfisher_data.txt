<p>The <b>White-throated Kingfisher</b> (<i>Halcyon smyrnensis</i>) also known as the <b>White-breasted Kingfisher</b> or <b>Smyrna Kingfisher</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Tree_kingfisher" title="Tree kingfisher">tree kingfisher</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> widely distributed in <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Turkey, east through South Asia to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Kingfisher" title="Kingfisher">kingfisher</a> is a resident over much of its range, although some populations may make short distance movements. It can often be found well away from water where it feeds on a wide range of prey that includes small reptiles, amphibians, crabs, small rodents and even birds. During the breeding season they call loudly in the mornings from prominent perches including the tops of buildings in urban areas or on wires.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_Kingfisher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg/220px-White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg/330px-White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg/440px-White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:White_throated_Kingfisher_I2-Haryana_IMG_9005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Adult (<a href="/wiki/Haryana" title="Haryana">Haryana</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>)</div>

<p>This is a large kingfisher, 28&#160;cm in length. The adult has a bright blue back, wings and tail. Its head, shoulders, flanks and lower belly are chestnut, and the throat and breast are white. The large bill and legs are bright red. The flight of the White-throated Kingfisher is rapid and direct, the short rounded wings whirring. In flight, large white patches are visible on the blue and black wings. Sexes are similar, but juveniles are a duller version of the adult.</p>
<p>This species forms a superspecies with <i><a href="/wiki/Halcyon_cyanoventris" title="Halcyon cyanoventris" class="mw-redirect">Halcyon cyanoventris</a></i> and most major works recognize four geographic races. They vary clinally in size, the shades of blue on the mantle which is more greenish in <i>smyrnensis</i> and <i>fusca</i> and more blue or purplish in <i>saturatior</i>.</p>
<ul>
<li><i>smyrnensis</i> (Linnaeus, 1758) is found in Turkey, Northern and Central Israel,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Egypt, Iraq, Pakistan, Afghanistan, northwestern India, China, Taiwan and the Philippines, south of Thailand, West Malaysia, Singapore, Sumatra and Java.</li>
<li><i>fusca</i> (Boddaert, 1783) is found in India, Sri Lanka, South China, Taiwan, Sumatra and West Java. Population from Burma to the Greater Sundas were treated under the name <i>perpulchra</i> but are now lumped into <i>fusca</i>. South China and Taiwanese populations are sometimes treated as subspecies <i>fokiensis</i>.</li>
<li><i>saturatior</i> <a href="/wiki/Allan_Octavian_Hume" title="Allan Octavian Hume">Hume</a>, 1874 is found in the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Islands" title="Andaman Islands">Andaman Islands</a>.</li>
<li><i>gularis</i> (Kuhl, 1820) is found in the Philippines.</li>
</ul>

<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg/170px-White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg" width="170" height="226" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg/255px-White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg/340px-White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:White-Throated_Kingfisher_by_Nabarun.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A pair of <i>fusca</i></div>
</div>
</div>
<p><i>H. s. gularis</i> of the Philippines has only the neck and throat white. Race <i>fusca</i> is found in Peninsular India and Sri Lanka and is slightly smaller, bluer and with a darker brown underside than the nominate race found in northwestern India. Race <i>saturatior</i> is found in the <a href="/wiki/Andaman_Islands" title="Andaman Islands">Andaman Islands</a> and is larger with darker brown underparts. Race <i>perpulchra</i> (not always recognized) is found in northeastern India and is smaller than <i>fusca</i> with paler underparts.<sup id="cite_ref-rasmussen_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rasmussen-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Albinism has been noted on occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> of <i>White-throated</i> was introduced since the range is large and geographic adjectives would make the name too restrictive, while the older name of <i>White-breasted</i> would not describe the eastern race which has only the throat white.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/w/index.php?title=File:Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg&amp;page=1" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg/220px-Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg.png" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg/330px-Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/68/Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg/440px-Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg.png 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Halcyonsmyrnensis.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Geographic variation in plumage</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The call of this kingfisher is a chuckling <i>chake-ake-ake-ake-ake</i>. They are particularly noisy in the breeding season.</p>
<p>Local names include Baluchistan: <i>Aspi chidok</i>; Sindhi: <i>Dalel</i>; Hindi: <i>Kilkila</i>, <i>Kourilla</i>;Himachal Pradesh: <i>Neela machhrala</i>; Punjabi: <i>Wadda machhera</i>; Bengali: <i>Sandabuk machhranga</i>; Assamese: <i>Masroka</i>; Cachar: <i>Dao natu gophu</i>; Gujarati: <i>Kalkaliyo</i>, <i>Safedchati kalkaliyo</i>; Marathi: <i>Khandya</i>; Tamil: <i>Vichuli</i>; Telugu: <i>Lakmuka</i>, <i>Buchegadu</i>; Malayalam: <i>Ponman</i>; Kannada: <i>Rajamatsi</i>; Sinhalese: <i>Pilihuduwa</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>White-throated Kingfisher is a common species of a variety of habitats, mostly open country in the plains (but has been seen at 7500&#160;ft in the Himalayas<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup>) with trees, wires or other perches. The range of the species is expanding.</p>
<p>This kingfisher is widespread and populations are not threatened. Average density of 4.58 individuals per km<sup>2</sup>. has been noted in the <a href="/wiki/Sundarbans" title="Sundarbans">Sundarbans</a> mangroves.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_Kingfisher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Feeding and diet">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding_and_diet">Feeding and diet</span></h3>
<p>It perches conspicuously on wires or other exposed perches within its territory, and is a frequent sight in south Asia. This species mainly hunts large crustaceans,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> insects, earthworms,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup> rodents, snakes, fish and frogs.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Predation of small birds such as the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_White-eye" title="Oriental White-eye">Oriental White-eye</a>, chick of a <a href="/wiki/Red-wattled_Lapwing" title="Red-wattled Lapwing">Red-wattled Lapwing</a>, sparrows and munias have been reported.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-freak_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-freak-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> The young are fed mostly on invertebrates.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> In captivity, it has been noted that it rarely drinks water although bathing regularly.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_Kingfisher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>The White-throated Kingfisher begins breeding at the onset of the <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">Monsoons</a>. Males perch on prominent high posts in their territory an call in the early morning. The tail may be flicked now and in its courtship display the wings are stiffly flicked open for a second or two exposing the white wing mirrors. They also raise their bill high and display the white throat and front. The female in invitation makes a rapid and prolonged <i>kit-kit-kit...</i> call. The nest is a tunnel (50 cms long, but a nest with a 3 foot tunnel has been noted<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup>) in an earth bank. The nest building begins with both birds flying into a suitable mud wall until an indentation is made where they can find a perch hold. They subsequently perch and continue digging the nest with their bills. Nest tunnels in a haystack have also been recorded.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup> A single clutch of 4-7 round white <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> is typical. The eggs take 20–22 days to hatch while the chicks fledge in 19 days.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_Kingfisher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Movements">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Movements">Movements</span></h3>
<p>Birds have sometimes been seen attracted to lights at night, especially during the monsoon season, suggesting that they are partly migratory.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-13"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_Kingfisher&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mortality">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Mortality">Mortality</span></h3>
<p>With a powerful bill and rapid flight, these kingfishers have few predators when healthy and rare cases of predation by a <a href="/wiki/Black_Kite" title="Black Kite">Black Kite</a><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/Jungle_Crow" title="Jungle Crow">Jungle Crow</a> may be of sick or injured birds.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> An individual found dead with its beak embedded into the wood of a tree has been suggested as an accident during rapid pursuit of prey, possibly an <a href="/wiki/Oriental_White-eye" title="Oriental White-eye">Oriental White-eye</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-freak_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-freak-15"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup> A few parasites have been noted.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>25<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In the 1800s these birds were hunted for their bright feathers that were used to adorn hats.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span>[</span>26<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
