<p>The <b>Squacco Heron</b> (<i>Ardeola ralloides</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a>, 44–47 centimetres (17–19 in) long, of which the body is 20–23 centimetres (7.9–9.1 in), with 80–92 centimetres (31–36 in) wingspan.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It is of Old World origins, breeding in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Middle_East" title="Greater Middle East">Greater Middle East</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Squacco_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>The squacco heron is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is rare north of its breeding range. This is a stocky species with a short neck, short thick bill and buff-brown back. In summer, adults have long neck <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>. Its appearance is transformed in flight, when it looks very white due to the colour of the wings. The squacco heron's breeding <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" title="Habitat (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">habitat</a> is marshy wetlands in warm countries. The birds nest in small colonies, often with other wading birds, usually on platforms of sticks in trees or shrubs. 3-4 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid. They feed on fish, frogs and insects.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Squacco_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span></h2>
<p>The English <a href="/wiki/Common_name" title="Common name">common name</a> <i>Squacco</i> comes via <a href="/wiki/Francis_Willughby" title="Francis Willughby">Francis Willughby</a> (circa 1672) quoting a local Italian name <i>Sguacco</i>. The current spelling comes <a href="/wiki/John_Hill_(author)" title="John Hill (author)" class="mw-redirect">John Hill</a> in 1752.<sup id="cite_ref-ODBBN_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODBBN-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Scientific_name" title="Scientific name" class="mw-redirect">scientific name</a> comes from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>ardeola</i>, little heron, and <i>ralloides</i>, Latin <i>rallus</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Rallidae" title="Rallidae">rail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>-oides</i>, resembling.<sup id="cite_ref-DSBN_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSBN-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
