<p>The <b>squacco heron</b> (<i>Ardeola ralloides</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a>, 44–47&#160;cm (17–19&#160;in) long, of which the body is 20–23&#160;cm (7.9–9.1&#160;in), with 80–92&#160;cm (31–36&#160;in) wingspan.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is of Old World origins, breeding in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Middle_East" title="Greater Middle East">Greater Middle East</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Squacco_heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The squacco heron is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is rare north of its breeding range. The species has been recorded in <a href="/wiki/Fernando_de_Noronha" title="Fernando de Noronha">Fernando de Noronha</a> islands, and more rarely in mainland <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>, as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a>. This is a stocky species with a short neck, short thick bill and buff-brown back. In summer, adults have long neck <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>. Its appearance is transformed in flight, when it looks very white due to the colour of the wings.
</p><p>The squacco heron's breeding <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat (ecology)">habitat</a> is marshy wetlands in warm countries. The birds nest in small colonies, often with other wading birds, usually on platforms of sticks in trees or shrubs. Three to four <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid. They feed on fish, <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a> and insects.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Squacco_heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The English <a href="/wiki/Common_name" title="Common name">common name</a> <i>squacco</i> comes via <a href="/wiki/Francis_Willughby" title="Francis Willughby">Francis Willughby</a> (c. 1672) quoting a local Italian name <i>sguacco</i>. The current spelling comes from <a href="/wiki/John_Hill_(author)" class="mw-redirect" title="John Hill (author)">John Hill</a> in 1752.<sup id="cite_ref-ODBBN_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ODBBN-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Scientific_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Scientific name">scientific name</a> comes from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>ardeola</i>, a small heron (<i>ardea</i>), and <i>ralloides</i>, <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>rallus</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Rallidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Rallidae">rail</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a> <i>-oides</i>, "resembling".<sup id="cite_ref-job_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
