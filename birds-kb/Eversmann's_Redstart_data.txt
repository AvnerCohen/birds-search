<p><b>Eversmann's Redstart</b> or <b>Rufous-backed Redstart</b> (<i>Phoenicurus erythronotus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Phoenicurus" title="Phoenicurus">Phoenicurus</a></i>, a genus of <a href="/wiki/Redstart" title="Redstart">redstarts</a>. It was formerly classified in the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae but is now placed in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> family Muscicapidae. It was described by the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> biologist <a href="/wiki/Eduard_Friedrich_Eversmann" title="Eduard Friedrich Eversmann">Eduard Friedrich Eversmann</a> who is commemorated in the bird's English name.</p>
<p>It is one of the larger redstarts, 15 to 16&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 25.5 to 27&#160;cm. The <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> and legs are black. The male in breeding plumage has a black mask and grey crown and nape. The back and rump are rufous and the tail is also rufous apart from the darker central feathers. The wings are dark with white patches on the scapulars and primary-coverts. The underparts are mainly rufous with white on the belly and undertail-coverts.</p>
<p>Non-breeding and first-winter males males are similar but much duller and browner. Females are mostly grey-brown. They have a rufous tail with a dark centre, a pale eye-ring, two buff wingbars and buff edges to the <a href="/wiki/Flight_feather#Tertials" title="Flight feather">tertials</a>.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> is loud and lively. The birds also have a soft, croaking call and a whistling call. The tail is often flicked up and down.</p>
<p>It breeds in the mountains of <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Tien_Shan" title="Tien Shan" class="mw-redirect">Tien Shan</a> range to the <a href="/wiki/Tarbagatai_Mountains" title="Tarbagatai Mountains">Tarbagatay</a> and <a href="/wiki/Altay_Mountains" title="Altay Mountains" class="mw-redirect">Altay Mountains</a> and near <a href="/wiki/Lake_Baikal" title="Lake Baikal">Lake Baikal</a>. Some birds move downhill for the winter while others, especially in the north-east of the range, <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> longer distances. The wintering range extends from southern <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> through <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> to the western <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kashmir" title="Kashmir">Kashmir</a>. A few birds reach eastern <a href="/wiki/Arabia" title="Arabia" class="mw-redirect">Arabia</a>. It has occurred as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" title="Vagrancy in birds" class="mw-redirect">vagrant</a> in the <a href="/wiki/State_of_Israel" title="State of Israel" class="mw-redirect">State of Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.</p>
<p>It inhabits <a href="/wiki/Forest" title="Forest">forest</a> and <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a> during the breeding season, reaching 5400 metres above sea-level. In winter it is seen in more open and arid habitats. <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">Insects</a> form the bulk of its diet during the breeding season but <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> are important in winter.</p>
