<p><b>Hume's Leaf Warbler</b> or <b>Hume's Warbler</b> (<i>Phylloscopus humei</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> which breeds in the mountains of inner <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. This warbler is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and winters mainly in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</p>
<p>This bird is named after <a href="/wiki/Allan_Octavian_Hume" title="Allan Octavian Hume">Allan Octavian Hume</a>. Like most similar <a href="/wiki/Songbird" title="Songbird">songbirds</a>, it was formerly included in the "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a>" assemblage.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_Leaf_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>Hume's Leaf Warbler is one of the smallest "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warblers</a>". Like most other <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warblers</a>, it has greenish upperparts and off-white underparts. With its long <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, crown stripe and yellow-margined tertial <a href="/wiki/Remige" title="Remige" class="mw-redirect">remiges</a>, it is very similar to the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-browed_Warbler" title="Yellow-browed Warbler">Yellow-browed Warbler</a> (<i>P. inornatus</i>). However, it has only one prominent light wing bar, just a faint vestige of the second shorter wing bar, and overall duller colours. It also has a dark lower <a href="/wiki/Mandible" title="Mandible">mandible</a> and legs.</p>
<p>Its song is buzzing and high pitched. The best distinction from the Yellow-browed Warbler is the more disyllabic call. While the Eastern and Western Hume's Leaf Warblers already show noticeable differences in <a href="/wiki/MtDNA" title="MtDNA" class="mw-redirect">mtDNA</a> <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" title="DNA sequence" class="mw-redirect">sequence</a> and calls, their songs do not differ; they are reproductively isolated only by <a href="/wiki/Allopatry" title="Allopatry" class="mw-redirect">allopatry</a> and not usually considered separate species.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_Leaf_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology and evolution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology_and_evolution">Ecology and evolution</span></h2>
<p>This is a common <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of mountain woodlands at altitudes of up to 3,500m <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a>. It occurs from the <a href="/wiki/Hindu_Kush" title="Hindu Kush">Hindu Kush</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karakoram" title="Karakoram">Karakoram</a> east and north to the <a href="/wiki/Tien_Shan" title="Tien Shan" class="mw-redirect">Tien Shan</a> in <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Altay_Mountains" title="Altay Mountains" class="mw-redirect">Altay Mountains</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>. The completely <a href="/wiki/Allopatric" title="Allopatric" class="mw-redirect">allopatric</a> <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>mandellii</i> (<b>Mandell's Leaf Warbler</b> or <b>Eastern Hume's Warbler</b>) – sometimes separated as a full <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, in which case the <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" title="Nominate subspecies" class="mw-redirect">nominate subspecies</a> is called <b>Western Hume's Warbler</b> – occurs on the eastern <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau" title="Tibetan Plateau">Tibetan Plateau</a><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>. Both populations <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a> to winter in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and adjacent regions. The species has also been recorded in the Kutch region.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>Particularly on autumn migration, this tiny warbler is prone to vagrancy as far as western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, despite a 3,000&#160;km distance from its breeding grounds. It is a rare vagrant in late autumn and winter in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. Non-breeding adults may stray around a lot in summer, when Mandell's Leaf Warblers are fairly common summer visitors to <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" title="Subtropical" class="mw-redirect">subtropical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate" class="mw-redirect">temperate</a> <a href="/wiki/Montane_forest" title="Montane forest" class="mw-redirect">montane</a> <a href="/wiki/Humid" title="Humid" class="mw-redirect">humid</a> forests of <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a>, around 2,000-3,500 m <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a> and dominated by <a href="/wiki/Bhutan_Fir" title="Bhutan Fir" class="mw-redirect">Bhutan Fir</a> (<i>Abies densa</i>) or by <a href="/wiki/Himalayan_Hemlock" title="Himalayan Hemlock" class="mw-redirect">Himalayan Hemlock</a> (<i>Tsuga dumosa</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Rhododendron" title="Rhododendron">rhododendrons</a>, though the subspecies is not a regular breeder in that country<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup>.</p>
<p>This bird is not shy, although its arboreal lifestyle and <a href="/wiki/Crypsis" title="Crypsis">cryptic</a> colours make it difficult to observe. It is constantly in motion. Like most "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warblers</a>", this small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" title="Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect">insectivorous</a>. The nest is built on the ground.</p>
<p>A common species in most of its wide range, Hume's Leaf-warbler is not considered <a href="/wiki/Threatened" title="Threatened" class="mw-redirect">threatened</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It was recently split from the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-browed_Warbler" title="Yellow-browed Warbler">Yellow-browed Warbler</a> (<i>Phylloscopus inornatus</i>), based on differences in <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bioacoustics" title="Bioacoustics">bioacoustics</a>, and molecular characters. The Western Hume's Leaf Warbler's range overlaps with that of the Yellow-browed Warbler in the western <a href="/wiki/Sayan_Mountains" title="Sayan Mountains">Sayan Mountains</a>, but the species apparently do not <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridize</a>. The divergence between the two species has been tentatively estimated at roughly 2,5 <a href="/wiki/Million_years_ago" title="Million years ago" class="mw-redirect">million years ago</a> (Ma), and that between <i>P. h. humei</i> and <i>P. h. mandelli</i> at about 1 mya.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
