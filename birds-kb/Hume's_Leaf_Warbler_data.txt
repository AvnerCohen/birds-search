<p><b>Hume's leaf warbler</b> or <b>Hume's warbler</b> (<i>Phylloscopus humei</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> which breeds in the mountains of inner <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. This warbler is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and winters mainly in India.
</p><p>The English name and the specific <i>humei</i> bird commemorate <a href="/wiki/Allan_Octavian_Hume" title="Allan Octavian Hume">Allan Octavian Hume</a>, a British civil
servant and ornithologist based in India. The genus name <i>Phylloscopus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>phullon</i>, "leaf", and <i>skopos</i>, "seeker" (from <i>skopeo</i>, "to watch").<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Like most similar <a href="/wiki/Songbird" title="Songbird">songbirds</a>, it was formerly included in the "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a>" assemblage.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_leaf_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg/220px-Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="205" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg/330px-Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg/440px-Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg 2x" data-file-width="697" data-file-height="650" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7299.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>At <a href="/w/index.php?title=Biskeri_Thatch&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Biskeri Thatch (page does not exist)">Biskeri Thatch</a> (1,1000 ft <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a>) in <a href="/wiki/Kullu_district" title="Kullu district">Kullu district</a>, <a href="/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh" title="Himachal Pradesh">Himachal Pradesh</a> (<a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>Hume's leaf warbler is one of the smallest "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warblers</a>". Like most other <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warblers</a>, it has greenish upperparts and off-white underparts. With its long <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, crown stripe and yellow-margined tertial <a href="/wiki/Remige" class="mw-redirect" title="Remige">remiges</a>, it is very similar to the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-browed_warbler" title="Yellow-browed warbler">yellow-browed warbler</a> (<i>P. inornatus</i>). However, it has only one prominent light wing bar, just a faint vestige of the second shorter wing bar, and overall duller colours. It also has a dark lower <a href="/wiki/Mandible" title="Mandible">mandible</a> and legs.
</p><p>Its song is buzzing and high pitched.  The best distinction from the yellow-browed warbler is the more disyllabic call. While the eastern and western Hume's leaf warblers already show noticeable differences in <a href="/wiki/MtDNA" class="mw-redirect" title="MtDNA">mtDNA</a> <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" class="mw-redirect" title="DNA sequence">sequence</a> and calls, their songs do not differ; they are reproductively isolated only by <a href="/wiki/Allopatry" class="mw-redirect" title="Allopatry">allopatry</a> and not usually considered separate species.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology_and_evolution">Ecology and evolution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_leaf_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology and evolution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a common <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of mountain woodlands at altitudes of up to 3,500m <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a>. It occurs from the <a href="/wiki/Hindu_Kush" title="Hindu Kush">Hindu Kush</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karakoram" title="Karakoram">Karakoram</a> east and north to the <a href="/wiki/Tien_Shan" class="mw-redirect" title="Tien Shan">Tien Shan</a> in China and the <a href="/wiki/Altay_Mountains" class="mw-redirect" title="Altay Mountains">Altay Mountains</a> in Mongolia. The completely <a href="/wiki/Allopatric" class="mw-redirect" title="Allopatric">allopatric</a> <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>mandellii</i> (<b>Mandell's leaf warbler</b> or <b>eastern Hume's warbler</b>) &#8211; sometimes separated as a full <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, in which case the <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate subspecies</a> is called <b>western Hume's warbler</b> &#8211; occurs on the eastern <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau" title="Tibetan Plateau">Tibetan Plateau</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Both populations <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> over the <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a> to winter in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and adjacent regions. The species has also been recorded in the Kutch region.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg/220px-Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg/330px-Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg/440px-Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="572" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hume%27s_Warbler_I_IMG_7271.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Foraging in the <a href="/wiki/Understory" title="Understory">understory</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kullu_district" title="Kullu district">Kullu district</a>, <a href="/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh" title="Himachal Pradesh">Himachal Pradesh</a> (India)</div></div></div>
<p>Particularly on autumn migration, this tiny warbler is prone to vagrancy as far as western Europe, despite a 3,000&#160;km distance from its breeding grounds. It is a rare vagrant in late autumn and winter in Great Britain. Non-breeding adults may stray around a lot in summer, when Mandell's leaf warblers are fairly common summer visitors to <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> and <a href="/wiki/Temperate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate">temperate</a> <a href="/wiki/Montane_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Montane forest">montane</a> <a href="/wiki/Humid" class="mw-redirect" title="Humid">humid</a> forests of <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a>, around 2,000-3,500 m <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a> and dominated by <a href="/wiki/Bhutan_Fir" class="mw-redirect" title="Bhutan Fir">Bhutan Fir</a> (<i>Abies densa</i>) or by <a href="/wiki/Himalayan_Hemlock" class="mw-redirect" title="Himalayan Hemlock">Himalayan Hemlock</a> (<i>Tsuga dumosa</i>) and <a href="/wiki/Rhododendron" title="Rhododendron">rhododendrons</a>, though the subspecies is not a regular breeder in that country.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This bird is not shy, although its arboreal lifestyle and <a href="/wiki/Crypsis" title="Crypsis">cryptic</a> colours make it difficult to observe. It is constantly in motion. Like most "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warblers</a>", this small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a>. The nest is built on the ground.
</p><p>A common species in most of its wide range, Hume's leaf-warbler is not considered <a href="/wiki/Threatened" class="mw-redirect" title="Threatened">threatened</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was recently split from the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-browed_warbler" title="Yellow-browed warbler">yellow-browed warbler</a> (<i>Phylloscopus inornatus</i>), based on differences in <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphology</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bioacoustics" title="Bioacoustics">bioacoustics</a>, and molecular characters. The Western Hume's leaf warbler's range overlaps with that of the yellow-browed warbler in the western <a href="/wiki/Sayan_Mountains" title="Sayan Mountains">Sayan Mountains</a>, but the species apparently do not <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridize</a>. The divergence between the two species has been tentatively estimated at roughly 2.5 <a href="/wiki/Million_years_ago" class="mw-redirect" title="Million years ago">million years ago</a> (mya), and that between <i>P. h. humei</i> and <i>P. h. mandelli</i> at about 1 mya.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
