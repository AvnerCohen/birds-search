<p>The <b>African skimmer</b> (<i>Rynchops flavirostris</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Near-threatened" class="mw-redirect" title="Near-threatened">near-threatened</a> species of bird belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Skimmer_(bird)" class="mw-redirect" title="Skimmer (bird)">skimmer</a> genus <i>Rynchops</i> in the family Laridae. It is found along rivers, lakes and lagoons in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_skimmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:African_skimmers.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/African_skimmers.jpg/250px-African_skimmers.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/African_skimmers.jpg/375px-African_skimmers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/African_skimmers.jpg/500px-African_skimmers.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:African_skimmers.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>African skimmers and <a href="/wiki/White-winged_tern" title="White-winged tern">white-winged terns</a> at Kazinga Channel, Uganda</div></div></div>
<p>African skimmers have long wings, with a black back, hindneck, and crown. The forehead and rest of the body is white, with a bright, long, orange beak that ends with a yellow tip (black tip when immature). Their short forked tail is white, and their legs are bright red. The average size is about 38&#160;cm (15&#160;in) long. Their voice is a sharp "kip-kip". Their bill structure is unique; the lower mandible is much longer than the upper mandible, and is flattened sideways like scissor blades.<sup id="cite_ref-B&amp;D_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-B&amp;D-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_skimmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The African skimmer is found from <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/Congo_River" title="Congo River">Congo River</a> and southern <a href="/wiki/Nile_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Nile Valley">Nile Valley</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Zambezi_Valley" class="mw-redirect" title="Zambezi Valley">Zambezi Valley</a>, and then to <a href="/wiki/KwaZulu-Natal_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="KwaZulu-Natal Province">KwaZulu-Natal Province</a> (South Africa) and <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">Angola</a>. They live at large tropical rivers with sandbanks for nesting and roosting, lake shores, and coastal lagoons. The African skimmer is generally uncommon and the total populations is estimated at 15,000-25,000 individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_skimmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:African_Skimmer_(Rynchops_flavirostris)_(24310826466).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/African_Skimmer_%28Rynchops_flavirostris%29_%2824310826466%29.jpg/250px-African_Skimmer_%28Rynchops_flavirostris%29_%2824310826466%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/African_Skimmer_%28Rynchops_flavirostris%29_%2824310826466%29.jpg/375px-African_Skimmer_%28Rynchops_flavirostris%29_%2824310826466%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/African_Skimmer_%28Rynchops_flavirostris%29_%2824310826466%29.jpg/500px-African_Skimmer_%28Rynchops_flavirostris%29_%2824310826466%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2596" data-file-height="2077" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:African_Skimmer_(Rynchops_flavirostris)_(24310826466).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>African skimmer feeding in <a href="/wiki/Chobe_National_Park" title="Chobe National Park">Chobe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Botswana" title="Botswana">Botswana</a></div></div></div>
<p>African skimmers fly in lines over calm waters, and dip their lower <a href="/wiki/Mandible" title="Mandible">mandibles</a> in the water to feed.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When the mandible touches a fish, the skimmer snaps its mouth shut. They feed mostly at dawn and dusk and have good night vision.  The following fish have been recorded as prey for African skimmers: <i><a href="/wiki/Micralestes" title="Micralestes">Micralestes</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Tilapia" title="Tilapia">Tilapia</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Barbus" title="Barbus">Barbus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Marcusenius" title="Marcusenius">Marcusenius </a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Hepsetus" title="Hepsetus">Hepsetus</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Aplocheilichthys" class="mw-redirect" title="Aplocheilichthys">Aplocheilichthys</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Petrocephalus" title="Petrocephalus">Petrocephalus</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_skimmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>African skimmers nest in loose colonies on large sandbanks. The colonies typically consist of less than 50 pairs and each pair lays 2–3 (rarely 4) eggs in a scrape in the sand.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Sometimes African skimmer colonies are mixed with those of other sand bank nesting birds such as <a href="/wiki/Collared_pratincole" title="Collared pratincole">collared pratincole</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pied_avocet" title="Pied avocet">pied avocet</a> and <a href="/wiki/White-fronted_plover" title="White-fronted plover">white-fronted plover</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Movements">Movements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=African_skimmer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Movements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>African skimmers are partial intra-African migrants, they arrive in southern Africa when the water level of rivers starts falling at the beginning of the dry season, i.e. April–June, returning northwards after breeding when rivers start rising again at the start of the rainy season in November–January.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
