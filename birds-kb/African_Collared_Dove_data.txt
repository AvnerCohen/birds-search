<p>The <b>African collared dove</b> (<i>Streptopelia roseogrisea</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Dove" class="mw-redirect" title="Dove">dove</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a>, northern parts of the <a href="/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">Horn of Africa</a> and southwestern <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>. Although it lives in arid lands, it is found around water sources.
</p><p>This bird is typically around 26&#160;cm in length. Its upper body, from shoulders to tail, is a pale grayish brown, though the wing edge has a bluish tinge. Flight <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a> are darker, and nearly black. Head, neck and breast are pinkish shading to white on the chin and belly.  There is little <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexual dimorphism</a>.
</p><p>The African collared dove is the species thought to be the wild ancestor of the domestic <a href="/wiki/Barbary_dove" title="Barbary dove">Barbary dove</a>, though some suggest the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_collared_dove" title="Eurasian collared dove">Eurasian collared dove</a> (<i>Streptopelia decaocto</i>) may also have been involved. The African collared dove is able to <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridise</a> with the Barbary dove, and it is thought that the increase in the range of colours of Barbary doves available that occurred in the later 20th century was the result of the importation of African collared doves into the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> for interbreeding.
</p><p>It is reported to have been introduced into <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, but it is more likely that the birds there are descended from domestic Barbary doves.
</p>
