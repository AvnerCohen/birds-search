<p>The <b>cinereous bunting</b> (<i>Emberiza cineracea</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family Emberizidae, a <i><a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a></i> family now separated by most modern authors from the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finches</a>, Fringillidae. This species was first described by <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Edwin_Strickland" title="Hugh Edwin Strickland">Hugh Edwin Strickland</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinereous_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds in southern Turkey and southern Iran, and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">winters</a> around the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> in north-eastern Africa and Yemen. A few isolated populations just about maintain a foothold within European borders, on islands in the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Sea" title="Aegean Sea">Aegean Sea</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinereous_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cinereous bunting breeds on dry stony mountain slopes.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinereous_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The cinereous bunting is a large (16–17&#160;cm), slim bunting with a long, white-cornered tail. The term <i><a href="/wiki/Cinereous" title="Cinereous">cinereous</a></i> describes its colouration.  It is less streaked than many buntings and has a thick pale bill. It has a greyish back with only subdued dark markings, and a browner tint to the wings.
</p><p>The adult male's head is dull yellow, with a brighter moustachial line and throat. In the nominate race of south-west Turkey, the rest of the underparts are grey, but the eastern form  <i>E. c. semenowi</i> has yellow underparts.
</p><p>Females are brownish grey above with a whitish throat and yellow only in the moustachial stripe. Young birds have a plain pale belly and streaking on the breast.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Foraging_and_breeding">Foraging and breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinereous_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Foraging and breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Like other buntings, the cinereous bunting feeds principally on seeds. It takes insects especially when feeding its young. Its normal clutch is three eggs.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Song">Song</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinereous_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Song">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The call is a harsh <i>tschrip</i>, and the song is a hoarse <i>zru- zru-zru-zru</i>.
</p>
