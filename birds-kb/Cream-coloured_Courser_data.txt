<p>The <b>cream-colored courser</b> (<i>Cursorius cursor</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pratincole" title="Pratincole">pratincole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Courser" title="Courser">courser</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Glareolidae" title="Glareolidae">Glareolidae</a>. Both parts of the scientific name derive from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>cursor</i>, "runner", from <i>currere</i>, "to run"<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> which describes their usual habit as they hunt their <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insect</a> prey on the ground in dry open semi-desert regions of <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">Western Asia</a> and northern Africa.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cream-colored_courser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These coursers are found in <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="Southwest Asia">Southwest Asia</a>. Their two eggs are laid in a ground scrape. The breeding season extends from February to September,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but they may breed also in autumn and winter when local conditions (especially rainfall) are favourable.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> They are partially <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, with northern and northwestern birds wintering in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> and across the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. Some birds also breed in the southern desert regions in northwestern India and Pakistan.<sup id="cite_ref-Rahmani1989_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rahmani1989-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They are rare north of the breeding range, but this species has occurred as far away as <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cream-colored_courser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> have long legs and long wings. They have slightly downcurved bills. The body plumage is sandy in colour, fading to whitish on the lower belly. The upperwing primary feathers and the underwings are black. The crown and nape are grey, and there is a black eyestripe and white supercilium.
</p><p>In flight, this species resembles a pratincole with its relaxed wingbeats, pointed wings and dark underwings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cream-colored_courser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are three <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the cream-colored courser:<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomic_note">Taxonomic note</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cream-colored_courser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taxonomic note">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hayman's <i>Shorebirds</i> treats the east African form <i>littoralis</i> as a race of the <a href="/wiki/Somali_courser" title="Somali courser">Somali courser</a> rather than of cream-colored.<sup id="cite_ref-Hayman_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hayman-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Some authorities in turn consider the <a href="/wiki/Somali_courser" title="Somali courser">Somali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burchell%27s_courser" title="Burchell&#39;s courser">Burchell's</a> and cream-colored coursers to be conspecific.
</p>
