<p>The <b>Cream-colored Courser</b> (<i>Cursorius cursor</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pratincole" title="Pratincole">pratincole</a> and <a href="/wiki/Courser" title="Courser">courser</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Glareolidae" title="Glareolidae">Glareolidae</a>.</p>
<p>Although classed as <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a>, these are <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> of dry open country, preferably semi-desert, where they typically hunt their <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insect</a> prey by running on the ground.</p>
<p>These coursers are found in <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and southwest <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. Their two eggs are laid in a ground scrape. They are partially <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, with northern and northwestern birds wintering in <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> and across the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. Some birds also breed in the southern desert regions in northwestern India and Pakistan.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>They are rare north of the breeding range, but this species has occurred as far away as <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.</p>
<p>These <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> have long legs and long wings. They have slightly downcurved bills. The body plumage is sandy in colour, fading to whitish on the lower belly. The upperwing primary feathers and the underwings are black. The crown and nape are grey, and there is a black eyestripe and white supercilium.</p>
<p>In flight this species resembles a pratincole, with its relaxed wingbeats, pointed wings and dark underwings.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Cream-coloured_Courser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Reference and taxonomic note">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reference_and_taxonomic_note">Reference and taxonomic note</span></h2>
<p><i>Shorebirds</i> by Hayman, Marchant and Prater <a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/0709920342" class="internal mw-magiclink-isbn">ISBN 0-7099-2034-2</a></p>
<p>This standard text treats the east African form <i>littoralis</i> as a race of the <a href="/wiki/Somali_Courser" title="Somali Courser">Somali Courser</a> rather than of Cream-colored. Some authorities in turn consider the <a href="/wiki/Somali_Courser" title="Somali Courser">Somali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burchell%27s_Courser" title="Burchell's Courser">Burchell's</a> and Cream-colored coursers to be conspecific.</p>
