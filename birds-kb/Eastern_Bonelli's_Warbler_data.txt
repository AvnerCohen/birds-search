<p>The <b>Eastern Bonelli's warbler</b> (sometimes known as <b>Balkan warbler</b>) is a "<a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warbler</a>" in the <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> genus <i>Phylloscopus</i>. It was formerly regarded as the eastern subspecies of a wider "Bonelli's warbler" species, but as a result of modern taxonomic developments, this species is now usually considered to be two species:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Western_Bonelli%27s_warbler" title="Western Bonelli&#39;s warbler">Western Bonelli's warbler</a>, <i>Phylloscopus bonelli</i>, which breeds in southwest <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a></li>
<li>Eastern Bonelli's warbler, <i>Phylloscopus orientalis</i>, which breeds in southeast <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia_Minor" class="mw-redirect" title="Asia Minor">Asia Minor</a></li></ul>
<p>The breeding ranges of the two species do not overlap; while their appearance and songs are very similar, the calls are completely different (see below). They also show marked difference in <a href="/wiki/MtDNA" class="mw-redirect" title="MtDNA">mtDNA</a> <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" class="mw-redirect" title="DNA sequence">sequence</a> <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The species is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is a rare vagrant in Northern Europe.
</p><p>Eastern Bonelli's warbler is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>, found in forest and woodland. 4–6 eggs are laid in a nest on the ground.  Like most warblers, eastern Bonelli's is insectivorous.
</p><p>It is a small warbler. The adult has a plain grey-green back, green-toned rump and wings and whitish underparts. The bill is small and pointed and the legs brown. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers.
</p><p>The eastern Bonelli's warbler lacks the browner tinge to the upperparts that western Bonelli's warbler has; it sometimes has a greenish tinge instead. The song is a fast monotone trill, only slightly different from western Bonelli's, and also some similarity to <a href="/wiki/Wood_warbler" title="Wood warbler">wood warbler</a>. The call of the eastern Bonelli's warbler is a hard <i>chup</i>, reminiscent of a <a href="/wiki/Crossbill" title="Crossbill">crossbill</a> or a <a href="/wiki/House_sparrow" title="House sparrow">house sparrow</a>, and completely different from the disyllabic <i>hu-it</i> of western.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The genus name <i>Phylloscopus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>phullon</i>, "leaf", and <i>skopos</i>, "seeker" (from <i>skopeo</i>, "to watch"). The specific <i>orientalis</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "eastern".<sup id="cite_ref-job_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The English name for this bird commemorates the Italian ornithologist <a href="/wiki/Franco_Andrea_Bonelli" title="Franco Andrea Bonelli">Franco Andrea Bonelli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
