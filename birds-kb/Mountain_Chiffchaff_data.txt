<p>The <b>mountain chiffchaff</b> or <b>eastern chiffchaff</b> (<i>Phylloscopus sindianus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a> (<i>P. s. lorenzii</i>) and Himalayas (<i>P. s. sindianus</i>), and is an altitudinal migrant, moving to lower levels in winter. The nominate subspecies is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Siberian_chiffchaff" title="Siberian chiffchaff">Siberian chiffchaff</a>, but with a finer darker bill, browner upperparts and buff flanks; its song is almost identical to the <a href="/wiki/Common_chiffchaff" title="Common chiffchaff">common chiffchaff</a>, but the call is a weak <i>psew</i>. <i>P. s. lorenzii</i> is warmer and darker brown than the nominate race; it is sympatric with common chiffchaff in a small area in the Western Caucasus, but interbreeding occurs rarely, if ever. The mountain chiffchaff differs from <i>tristis</i> in vocalisations, external morphology and <a href="/wiki/MtDNA" class="mw-redirect" title="MtDNA">mtDNA</a> sequences. Its two subspecies appear to be distinct vocally, and also show some difference in mtDNA sequences.
