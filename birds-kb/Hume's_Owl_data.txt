<p>The <b>Humeâ€™s Owl</b> or <b>Humeâ€™s Tawny Owl</b> (<i>Strix butleri</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>. As its alternative name implies, it is closely related to the more widespread <a href="/wiki/Tawny_Owl" title="Tawny Owl">Tawny Owl</a>.</p>
<p>This species is a part of the larger grouping of owls known as <a href="/wiki/Typical_owl" title="Typical owl" class="mw-redirect">typical owls</a>, Strigidae, which contains most species of owl. The other grouping is the <a href="/wiki/Barn_owl" title="Barn owl" class="mw-redirect">barn owls</a>, Tytonidae.</p>
<p>Humeâ€™s Owl breeds in <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, northeast <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_peninsula" title="Arabian peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Arabian peninsula</a>. Its <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" title="Habitat (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">habitat</a> is palm groves, desert, semi-desert and rocky ravines. It nests in crevices and holes in <a href="/wiki/Cliff" title="Cliff">cliffs</a>. Its diet consists of <a href="/wiki/Vole" title="Vole">voles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mouse" title="Mouse">mice</a> and large <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>.</p>
<p>This is a medium-sized earless owl, smaller than Tawny Owl at 29â€“33&#160;cm in length. It is largely <a href="/wiki/Nocturnal" title="Nocturnal" class="mw-redirect">nocturnal</a> and sedentary. Its stocky body and round head recall a small Tawny Owl, but it is paler, less streaked, particularly on the underparts, and has yellow eyes.</p>
<p>The call of the Humeâ€™s Owl is a <i>hoooo-ho-ho-ho-ho</i>, described as similar in rhythm to <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Collared_Dove" title="Eurasian Collared Dove">Eurasian Collared Dove</a>. The female version is deeper and less distinct than the maleâ€™s.</p>
<p>The scientific specific name <i>butleri</i> refers to Colonel <a href="/wiki/Edward_Arthur_Butler" title="Edward Arthur Butler">Edward Arthur Butler</a>, English <a href="/wiki/Ornithology" title="Ornithology">ornithologist</a> who sent specimens of the species to <a href="/wiki/Allan_Octavian_Hume" title="Allan Octavian Hume">Allan Octavian Hume</a>.</p>
