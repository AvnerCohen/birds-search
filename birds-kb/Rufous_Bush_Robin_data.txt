<p>The <b>Rufous-tailed Scrub Robin</b> (<i>Cercotrichas galactotes</i>), also called the <b>Rufous Scrub Robin</b> or <b>Rufous Bush Chat</b>, is a small member of the Muscicapidae family. It breeds around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> and east to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It also breeds south of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Sahel" title="Sahel">Sahel</a> region east to <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>; these birds are sometimes considered to be a separate species, <b>African Scrub Robin</b> (<i>C. minor</i>). It is partially <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. This is a very rare visitor to northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>This is a bird of dry open country with bushes and shrubs. It builds its nest in a bush; three to five eggs is a typical clutch.</p>

<p>The Rufous-tailed Bush Robin is larger than the <a href="/wiki/European_Robin" title="European Robin">European Robin</a>. It has brown upper parts, whitish under parts, and a prominent whitish eye-stripe. The western race has a paler, warmer brown back than birds in southeast Europe and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.</p>
<p>The long rufous tail is frequently fanned, showing the black and white tips of the tail feathers. All plumages are similar.</p>
<p>These birds feed on insects mostly caught on the ground. Their clear thrush-like <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> has a sad tone.</p>
