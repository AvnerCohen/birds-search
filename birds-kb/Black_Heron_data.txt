<p>The <b>Black Heron</b> (<i><b>Egretta ardesiaca</b></i>) also known as the <b>Black Egret</b>, is an <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">African</a> <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a>. It is a medium-sized (42.5–66&#160;cm in height), black-plumaged heron with yellow legs and feet. It is found south of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert" class="mw-redirect">Sahara Desert</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>, and prefers shallow open waters, such as the edges of freshwater <a href="/wiki/Lakes" title="Lakes" class="mw-redirect">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ponds" title="Ponds" class="mw-redirect">ponds</a>. It may also be found in <a href="/wiki/Marshes" title="Marshes" class="mw-redirect">marshes</a>, <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> edges, <a href="/wiki/Rice_fields" title="Rice fields" class="mw-redirect">rice fields</a>, and seasonally flooded <a href="/wiki/Grasslands" title="Grasslands" class="mw-redirect">grasslands</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Coastal" title="Coastal" class="mw-redirect">coastal</a> areas, it may be found feeding along tidal rivers and creeks, <a href="/wiki/Alkalinity" title="Alkalinity">alkaline</a> lakes, and <a href="/wiki/Tidal_flats" title="Tidal flats" class="mw-redirect">tidal flats</a>. Its breeding range is between <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> and to the south. It is found mainly on the eastern half of the continent.</p>
<p>The Black Heron has an interesting hunting method called <b>canopy feeding</b>—it uses its wings like an umbrella, and uses the shade it creates to attract <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>. This technique was well documented on episode 5 of the BBC's <a href="/wiki/The_Life_of_Birds" title="The Life of Birds">The Life of Birds</a>. Some Black Herons feed solitarily, while others feed in groups of up to 50 individuals, 200 being the highest number reported. The Black Heron feeds by day but especially prefers the time around sunset. It roosts communally at night, and coastal flocks roost at <a href="/wiki/High_tide" title="High tide" class="mw-redirect">high tide</a>. The primary food of the Black Heron is small fish, but it will also eat aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insects" title="Insects" class="mw-redirect">insects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crustaceans" title="Crustaceans" class="mw-redirect">crustaceans</a> and amphibians.</p>
<p>The nest of the Black Heron is constructed of twigs placed over water in trees, bushes, and <a href="/wiki/Phragmites" title="Phragmites">reed</a> beds, forming a solid structure. The heron nests at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Rainy_season" title="Rainy season" class="mw-redirect">rainy season</a>, in single or mixed-species colonies that may number in the hundreds. The <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are dark blue and the <a href="/wiki/Clutch_(eggs)" title="Clutch (eggs)">clutch</a> is two to four eggs.</p>
