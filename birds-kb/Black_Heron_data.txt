<p>The <b>black heron</b> (<i>Egretta ardesiaca</i>) also known as the <b>black egret</b>,  is an African <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a>. It is well known for its habit of using its wings to form a canopy when fishing.
</p>

<p>The black heron is a medium-sized (42.5–66&#160;cm in height), black-plumaged heron with black bill, lores, legs and yellow feet. In breeding plumage it grows long plumes  on the crown and nape.<sup id="cite_ref-Hancock_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hancock-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_Habitat">Distribution and Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The black heron occurs patchily through <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> to <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>, but is found mainly on the eastern half of the continent and in <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hancock_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hancock-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It has also been observed in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a><sup id="cite_ref-Hellenic_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hellenic-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and Italy.
</p><p>It prefers shallow open waters, such as the edges of freshwater <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ponds" class="mw-redirect" title="Ponds">ponds</a>. It may also be found in <a href="/wiki/Marshes" class="mw-redirect" title="Marshes">marshes</a>, <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">river</a> edges, <a href="/wiki/Rice_fields" class="mw-redirect" title="Rice fields">rice fields</a>, and seasonally flooded <a href="/wiki/Grasslands" class="mw-redirect" title="Grasslands">grasslands</a>. In <a href="/wiki/Coastal" class="mw-redirect" title="Coastal">coastal</a> areas, it may be found feeding along tidal rivers and creeks, in <a href="/wiki/Soda_lake" title="Soda lake">alkaline lakes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tidal_flats" class="mw-redirect" title="Tidal flats">tidal flats</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Black_heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The black heron uses a hunting method called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Canopy_feeding&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Canopy feeding (page does not exist)">canopy feeding</a>&#8212;it uses its wings like an umbrella, creating shade that attracts <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>. This technique was well documented on episode 5 of the BBC's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Life_of_Birds" title="The Life of Birds">The Life of Birds</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LoB_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LoB-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Some have been observed feeding in solitary, while others feed in groups of up to 50 individuals, 200 being the highest number reported. The black heron feeds by day but especially prefers the time around sunset. It roosts communally at night, and coastal flocks roost at <a href="/wiki/High_tide" class="mw-redirect" title="High tide">high tide</a>. The primary food of the black heron is small fish, but it will also eat aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insects" class="mw-redirect" title="Insects">insects</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Crustaceans" class="mw-redirect" title="Crustaceans">crustaceans</a> and amphibians.<sup id="cite_ref-Hancock_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hancock-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The nest of the black heron is constructed of twigs placed over water in trees, bushes, and <a href="/wiki/Reed_bed" title="Reed bed">reed beds</a>, forming a solid structure. The heron nests at the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Rainy_season" class="mw-redirect" title="Rainy season">rainy season</a>, in single or mixed-species colonies that may number in the hundreds. The <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are dark blue and the <a href="/wiki/Clutch_(eggs)" title="Clutch (eggs)">clutch</a> is two to four eggs.<sup id="cite_ref-Hancock_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hancock-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
