<p><b>Blyth's pipit</b> (<i>Anthus godlewskii</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> which breeds in <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a> and neighbouring areas of China, Tibet and India . It is a long distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> moving to open lowlands in Southern Asia. It is a very rare vagrant to western Europe.
</p><p>This is a large <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipit</a>, but is an undistinguished looking species on the ground, mainly brown above and pale below. It is very similar to <a href="/wiki/Richard%27s_pipit" title="Richard&#39;s pipit">Richard's pipit</a>, but is slightly smaller, shorter legs and a shorter dark bill. Its flight is strong and direct, and it gives a characteristic "pshee" call, higher pitched than Richard's.
</p><p>In south Asia, in winter some care must be taken to distinguish this from other large pipits which winter or are resident in the area, including Richard's. This species is insectivorous.
</p><p>This bird was named after the English zoologist <a href="/wiki/Edward_Blyth" title="Edward Blyth">Edward Blyth</a>. The genus name <i>Anthus</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> name for a small bird of grasslands. The specific <i>godlewskii</i> commemorates Polish nobleman and field naturalist <a href="/wiki/Victor_Godlewski" title="Victor Godlewski">Victor Godlewski</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
