<p>The <b>Blyth's Pipit</b> (<i>Anthus godlewskii</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> which breeds in <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a> and neighbouring areas. It is a long distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> moving to open lowlands in southern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is a very rare vagrant to western Europe.</p>
<p>This is a large <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipit</a>, but is an undistinguished looking species on the ground, mainly brown above and pale below. It is very similar to <a href="/wiki/Richard%27s_Pipit" title="Richard's Pipit">Richard's Pipit</a>, but is slightly smaller, shorter legs and a shorter dark bill. Its flight is strong and direct, and it gives a characteristic "pshee" call, higher pitched than Richard's.</p>
<p>In south Asia, in winter some care must be taken to distinguish this from other large pipits which winter or are resident in the area, including Richard's. This species is insectivorous.</p>
<p>This bird was named after the English zoologist <a href="/wiki/Edward_Blyth" title="Edward Blyth">Edward Blyth</a>.</p>
