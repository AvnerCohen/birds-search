<p>The <b>Common Rosefinch</b> (<i>Carpodacus erythrinus</i>) is the most widespread and common <a href="/wiki/Rosefinch" title="Rosefinch">rosefinch</a> of Asia and Europe.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_Rosefinch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Common_Rosefinch_I_IMG_3937-edited.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/Common_Rosefinch_I_IMG_3937-edited.jpg/200px-Common_Rosefinch_I_IMG_3937-edited.jpg" width="200" height="201" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Common_Rosefinch_I_IMG_3937-edited.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Female at around 8,000 feet (2,400&#160;m) in <a href="/wiki/Kullu" title="Kullu">Kullu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Himachal_Pradesh" title="Himachal Pradesh">Himachal Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a></div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Common Rosefinch is larger than a sparrow. It has a stout and conical bill. The mature male has brilliant rosy-carmine head, breast and rump; heavy bill; dark brown wings with two indistinct bars, and a white belly. Females and young males are dull-colored with yellowish-brown above, brighter on the rump and greyer on head; buff below.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_Rosefinch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>It has spread westward through <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> in recent decades, even breeding in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> once. Common Rosefinches breed from the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> valley, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Sea" title="Bering Sea">Bering Sea</a>; the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a>, northern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, the western <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">Himalayas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tibet" title="Tibet">Tibet</a> and <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>; to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> between latitudes 25° and 68°. In winter they are found from southern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> to south-east China <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burma" title="Burma">Burma</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indochina" title="Indochina">Indochina</a>.</p>
<p>They are found in summer in thickets, woodland and forest edges near rivers and in winter in gardens and orchards, wetlands and locally in dry oak woods.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_Rosefinch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>The nest is placed low in a bush. The eggs are dark blue with coarse dark brown spots, and a typical clutch contains five eggs.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_Rosefinch&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>Because this species and the <a href="/wiki/Scarlet_Finch" title="Scarlet Finch">Scarlet Finch</a> form a phylogenetic group, it has been removed from the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Carpodacus" title="Carpodacus" class="mw-redirect">Carpodacus</a></i> in some recent taxonomies.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
