<p>The <b>Sinai rosefinch</b> (<i>Carpodacus synoicus</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Fringillidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Fringillidae">Fringillidae</a>. It is found in the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Negev" title="Negev">Negev</a> region of the Middle East, within the borders of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>. Its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> is hot <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">deserts</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Pale_rosefinch" title="Pale rosefinch">pale rosefinch</a> is sometimes considered a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>. It is the national bird of <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>.
</p>
