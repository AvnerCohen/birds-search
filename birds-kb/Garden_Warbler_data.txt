<p>The <b>Garden Warbler</b> (<i>Sylvia borin</i>) is a common and widespread <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> which breeds throughout northern and temperate Europe into western Asia. This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird is strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, and winters in central and southern Africa. It is fairly common and widespread in England and Wales; in Ireland it is largely confined to the midlands where it breeds on the shores of small lakes.</p>
<p>This is a nondescript bird, 13–14.5&#160;cm long, mainly brown-grey above and whitish below. It has no obvious distinctive features. Like most "warblers", it is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" title="Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect">insectivorous</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of shady woodlands with ground cover for nesting. The nest is built in low shrub or brambles, and 3–7 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid.</p>
<p>The Garden Warbler's <span class="unicode"><a href="/wiki/File:Sylvia_borin.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" /></a>&#160;<a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/37/Sylvia_borin.ogg" class="internal" title="Sylvia borin.ogg">song</a><small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Sylvia_borin.ogg" title="File:Sylvia borin.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> is a pleasant chattering with many clearer notes like a <a href="/wiki/Common_Blackbird" title="Common Blackbird">Blackbird</a>. The song can be confused with that of <a href="/wiki/Blackcap" title="Blackcap" class="mw-redirect">Blackcap</a>, but is more melodious and lacks the warbling end-phrase found in Blackcap songs. Indeed, despite their dissimilar colour pattern, these two species are probably more closely related to each other than to any other typical warbler (The Sylvia Monograph, A &amp; C Black, London; Jønsson &amp; Fjeldså 2006). The composer <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Messiaen" title="Olivier Messiaen">Olivier Messiaen</a>, who admired birdsong, used the song of <i>S. borin</i> as basis for his 1971 work <i>La fauvette des jardins</i>, the title being the French name of the species. The <i>beccafico</i>, or fig-eater, a much prized delicacy in Italy, is not as sometimes thought the Garden Warbler but the closely related <a href="/wiki/Orphean_Warbler" title="Orphean Warbler">Orphean Warbler</a>.</p>
