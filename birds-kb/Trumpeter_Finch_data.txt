<p>The <b>Trumpeter Finch</b> (<i>Rhodopechys githaginea</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> family Fringillidae.</p>
<p>This bird breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, across north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and into central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. There is a small <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> population in southern <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. Many birds are largely resident, but there is post-breeding dispersal, and some Asian breeders <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> into <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> for the winter.</p>
<p>In the summer of 2005 there was a notable eruption of this species into northwestern Europe, with several birds reaching as far as <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.</p>
<p>Stony desert or semi-desert is favoured for breeding. Four eggs are laid in a nest in a rock crevice.</p>
<p>This gregarious terrestrial finch's food is mainly seeds, and, particularly in the breeding season, insects.</p>
<p>The Trumpeter Finch is a small, long-winged bird. It has a large head and short, very thick bill. The summer male has a red bill, grey head and neck, and pale brown upper parts. The breast, rump and tail are pink, the last having dark terminal feathers. Winter males, females and young birds are a very washed-out version of the breeding male. The song of this bird is a buzzing nasal trill, like a tin trumpet.</p>
<p><i>Rhodopechys</i> means "rosy forearm" in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> and as a term goes all the way back to <a href="/wiki/Homer" title="Homer">Homer</a>, who used it often as an <a href="/wiki/Epithet" title="Epithet">epithet</a> to describe women and goddesses.</p>
