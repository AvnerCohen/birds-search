<p>The <b>Atlantic petrel</b> (<i>Pterodroma incerta</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Gadfly_petrel" title="Gadfly petrel">gadfly petrel</a> <a href="/wiki/Endemic_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic (ecology)">endemic</a> to the South <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. It breeds in enormous <a href="/wiki/Seabird_colony" class="mw-redirect" title="Seabird colony">colonies</a> on <a href="/wiki/Tristan_da_Cunha" title="Tristan da Cunha">Tristan da Cunha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gough_Island" title="Gough Island">Gough Island</a>, and ranges at sea from <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> to <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>, with most records at sea being to the west of the breeding islands, and along the subtropical convergence.
</p><p>The species feeds mostly on <a href="/wiki/Squid" title="Squid">squid</a>, which comprises 87% of its diet in some studies; it will also feed on <a href="/wiki/Lanternfish" title="Lanternfish">lanternfishes</a> (Myctophidae) as they ascend to the surface at night, as well as on <a href="/wiki/Crustaceans" class="mw-redirect" title="Crustaceans">crustaceans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the species exists in large numbers, the world population being estimated at around 5 million birds, it is listed as endangered by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a>. It is restricted to just two breeding islands and has declined historically due to exploitation for food. It is also currently thought to be threatened by <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">introduced</a> <a href="/wiki/House_Mouse" class="mw-redirect" title="House Mouse">house mice</a>, which attack chicks leading to low breeding success.
</p><p>It was formerly classified as a <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable_species" title="Vulnerable species">vulnerable species</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, new research has demonstrated the severe impact of predation by mice. Consequently, it was uplisted to <a href="/wiki/Endangered" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered">endangered</a> status in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
