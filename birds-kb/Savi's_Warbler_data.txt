<p>The <b>Savi's Warbler</b> (<i>Locustella luscinioides</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grass_warbler" title="Grass warbler">grass warbler</a> genus <i>Locustella</i>. It breeds in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> into temperate western <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
<p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in reed beds, usually with some bushes. 3-6 eggs are laid in a nest in reeds. It is a medium-sized warbler. The adult has an unstreaked grey-brown back, whitish grey underparts and a concolourous undertail, which is a distinction from <a href="/wiki/River_Warbler" title="River Warbler">River Warbler</a>. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers, but young birds are yellower below. Like most warblers, it is insectivorous.</p>
<p>This is not a skulky species, but can be difficult to see in the reeds except sometimes when singing.</p>
<p>The <span class="unicode"><a href="/wiki/File:Locustella_luscinioides.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" width="11" height="11" /></a>&#160;<a href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8f/Locustella_luscinioides.ogg" class="internal" title="Locustella luscinioides.ogg">"song"</a><small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">&#160;(<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Locustella_luscinioides.ogg" title="File:Locustella luscinioides.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> is a monotonous mechanical <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insect</a>-like reeling, often given at dusk. It is similar to the song of other species in the group, but is generally faster and deeper. Its song bears a strong resemblance to that of <a href="/wiki/Roesel%27s_Bush-cricket" title="Roesel's Bush-cricket" class="mw-redirect">Roesel's Bush-cricket</a>.</p>
<p>This bird is named after the Italian ornithologist <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Savi" title="Paolo Savi">Paolo Savi</a>.</p>
