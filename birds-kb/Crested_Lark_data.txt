<p>The <b>Crested Lark</b> (<i>Galerida cristata</i>) breeds across most of <a href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate" class="mw-redirect">temperate</a> <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> to northeast <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and eastern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> south to <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">non-migratory</a>, and the sedentary nature of this species is illustrated by the fact that it is only a very rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, despite breeding as close as northern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.</p>
<p>This is a common bird of dry open country and cultivation. It nests on the ground, laying two or three <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>. Its food is weed <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, the latter especially in the breeding season.</p>
<p>This is a smallish <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a>, slightly larger and plumper than the <a href="/wiki/Skylark" title="Skylark" class="mw-redirect">Skylark</a>. It has a long spiky erectile crest. It is greyer than the Skylark, and lacks the white wing and tail edges of that species.</p>
<p>In flight it shows reddish underwings. The body is mainly dark-streaked grey above and whitish below. The song is melodious and varied, with mournful whistles and mimicry included.</p>
<p>Some care must be taken to distinguish this lark, which has many <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>, from its close relatives in areas where they also occur. In the west of its range the <a href="/wiki/Thekla_Lark" title="Thekla Lark">Thekla Lark</a>, <i>Galerida theklae</i>, is very similar, as is the <a href="/wiki/Malabar_Lark" title="Malabar Lark">Malabar Lark</a>, <i>Galerida malabarica</i>, in western <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cultural significance">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_significance">Cultural significance</span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis of Assisi</a> considered the Crested Lark a bird of special significance, based on similarities he perceived between it and the life of the Friars Minor: its plain earth-colored plumage and hood, its humility ("for it goes willingly along the wayside and finds a grain of corn for itself"), and its time spent in song.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
