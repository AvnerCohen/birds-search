<p>The <b>crested lark</b> (<i>Galerida cristata</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a> distinguished from the other 81 species of lark by the <a href="/wiki/Crest_(feathers)" title="Crest (feathers)">crest of feathers</a> that rise up in territorial or courtship displays and when singing. Common to mainland Europe, the birds can also be found in northern Africa and in parts of western Asia and China. It is a <a href="/wiki/Non-migratory" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-migratory">non-migratory</a> bird, but can occasionally be found as a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a> in Great Britain.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crested lark was one of the many species originally described by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> in his landmark 1758 <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">10th edition of <i>Systema Naturae</i></a>. It was classified in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i> until German naturalist <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Boie" title="Friedrich Boie">Friedrich Boie</a> placed it in the new genus <i><a href="/wiki/Galerida" title="Galerida">Galerida</a></i> in 1821. <a href="/wiki/Colin_Harrison_(ornithologist)" title="Colin Harrison (ornithologist)">Colin Harrison</a> recommended lumping members of <i>Galerida</i> and <i>Lullula</i> back into <i>Alauda</i> in 1865 due to a lack of defining characteristics.<sup id="cite_ref-harrison65_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-harrison65-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The current scientific name is derived from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. <i>Galerida</i> was the name for a lark with a crest, from <i>galerum</i>, "cap", and <i>cristata</i> means "crested"<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Alban Guillaumet and colleagues noted the distinctiveness of populations from the <a href="/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> - birds in the dryer parts of Morocco and Tunisia had longer bills while those in more coastal northern parts had shorter bills typical of the European subspecies. The authors sampled the mitochondrial DNA and found they were distinct genetically.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Formerly, the <a href="/wiki/Maghreb_lark" title="Maghreb lark">Maghreb lark</a> was considered as a subspecies of the crested lark.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thirty-three <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Iberian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. pallida</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Christian_Ludwig_Brehm" title="Christian Ludwig Brehm">Brehm, CL</a>, 1858</small>: Found in Portugal and Spain</li>
<li><b>Central European crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. cristata</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a>, <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">1758</a>)</small>: Found from southern Scandinavia and France east to Ukraine and Hungary</li>
<li><b>West Italian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. neumanni</i>) - <small><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carl_Hilgert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carl Hilgert (page does not exist)">Hilgert</a>, 1907</small>: Found in west-central Italy</li>
<li><b>Greecian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. meridionalis</i>) - <small>Brehm, CL, 1841</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found from eastern Croatia to central Greece and western Turkey</li>
<li><b>Cyprus crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. cypriaca</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Valentin_Bianchi" title="Valentin Bianchi">Bianchi</a>, 1907</small>: Found on <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">Rhodes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karpathos" title="Karpathos">Karpathos</a> and Cyprus</li>
<li><b>South-east European crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. tenuirostris</i>) - <small>Brehm, CL, 1858</small>: Found from eastern Hungary and Romania to southern Russia and Kazakhstan</li>
<li><b>Caucasian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. caucasica</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/W%C5%82adys%C5%82aw_Taczanowski" title="Władysław Taczanowski">Taczanowski</a>, 1888</small>: Found on eastern <a href="/wiki/Aegean_islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Aegean islands">Aegean islands</a>, northern Turkey and southern <a href="/wiki/Caucasus" title="Caucasus">Caucasus</a></li>
<li><b>North-west Moroccan crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. kleinschmidti</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Carlo_von_Erlanger" title="Carlo von Erlanger">Erlanger</a>, 1899</small>: Found in north-western Morocco</li>
<li><b>West Moroccan crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. riggenbachi</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hartert" title="Ernst Hartert">Hartert</a>, 1902</small>: Found in western Morocco</li>
<li><b>North Algerian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. carthaginis</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Otto_Kleinschmidt" title="Otto Kleinschmidt">Kleinschmidt, O</a> &amp; Hilgert, 1905</small>: Found from north-eastern Morocco to northern Tunisia</li>
<li><b>North-east Algerian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. arenicola</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Henry_Baker_Tristram" title="Henry Baker Tristram">Tristram</a>, 1859</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in north-eastern Algeria, southern Tunisia and north-western Libya</li>
<li><b>Cyrenaica crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. festae</i>) - <small>Hartert, 1922</small>: Found in coastal north-eastern Libya</li>
<li><b>North-east Libyan crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. brachyura</i>) - <small>Tristram, 1865</small>: Found from inland north-eastern Libya to southern Iraq and northern Arabia</li>
<li><b>South-east Algerian crested lark</b> (<i>G. c. helenae</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Louis_Lavauden" title="Louis Lavauden">Lavauden</a>, 1926</small>: Found in south-eastern Algeria and south-western Libya</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A fairly small lark, the crested lark is roughly the same size as a <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_skylark" title="Eurasian skylark">Eurasian skylark</a>, but shorter overall and bulkier around the head and body, and very similar in appearance,<sup id="cite_ref-Cerny_1975_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cerny_1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with a height of 17&#160;cm (6.7&#160;in) and a wingspan of 29 to 38&#160;cm (11 to 15&#160;in), weighing between 37 and 55&#160;g (1.3 and 1.9&#160;oz).<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1998_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP_1998-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It is a small, brown bird which has a short tail with light brown outer feathers. Male and females have no real differences, but young crested larks have more spots on their back than their older counterparts.<sup id="cite_ref-Cerny_1975_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cerny_1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Its plumage is downy but sparse and appears whitish. The distinct crest from which the crested lark gets its name is conspicuous at all times but is more pronounced during territorial or courtship displays and when singing.<sup id="cite_ref-Harbard_1989_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harbard_1989-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In flight it shows reddish underwings. It shares many characteristics with the <a href="/wiki/Thekla_lark" class="mw-redirect" title="Thekla lark">Thekla lark</a>, with the main distinctions between the two being the beak, the Thekla's heavier black-brown streaks and its grey underwing, present in European specimens.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1998_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP_1998-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Crested lark in Behbahan, Iran" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg/220px-%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="305" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg/330px-%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg/440px-%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1452" data-file-height="2016" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:%DA%86%DA%A9%D8%A7%D9%88%DA%A9_%DA%A9%D8%A7%DA%A9%D9%84%DB%8C%D8%8C_%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A8%D9%87%D8%A7%D9%86.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Crested lark in <a href="/wiki/Behbahan" title="Behbahan">Behbahan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a></div></div></div>
<p>The crested lark breeds across most of <a href="/wiki/Temperate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate">temperate</a> Eurasia from Portugal to north-eastern China and eastern India, and in Africa south to Niger. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">non-migratory</a>, and the sedentary nature of this species is illustrated by the fact that it is only a very rare vagrant to Great Britain,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> despite breeding as close as northern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hayman_2004_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hayman_2004-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> While the bird is not commonly found in Scandinavia today, it could be found in Sweden until the 1990s, with sources reporting six individual birds in 1992 before becoming <a href="/wiki/Local_extinction" title="Local extinction">extirpated</a> in Sweden in 1993.<sup id="cite_ref-BritishBirds_88_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BritishBirds_88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The birds have also been extirpated in several other European countries, including Norway (1972), Luxembourg (1973) and Switzerland (1980s).<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1998_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP_1998-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This is a common bird of dry, open country and is often seen by roadsides or in cereal fields, although it is also found occupying small, sandy patches by railways, docks and airfields.<sup id="cite_ref-Hayman_2004_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hayman_2004-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The crested lark is a songbird, and has a liquid, warbling <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">song</a> described <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeia" title="Onomatopoeia">onomatopoeically</a> as a <i>whee-whee-wheeoo</i><sup id="cite_ref-Harbard_1989_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harbard_1989-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> or a <i>twee-tee-too</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Cerny_1975_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cerny_1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It sings in flight from high in the sky, at roughly 30 to 60&#160;m (98 to 197&#160;ft) above the ground. The related Eurasian skylark exhibits similar behaviour but also sings during its ascent, whereas the crested lark sings either at altitude or on the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-Hayman_1979_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hayman_1979-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  Their flight pattern is an example of <a href="/wiki/Undulatory_locomotion" title="Undulatory locomotion">undulatory locomotion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Harbard_1989_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harbard_1989-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg/220px-Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg/330px-Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg/440px-Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4558" data-file-height="4138" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Galerida_cristata_MHNT_232_Sbekia_Alg%C3%A9rie.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs of <i>Galerida cristata</i> - <a href="/wiki/MHNT" class="mw-redirect" title="MHNT">MHNT</a></div></div></div>
<p>It nests in small depressions in the ground, often in wastelands and on the outskirts of towns. The nests are untidy structures composed primarily of dead grasses and roots.<sup id="cite_ref-Cerny_1975_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cerny_1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Three to five brown, finely speckled <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>, similar to those of the Eurasian skylark, are laid at a time and will hatch after <span class="nowrap">11–12 days</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-Hayman_1979_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hayman_1979-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  As with most larks, the chicks leave the nest early, after about eight days and take flight after reaching <span class="nowrap">15–16 days</span> old.<sup id="cite_ref-Hayman_2004_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hayman_2004-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Two broods will usually be raised each year.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg/220px-Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="158" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg/330px-Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg/440px-Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2366" data-file-height="1700" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Galerida_cristata_meridionalis.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eating an insect</div></div></div>
<p>Largely vegetarian birds, the crested lark primarily feeds on grains and seeds, such as oats, wheat and barley,<sup id="cite_ref-Harbard_1989_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harbard_1989-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> but will also eat insects, particularly beetles,<sup id="cite_ref-Cerny_1975_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cerny_1975-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> with food either being scavenged from the ground or dug up.<sup id="cite_ref-BirdFacts_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdFacts-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Juvenile birds are fed by both parents, and generally leave the nest before they are able to fly to start foraging for food themselves.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationship_to_humans">Relationship to humans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Relationship to humans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Francis_of_Assisi" title="Francis of Assisi">Francis of Assisi</a> considered the crested lark a bird of special significance, based on similarities he perceived between it and the life of the <a href="/wiki/Friars_Minor" class="mw-redirect" title="Friars Minor">Friars Minor</a>: its plain earth-coloured plumage and hood, its humility ("for it goes willingly along the wayside and finds a grain of corn for itself"), and its time spent in song.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crested_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crested lark has been categorised by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List of Threatened Species</a> as being of <a href="/wiki/Least_concern" class="mw-redirect" title="Least concern">least concern</a>, meaning that it is not currently threatened with extinction.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Estimates for the global population of mature individuals of the species range from 22,000,000 to 91,200,000.<sup id="cite_ref-BirdLife_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLife-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Figures for Europe are less varied, with estimates putting the number of breeding pairs at between 3,600,000 and 7,600,000, or between 7,200,000 and 15,200,000 individuals. In Europe, trends since 1982 have shown an overall decline in the population of the species, resulting in the assumption that the crested lark is in decline globally.<sup id="cite_ref-BirdLife_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLife-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
