<p>The <b>Mediterranean gull</b> (<i>Ichthyaetus melanocephalus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a>. The scientific name is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>. The genus <i>Ichthyaetus</i> is from <i>ikhthus</i>, "fish", and <i>aetos</i>, "eagle", and the specific <i>melanocephalus</i> is from <i>melas</i>, "black", and <i>-kephalos</i> "-headed".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This gull breeds almost entirely in the <a href="/wiki/Western_Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Palearctic">Western Palearctic</a>, mainly in the south east, especially around the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a>, and in central <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. There are colonies elsewhere in southern Europe, and this species has undergone a dramatic range expansion in recent decades. As is the case with many gulls, it has traditionally been placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Larus" title="Larus">Larus</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mediterranean_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mediterranean gull is slightly larger and bulkier than the <a href="/wiki/Black-headed_gull" title="Black-headed gull">black-headed gull</a> with a heavier <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> and longer, darker legs. The breeding plumage adult is a distinctive white gull, with a very pale grey mantle and wings with white <a href="/wiki/Primary_feathers" class="mw-redirect" title="Primary feathers">primary feathers</a> without black tips. The black hood extends down the nape and shows distinct white eye crescents.<sup id="cite_ref-Peterson1967_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Peterson1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The blunt tipped, parallel sided, dark red bill has a black subterminal band. The non breeding adult is similar but the hood is reduced to an extensive dusky "bandit" mask through the eye. This bird takes two years to reach maturity. First year birds have a black terminal tail band and more black areas in the upperwings, but have pale underwings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mediterranean_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formerly restricted to the <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">Black Sea</a> and the eastern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> this species has now expanded over most of Europe as far as <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, with 37 sites: 543–592 pairs in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-rbbp_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rbbp-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> breeding has been recorded in at least four counties. Breeding has also occurred in <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sweden" title="Sweden">Sweden</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estonia" title="Estonia">Estonia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Czech_Republic" title="Czech Republic">Czech Republic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>.
</p><p>In winter, this <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> to Mediterranean and Atlantic <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coasts</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mediterranean_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG/220px-Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG/330px-Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG/440px-Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_melanocephalus_MWNH_0341.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This gull breeds in colonies in large reed beds or marshes, or on islands in <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a>; where its population is small, it nests in <a href="/wiki/Black-headed_gull" title="Black-headed gull">black-headed gull</a> colonies. Like most gulls, it is highly gregarious in winter, both when feeding or in evening roosts. It is not a pelagic species, and is rarely seen at sea far from coasts.
</p><p>The Mediterranean gull's feeding habits are much an opportunistic <a href="/wiki/Omnivore" title="Omnivore">omnivore</a>, eating fish, worms, scraps, insects, offal and carrion.
</p><p>This is a noisy species, especially at colonies, with a nasal "yeah" <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">call</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Mediterranean_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mediterranean gull is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
