<p>The <b>Yellowhammer</b> (<i>Emberiza citrinella</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family Emberizidae. It is common in all sorts of open areas with some scrub or trees and form small flocks in winter.</p>
<p>The Yellowhammer is a robust 15.5–17&#160;cm long bird, with a thick seed-eater's bill. The male has a bright yellow head, yellow underparts, and a heavily streaked brown back. The female is much duller, and more streaked below. The familiar, if somewhat monotonous, song of the cock is often described as <i>A little bit of bread and no cheese</i>.</p>
<p>Its natural diet consists of <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> when feeding young, and otherwise <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a>. The nest is on the ground. 3-6 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid, which show the hair-like markings characteristic of those of buntings.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellowhammer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span></h2>
<p>It breeds across <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and much of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. In parts of Europe it is in serious decline; in the UK the species fell by 54% between 1970 and 2003. In Europe and Asia most birds are resident, but some far northern birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> south in winter.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Yellowhammer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span></h2>
<p>It is most commonly found on lowland arable and mixed farmland, probably due to the greater availability of seeds. It nests in hedges, patches of scrub, and ditches, especially if these have a wide grass margin next to them, and a cereal crop next to the margin. Hedges of up to two metres tall are preferred, and they will not nest until it is in full leaf, building the nest next to the hedge if it is built before this. In winter, the flocks feed at good seed sites, such as newly-sown fields and over-wintered stubbles.</p>
<p>The Yellowhammer was introduced to <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> in 1862 and is now common and widespread there.</p>

<ul>
<li>Cereal, grasses (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Poa" title="Poa">Meadow Grass</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fescue" title="Fescue" class="mw-redirect">Fescue</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ryegrass" title="Ryegrass">Ryegrass</a>), <a href="/wiki/Common_Nettle" title="Common Nettle" class="mw-redirect">Common Nettle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rumex" title="Rumex">dock</a>, knotgrass <i><a href="/wiki/Polygonum_aviculare" title="Polygonum aviculare">Polygonum aviculare</a></i>, Fat Hen <i><a href="/wiki/Chenopodium_album" title="Chenopodium album">Chenopodium album</a></i>, Common Chickweed <i><a href="/wiki/Stellaria_media" title="Stellaria media">Stellaria media</a></i>, Mouse-ear <i><a href="/wiki/Cerastium" title="Cerastium">Cerastium</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Bramble" title="Bramble">Bramble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vetches" title="Vetches" class="mw-redirect">Vetches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clover" title="Clover">Clover</a>, <a href="/wiki/Forget-me-not" title="Forget-me-not">Forget-me-not</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dandelion" title="Dandelion" class="mw-redirect">Dandelion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Knapweed" title="Knapweed" class="mw-redirect">Knapweed</a>, Sow-thistle <i><a href="/wiki/Cicerbita" title="Cicerbita">Cicerbita</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Yarrow" title="Yarrow" class="mw-redirect">Yarrow</a>, Plantains <i><a href="/wiki/Plantago" title="Plantago">Plantago</a></i></li>

<li><a href="/wiki/Springtails" title="Springtails" class="mw-redirect">Springtails</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mayfly" title="Mayfly">mayflies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grasshopper" title="Grasshopper">grasshopper</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cockroach" title="Cockroach">cockroach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earwigs" title="Earwigs" class="mw-redirect">earwigs</a>, bugs <a href="/wiki/Hemiptera" title="Hemiptera">Hemiptera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lacewings" title="Lacewings" class="mw-redirect">lacewings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caddis_flies" title="Caddis flies" class="mw-redirect">caddis flies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sawflies" title="Sawflies" class="mw-redirect">sawflies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spiders" title="Spiders" class="mw-redirect">spiders</a>, <a href="/wiki/Woodlice" title="Woodlice" class="mw-redirect">woodlice</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caterpillar" title="Caterpillar">caterpillars</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fly" title="Fly">flies</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Earthworm" title="Earthworm">earthworms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Snail" title="Snail">snails</a></li>
