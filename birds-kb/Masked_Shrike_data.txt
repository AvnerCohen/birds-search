<p>The <b>Masked Shrike</b> ( <i>Lanius nubicus</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family <a href="/wiki/Laniidae" title="Laniidae" class="mw-redirect">Laniidae</a>. It breeds in southeastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the eastern end of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>, with a separate population in western <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. It is a common species in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in northeast <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</p>
<p>Since this is a short-range migrant, a bird in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> in October 2004 was a remarkable example of vagrancy, though a second British record occurred on <a href="/wiki/St._Mary%27s,_Isles_of_Scilly" title="St. Mary's, Isles of Scilly" class="mw-redirect">St. Mary's</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a> in November 2006.</p>
<p>Masked Shrike breeds in open woodland and cultivated country. The nest is built in a tree and 4-7 eggs are laid.</p>
<p>The male Masked Shrike is a striking bird, with black and white plumage and orange flanks. This medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> is the smallest shrike at 17–18&#160;cm in length. Its upperparts, crown, long tail and wings are mainly black; there are large white patches on the wings which are conspicuous in flight. The face is white except for a broad black line running back from the eye. The rest of the underparts are white with orange flanks.</p>
<p>In the female and young birds the upperparts are dark grey rather than black, and the orange flanks are less bright and reduced in extent. The juvenile has heavily <a href="/wiki/Vermiculated" title="Vermiculated">vermiculated</a> grey upperparts, and white underparts which are also vermiculated.</p>
<p>Masked Shrike eats large insects, small birds, <a href="/wiki/Vole" title="Vole">voles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a> which it impales on thorns or barbed wire as a "larder". It sometimes hunts from prominent perches, like other shrikes, but as often from semi-cover.</p>
<p>The song is a somewhat jagged warble.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Masked_Shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cultural references">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Cultural_references">Cultural references</span></h2>
<ul>
<li>In the 1963 movie <i><a href="/wiki/The_Great_Escape_(film)" title="The Great Escape (film)">The Great Escape</a></i>, a P.O.W. named Colin (<a href="/wiki/Donald_Pleasence" title="Donald Pleasence">Donald Pleasence</a>) teaches bird identification lessons. In the scene, there is a drawing of a masked shrike on a blackboard, which Colin describes as "the butcherbird", plumed in "uniform black above, from head to tail". At this point a black-clad prison camp guard is seen entering. Colin continues his description, describing the behaviour of the bird as "unlovable". The guard comments that he has "better things to do than draw birds", after which Colin continues that the Shrike's voice "is a monotonous succession of scratchy, complaining notes."</li>
</ul>
