<p>The <b>Yellow-billed Stork</b> (<i>Mycteria ibis</i>) is a large wading <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Stork" title="Stork">stork</a> family Ciconiidae. It occurs in Africa south of the Sahara and in Madagascar. Its a medium-sized stork. Length: 97&#160;cm; average body weight for males: 2.3&#160;kg; for females: 1.9&#160;kg. Plumage mainly pinkish-white with black wings and tail; bill yellow, blunt, and decurved at tip. Immature birds are greyish brown with dull greyish brown bill, dull orange face and brownish legs. The similar <a href="/wiki/Painted_Stork" title="Painted Stork">Painted Stork</a> <i>(Mycteria leucocephala)</i> is an Asian bird.</p>
<p>The Yellow-billed Stork is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
