<p>The <b>greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>Calandrella brachydactyla</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird. The current scientific name is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>. The genus name, <i>Calandrella</i>, is a diminutive of <i>kalandros</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Calandra_lark" title="Calandra lark">calandra lark</a>, and <i>brachydactila</i> is from <i>brakhus</i>, "short", and <i>daktulos</i>, "toe".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds in southern Europe, north-west Africa, and across the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> from Turkey and southern Russia to Mongolia. During migration they form large, tight flocks that move in unison; at other times they form loose flocks.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The greater short-toed lark was <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">described</a> by the German naturalist <a href="/wiki/Johann_Philipp_Achilles_Leisler" title="Johann Philipp Achilles Leisler">Johann Leisler</a> in 1814 and given the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Alauda brachydactila</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This lark is now placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Calandrella" title="Calandrella">Calandrella</a></i> that was established by another German naturalist, <a href="/wiki/Johann_Jakob_Kaup" title="Johann Jakob Kaup">Johann Jakob Kaup</a>, in 1829.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Specific_name_(zoology)" title="Specific name (zoology)">specific name</a> <i>brachydactyla</i> is from the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> βραχυδακτυλος <i>brakhudaktulos</i> "short-toed" from <i>brakhus</i> "short" and <i>daktulos</i> "toe".<sup id="cite_ref-hbwkey_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbwkey-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The alternate name <b>short-toed lark</b> may also be used for three other species in the genus <i>Calandrella</i>. The <a href="/wiki/Mongolian_short-toed_lark" title="Mongolian short-toed lark">Mongolian short-toed lark</a> was formerly considered as a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the greater short-toed lark (as <i>C. b. dukhunensis</i>) until split in 2016 by the IOC.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Formerly, some authorities also considered the <a href="/wiki/Red-capped_lark" title="Red-capped lark">red-capped lark</a> to be either <a href="/wiki/Conspecific" class="mw-redirect" title="Conspecific">conspecific</a> (as <i>C. cinerea</i>) with or a subspecies (as <i>C. b. cinerea</i>) of the greater short-toed lark.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Eight <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li><b>European greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. brachydactyla</i>) or <b>Palestine short-toed lark</b> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Johann_Philipp_Achilles_Leisler" title="Johann Philipp Achilles Leisler">Leisler</a>, 1814)</small>: Found in southern Europe, on Mediterranean islands and in north-western Africa.</li>
<li><b>Hungarian greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. hungarica</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Lajos_Horv%C3%A1th" title="Lajos Horváth">Horváth</a>, 1956</small>: Found in Hungary and northern Serbia</li>
<li><b>North African greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. rubiginosa</i>) - <small><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rudolph_John_Fromholz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rudolph John Fromholz (page does not exist)">Fromholz</a>, 1913</small>: Found in northern Africa</li>
<li><b>Levant greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. hermonensis</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Henry_Baker_Tristram" title="Henry Baker Tristram">Tristram</a>, 1865</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found from southern Turkey and Syria to north-eastern Egypt</li>
<li><b>BIrelandcik greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. woltersi</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Hans_Kumerloeve" class="mw-redirect" title="Hans Kumerloeve">Kumerloeve</a>, 1969</small>: Found in southern Turkey and north-western Syria</li>
<li><b>Transcaucasian greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. artemisiana</i>) - <small><a href="/w/index.php?title=V._B._Banjkovski&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="V. B. Banjkovski (page does not exist)">Banjkovski</a>, 1913</small>: Found from central Turkey and <a href="/wiki/Transcaucasia" title="Transcaucasia">Transcaucasia</a> to north-western Iran</li>
<li><b>Steppe greater short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. b. longipennis</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Eduard_Friedrich_Eversmann" title="Eduard Friedrich Eversmann">Eversmann</a>, 1848)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>. Also known as <b>Eastern short-toed lark</b> (a name also used by the <a href="/wiki/Asian_short-toed_lark" title="Asian short-toed lark">Asian short-toed lark</a>) or <b>Yarkand short-toed lark</b>. Found from Ukraine and southern Russia to south-central Siberia and southern Mongolia</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg/220px-Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg/330px-Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg/440px-Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Greater_Short-toed_Lark_Khangchendzonga_National_park_West_Sikkim_India_31.03.2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>From <a href="/wiki/Khangchendzonga_National_Park" title="Khangchendzonga National Park">Khangchendzonga National Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Sikkim" class="mw-redirect" title="West Sikkim">West Sikkim</a>, India.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg/220px-Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg/330px-Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg/440px-Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1489" data-file-height="1073" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Greater_Short-toed_Lark.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Greater Short-toed Lark</div></div></div>
<p>Several subspecies have been named but there is considerable geneflow and the species itself forms part of a larger complex. This is a small pale <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a>, smaller than the <a href="/wiki/Skylark" class="mw-redirect" title="Skylark">skylark</a>. It is dark-streaked greyish-brown above, and white below, and has a strong pointed bill that is pinkish with a grey culmen. It has a pale supercilium, dark patches on each side of its neck and a dark tail. Some birds in the west of the range have a rufous crown. The sexes are similar. The greater short-toed lark is paler than the Mongolian short-toed lark which also has a shorter bill.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In winter they fly in large and compact flocks that swing in synchrony.<sup id="cite_ref-whistler_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whistler-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Care must be taken to distinguish this species from other similar <i>Calandrella</i> larks, such as the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_short-toed_lark" title="Lesser short-toed lark">lesser short-toed lark</a>.
</p><p>The nominate form breeds in Europe (Iberia, France, Italy, the Balkans and Romania) and winters in Africa. Subspecies <i>hungarica</i> breeds in the eastern parts of Europe while <i>rubiginosa</i> breeds in north-western Africa. Subspecies <i>hermonensis</i> (sometimes including <i>woltersi</i>) breeds in Turkey, Syria and Egypt. Subspecies <i>artemisiana</i> (considered by some to be synonymous with <i>longipennis</i><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>) breeds in Asia Minor and winters in southern west Asia. Subspecies <i>longipennis</i> breeds in Ukraine, Mongolia and Manchuria and winters in South Asia mainly in the drier zone of north-western India.<sup id="cite_ref-whistler_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whistler-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song varies between a dry twittering and a more varied and imitative melody. Flocks will often fly together to water in the mornings at favourite spots. In the evenings they roost in open ground, with each bird squatting in a small depression made in the soil.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg/220px-Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="239" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg/330px-Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg/440px-Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2923" data-file-height="3179" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Calandrella_brachydactyla_MHNT.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i> Calandrella brachydactyla </i> - <a href="/wiki/MHNT" class="mw-redirect" title="MHNT">MHNT</a></div></div></div> 
<p>All but some southernmost populations are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering south to the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. This species is a fairly common wanderer to northern and western Europe in spring and autumn.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Populations breeding in the Iberian Peninsula winter south of the Sahara in Africa. Here they prefer crop land and dry pastures with short shrubs while the syntopic lesser short-toed larks (<i>Calandrella rufescens</i>) prefer drier areas.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This is a common bird of dry open country and cultivation. It nests on the ground, laying two to three <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>. Its food is <a href="/wiki/Seed_predation" title="Seed predation">seeds</a> and insects, the latter especially in the breeding season.
</p><p>In colonial India, they were hunted for food as <a href="/wiki/Ortolan_bunting" title="Ortolan bunting">ortolans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-whistler_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whistler-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They visit parts of South Asia in large flocks during winter and are sometimes attracted to short grass areas along aerodromes and become a <a href="/wiki/Bird_strike" title="Bird strike">bird strike</a> risk to aircraft.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
