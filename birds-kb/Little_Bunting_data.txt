<p>The <b>little bunting</b> (<i>Emberiza pusilla</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird belonging to the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family (<a href="/wiki/Emberizidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Emberizidae">Emberizidae</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>First described by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">Peter Simon Pallas</a> in 1776, the little bunting is a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> species,<sup id="cite_ref-Byers154_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Byers154-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> with no geographical variation across its extensive <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> range.<sup id="cite_ref-Byers156_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Byers156-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The genus name <i>Emberiza</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Old_High_German" title="Old High German">Old German</a> <i>Embritz</i>, a bunting. The specific <i>pusilla</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "very small".<sup id="cite_ref-job_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a small bunting, measuring only 12–14&#160;cm (4.7–5.5&#160;in) in length.<sup id="cite_ref-Byers154_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Byers154-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It has a heavily streaked brown back and white underparts with fine dark streaking. With its chestnut face and white malar stripe, it resembles a small female <a href="/wiki/Reed_bunting" class="mw-redirect" title="Reed bunting">reed bunting</a>, but has black crown stripes, a white eye-ring, and a fine dark border to the rear of its chestnut cheeks. The sexes are similar.
</p><p>The call is a distinctive <i>zik</i>, and the song is a rolling <i>siroo-sir-sir-siroo</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The little bunting breeds across the <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a> of the far north-east of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Eurosiberia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">Russian Far East</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in the subtropics in northern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and the northern parts of south-east Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-Byers156_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Byers156-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The birds remain in their winter quarters for quite long; specimens were taken in <a href="/wiki/Yunnan" title="Yunnan">Yunnan</a> in late March.<sup id="cite_ref-Bangs_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bangs-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is a rare <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Vagrancy in birds">vagrant</a> to western Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-Byers156_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Byers156-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> is adaptable; in the mountains of <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">Bhutan</a> for example, where small numbers winter, it is typically found in an <a href="/wiki/Agricultural" class="mw-redirect" title="Agricultural">agricultural</a> <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a>, mostly between 1,000 and 2,000 metres (3,300 and 6,600&#160;ft) <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Inskipp_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Inskipp-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds in open <a href="/wiki/Conifer" class="mw-redirect" title="Conifer">coniferous</a> woodland, often with some <a href="/wiki/Birch" title="Birch">birch</a> or <a href="/wiki/Willow" title="Willow">willow</a>. Four to six <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a> are laid in a tree nest. Its natural food consists of seeds, or when feeding young, <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>.
</p>

<p>A common and widely-ranging species, it is not considered threatened on the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
