<p>The <b>Cretzschmar's Bunting</b> (<i>Emberiza caesia</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family Emberizidae, a group now separated by most modern authors from the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finches</a>, Fringillidae.</p>
<p>It breeds in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> and the coastal countries along the eastern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in the <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>. It is a very rare wanderer to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>Cretzschmar's Bunting breeds on sunny open hillsides, with some bushes. It is mainly coastal or insular, and often breeds at lower levels than the closely related <a href="/wiki/Ortolan_Bunting" title="Ortolan Bunting">Ortolan Bunting</a> where both occur. It lays four to six eggs in a ground nest. Its natural food consists of insects when feeding young, and otherwise seeds.</p>
<p>This bird is smaller than Ortolan. The breeding male has a grey head with orange moustaches. The upperparts are brown and heavily streaked, except on the rump, and the underparts are rusty orange. The stout bill is pink.</p>
<p>Females and young birds have a weaker head pattern, and are more similar to Ortolans. They can be distinguished by the warm brown rump and white eye-ring.</p>
<p>This bird was named after the German physician <a href="/wiki/Philipp_Jakob_Cretzschmar" title="Philipp Jakob Cretzschmar">Philipp Jakob Cretzschmar</a>.</p>
