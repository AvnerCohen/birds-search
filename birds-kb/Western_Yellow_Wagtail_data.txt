<p>The <b>western yellow wagtail</b> (<i>Motacilla flava</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Perching_bird" class="mw-redirect" title="Perching bird">passerine</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Wagtail" title="Wagtail">wagtail</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Motacillidae" title="Motacillidae">Motacillidae</a>, which also includes the <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipits</a> and <a href="/wiki/Longclaw" title="Longclaw">longclaws</a>.
</p><p>This species breeds in much of temperate <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Europe and Asia</a>. It is resident in the milder parts of its range, such as western Europe, but northern and eastern populations <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> to <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and south Asia.
</p><p>It is a slender 15–16&#160;cm long bird, with the characteristic long, constantly wagging tail of its genus. It is the shortest tailed of the European wagtails. The breeding adult male is basically <a href="/wiki/Olive_(color)" title="Olive (color)">olive</a> above and yellow below. In other plumages, the yellow may be diluted by white. The heads of breeding males come in a variety of colours and patterns depending on <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>.
</p><p>The call is a high-pitched <i>jeet</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEWiles_et_al.2000279_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEWiles_et_al.2000279-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> inhabits open country near water, such as wet meadows. It nests in tussocks, laying 4–8 speckled eggs.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Systematics">Systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_yellow_wagtail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i>Motacilla</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> name for the wagtail; although actually a diminutive of <i>motare</i>, " to move about", from medieval times it led to the misunderstanding of <i>cilla</i> as "tail". The specific <i>flava</i> is Latin for golden-yellow.<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This species' systematics and <a href="/wiki/Phylogeny" class="mw-redirect" title="Phylogeny">phylogeny</a> is extremely confusing. Literally dozens of subspecies have been described at one time or another, and some 15-20 are currently considered valid depending on which author reviews them. In addition, the <a href="/wiki/Citrine_wagtail" title="Citrine wagtail">citrine wagtail</a> (<i>M. citreola</i>) forms a <a href="/wiki/Cryptic_species_complex" class="mw-redirect" title="Cryptic species complex">cryptic species complex</a> with this bird;<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEVoelker2002_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEVoelker2002-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific page or range of pages in which the material appears. (May 2019)">page&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> both taxa as conventionally delimited are <a href="/wiki/Paraphyletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Paraphyletic">paraphyletic</a> in respect to each other. The populations of the <a href="/wiki/Beringia" title="Beringia">Beringian</a> region are sometimes separated as <a href="/wiki/Eastern_yellow_wagtail" title="Eastern yellow wagtail">eastern yellow wagtail</a> (<i>M. tschutschensis</i>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Currently_recognized_subspecies">Currently recognized subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_yellow_wagtail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Currently recognized subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Colouration refers to males except when noted.
</p>
<ul><li><i>M. f. flava</i> <small><a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a>, <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">1758</a></small> – <b>blue-headed wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Blue-grey head with white <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> stripe in males, much washed with <a href="/wiki/Buff_(color)" class="mw-redirect" title="Buff (color)">buffish</a> green in females.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: southern <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> to <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and central European mountain ranges, east to Urals. Winter: <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. flavissima</i> <small>Blyth, 1834</small> – <b>yellow-crowned wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Yellow-green head with a brighter yellow supercilium. Females markedly paler below than males.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Britain</a> and <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">English Channel</a> coast. Winter: <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. thunbergi</i> <small>Billberg, 1828</small> – <b>dark-headed wagtail</b> or grey-headed wagtail</li></ul>
<dl><dd>Head dark grey, reaching down to the cheeks, and without white in males; lighter and washed greenish, with vestigial greenish supercilium in females.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: central and northern Scandinavia east to north-west <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. Winter: eastern Africa, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> subcontinent, <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">Southeast Asia</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. iberiae</i> <small>Hartert, 1921</small> – <b>Iberian yellow wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Like <i>flava</i>, but throat white and grey darker, almost black behind eyes.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: south-eastern France, <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a> from <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Banc_d%27Arguin" class="mw-redirect" title="Banc d&#39;Arguin">Banc d'Arguin</a>. Winter: <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">The Gambia</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. cinereocapilla</i> <small>Savi, 1831</small> – <b>ashy-headed wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Like <i>iberiae</i> but supercilium absent or vestigial.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Slovenia" title="Slovenia">Slovenia</a>. Winter: coastal <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a> to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Chad" title="Lake Chad">Lake Chad</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. pygmaea</i> <small>(A. E. Brehm, 1854)</small> – <b>Egyptian yellow wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Similar to <i>cinereocapilla</i>, smaller, less bright.</dd>
<dd><a href="/wiki/Nile_delta" class="mw-redirect" title="Nile delta">Nile delta</a> and lower Nile, resident all year.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. feldegg</i> <small>Michahelles, 1830</small> – <b>black-headed wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Like <i>thunbergi</i> but black cap in males, females like a dull <i>thunbergi</i> male above, very washed-out dirty yellowish below, throat white.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a> east to the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a>, south to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>; also <a href="/wiki/Levant" title="Levant">Levant</a>. Winter: central Africa from <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a> and south <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. lutea</i> <small>(S. G. Gmelin, 1774)</small> – <b>yellow-headed wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Head yellow with green neck in males, females like a somewhat more vivid <i>flava</i> female.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: Lower <a href="/wiki/Volga" title="Volga">Volga</a> to <a href="/wiki/Irtysh_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Irtysh River">Irtysh River</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Zaysan" title="Lake Zaysan">Lake Zaysan</a>. Winter: Africa and Indian subcontinent.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. beema</i> <small>(Sykes, 1832)</small> – <b>Sykes' wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Like <i>flava</i> but head lighter grey, ears washed white; sexes often similar.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: North of <i>lutea</i>, east to <a href="/wiki/Ladakh" title="Ladakh">Ladakh</a> area. Winter: Indian subcontinent, also east Africa and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia">Arabia</a>.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. melanogrisea</i> <small>(Homeyer, 1878)</small> – <b>Turkestan black-headed wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Similar to <i>feldegg</i>, but white malar stripe and sometimes green neck.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: <a href="/wiki/Volga_Delta" title="Volga Delta">Volga Delta</a> east around Caspian Sea to northern Afghanistan. Winter: <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and north-west India to western <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, possibly also north-eastern Africa.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. plexa</i> <small>(Thayer &amp; Bangs, 1914)</small> – <b>north Siberian yellow wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Males like <i>thunbergi</i> but ears darker, top of head lighter, vestigial supercilium. Females like dull males.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: Siberia between <a href="/wiki/Khatanga_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Khatanga River">Khatanga</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kolyma_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Kolyma River">Kolyma Rivers</a>. Winter: India, south-east Asia.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. f. leucocephala</i> <small>(Przevalski, 1887)</small> – <b>white-headed yellow wagtail</b></li></ul>
<dl><dd>Male like <i>flava</i>, but grey of head very pale, almost white. Female like <i>flava</i> females, but head somewhat darker.</dd>
<dd>Breeding: North-west Mongolia and adjacent <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Republic of China">PRC</a> and Russia. Winter: probably India.</dd></dl>
<p>The nominate blue-headed wagtail and yellow wagtail form a narrow hybrid zone in northern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. Birds from this zone vary in appearance, but one type, which resembles nominate blue-headed wagtail (except that the blue tones to the head are paler and more mauve and the white of the head is more extensive, particularly on the throat, ear-coverts, and supercilium) is colloquially referred to as <b>Channel wagtail</b>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_culture">In culture</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Western_yellow_wagtail&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In culture">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Pyramid_Texts" title="Pyramid Texts">Pyramid Texts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Old_Kingdom_of_Egypt" title="Old Kingdom of Egypt">Old Kingdom</a> <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a>, the yellow wagtail was considered a representation of <a href="/wiki/Atum" title="Atum">Atum</a> himself and might have been the first inspiration for the <a href="/wiki/Bennu" title="Bennu">Bennu</a> bird, which, in turn, is the supposed inspiration for the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_(mythology)" title="Phoenix (mythology)">phoenix</a> of <a href="/wiki/Greek_mythology" title="Greek mythology">Greek mythology</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Salvador_2015_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Salvador_2015-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
