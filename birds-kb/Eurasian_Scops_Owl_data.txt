<p>The <b>Eurasian scops owl</b> (<i>Otus scops</i>), also known as the <b>European scops owl</b> or just <b>scops owl</b>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>. This species is a part of the larger grouping of owls known as <a href="/wiki/Typical_owl" class="mw-redirect" title="Typical owl">typical owls</a>, Strigidae, which contains most species of owl. The other grouping is the <a href="/wiki/Barn_owl" title="Barn owl">barn owls</a>, Tytonidae. The scientific name is from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>otus</i> for an eared owl and <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>skopos</i>,  "watcher".<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_scops_owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG/220px-Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG/330px-Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG/440px-Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2442" data-file-height="1606" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Otus_scops_MWNH_0608.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This bird breeds in southern Europe eastwards into western and central Asia. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in southernmost Europe and sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> Africa. It is rare any distance north of its breeding range, usually occurring as a spring overshoot. It is unlikely that this nocturnal owl would be found outside the breeding season when it is not calling.
It breeds in open woodland, parks and gardens. It lays 3-6 eggs in a tree hole.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_scops_owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a small owl, and at 19–21&#160;cm (7.5–8.3&#160;in) in length and a 47–54&#160;cm (19–21&#160;in) wingspan is not as large as the <a href="/wiki/Little_owl" title="Little owl">little owl</a>. Body mass ranges from 64 to 135&#160;g (2.3 to 4.8&#160;oz).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
The scops owl perches upright and shows small ear tufts. It is predominantly grey-brown in colour, with paler face, underparts and shoulder line. This species has a strong direct flight on long narrow wings, reflecting its migratory habits.
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_scops_owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The call is a deep whistle given by both sexes. It is similar to the call of the <a href="/wiki/Midwife_toad" title="Midwife toad">midwife toad</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_scops_owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It takes small prey such as <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other invertebrates. It is largely <a href="/wiki/Nocturnal" class="mw-redirect" title="Nocturnal">nocturnal</a>.
</p>
