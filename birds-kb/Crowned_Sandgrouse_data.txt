<p>The <b>crowned sandgrouse</b> (<i>Pterocles coronatus</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the sandgrouse family, the  <a href="/wiki/Pteroclidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Pteroclidae">Pteroclidae</a> from <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crowned_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A fairly small sandgrouse which appears rather uniformly coloured from a distance except for darker flight feathers, the wholly dark flight feathers being the best feature to identify crowned sandgrouse from the similar <a href="/wiki/Spotted_sandgrouse" title="Spotted sandgrouse">spotted sandgrouse</a>. The dark flight feathers contrast with the sandy upper wing coverts and the creamy underwing coverts. The adult male has a black mask and lacks any grey tones on the neck and breast. The females and immatures lack the black mask and differ from spotted sandgrouse in having dark vermiculations, rather than spots, across the breast and all of the belly and not confined to the breast and upper belly as in spotted sandgrouse. They are 27–29&#160;cm long and have a wingspan of 52–63&#160;cm.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crowned_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crowned sandgrouse occurs in North Africa and south Asia and is found from <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> in the west through the Middle East to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crowned_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crowned sandgrouse is a bird of deserts, preferring stony deserts rather than sandy ones.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In North Africa breeds among dark red sandstone which matches its plumage colour. Avoids areas with too much vegetation.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crowned_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The crowned sandgrouse is rather nomadic in the dry season and is recorded away from areas where breeding is known. For example, it is scarce in <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> north of 32°N but is  frequent at <a href="/wiki/Cinyps,_Libya" title="Cinyps, Libya">Wadi Caam</a> in the dry season of June to September. It is gregarious outside the breeding season, especially when visiting water resources which it flies to in early and mid morning and again towards sunset. It mainly feeds on hard seeds but also feeds on shoots of grass and other plants.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The nest is a shallow depression on open ground and has no lining, although there may be a circle of stomes around the nest as the birds move any small stomes within the depression to the rim when they start laying. Clutch is 2-3 eggs which are incubated for just over three weeks and the chicks fledge in 24–28 days.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Crowned_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Previously the crowned sandgroupse was included in genus <i>Eremialector</i> and recent studies suggest that it is part of a <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> that also includes the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-throated_sandgrouse" title="Yellow-throated sandgrouse">yellow-throated sandgrouse</a> <i>P. gutturalis</i> and the <a href="/wiki/Madagascan_sandgrouse" title="Madagascan sandgrouse">Madagascan sandgrouse</a> <i>P. personatus</i>; and these may form a group along with the two <i><a href="/wiki/Syrrhaptes" title="Syrrhaptes">Syrrhaptes</a></i> species, <a href="/wiki/Black-bellied_sandgrouse" title="Black-bellied sandgrouse">black-bellied sandgrouse</a> <i>P. orientalis</i>, <a href="/wiki/Namaqua_sandgrouse" title="Namaqua sandgrouse">Namaqua sandgrouse</a> <i>P. namaqua</i> and <a href="/wiki/Chestnut-bellied_sandgrouse" title="Chestnut-bellied sandgrouse">chestnut-bellied sandgrouse</a> <i>P. exustus</i>, and possibly including also P. alchata and P. burchelli; further study is required.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Five subspecies are currently recognised:<sup id="cite_ref-ITIS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBW_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>Pterocles coronatus coronatus</i> <small>M. H. C. Lichtenstein, 1823</small>: <a href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" class="mw-redirect" title="Sahara Desert">Sahara Desert</a> from southern Morocco, Western Sahara and Mauritania east to north central <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> coast.</li>
<li><i>Pterocles coronatus vastitas</i> <small>R. Meinertzhagen, 1928</small> – <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai</a> and neighbouring areas of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>.</li>
<li><i>Pterocles coronatus saturatus</i> <small>Kinnear, 1927</small> –the  hilly country of interior <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>.</li>
<li><i>Pterocles coronatus atratus</i> <small>E. J. O. Hartert, 1902</small> – Southern <a href="/wiki/Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia">Arabia</a> (except interior Oman) and southern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> east to south-western Afghanistan and <a href="/wiki/Baluchistan" class="mw-redirect" title="Baluchistan">Baluchistan</a>.</li>
<li><i>Pterocles coronatus ladas</i> <small>Koelz, 1954</small> – <a href="/wiki/Sind" class="mw-redirect" title="Sind">Sind</a>.</li></ul>
