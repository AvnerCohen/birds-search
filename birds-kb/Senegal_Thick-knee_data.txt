<p>The <b>Senegal thick-knee</b> (<i>Burhinus senegalensis</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Stone-curlew" title="Stone-curlew">stone-curlew</a>, a group of <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Burhinidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Burhinidae">Burhinidae</a>. Their vernacular scientific name refers to the prominent joints in the long yellow or greenish legs.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Senegal_thick-knee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a resident breeder in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> between the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> and the equator, and in the <a href="/wiki/Nile" title="Nile">Nile</a> valley.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Senegal_thick-knee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_(6843596220),_crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_%286843596220%29%2C_crop.jpg/220px-BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_%286843596220%29%2C_crop.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_%286843596220%29%2C_crop.jpg/330px-BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_%286843596220%29%2C_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_%286843596220%29%2C_crop.jpg/440px-BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_%286843596220%29%2C_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="465" data-file-height="263" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BIRDS_GAMBIA-1274_(6843596220),_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center>Pair in flight, <a href="/wiki/The_Gambia" title="The Gambia">The Gambia</a></center></div></div></div>
<p>Senegal thick-knees are medium-large waders with strong black and yellow black bills, large yellow eyes &#8212; which give them a reptilian appearance &#8212; and cryptic plumage. They are similar but slightly smaller than the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_stone-curlew" title="Eurasian stone-curlew">Eurasian stone-curlew</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">winters</a> in Africa. The long dark bill, single black bar on the folded wing, and darker cheek stripe are distinctions from the <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> species. Senegal thick-knee is striking in flight, with a broad white wing bar.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits_and_food">Habits and food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Senegal_thick-knee&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habits and food">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853,_white_background.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853%2C_white_background.jpg/200px-Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853%2C_white_background.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853%2C_white_background.jpg/300px-Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853%2C_white_background.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853%2C_white_background.jpg/400px-Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853%2C_white_background.jpg 2x" data-file-width="609" data-file-height="486" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Burhinus_senegalensis_egg_1853,_white_background.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center>Illustration of egg</center></div></div></div>
<p>This species has a preference for dry open habitats with some bare ground, preferably near water. It lays two blotchy light brown eggs on a ground scrape. It is most active at dawn and dusk. The song is a loud <i>pi-pi-pi-pi-pi-pi-pi</i>.
</p><p>Food is <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, crustaceans and other invertebrates. It will also take other small prey.
</p>
