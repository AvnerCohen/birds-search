<p>The <b>desert lark</b> (<i>Ammomanes deserti</i>) breeds in deserts and semi-deserts from Morocco to western India.  It has a very wide distribution and faces no obvious threats, and surveys have shown that it is slowly increasing in numbers as it expands its range. The <a href="/wiki/International_Union_for_Conservation_of_Nature" title="International Union for Conservation of Nature">International Union for Conservation of Nature</a> has rated its conservation status as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least-concern_species" title="Least-concern species">least concern</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally, the desert lark was classified as belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i> until moved to <i>Ammomanes</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alternate names include <b>desert finch lark</b> and <b>sand lark</b>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>

<li><b>Algerian desert lark</b> or <b>North Algerian desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. algeriensis</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Richard_Bowdler_Sharpe" title="Richard Bowdler Sharpe">Sharpe</a>, 1890</small>: Originally described as a separate species. <li><b>South Algerian desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. whitakeri</i>) - <small>Hartert, 1911</small>: Found in south-eastern Algeria and south-western Libya</li>
<li><b>Central Algerian desert lark</b> or <b>Saharan desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. mya</i>) - <small>Hartert, 1912</small>: Found in central Algeria</li>
<li><b>Damergu desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. geyri</i>) - <small>Hartert, 1924</small>: Found in Mauritania to southern Algeria and north-western Niger</li>
<li><i>A. d. kollmannspergeri</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Niethammer" title="Günther Niethammer">Niethammer</a>, 1955</small>: Found in north-eastern Chad and western Sudan</li>

<li><b>Middle East desert lark</b> or <b>Sinai desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. isabellina</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Coenraad_Jacob_Temminck" title="Coenraad Jacob Temminck">Temminck</a>, 1823)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>. <li><b>Eritrea desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. samharensis</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/George_Ernest_Shelley" title="George Ernest Shelley">Shelley</a>, 1902</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in north-eastern Sudan, Eritrea and southern Arabia</li>

<li><b>Danakil desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. assabensis</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Tommaso_Salvadori" title="Tommaso Salvadori">Salvadori</a>, 1902</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in southern Eritrea, Ethiopia and north-western Somalia</li>
<li><b>Somali desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. akeleyi</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Giraud_Elliot" title="Daniel Giraud Elliot">Elliot, DG</a>, 1897</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in northern Somalia</li>
<li><i>A. d. azizi</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Claud_B._Ticehurst" title="Claud B. Ticehurst">Ticehurst</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Robert_Ernest_Cheesman" title="Robert Ernest Cheesman">Cheesman</a>, 1924</small>: Found in east-central Arabia</li>
<li><i>A. d. saturata</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/William_Robert_Ogilvie-Grant" title="William Robert Ogilvie-Grant">Ogilvie-Grant</a>, 1900</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found in southern Arabia</li>
<li><i>A. d. insularis</i> - <small>Ripley, 1951</small>: Found in Bahrain</li>

<li><i>A. d. parvirostris</i> - <small>Hartert, 1890</small>: Found in north-eastern Iran and western Turkmenistan</li>
<li><i>A. d. orientalis</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Zarudny" title="Nikolai Zarudny">Zarudny</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Harald_von_Loudon" title="Harald von Loudon">Loudon</a>, 1904</small>: Found in north-eastern Iran, northern  Afghanistan, southern Turkmenistan, southern Uzbekistan and southern Tajikistan</li>
<li><i>A. d. iranica</i> - <small>Zarudny, 1911</small>: Found in central, southern, eastern Iran to southern Afghanistan and western Pakistan</li>
<li><b>Indian desert finch-lark</b> or <b>Indian desert lark</b> (<i>A. d. phoenicuroides</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Edward_Blyth" title="Edward Blyth">Blyth</a>, 1853)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in genus <i><a href="/wiki/Mirafra" title="Mirafra">Mirafra</a></i>. Found in south-eastern Afghanistan, eastern Pakistan and north-western India</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The desert lark is a medium-sized, big-headed and long-billed lark, growing to a length of 16 to 17&#160;cm (6.3 to 6.7&#160;in). The sexes are alike, but there is considerable geographical variation. It has a rather dull plumage which can vary from quite pale to rather dark. Plumage may vary between <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> but individuals within populations can be variable too. It is similar in appearance to the <a href="/wiki/Bar-tailed_lark" title="Bar-tailed lark">bar-tailed lark</a> but is slightly larger, has a less-domed head, a larger broader beak, stouter legs and a longer tail. The upper parts of the many subspecies vary in colour; most are pale greyish brown, but some races are very washed out and others are quite a deep colour. Some have some rufous colour on wings and tail, similar to the bar-tailed lark, but the underparts are a pale pinkish grey and have much more streaking than that species, and the desert lark lacks the clearly defined terminal black band on the tail of that species, although it may have a rather diffuse dark patch.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The colour variation seems to mainly match the habitat, so sandy-coloured birds are commoner in sandy areas, greyer birds in rocky areas and the darkest birds in desert dominated by basalt.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song is a mournful <i>choo-wee-chacha wooee</i>, uttered from the ground or a boulder, or during undulating flight. It is more mellow and less squeaky than that of the bar-tailed lark. When flushed it utters a short <i>churree</i> or <i>chee-lu</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The desert lark is found from Mauritania, Western Sahara and southern Morocco in the west east through northern Africa south to Chad and Somalia through south west Asia to western India.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is resident (non-<a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>) apart from local movements in arid stony areas, and avoids flat sand and will always be found in broken terrain with a vertical element to it<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Desert_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The desert lark is not very sociable and does not form large flocks, being mainly encountered in small groups or as single birds. They are territorial when breeding, nesting on the ground under the shelter of a tussock or stone in a shallow scrape lined with plant material and having a rim of pebbles, which may surround the nest or be on the more exposed side of the nest.  The clutch is 1-5 eggs and these are laid at different times in various parts of its range, e.g. January–February in the south and March–April in the north. The desert lark eats seeds and insects, the latter especially in the breeding season.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
