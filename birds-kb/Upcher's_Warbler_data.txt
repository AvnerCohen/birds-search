<p><b>Upcher's warbler</b> (<i>Hippolais languida</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> genus <i>Hippolais</i>. It breeds in an area from <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> south and east to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in eastern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> south to <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>.
</p><p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in semi-desert habitats, frequenting bushy scrub and thickets of <a href="/wiki/Tamarisk" class="mw-redirect" title="Tamarisk">tamarisk</a>. 4-5 eggs are laid in a nest in bushes and low trees.
</p><p>This is a medium-sized warbler, similar in size to the <a href="/wiki/Icterine_warbler" title="Icterine warbler">icterine warbler</a>, with a slightly longer bill and shorter wings and a longer tail. Its frequent tail movement is reminiscent of a <i><a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">Sylvia</a></i> warbler or a <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chat</a>. Its rather grey plumage is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_olivaceous_warbler" title="Eastern olivaceous warbler">olivaceous warbler</a>, but tail movements are diagnostic.
</p><p>It feeds on invertebrates. Its song is similar to that of other <i>Hippolais</i> warblers, but distinctive and unmistakable, and entirely different from that of the olivaceous warbler.
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Christian_Gottfried_Ehrenberg" title="Christian Gottfried Ehrenberg">Ehrenberg</a>'s original description of this bird was 'rather vague' and it was redescribed by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Baker_Tristram" title="Henry Baker Tristram">Henry Baker Tristram</a> in 1864, naming it <i>Hippolais upcheri</i> after his friend <a href="/wiki/Henry_Morris_Upcher" title="Henry Morris Upcher">Henry Morris Upcher</a>. This is the origin of the bird's common name.
</p>
