<p>The <b>Grey Heron</b> (<i>Ardea cinerea</i>), is a wading <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a> family Ardeidae, native throughout temperate <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and also parts of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is resident in the milder south and west, but many birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">retreat</a> in winter from the ice in colder regions. It has become common in summer even inside the <a href="/wiki/Arctic_circle" title="Arctic circle" class="mw-redirect">Arctic circle</a> along the <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norwegian</a> coast.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Grey_Heron.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/The_Grey_Heron.jpg/150px-The_Grey_Heron.jpg" width="150" height="134" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/The_Grey_Heron.jpg/225px-The_Grey_Heron.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/The_Grey_Heron.jpg/300px-The_Grey_Heron.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_Grey_Heron.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
The Grey Heron</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It is a large bird, standing up to 100&#160;cm (39&#160;in) tall and measuring 84–102 cm (33–40 in) long with a 155–195 cm (61–77 in) wingspan.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The body weight can range from 1.02–2.08 kg (2.2–4.6 lb).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Its <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> is largely grey above, and off-white below. Adults have a white head with a broad black <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a> and slender crest, while immatures have a dull grey head. It has a powerful, pinkish-yellow bill, which is brighter in breeding adults. It has a slow flight, with its long neck retracted (S-shaped). This is characteristic of herons and <a href="/wiki/Bittern" title="Bittern">bitterns</a>, and distinguishes them from <a href="/wiki/Stork" title="Stork">storks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crane_(bird)" title="Crane (bird)">cranes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spoonbill" title="Spoonbill">spoonbills</a>, which extend their necks. The call is a loud croaking "fraaank". The Australian <a href="/wiki/White-faced_Heron" title="White-faced Heron">White-faced Heron</a> is often incorrectly called a Grey Heron. In Ireland the grey heron is often colloquially called a "crane".</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>There are four <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Ardea cinerea cinerea</i> <a href="/wiki/Carolus_Linnaeus" title="Carolus Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a>, 1758. <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, western <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a></li>
<li><i>Ardea cinerea jouyi</i> Clark, 1907. Eastern Asia</li>
<li><i>Ardea cinerea firasa</i> Hartert, 1917. <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a></li>
<li><i>Ardea cinerea monicae</i> Jouanin &amp; Roux, 1963. Islands off <a href="/wiki/Banc_d%27Arguin" title="Banc d'Arguin" class="mw-redirect">Banc d'Arguin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>It is closely related and similar to the American <a href="/wiki/Great_Blue_Heron" title="Great Blue Heron">Great Blue Heron</a>, which differs in slightly larger size, and chestnut-brown flanks and thighs.</p>
<p><br /></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grey_Heron,_Leighton_Moss.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Grey_Heron%2C_Leighton_Moss.jpg/220px-Grey_Heron%2C_Leighton_Moss.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Grey_Heron%2C_Leighton_Moss.jpg/330px-Grey_Heron%2C_Leighton_Moss.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Grey_Heron%2C_Leighton_Moss.jpg/440px-Grey_Heron%2C_Leighton_Moss.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grey_Heron,_Leighton_Moss.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Grey Heron swallowing an eel</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It feeds in shallow water, catching <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a>, and insects with its long bill. Herons will also take small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a> and reptiles. They occasionally take birds up to the size of a <a href="/wiki/Water_Rail" title="Water Rail">Water Rail</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> It will often wait motionless for prey, or slowly stalk its victim.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: City life">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="City_life">City life</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:192px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg/190px-GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg" width="190" height="96" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg/285px-GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg/380px-GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GreyHeron3_MJF.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
<i>Ardea cinerea cinerea</i> with neck retracted</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, the Grey Heron has established itself over the past decades in great numbers in urban environments. In cities like <a href="/wiki/Amsterdam" title="Amsterdam">Amsterdam</a>, they are ever present and well adapted to modern city life. They hunt as usual but also visit street markets and snackbars. They are also present at the zoo during feeding times for <a href="/wiki/Penguins" title="Penguins" class="mw-redirect">penguins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pelicans" title="Pelicans" class="mw-redirect">pelicans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">seals</a>. Some individuals make use of people feeding them at their homes or recreational fishermen to share their catch. Similar behaviour on a smaller scale has been reported in Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>This species breeds in <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonies</a> in trees close to lakes, the seashore or other wetlands, although it will also nest in <a href="/wiki/Reedbed" title="Reedbed" class="mw-redirect">reedbeds</a>. It builds a bulky stick nest.</p>
