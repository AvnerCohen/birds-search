<p>The <b>Subalpine Warbler</b> (<i>Sylvia cantillans</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">typical warbler</a> which breeds in the southernmost areas of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and northwest <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, and winters along the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. It occurs as a vagrant well away from the breeding range, in both spring and autumn as far north as <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.</p>
<p>Like most <i>Sylvia</i> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, it has distinct male and female <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumages</a>. The adult male has a grey back and head, brick-red underparts, and white <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> streaks ("moustaches"). The female is mainly brown above, with a greyer head, and whitish below with a pink flush. The Subalpine Warbler's song is fast and rattling, and is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Whitethroat" title="Lesser Whitethroat">Lesser Whitethroat</a>.</p>
<p>This bird seems to be related to the <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_Warbler" title="Sardinian Warbler">Sardinian Warbler</a>-<a href="/wiki/Menetries%27_Warbler" title="Menetries' Warbler" class="mw-redirect">Menetries' Warbler</a> superspecies. They all have white <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> areas, the heads being dark above in adult males, and naked eye-rings. These three species are related to a superspecies consisting of <a href="/wiki/R%C3%BCppell%27s_Warbler" title="Rüppell's Warbler">Rüppell's Warbler</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cyprus_Warbler" title="Cyprus Warbler">Cyprus Warbler</a>, which also share the white malar area with blackish above.(Shirihai et al. 2001,<sup id="cite_ref-Shirihai_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shirihai-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Jønsson &amp; Fjeldså 2006 <sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>) The Subalpine Warbler is divided into three distinct <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> groups, which may possibly be sufficiently diverged to qualify as three separate <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> (Shirihai et al. 2001).<sup id="cite_ref-Shirihai_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shirihai-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The three groups have differing male plumages, distinctive calls, and are <a href="/wiki/Allopatric" title="Allopatric" class="mw-redirect">allopatric</a>; further study is needed.</p>
<p>These groups are as follows (areas given below are breeding ranges; all forms winter in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>):</p>
<ul>
<li><b>Western Subalpine Warbler</b> - subspecies <i>cantillans</i>, found in north-west and peninsular <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula" title="Iberian Peninsula">Iberia</a>, and <i>inornata</i> found in north-west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a></li>
<li><b>Eastern Subalpine Warbler</b> - subspecies <i>albistriata</i>, found from north-east <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> southeastwards to southern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a></li>
<li><b>Moltoni's (Subalpine) Warbler</b> - subspecies <i>moltonii</i>, an endemic <a href="/wiki/Taxon" title="Taxon">taxon</a> of the western <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> islands: <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>; and the <a href="/wiki/Apennine_Mountains#Ligurian_Apennines" title="Apennine Mountains">Ligurian Apennines</a><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<p>Eastern Subalpine Warbler differs from the nominate race by its deeper blue-grey upperparts, a blackish mask on the lores and ear-coverts, brick reddish-brown coloration confined to the throat and breast and sharply demarcated from a largely white belly, paler flanks and a wider white submoustachial stripe.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>This is a bird of dry open country, often on hill slopes, with bushes for nesting. The nest is built in low shrub or <a href="/wiki/Gorse" title="Gorse">gorse</a>, and 3-5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid. Like most "<a href="/wiki/Warbler" title="Warbler">warblers</a>", it is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" title="Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect">insectivorous</a>, but will also take berries.</p>
