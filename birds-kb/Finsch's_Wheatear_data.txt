<p><b>Finsch's wheatear</b> (<i>Oenanthe finschii</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wheatear" title="Wheatear">wheatear</a>, a small insectivorous <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> that was formerly classed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Turdidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Turdidae">Turdidae</a>, but is now more generally considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> of the family Muscicapidae.
</p><p>This 15–16&#160;cm long <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> breeds in semi-desert and stony hillsides from <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> east to <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">short-distance migrant</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Middle_East" title="Greater Middle East">Greater Middle East</a>. The nest is built in a rock crevice, and 4-5 eggs is the normal clutch.
</p><p>In summer the male Finsch's wheatear is a white and black bird. The white crown, central back and belly contrast with the black face, throat and wings. The tail and rump are white, with an inverted black T giving a pattern like <a href="/wiki/Black-eared_wheatear" title="Black-eared wheatear">black-eared wheatear</a>, but with a uniformly wide terminal band.
</p><p>The female is brown-grey above, becoming dirty white below. The tail pattern is similar to the male's.
</p><p>Finsch's wheatear feeds mainly on insects. Its call is a whistled <i>tsit</i>, and the song is a mix of clear notes with whistles and crackling.
</p><p>The common name and scientific name commemorate the German ethnographer, naturalist and colonial explorer <a href="/wiki/Friedrich_Hermann_Otto_Finsch" class="mw-redirect" title="Friedrich Hermann Otto Finsch">Friedrich Hermann Otto Finsch</a> (8 August 1839 – 31 January 1917, Braunschweig).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
