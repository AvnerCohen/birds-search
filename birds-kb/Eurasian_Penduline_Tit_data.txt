<p>The <b>Eurasian penduline tit</b> or <b>European penduline tit</b> (<i>Remiz pendulinus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird of the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Remiz" title="Remiz">Remiz</a></i>. The genus name is the Polish word for the Eurasian penduline tit, and <i>pendulinus</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "hanging down”, which refers to its nest.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is relatively widespread throughout the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>. The breeding range of the species in Western Europe experienced an expansion during the 1980s and 1990s.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was accompanied by an expansion of the species’ winter range and reached as far south as northern Morocco.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It builds an elaborate hanging nest, formerly used in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a> as children's slippers.<sup id="cite_ref-Harrap_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrap-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This species was first described as <i>Motacilla pendulinus</i> by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a> in his <i>Systema naturae</i> in 1758.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status_and_conservation">Status and conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_penduline_tit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Status and conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg/170px-Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg/255px-Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg/340px-Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2736" data-file-height="3648" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nest in Poland</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG/220px-Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="207" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG/330px-Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG/440px-Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2032" data-file-height="1912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Remiz_pendulinus_MWNH_1437.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>The penduline tit has a large range, estimated at 1-10&#160;million square kilometres (0.4-3.8&#160;million square miles), and a population estimated at 420,000–840,000 individuals in Europe alone, and there is evidence that the population is increasing. It is therefore not believed to meet the IUCN Red List threshold criterion of a population decline of more than 30% in ten years or three generations, and is evaluated as Least Concern.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
