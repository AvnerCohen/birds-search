<p>The <b>Eurasian Penduline Tit</b> or <b>European Penduline Tit</b> (<i>Remiz pendulinus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Remiz" title="Remiz">Remiz</a></i>. It is relatively widespread throughout <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. The Asian (sub)species include the <b>Black-headed Penduline Tit</b>, <i>Remiz macronyx</i>, the <b>White-crowned Penduline Tit</b>, <i>Remiz coronatus</i>, and the <b>Chinese Penduline Tit</b>, <i>Remiz consobrinus</i>.</p>
<p>It builds an elaborate hanging nest, formerly used in <a href="/wiki/Central_Europe" title="Central Europe">Central Europe</a> as children's slippers.<sup id="cite_ref-Harrap_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrap-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>This species was first described as <i>Motacilla pendulinus</i> by <a href="/wiki/Carolus_Linnaeus" title="Carolus Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a> in his <i>Systema naturae</i> in 1758.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Penduline_Tit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg/170px-Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg/255px-Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg/340px-Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Remiz_pendulinis_6.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Nest in Poland</div>

<p>Penduline Tit has a large range, estimated at 1-10&#160;million square kilometres (0.4-3.8&#160;million square miles), and a population estimated at 420,000–840,000 individuals in Europe alone, and there is evidence that the population is increasing. It is therefore not believed to meet the IUCN Red List threshold criterion of a population decline of more than 30% in ten years or three generations, and is evaluated as Least Concern.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN2012_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN2012-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
