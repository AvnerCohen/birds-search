<p>The <b>collared pratincole</b> (<i>Glareola pratincola</i>), also known as the <b>common pratincole</b> or <b>red-winged pratincole</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pratincole" title="Pratincole">pratincole</a> family, Glareolidae. As with other pratincoles, it is native to the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sinc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sinc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The genus name is a diminutive of  <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>glarea</i>, "gravel", referring to a typical nesting habitat for pratincoles. The species name <i>pratincola</i> means an inhabitant of meadows, from  <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>pratum, prati</i>,  "meadow" and <i>incola</i>,  "inhabitant",  from <i>incolere</i>, "to inhabit".<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This pratincole is 24–28&#160;cm (9.4–11.0&#160;in) long with a 60–70&#160;cm (24–28&#160;in) wingspan. It has short legs, long pointed wings, a long forked tail, and a short bill, which is an adaptation to aerial feeding. The back and head are brown, and the wings are brown with darker flight feathers. The belly is white. The underwings are chestnut, but look dark below.
</p><p>Very good views are needed to distinguish this species from other pratincoles, such as the <a href="/wiki/Black-winged_pratincole" title="Black-winged pratincole">black-winged pratincole</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Oriental_pratincole" title="Oriental pratincole">oriental pratincole</a>, which may occur in its range. The latter species also has a chestnut underwing, but is shorter-tailed.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The collared pratincole is divided into two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>:<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>G. p. pratincola</i>, <small>(<a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a>, 1766)</small>: <a href="/wiki/Southern_Europe" title="Southern Europe">southern Europe</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a></li>
<li><i>G. p. fuelleborni</i>, <small>(<a href="/wiki/Oscar_Neumann" title="Oscar Neumann">Neumann</a>, 1910)</small>: <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pratincoles are unusual among <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a> in that they typically hunt their <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insect</a> prey on the wing like <a href="/wiki/Hirundinidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Hirundinidae">swallows</a>, although they can also feed on the ground.
</p>

<p>The species lays 2–4 eggs on the ground.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The collared pratincole is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of open country, and is often seen near water in the evening, hawking for insects. It is found in the warmer parts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, southwest <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in tropical Africa, and is rare north of the breeding range.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status_and_conservation">Status and conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Collared_pratincole&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Status and conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The collared pratincole is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
