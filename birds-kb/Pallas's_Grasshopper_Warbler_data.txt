<p><b>Pallas's grasshopper warbler</b> (<i>Helopsaltes certhiola</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grass_warbler" title="Grass warbler">grass warbler</a> genus <i>Helopsaltes</i>. It breeds in the east <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> - Siberia to Manchuria and Sea of Japan, NE China, Altai Mountains, Sayan Mountains and Transbaikalia
Sea of Okhotsk and Kamchatka. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering from <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> east to <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, where it is a rare migrant in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_grasshopper_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird was named after the German zoologist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">Peter Simon Pallas</a>. The specific <i>certhiola</i> is a diminutive from the genus <i>Certhia</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Treecreeper" title="Treecreeper">treecreepers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The sixth edition of <i><a href="/wiki/Clements_Checklist" class="mw-redirect" title="Clements Checklist">Clements Checklist</a></i> <sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> refers to this species as "Pallas's warbler", a name more commonly used for <i><a href="/wiki/Phylloscopus_proregulus" class="mw-redirect" title="Phylloscopus proregulus">Phylloscopus proregulus</a>.</i>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_grasshopper_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is found in tall grass with some thicker vegetation, usually close to water in bogs or wet meadows. From 4 to 7 eggs are laid in a nest on the ground in grass. This species is a very rare <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a> to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. One of the best places to see this <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/skulk" class="extiw" title="wikt:skulk">skulking</a> species as a vagrant is <a href="/wiki/Fair_Isle" title="Fair Isle">Fair Isle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shetland_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Shetland Islands">Shetland</a>; for a species that only rarely appears in western Europe, it can be found there with some regularity.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_grasshopper_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a medium-sized warbler. The adult has a streaked brown back, whitish grey underparts, unstreaked except on the undertail. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers, but young birds are yellower below. Like most warblers, it is insectivorous. It is very similar to the <a href="/wiki/Common_grasshopper_warbler" title="Common grasshopper warbler">common grasshopper warbler</a>, but is slightly larger, has white tips to the tail and tertial feathers, and a warmer brown rump. The white tips are the reason for its <a href="/wiki/Colloquial" class="mw-redirect" title="Colloquial">colloquial</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mnemonic" title="Mnemonic">mnemonic</a> name of "<a href="/wiki/PG_Tips" title="PG Tips">PG Tips</a>".
</p><p>This is a skulky species which is very difficult to see except sometimes when singing. It creeps through grass and low foliage.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_grasshopper_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The song is not the mechanical insect-like reeling produced by the common grasshopper warbler and some other <i>Locustella</i> warblers, but an inventive <i>Acrocephalus</i>-like melody.
</p>
