<p>The <b>Mistle Thrush</b> (<i>Turdus viscivorus</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae.</p>
<p>It is found in open woods and cultivated land over all of Europe and much of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. Many northern birds move south during the winter, with <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrating</a> birds sometimes forming small flocks.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistle_Thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>This species was first described by <a href="/wiki/Carolus_Linnaeus" title="Carolus Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a> in his <i>Systema naturae</i> in 1758 under its current scientific name.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a> name refers to its mistletoe eating, as does the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">scientific</a> name, which is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> words <i>Turdus</i>, "thrush", and <i>viscivorus</i> meaning "mistletoe eater".</p>
<p>A recent molecular study places the Mistle Thrush's closest relatives as the similarly plumaged <a href="/wiki/Song_Thrush" title="Song Thrush">Song Thrush</a> (<i>T. philomelos</i>) and the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Thrush" title="Chinese Thrush">Chinese Thrush</a> (<i>T. mupinensis</i>), all three species early offshoots from the main <i>Turdus</i> radiation around the world, and hence only distantly related to other European species such as the <a href="/wiki/Common_Blackbird" title="Common Blackbird">Common Blackbird</a> (<i>T. merula</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistle_Thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Mistle Thrush is the largest thrush native to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, being distinctly larger than the similar <a href="/wiki/Song_Thrush" title="Song Thrush">Song Thrush</a> and larger even than the <a href="/wiki/Common_Blackbird" title="Common Blackbird">Common Blackbird</a>. The species measures 25 to 30 cm (9.8 to 12 in) in length with a wingspan of 42 to 50 cm (17 to 20 in).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> Body mass can vary from 93 to 167 g (3.3 to 5.9 oz), with an average of around 115&#160;g (4.1&#160;oz).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HBW_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The sexes are similar, with plain greyish brown backs and neatly round-spotted underparts. The breast has much less buff than the <a href="/wiki/Song_Thrush" title="Song Thrush">Song Thrush</a>.</p>

<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistle_Thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span></h3>
<p>The male sings its loud melodious song from a tree, rooftop or other elevated perch, often during bad weather or at night, and starting relatively early in the spring — hence the Mistle Thrush's old name of "Stormcock". The song is like a harder and simpler version of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Blackbird" title="Common Blackbird">Blackbird's</a>. The alarm call is said to sound like a <a href="/wiki/Football_rattle" title="Football rattle" class="mw-redirect">football rattle</a> (a form of <a href="/wiki/Ratchet_(instrument)" title="Ratchet (instrument)">musical ratchet</a>) or machine gun.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistle_Thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span></h3>
<p>It is omnivorous, eating insects, worms, small reptiles, seeds and berries. A Mistle Thrush will defend a berry-bearing tree against other thrushes in winter. <a href="/wiki/Viscum_album" title="Viscum album">Mistletoe</a> berries are amongst its diet.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Mistle_Thrush&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>They nest in trees, laying several <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a neat cup-shaped nest lined with grass.</p>
