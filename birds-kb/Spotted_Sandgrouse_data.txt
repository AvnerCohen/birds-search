<p>The <b>Spotted Sandgrouse</b> (<i>Pterocles senegallus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of ground dwelling <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pteroclididae" title="Pteroclididae" class="mw-redirect">Pteroclididae</a> family. It is found in dry areas of northern and eastern Africa and across parts of Asia as far east as north west India. It is listed as being of "Least concern" in the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN2012_0-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN2012-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Spotted Sandgrouse reaches a length of about 33 centimetres (13&#160;in). The male has a small reddish-brown nape surrounded by a band of pale grey that extends to the bill and round the neck in a collar. The chin, neck and throat are orange and the breast grey. The upper parts are pinkish-grey with blackish patches on the wings, tail and lower belly. The female also has an orange throat region but is generally duller in plumage than the male. The body colour is greyish-brown liberally spotted with small dark markings and with dark patches on the wings, tail and lower belly. The central tail feathers in both sexes are elongated but not to the extent that they are in the <a href="/wiki/Pin-tailed_Sandgrouse" title="Pin-tailed Sandgrouse">Pin-tailed Sandgrouse</a> (<i>Pterocles alchata</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>The Spotted Sandgrouse is found in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. It inhabits arid and semi-arid countryside and is largely resident although there is some local movement of flocks.<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The population size has not been firmly stablished, but the bird seems to be common over most of its extensive range and the species is not considered to be vulnerable.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>The Spotted Sandgrouse has a rapid wing beat and flies swiftly. Its call is a musical "queeto-queeto" which distinguishes it from other species of sandgrouse with similar plumage.<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Spotted Sandgrouse is a ground-dwelling bird and feeds on seeds and other plant material that it finds in the scrubby vegetation of its dry habitat. It needs to make a daily journey to a drinking hole, which may be many kilometres from its feeding ground.<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> It nests in a hollow in the earth and two, occasionally three, eggs are laid in February or March. The eggs are elongated ovals in shape, buff with grey and brown speckles. Both parents incubate the eggs, which hatch after about 20 days, and both tend the young. Like other sandgrouse, they shade the chicks from the heat of the midday sun and they bring water to them in specially adapted, absorbent feathers on the breast.<sup id="cite_ref-Gooders_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gooders-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
