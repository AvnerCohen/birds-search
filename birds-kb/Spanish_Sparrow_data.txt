<p>The <b>Spanish sparrow</b> or <b>willow sparrow</b> (<i>Passer hispaniolensis</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird of the <a href="/wiki/Old_World_sparrow" title="Old World sparrow">sparrow</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> Passeridae. It is found in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean region">Mediterranean region</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">south-west and central Asia</a>. It is very similar to the closely related <a href="/wiki/House_sparrow" title="House sparrow">house sparrow</a>, and the two species show their close relation in a "biological mix-up" of hybridisation in the Mediterranean region, which complicates the taxonomy of this species.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands,_Spain_-female-8_(1).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands%2C_Spain_-female-8_%281%29.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands%2C_Spain_-female-8_%281%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands%2C_Spain_-female-8_%281%29.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands%2C_Spain_-female-8_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands%2C_Spain_-female-8_%281%29.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands%2C_Spain_-female-8_%281%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_-Canary_Islands,_Spain_-female-8_(1).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Female in the Canary Islands</div></div></div>
<p>The Spanish sparrow is a rather large sparrow, at 15–16&#160;cm (6–6.5&#160;in) in length, and 22–36&#160;g (0.78–1.27&#160;oz) in weight. It is slightly larger and heavier than house sparrows, and also has a slightly longer and stouter bill.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The male is similar to the house sparrow in plumage, but differs in that its underparts are heavily streaked with black, has a <a href="/wiki/Chestnut_(color)" title="Chestnut (color)">chestnut</a> rather than grey crown, and has white rather than grey cheeks.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The female is effectively inseparable from the house sparrow in its basic plumage, which is grey-brown overall but more boldly marked. The female has light streaking on its sides, a pale cream <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, and broad cream streaks on its back.<sup id="cite_ref-Summers164–165_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summers164–165-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two subspecies of the Spanish sparrow – the <b>western Spanish sparrow</b> (<i>P. h. hispaniolensis</i>) and <b>eastern Spanish sparrow</b> (<i>P. h. transcaspicus</i>) – are recognised, with little visible difference between them in worn breeding plumage. They are more easily distinguished in fresh winter plumage, with the eastern subspecies <i>P. h. transcaspicus</i> being paler with less chestnut.<sup id="cite_ref-Summers164–165_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summers164–165-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="66" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg 2x" data-file-width="767" data-file-height="229" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_song_algeria.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sonogram of a male Spanish sparrow's song</div></div></div>
<p>The Spanish sparrow's <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">vocalisations</a> are similar to those of the house sparrow. The male gives a call somewhat different from that of the house sparrow when displaying at its nest. This call is a pair of strident, disyllabic chirps, similar to those of the house sparrow, but louder and high-pitched, transcribed as <i>chweeng-chweeng, cheela-cheeli</i>. A similar call, softer and more like the house sparrow's <i>tschilp</i>, is used by birds arriving or departing at roosting sites. The Spanish sparrow's other calls are almost the same as those of the house sparrow. A soft <i>quer quer quer</i> is given at the nest by mated pairs, a <i>quer-it</i> <a href="/wiki/Flight_call" title="Flight call">flight call</a> is given by flocking birds, and a <i>chur-chur-it</i> call is given as a threat.<sup id="cite_ref-Summers177_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summers177-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg/170px-PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="228" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg/255px-PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg/340px-PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg 2x" data-file-width="472" data-file-height="634" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PasserHispaniolensisGould.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An illustration by <a href="/wiki/John_Gould" title="John Gould">John Gould</a> of a male Spanish sparrow (above) and an Italian sparrow pair</div></div></div>
<p>The Spanish sparrow is a close relative of the house sparrow in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Passer" title="Passer">Passer</a></i> and the sparrow family Passeridae.<sup id="cite_ref-Arn1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arn1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Its taxonomy is greatly complicated by the "biological mix-up" it forms with the house sparrow in the Mediterranean. In most of the Mediterranean, one or both of the two species occurs, with only a limited degree of <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(biology)" title="Hybrid (biology)">hybridisation</a>. On the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Peninsula" title="Italian Peninsula">Italian Peninsula</a> and <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, the two species are replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Italian_sparrow" title="Italian sparrow">Italian sparrow</a>, a puzzling type of sparrow apparently intermediate between the Spanish sparrow and the house sparrow.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Töpfer_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Töpfer-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Italian sparrow has been classified as a hybrid with the house sparrow, the same species as the Spanish sparrow, the same species as the house sparrow, and as a separate species. The Spanish sparrow also hybridises freely with house sparrow in parts of northern Africa (north-eastern <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tunisia" title="Tunisia">Tunisia</a>, and north-western <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>), forming highly variable mixed populations with a full range of characters from pure house sparrow to pure Spanish sparrow.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Töpfer_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Töpfer-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On the Mediterranean islands of <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gozo" title="Gozo">Gozo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhodes" title="Rhodes">Rhodes</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Karpathos" title="Karpathos">Karpathos</a>, there are more apparently intermediate birds of unknown status.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Phylogenetic studies of nuclear mitochondrial DNA pseudogenes show that the house sparrow is closely related genetically to the Italian sparrow but not the Spanish sparrow.<sup id="cite_ref-Arn1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Arn1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Spanish sparrow was first described by the Dutch zoologist <a href="/wiki/Coenraad_Jacob_Temminck" title="Coenraad Jacob Temminck">Coenraad Jacob Temminck</a> as <i>Fringilla hispaniolensis</i>, from a specimen collected at <a href="/wiki/Algeciras" title="Algeciras">Algeciras</a>, in southern Spain. The usual English name refers to the description of the species from Spain. The name <i>willow sparrow</i>, referring to the moist habitat of this bird, is sometimes used, especially when the Italian sparrow is considered the same species.<sup id="cite_ref-Töpfer_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Töpfer-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Summers162–163_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summers162–163-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The genus name <i>Passer</i> is the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for sparrow, and <i>hispaniolensis</i> is  <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> for "Spanish".<sup id="cite_ref-job_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of Spanish sparrow are usually recognised, the western <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate subspecies</a> <i>hispaniolensis</i>, and the eastern <i>transcaspicus</i>, described by Austrian ornithologist <a href="/wiki/Viktor_von_Tschusi_zu_Schmidhoffen" title="Viktor von Tschusi zu Schmidhoffen">Viktor von Tschusi zu Schmidhoffen</a> in 1902 from <a href="/wiki/%C3%9Dol%C3%B6ten" title="Ýolöten">Ýolöten</a>, Turkmenistan.<sup id="cite_ref-Summers162–163_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summers162–163-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Birds in <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> are usually considered to belong to <i>P. h. transcaspicus</i>, but birds as far east as <a href="/wiki/Ceylanp%C4%B1nar" title="Ceylanpınar">Ceylanpınar</a> have been noted as intermediates, and the difference between the two subspecies may be <a href="/wiki/Cline_(biology)" title="Cline (biology)">clinal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersTranscaspicus_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersTranscaspicus-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Spanish sparrow has a highly complex distribution in the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean region">Mediterranean region</a>, <a href="/wiki/Macaronesia" title="Macaronesia">Macaronesia</a>, and south-west to central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It breeds mostly in a band of <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a> about fifteen degrees wide, from the <a href="/wiki/Danube" title="Danube">Danube</a> valley and the <a href="/wiki/Aral_Sea" title="Aral Sea">Aral Sea</a> in the north to <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a> and central <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> in the south.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Its range has expanded greatly by natural colonisation over the last two centuries, in the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>, where it reached <a href="/wiki/Romania" title="Romania">Romania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">Moldova</a> from 1950 onwards;<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and in Macaronesia, where its range expansion has been attributed to introductions and travel by ship, but was more likely natural colonisation by migrating birds.<sup id="cite_ref-colonists_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colonists-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">Vagrants</a> occur widely, as far north as <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Norway" title="Norway">Norway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A study has shown that vagrant individuals tend to occur very close to railway lines, much closer than with other vagrant passerines. This results suggests that Spanish sparrows' vagrancy and expansion may be facilitated in part by cargo trains.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies_hispaniolensis">Subspecies <i>hispaniolensis</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Subspecies hispaniolensis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg/170px-Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="179" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg/255px-Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg/340px-Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg 2x" data-file-width="994" data-file-height="1047" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_male_on_wire.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A male in Sardinia</div></div></div>
<p>The western subspecies <i>hispaniolensis</i> breeds in parts of <a href="/wiki/Iberia" class="mw-redirect" title="Iberia">Iberia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>, some islands, and the Balkans. In Iberia it is uncommon, occurring in the <a href="/wiki/Tagus" title="Tagus">Tagus</a> valley and sporadically in the northern <a href="/wiki/Meseta_Central" class="mw-redirect" title="Meseta Central">meseta</a>, the eastern coast, and in the <a href="/wiki/Guadalquivir" title="Guadalquivir">Guadalquivir</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guadiana" title="Guadiana">Guadiana</a> valleys.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> While the house sparrow and the Spanish sparrow form a "hybrid swarm" in the eastern half of the <a href="/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">Maghreb</a>, they coexist with little hybridisation in the western half.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In northern Italy and Corsica the Spanish sparrow is replaced by the Italian sparrow, and the two <a href="/wiki/Intergradation" title="Intergradation">intergrade</a> in southern Italy, as well as <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crete" title="Crete">Crete</a>, and nearby islands such as Rhodes.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The Spanish sparrow is not known to breed in the <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Aegean_Islands" title="Aegean Islands">Aegean Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corfu" title="Corfu">Corfu</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/Peloponnese" title="Peloponnese">Peloponnese</a>, but it occurs on Sardinia, <a href="/wiki/Pantelleria" title="Pantelleria">Pantelleria</a>, and smaller islands near the coast. In the Balkans, it occurs patchily from <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> across into the Danube valley of Romania and northern Serbia. It is found in mainland <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bulgaria" title="Bulgaria">Bulgaria</a>, where it is also uncommon.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Spanish sparrow is likely to have been established on the western Canary Islands for some time, as it was found on <a href="/wiki/Lanzarote" title="Lanzarote">Lanzarote</a> when a naturalist first visited the island in 1828. In the 1830s, it was recorded on <a href="/wiki/Fuerteventura" title="Fuerteventura">Fuerteventura</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gran_Canaria" title="Gran Canaria">Gran Canaria</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tenerife" title="Tenerife">Tenerife</a> and since the 1940s it has reached all the other islands.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-colonists_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colonists-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It reached Madeira in May 1935, when numbers of sparrows were found across the island after nine days of strong, continuous easterly winds.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-colonists_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colonists-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It seems to have reached Cape Verde around the same time it reached the Canaries, and it was first recorded there on <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Cape_Verde" title="Santiago, Cape Verde">Santiago</a> by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a> in 1832. From then onwards it reached all the other larger islands, in a poorly recorded extension of its range.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-colonists_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colonists-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies_transcaspicus">Subspecies <i>transcaspicus</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Subspecies transcaspicus">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="134" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="971" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis-2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A male and a female of the eastern subspecies (<i>P. h. transcaspicus</i>) in southeastern Turkey</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg/170px-Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg/255px-Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg/340px-Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1517" data-file-height="1752" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_Israel_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A male of the eastern subspecies (<i>P. h. transcaspicus</i>) in Israel</div></div></div>
<p>The eastern subspecies <i>transcaspicus</i> breeds from <a href="/wiki/Anatolia" title="Anatolia">Anatolia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> through the Middle East and Central Asia to far western China. In the Middle East it breeds through <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> to about as far south as <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>. It breeds in eastern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, but is a very rare breeder in <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersTranscaspicus_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersTranscaspicus-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> It breeds sporadically in <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dagestan" title="Dagestan">Dagestan</a>, north to the <a href="/wiki/Terek_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Terek River">Terek River</a> valley. In Iran, it breeds in most of the country except the Persian Gulf region, also breeding in central and northern Afghanistan. In Central Asia, it breeds from the regions of the <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>-Iran and <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>-Afghanistan borders north to parts of the <a href="/wiki/Syr_Darya" title="Syr Darya">Syr Darya</a> basin in Kazakhstan, and westwards to <a href="/wiki/Lake_Alakol" title="Lake Alakol">Lake Alakol</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Karatal_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Karatal River">Karatal River</a>, and a corner of China.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersTranscaspicus_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersTranscaspicus-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Here it has also expanded its range, in the area around Lake Alakol in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, where agriculture was not developed until the 1950s.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> It winters in the plains of the Indian subcontinent and the Persian Gulf.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersTranscaspicus_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersTranscaspicus-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1181" data-file-height="787" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_nid_Oranie.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A nest in an acacia hedge in <a href="/wiki/Es_S%C3%A9nia" title="Es Sénia">Es Sénia</a>, Algeria</div></div></div>
<p>In most of its range, the Spanish sparrow occurs alongside the house sparrow. In such areas, both species breed in farmland and open <a href="/wiki/Woodland" title="Woodland">woodland</a>, with the Spanish sparrow preferring moister habitats. In areas where house sparrows are absent, the Spanish sparrow may live in urban habitats, as in the Canary Islands, Madeira, and some Mediterranean islands. In a few urban areas, such as those in eastern <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, the primary sparrow species is the <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_tree_sparrow" title="Eurasian tree sparrow">Eurasian tree sparrow</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Before the Spanish sparrow arrived in the Canary Islands and Madeira, the <a href="/wiki/Rock_sparrow" title="Rock sparrow">rock sparrow</a> was the sole native sparrow. In the Canaries, the Spanish sparrow occurs in most habitats, having ousted the rock sparrow from all but the driest localities. In Madeira the Spanish sparrow is common in cultivated areas, but it has not fully adapted to nesting in buildings or breeding in the drier north of the island.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-colonists_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colonists-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The Spanish sparrow is not common on most of the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> islands, due to the presence of the <a href="/wiki/Endemism" title="Endemism">endemic</a> <a href="/wiki/Iago_sparrow" title="Iago sparrow">Iago sparrow</a>, and the house sparrow on <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Vicente,_Cape_Verde" title="São Vicente, Cape Verde">São Vicente</a>. On <a href="/wiki/Fogo,_Cape_Verde" title="Fogo, Cape Verde">Fogo</a>, where it is the sole species of sparrow, it is common in all habitats, breeding both in the houses of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Filipe,_Cape_Verde" title="São Filipe, Cape Verde">São Filipe</a> and on the cliff walls of the volcano <a href="/wiki/Pico_do_Fogo" title="Pico do Fogo">Pico do Fogo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersHispaniolensis_17-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersHispaniolensis-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-colonists_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-colonists-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="185" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1224" data-file-height="1028" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_parade.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Males displaying in an acacia hedge in Es Sénia, Algeria</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1073" data-file-height="733" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_nest_Lesbos.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A male in a nest in <a href="/wiki/Lesbos" title="Lesbos">Lesbos</a>, Greece</div></div></div>
<p>The Spanish sparrow is strongly gregarious, flocking and breeding in groups. In the winter, it mostly wanders nomadically or makes regular <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Little is known of the Spanish sparrow's survival, and the maximum age recorded is 11 years.<sup id="cite_ref-Summers177_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Summers177-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like other sparrows, it feeds principally on the seeds of grains and other grasses, also eating leaves, fruits, and other plant materials. Young birds are fed mostly on insects, and adults also feed on insects and other animals during and before the breeding season.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersFood_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersFood-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Nestlings are fed almost exclusively on insects for their first few days, and are gradually fed larger amounts of grains.<sup id="cite_ref-GavrilovJBNHS_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GavrilovJBNHS-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The portion of insects in nestling diets is recorded at a range from 75 to over 90 percent.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> In preying on insects, the Spanish sparrow is opportunistic, feeding on whichever insects are most common.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In Central Asia, these are caterpillars, ants, grasshoppers, and crickets.<sup id="cite_ref-SummersFood_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SummersFood-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> While migrating through Central Asia in the spring, the Spanish sparrow feeds mostly on crops in cultivated areas, and while breeding it feeds mostly on insects, wild plants, and seeds from the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-GavrilovJBNHS_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GavrilovJBNHS-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg/220px-Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg/330px-Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg/440px-Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3512" data-file-height="3678" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Passer_hispaniolensis_hispaniolensis_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.208.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs from the collection of the <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9um_de_Toulouse" title="Muséum de Toulouse">Muséum de Toulouse</a></div></div></div>
<p>The Spanish sparrow nests in large colonies of closely spaced or even multiple shared nests. Nests are usually placed in trees or bushes, amongst branches or underneath the nests of larger birds such as <a href="/wiki/White_stork" title="White stork">white storks</a>. Colonies may hold from ten pairs to hundreds of thousands of pairs.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Each pair lays 3–8 eggs, which hatch in 12 days, with the chicks fledging when about 14 days old.<sup id="cite_ref-bwp_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GavrilovIbis_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GavrilovIbis-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Males spend more time constructing nests than females.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_sparrow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The European population of the Spanish sparrow comprises between 2,800,000 and 6,200,000 breeding pairs or 8,400,000–18,600,000 individuals. Partly from the European population, the global population is estimated to be between 17 and 74 million individuals.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BiE_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BiE-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> There have been population decreases in some parts of Europe, but in other areas the population has increased<sup id="cite_ref-BiE_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BiE-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Dinetti_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dinetti-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and the species is not seriously threatened, so it is assessed as <a href="/wiki/Least_concern" class="mw-redirect" title="Least concern">least concern</a> on the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
