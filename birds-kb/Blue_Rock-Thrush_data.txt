<p>The <b>Blue Rock Thrush</b> (<i>Monticola solitarius</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chat</a>. This <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a>-like <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a> was formerly placed in the <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Turdidae" title="Turdidae" class="mw-redirect">Turdidae</a>.</p>
<p>This species breeds in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Europe" title="Southern Europe">southern Europe</a> and northwest <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and from <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">central Asia</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.</p>
<p>The European, <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">north African</a> and southeast Asian birds are mainly resident, apart from altitudinal movements. Other Asian populations are more <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">southeast Asia</a>. This bird is a very uncommon visitor to <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">northern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Europe" title="Western Europe">western Europe</a>.</p>
<p>Blue Rock Thrush breeds in open mountainous areas, usually higher than the breeding zone of the related <a href="/wiki/Common_Rock_Thrush" title="Common Rock Thrush">Common Rock Thrush</a>. It nests in rock cavities and walls, and usually lays 3-5 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a>. An <a href="/wiki/Omnivore" title="Omnivore">omnivore</a>, the Blue Rock Thrush eats a wide variety of <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and small reptiles in addition to berries and seeds.</p>
<p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Starling" title="Starling">starling</a>-sized bird, 21–23&#160;cm in length with a long slim bill. The summer male is unmistakable, with all blue-grey plumage apart from its darker wings. Females and immatures are much less striking, with dark brown upperparts, and paler brown scaly underparts. Both sexes lack the reddish outer tail feathers of Rock Thrush.</p>
<p>The male Blue Rock Thrush sings a clear, melodious call that is similar to, but louder than the call of the Rock Thrush.</p>
<p>The Blue Rock Thrush is <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>'s national bird and is shown on the <a href="/wiki/Maltese_lira" title="Maltese lira">Lm 1 coins</a> that was part of the previous currency of the country.</p>
