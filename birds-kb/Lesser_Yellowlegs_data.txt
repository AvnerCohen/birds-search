<p>The <b>Lesser Yellowlegs</b> (<i>Tringa flavipes</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Shorebird" title="Shorebird" class="mw-redirect">shorebird</a> similar in appearance to the larger <a href="/wiki/Greater_Yellowlegs" title="Greater Yellowlegs">Greater Yellowlegs</a>. It is not closely related to this bird, however, but instead to the much larger and quite dissimilar <a href="/wiki/Willet" title="Willet">Willet</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> merely the fine, clear and dense pattern of the neck shown in breeding plumage indicates these species' actual relationships.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is clearings near ponds in the <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">boreal forest</a> region from <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> to <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. They nest on the ground, usually in open dry locations.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Yellowlegs_chicks.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Lesser_Yellowlegs_chicks.JPG/220px-Lesser_Yellowlegs_chicks.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Yellowlegs_chicks.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Chicks</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>They <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf</a> coast of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and south to <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.</p>
<p>This species is a regular vagrant to western Europe, and the odd bird has wintered in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>.</p>
<p>These birds forage in shallow water <a rel="nofollow" class="external autonumber" href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ewP58XJLO2I">[1]</a>, sometimes using their bill to stir up the water. They mainly eat insects, small fish and crustaceans.</p>
<p>The call of this bird is softer than that of the Greater Yellowlegs.</p>
