<p>The <b>lesser yellowlegs</b> (<i>Tringa flavipes</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Shorebird" class="mw-redirect" title="Shorebird">shorebird</a>. The genus name <i>Tringa</i> is the <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> name given to the <a href="/wiki/Green_sandpiper" title="Green sandpiper">green sandpiper</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ulisse_Aldrovandi" title="Ulisse Aldrovandi">Aldrovandus</a> in 1599 based on <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>trungas</i>, a thrush-sized, white-rumped, tail-bobbing wading bird mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a>. The specific <i>flavipes</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>flavus</i>, "yellow", and <i>pes</i>, "foot".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_yellowlegs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is similar in appearance to the larger <a href="/wiki/Greater_yellowlegs" title="Greater yellowlegs">greater yellowlegs</a>, although it is more closely related to the much larger <a href="/wiki/Willet" title="Willet">willet</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> the fine, clear and dense pattern of the neck shown in breeding plumage indicates these species' actual relationships.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_yellowlegs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lesser yellowlegs is a medium-large shorebird. The legs are yellow. Compared to the greater yellowlegs, the bill is shorter (visually about the same length as the head), slim, straight, and uniformly dark. The breast is streaked and the flanks are finely marked with short bars.<sup id="cite_ref-NGSBirds_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NGSBirds-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Measurements</b><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Length</b>: 9.1-10.6 in (23-27 cm)</li>
<li><b>Weight</b>: 2.8-3.2 oz (79.5-90.9 g)</li>
<li><b>Wingspan</b>: 23.2-25.2 in (59-64 cm)</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_yellowlegs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Their breeding habitat is clearings near ponds in the <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">boreal forest</a> region from <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> to <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. They nest on the ground, usually in open dry locations.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Migration">Migration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_yellowlegs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Migration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>They <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf</a> coast of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">Caribbean</a>, and south to <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.
This species is a regular vagrant to western Europe; in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> about five birds arrive each year, mostly between August and October,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> with the occasional individual overwintering.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_yellowlegs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These birds forage in shallow water, sometimes using their bill to stir up the water. They mainly eat insects, small fish and crustaceans.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Call">Call</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_yellowlegs&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Call">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The call of this bird is softer than that of the greater yellowlegs.
</p>
