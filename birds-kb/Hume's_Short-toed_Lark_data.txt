<p><b>Hume's short-toed lark</b> (<i>Calandrella acutirostris</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Alaudidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Alaudidae">Alaudidae</a>. It is found in south-central Asia from Iran and Kazakhstan to China.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name commemorates the British naturalist <a href="/wiki/Allan_Octavian_Hume" title="Allan Octavian Hume">Allan Octavian Hume</a> who described the species.<sup id="cite_ref-Beolens2003_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beolens2003-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The alternate name <b>short-toed lark</b> may also be used for three other species in the genus <i>Calandrella</i>. The alternate name <b>lesser short-toed lark</b> should not be confused with the species of the same name, <i><a href="/wiki/Alaudala_rufescens" class="mw-redirect" title="Alaudala rufescens">Alaudala rufescens</a></i>. Other alternate names for Hume's short-toed lark include <b>Hume's lark</b> and <b>Karakoram short-toed lark</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<ul><li><i>C. a. acutirostris</i> - <small><a href="/wiki/Allan_Octavian_Hume" title="Allan Octavian Hume">Hume</a>, 1873</small>: Found from north-eastern Iran and eastern Kazakhstan to western China</li>
<li><b>Tibet short-toed lark</b> (<i>C. a. tibetana</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/William_Edwin_Brooks" title="William Edwin Brooks">Brooks, WE</a>, 1880</small>: Originally described as a separate species. Found from north-eastern Pakistan to <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau" title="Tibetan Plateau">Tibetan Plateau</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hume%27s_short-toed_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Hume's short-toed lark is similar in size and appearance to the <a href="/wiki/Greater_short-toed_lark" title="Greater short-toed lark">greater short-toed lark</a> but is generally a duller-looking bird with slightly darker plumage and a slightly smaller beak. As with the greater short-toed lark, the colour varies across the broad range and is not a good distinguishing feature. Hume's short-toed lark grows to a length of from 13 to 14&#160;cm (5.1 to 5.5&#160;in) and the sexes are similar. The crown is brown with slight diffuse streaking, the cheeks are rufous-brown and the <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a> white. The upper parts are greyish-brown or sandy brown with darker streaking, and the upper tail coverts are washed with rufous-brown. The wings are greyish-brown with black barring and pale tips to the feathers. The underparts are mostly whitish, but there is a dark neck patch and a buffish-grey breast band. The breast is unstreaked. The voice helps distinguish this species; vocalisations include a shrill "<i>trree</i>" and a more rolling "<i>drreep</i>".<sup id="cite_ref-Beaman_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beaman-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
