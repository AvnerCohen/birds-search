<p>The <b>Yelkouan Shearwater</b>, <b>Levantine Shearwater</b> or <b>Mediterranean Shearwater</b> (<i>Puffinus yelkouan</i><sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Shearwater" title="Shearwater">shearwater</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> family <a href="/wiki/Procellariidae" title="Procellariidae">Procellariidae</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Yelkouan_Shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description and identification">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description_and_identification">Description and identification</span></h2>
<p>Yelkouan Shearwaters are 30–38&#160;cm long, with a 76–89&#160;cm wingspan. It has the typically "shearing" flight of the genus, dipping from side to side on stiff wings with few wingbeats, the wingtips almost touching the water. This bird looks like a flying cross, with its wing held at right angles to the body, and it changes from very dark brown to white as the dark upperparts and paler undersides are alternately exposed as it travels low over the sea.</p>
<p>It is silent at sea, but at night the breeding colonies are alive with raucous cackling calls, higher pitched and more drawn out than the Manx Shearwater's.</p>
<p>The Yelkouan Shearwater has a more contrasted appearance than the Balearic Shearwater with which its winter range overlaps, since the latter species is brown above and dirty white below. It is very similar to the black-and-white Manx Shearwater of the Atlantic, and stray birds out of their usual range are very difficult to identify with certainty.</p>
<p>Also, at least one mixed breeding colony of the Yelkouan and the Balearic Shearwater exists on <a href="/wiki/Minorca" title="Minorca">Minorca</a>. A study of these birds recommended that a combination of <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(biology)" title="Morphology (biology)">morphological</a> characteristics and <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" title="DNA sequence" class="mw-redirect">DNA sequence</a> data should be required at least for scientific purposes to assign individual birds to either species.<sup id="cite_ref-genovartetal2005_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genovartetal2005-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Yelkouan_Shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Systematics">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Systematics">Systematics</span></h2>
<p>It was formerly considered a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Manx_Shearwater" title="Manx Shearwater">Manx Shearwater</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> see there for more on the <i><a href="/wiki/Puffinus_puffinus" title="Puffinus puffinus" class="mw-redirect">Puffinus puffinus</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a>. After the first split,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> it was the <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" title="Nominate subspecies" class="mw-redirect">nominate subspecies</a> of the so-called "Mediterranean Shearwater" for nearly ten more years; it is considered a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic" class="mw-redirect">monotypic</a> species nowadays, as the Balearic form <i>mauretanicus</i> has been separated as <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Shearwater" title="Balearic Shearwater">Balearic Shearwater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Yelkouan Shearwater appears to belong to a group of Mediterranean and adjacent <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic" class="mw-redirect">Atlantic</a> shearwaters which includes the Balearic Shearwater<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> and one to three <a href="/wiki/Prehistory" title="Prehistory">prehistorically</a> <a href="/wiki/Extinct" title="Extinct" class="mw-redirect">extinct</a> taxa, <a href="/wiki/Hole%27s_Shearwater" title="Hole's Shearwater" class="mw-redirect">Hole's</a> and possibly also <a href="/wiki/Olson%27s_Shearwater" title="Olson's Shearwater" class="mw-redirect">Olson's Shearwater</a> as well as an undescribed population of uncertain distinctness from <a href="/wiki/Menorca" title="Menorca" class="mw-redirect">Menorca</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> The two living Mediterranean lineages had probably separated before the end of the <a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> (c.2 million years ago), as indicated by molecular differences and putative direct ancestor of the Balearic Shearwater, the <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibizan</a> fossil <i><a href="/wiki/Puffinus_nestori" title="Puffinus nestori">Puffinus nestori</a></i> from the Late Pliocene or Early <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">Pleistocene</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Yelkouan_Shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology and status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology_and_status">Ecology and status</span></h2>
<p>Yelkouan Shearwaters breed on islands and coastal cliffs in the eastern and central <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>. Most winter in that sea, but small numbers <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">enter</a> the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> in late summer. This species nests in burrows which are only visited at night to avoid predation by large <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gulls</a>.</p>
<p>This is a gregarious species, which can be seen in large numbers from boats or headlands, especially in autumn. It is under some threat from the development of holiday resorts near its breeding sites, and also from animals such as cats and rats. The Yelkouan Shearwater feeds on <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" title="Mollusc" class="mw-redirect">molluscs</a>. It follows fishing ships when offal is being thrown.</p>
<p>The study of the Minorcan colony concluded that at least in these westernmost birds, genetic variation was extremely low, suggesting that the Yelkouan Shearwater may have suffered a marked population decline historically and thus, while not <a href="/wiki/Threatened" title="Threatened" class="mw-redirect">threatened</a> judging from its absolute numbers, could be vulnerable to adverse effects of <a href="/wiki/Inbreeding" title="Inbreeding">inbreeding</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-genovartetal2005_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-genovartetal2005-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It was formerly classified as a Species of <a href="/wiki/Least_Concern" title="Least Concern">Least Concern</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup> But new research has shown it to be rarer than it was believed. Consequently, it was uplisted to <a href="/wiki/Near_Threatened" title="Near Threatened">Near Threatened</a> status in 2008 <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Vulnerable" title="Vulnerable">Vulnerable</a> in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
