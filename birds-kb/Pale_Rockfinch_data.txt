<p>The <b>pale rockfinch</b> or <b>pale rock sparrow</b> (<i>Carpospiza brachydactyla</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Old_World_sparrow" title="Old World sparrow">sparrow</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a>. It is the only member of the genus <i><b>Carpospiza</b></i>. Some authorities include it in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Petronia" class="mw-redirect" title="Petronia">Petronia</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Others have placed it in the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finch</a> family due to some similarities in behaviour and form, but the anatomy of its tongue exhibits characteristic sparrow features.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The range of pale rockfinch extends from eastern Turkey to Afghanistan, and south into the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a> and northern Africa as far as Ethiopia.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitats</a> are <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> or <a href="/wiki/Tropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Tropical">tropical</a> dry <a href="/wiki/Shrubland" title="Shrubland">shrubland</a> and temperate <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HBW_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HBW-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
