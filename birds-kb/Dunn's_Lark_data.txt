<p><b>Dunn's Lark</b> (<i>Eremalauda dunni</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a> family. It is a <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> bird which is found across parts of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arabia" title="Arabia" class="mw-redirect">Arabia</a>. There are two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>: <i>E. d. dunni</i> in the Sahara and <i>E. d. eremodites</i> in Arabia.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The species belongs to the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><b>Eremalauda</b></i>; <a href="/wiki/Stark%27s_Lark" title="Stark's Lark">Stark's Lark</a> of <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">Southern Africa</a> is sometimes placed in this genus as well but other authorities such as <i><a href="/wiki/Handbook_of_the_Birds_of_the_World" title="Handbook of the Birds of the World">Handbook of the Birds of the World</a></i> place it in <i><a href="/wiki/Spizocorys" title="Spizocorys">Spizocorys</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>Dunn's Lark is a stocky bird with a large head and broad <a href="/wiki/Wing" title="Wing">wings</a>. It is 14 to 15&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 25 to 30&#160;cm.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The upperparts are pale sandy-brown with darker streaks. The underparts are whitish with some dark streaks on the breast. There is a pale stripe over the eye and a pale ring around it. Birds have dark moustachial and malar stripes and a dark mark under the eye. The short, broad tail is black below while the upperside is black with rusty-brown central feathers and pale outer <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is large, heavy and pale pinkish or yellowish in colour. After <a href="/wiki/Moult" title="Moult" class="mw-redirect">moulting</a>, the birds become gradually duller and the dark markings fainter as the <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> becomes more worn.<sup id="cite_ref-Harris_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> is a scratchy warbling with short whistling phrases. Males sing either from the ground or in flight, hovering 30 metres or more above the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat and range">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_range">Habitat and range</span></h2>
<p>Dunn's Larks are found in flat, arid areas with light vegetation such as <a href="/wiki/Grass" title="Grass">grass</a> and scattered bushes.<sup id="cite_ref-Harris_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>In Africa, the species has a scattered range from <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> through <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a> to <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sinclair_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinclair-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, it breeds in <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>. It has also bred occasionally in <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and may have bred in <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> of <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hollom_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hollom-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" title="Vagrancy in birds" class="mw-redirect">Vagrants</a> have been recorded from <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kuwait" title="Kuwait">Kuwait</a>, <a href="/wiki/Qatar" title="Qatar">Qatar</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span></h3>
<p>The diet mainly includes <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>. Birds feed on the ground and sometimes dig for food with their bills. They typically feed in flocks outside the breeding season, moving nomadically in response to rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_Lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nest</a> is a scrape on the ground which is lined with vegetation. Two or three <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid and are incubated for 13 to 16 days. They are white with blackish and lavender spots.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
