<p><b>Dunn's lark</b> (<i>Eremalauda dunni</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a> family. It is a <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">desert</a> bird which is found across parts of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia">Arabia</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dunn's lark was originally described as belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Galerida" title="Galerida">Calendula</a></i> and has also been classified by some authorities as belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Ammomanes" title="Ammomanes">Ammomanes</a></i>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stark%27s_lark" title="Stark&#39;s lark">Stark's lark</a> was formerly also classified in the genus <i>Eremalauda</i>; it has since been moved to <i><a href="/wiki/Spizocorys" title="Spizocorys">Spizocorys</a></i>, leaving Dunn's lark as the <a href="/wiki/Monotypic_taxon" title="Monotypic taxon">only species</a> in the genus <i>Eremalauda</i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Saharan Dunn's lark</b> (<i>E. d. dunni</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/George_Ernest_Shelley" title="George Ernest Shelley">Shelley</a>, 1904)</small>: Found from Mauritania to central Sudan</li>
<li><b>Arabian Dunn's lark</b> (<i>E. d. eremodites</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Richard_Meinertzhagen" title="Richard Meinertzhagen">Meinertzhagen, R</a>, 1923)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Pyrrhulauda" class="mw-redirect" title="Pyrrhulauda">Pyrrhulauda</a></i>. Found from Syria to Jordan and through Saudi Arabia to Oman.</li></ul>
<p>The latter taxon is recognised as a separate species, the <b>Arabian lark</b> (<i>E. eremodites</i>) in <a href="/wiki/Handbook_of_the_Birds_of_the_World" title="Handbook of the Birds of the World">Handbook of the Birds of the World Alive</a> and by <a href="/wiki/Birdlife_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Birdlife International">Birdlife International</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-delHoyo2017_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-delHoyo2017-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BirdLifeChecklist2017_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLifeChecklist2017-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dunn's lark is a stocky bird with a large head and broad wings. It is 14 to 15&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 25 to 30&#160;cm.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The upperparts are pale sandy-brown with darker streaks. The underparts are whitish with some dark streaks on the breast. There is a pale stripe over the eye and a pale ring around it. Birds have dark moustachial and <a href="/wiki/Cheek" title="Cheek">malar</a> stripes and a dark mark under the eye. The short, broad tail is black below while the upperside is black with rusty-brown central feathers and pale outer feathers. The <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is large, heavy and pale pinkish or yellowish in colour. After <a href="/wiki/Moult" class="mw-redirect" title="Moult">moulting</a>, the birds become gradually duller and the dark markings fainter as the <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> becomes more worn.<sup id="cite_ref-Harris_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" class="mw-redirect" title="Bird song">song</a> is a scratchy warbling with short whistling phrases. Males sing either from the ground or in flight, hovering 30 metres or more above the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Africa, the species has a scattered range from Mauritania through Mali, Niger and Chad to Sudan.<sup id="cite_ref-Sinclair_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sinclair-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the Middle East, it breeds in Saudi Arabia and Oman. It has also bred occasionally in Jordan and Israel and may have bred in Yemen and the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> of Egypt.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hollom_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hollom-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Vagrancy in birds">Vagrants</a> have been recorded from Cyprus, Syria, Lebanon, Kuwait, Qatar and the United Arab Emirates.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dunn's larks are found in flat, arid areas with light vegetation such as grass and scattered bushes.<sup id="cite_ref-Harris_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harris-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The nest is a <a href="/wiki/Scrape_nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Scrape nest">scrape</a> on the ground which is lined with vegetation. Two or three <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a> are laid and are incubated for 13 to 16 days. They are white with blackish and lavender spots.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dunn%27s_lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The diet mainly includes <a href="/wiki/Seed_predation" title="Seed predation">seeds</a> and insects. Birds feed on the ground and sometimes dig for food with their bills. They typically feed in flocks outside the breeding season, moving nomadically in response to rainfall.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_5-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
