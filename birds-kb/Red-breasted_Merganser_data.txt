<p>The <b>red-breasted merganser</b> (<i>Mergus serrator</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Diving_duck" title="Diving duck">diving duck</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Mergus" title="Mergus">sawbills</a>.  The genus name is a <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> word used by <a href="/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder" title="Pliny the Elder">Pliny</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Roman</a> authors to refer to an unspecified waterbird, and <i>serrator</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Sawyer_(occupation)" title="Sawyer (occupation)">sawyer</a> from Latin <i>serra</i>, "saw".<sup id="cite_ref-job90_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
The red-breasted merganser was one of the many <a href="/wiki/Aves_in_the_10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="Aves in the 10th edition of Systema Naturae">bird species originally described</a> by <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> in his landmark 1758 <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">10th edition of <i>Systema Naturae</i></a>, where it was given the binomial name <i>Mergus serrator</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The adult red-breasted merganser is 51–62&#160;cm (20–24&#160;in) long with a 70–86&#160;cm (28–34&#160;in) <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Jonsson1992_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Jonsson1992-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The red-breasted merganser weight ranges from 28.2-47.6 oz (800-1350 g)<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>.
</p><p>It has a spiky crest and long thin red bill with serrated edges. The male has a dark head with a green sheen, a white neck with a rusty breast, a black back, and white underparts. Adult females have a rusty head and a greyish body. The juvenile is like the female, but lacks the white collar and has a smaller white wing patch.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The call of the female is a rasping <i>prrak prrak</i>, while the male gives a feeble hiccup-and-sneeze display call.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Red-breasted mergansers dive and swim underwater. They mainly eat small <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, but also aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Its <a href="/wiki/Reproduction" title="Reproduction">breeding</a> <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Habitat (ecology)">habitat</a> is freshwater <a href="/wiki/Lake" title="Lake">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">rivers</a> across northern North America, <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>, Europe, and the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>. It nests in sheltered locations on the ground near <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and many northern breeders winter in <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coastal</a> <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">waters</a> further south.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Speed_record">Speed record</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Speed record">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The fastest duck ever recorded was a red-breasted merganser that attained a top airspeed of 100&#160;mph while being pursued by an airplane. This eclipsed the previous speed record held by a canvasback clocked at 72&#160;mph.<sup id="cite_ref-ducks-unl_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ducks-unl-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The red-breasted merganser is one of the <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
