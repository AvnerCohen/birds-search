<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>The Red-breasted Merganser was one of the many <a href="/wiki/Aves_in_the_10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="Aves in the 10th edition of Systema Naturae">species originally described</a> by <a href="/wiki/Carolus_Linnaeus" title="Carolus Linnaeus" class="mw-redirect">Linnaeus</a> in his 18th-century work, <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_Naturae" title="Systema Naturae">Systema Naturae</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Red-breasted_Merganser,_juvenile.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Red-breasted_Merganser%2C_juvenile.jpg/220px-Red-breasted_Merganser%2C_juvenile.jpg" width="220" height="123" class="thumbimage" /></a>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The adult Red-breasted Merganser is 51–62 cm (20–24 in) long with a 70–86 cm (28–34 in) <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> It has a spiky crest and long thin red bill with serrated edges. The male has a dark head with a green sheen, a white neck with a rusty breast, a black back, and white underparts. Adult females have a rusty head and a greyish body. The juvenile is like the female, but lacks the white collar and has a smaller white wing patch.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span></h3>
<p>The call of the female is a rasping <i>prrak prrak</i>, while the male gives a feeble hiccup-and-sneeze display call.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>It has been claimed to be the fastest bird in level flight, reaching speeds of 161&#160;km/h (100&#160;mph),<sup id="cite_ref-trails_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trails-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-aerospaceweb_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aerospaceweb-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> but is disputed whether the <a href="/wiki/White-throated_Needletail" title="White-throated Needletail">White-throated Needletail</a> is faster, reportedly flying at 170&#160;km/h (105&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-travelalmanac_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-travelalmanac-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span></h3>
<p>Red-breasted Mergansers dive and swim underwater. They mainly eat small <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, but also aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frogs</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>Its <a href="/wiki/Reproduction" title="Reproduction">breeding</a> <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" title="Habitat (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">habitat</a> is freshwater <a href="/wiki/Lakes" title="Lakes" class="mw-redirect">lakes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rivers" title="Rivers" class="mw-redirect">rivers</a> across northern <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It nests in sheltered locations on the ground near <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">water</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and many northern breeders winter in <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">coastal</a> <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">waters</a> further south.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Red-breasted_Merganser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span></h2>
<p>The Red-breasted Merganser is one of the <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
