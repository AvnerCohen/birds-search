<p>The <b>Fea's petrel</b> (<i>Pterodroma feae</i>), is a small <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Gadfly_petrel" title="Gadfly petrel">gadfly petrel</a> <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pterodroma" class="mw-redirect" title="Pterodroma">Pterodroma</a></i>. It was previously considered to be a <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soft-plumaged_petrel" title="Soft-plumaged petrel">soft-plumaged petrel</a>, <i>P. mollis</i>, but they are actually not closely related at all. However, <i>P. feae</i> is very closely related to <a href="/wiki/Zino%27s_petrel" title="Zino&#39;s petrel">Zino's petrel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desertas_petrel" title="Desertas petrel">Desertas petrel</a>, two other species recently split from <i>P. mollis</i>. The gadfly petrels are named for their speedy weaving flight, as if evading <a href="/wiki/Horsefly" class="mw-redirect" title="Horsefly">horseflies</a> and the flight action is also reflected in the genus name <i>Pterodroma</i>, from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>pteron</i>, "wing" and <i>dromos</i>, " runner".<sup id="cite_ref-job90_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job90-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This species is named after the Italian zoologist <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Fea" title="Leonardo Fea">Leonardo Fea</a> (1852-1903).<sup id="cite_ref-Beolens_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Beolens-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fea%27s_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first known recording of the "soft-plumaged petrel" appears to have been in October 1768 off the coast of West Africa during <a href="/wiki/Captain_Cook" class="mw-redirect" title="Captain Cook">Captain Cook</a>'s <a href="/wiki/First_voyage_of_James_Cook" title="First voyage of James Cook">first voyage</a>. While no description was published at the time, a drawing of the specimen produced by <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Parkinson" title="Sydney Parkinson">Sydney Parkinson</a> can be recognised as the form breeding on the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cape Verde Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bourne_1983a_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bourne_1983a-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This specimen was later described as a distinct species by Salvadori in 1899, <i>Oestrelata feae</i>, named after the Italian ornithologist <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_Fea" title="Leonardo Fea">Leonardo Fea</a> and was later reclassified again as <i>Pterodroma feae</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Salvadori_1899_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Salvadori_1899-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Harrop_2004_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrop_2004-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The gadfly petrels in the genus <i>Pterodroma</i> are seabirds of temperate and tropical oceans. Many are little-known and poorly studied, and their often similar appearance has meant that the taxonomy of the group has been rather fluid.<sup id="cite_ref-Onley_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Onley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The forms breeding in Macaronesia on Madeira, <a href="/wiki/Bugio_Island" title="Bugio Island">Bugio</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Desertas_Islands" title="Desertas Islands">Desertas Islands</a>, and in the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> archipelago were long considered to be subspecies of the southern hemisphere soft-plumaged petrel <i>P. mollis</i>, but differences in size, vocalisations, breeding behaviour and mitochondrial DNA analysis showed that the northern birds are not closely related to <i>P. mollis</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-sangster_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sangster-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and that the <a href="/wiki/Bermuda_petrel" title="Bermuda petrel">Bermuda petrel</a> or cahow may be the closest relative of the Macaronesian birds.<sup id="cite_ref-Onley_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Onley-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Sangster recommended establishing Zino's petrel on Madeira and Fea's petrel on the Desertas and Cape Verde as full species,<sup id="cite_ref-sangster_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sangster-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the species split was accepted by the <a href="/wiki/Association_of_European_Rarities_Committees" title="Association of European Rarities Committees">Association of European Rarities Committees</a> (AERC) in 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-aerc_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aerc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> More recently, some authorities have further split Fea's petrel, separating the <a href="/wiki/Desertas_Islands" title="Desertas Islands">Desertas Islands</a> breeding birds from those in the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> archipelago.<sup id="cite_ref-shirihai_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-shirihai-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sangster estimated that the two Macaronesian species diverged at the end of the <a href="/wiki/Early_Pleistocene" title="Early Pleistocene">Early Pleistocene</a>, 850,000 years ago,<sup id="cite_ref-sangster_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sangster-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> although the methodology used to establish this time scale has subsequently been questioned.<sup id="cite_ref-Rheindt_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rheindt-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> An analysis of feather lice from Fea's petrels <i>Pterodroma feae deserti</i> from Bugio Island and Zino's petrels from the Madeiran mainland showed that there were marked differences between the two seabirds in terms of the parasites they carried, suggesting that they have long been isolated, since lice can normally only be transferred through physical contact in the nest. The species on Zino's petrel are most similar to those of the Bermuda petrel, whereas Fea's petrel's lice are like those of Caribbean and Pacific <i>Pterodromas</i>. This suggests that despite the close physical proximity of the two species of gadfly petrel found in the Madeiran archipelago, they may have arisen from separate colonisations of mainland Madeira and, later, the <a href="/wiki/Desertas_Islands" title="Desertas Islands">Desertas Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Zonfrillo_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Zonfrillo-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fea%27s_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This long-winged petrel is 33&#8211;36&#160;cm in length with an 86&#8211;94&#160;cm wingspan. It has a grey back and wings, with a dark "W" marking across the wings. The undersides of the wings are dark and the belly is white.  It has a fast impetuous flight. It picks <a href="/wiki/Plankton" title="Plankton">planktonic</a> food items from the <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a> surface. This species is very similar to the <a href="/wiki/Zino%27s_petrel" title="Zino&#39;s petrel">Zino's petrel</a>, but is larger and has a thicker black bill.
</p><p>As all three species in the <i>Pterodroma feae/<a href="/wiki/Pterodroma_madeira" class="mw-redirect" title="Pterodroma madeira">madeira</a>/<a href="/wiki/Pterodroma_desertae" class="mw-redirect" title="Pterodroma desertae">desertae</a></i> complex were once believed to be subspecies of a single species: <i>Pterodroma mollis</i>, the extreme difficulty in telling them apart is easily understood. A summary of data from a morphological study sheds light on the issue:<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>In overall size <i>feae</i> is closer to <i>deserta</i> and both are bigger than <i>madeira</i> (<b>wing length</b> - <i>f</i>:262, <i>d</i>: 264, <i>m</i>: 248; <b>tarsus</b> - <i>f</i>:34.7, <i>d</i>: 35.2, <i>m</i>: 32.7).</li>
<li>Bill length once again similar in <i>feae</i> and <i>deserta</i> and both larger than <i>madeira</i> (<i>f</i>:29.0, <i>d</i>: 28.6, <i>m</i>: 26.3).</li>
<li>Bill depth by far biggest in <i>deserta</i>, lowest in <i>madeira</i>, and intermediate in <i>feae</i> (<i>f</i>:11.9, <i>d</i>: 12.9, <i>m</i>: 10.5).</li></ul>
<p>In terms of ratios:
</p>
<ul><li>(Bill length/wing length) (<i>f</i>:11.1%, <i>d</i>: 10.8%, <i>m</i>: 10.6%) is rather similar in the three taxa.</li>
<li>(Bill length/bill depth) (<i>f</i>: 2.44, <i>d</i>: 2.22, <i>m</i>: 2.53) is lowest in <i>deserta</i>, and highest in <i>madeira</i>. In <i>feae</i> it is intermediate, but more than twice closer to <i>madeira</i> than to <i>deserta</i>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fea%27s_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds on four islands of <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">Cape Verde</a> in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>: <a href="/wiki/Fogo,_Cape_Verde" title="Fogo, Cape Verde">Fogo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Santo_Ant%C3%A3o,_Cape_Verde" title="Santo Antão, Cape Verde">Santo Antão</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Nicolau,_Cape_Verde" title="São Nicolau, Cape Verde">São Nicolau</a> and <a href="/wiki/Santiago,_Cape_Verde" title="Santiago, Cape Verde">Santiago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fea%27s_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fea%27s_petrel&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg/220px-Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="277" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg/330px-Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg/440px-Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4204" data-file-height="5293" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pterodroma_feae_MHNT_ZOO_2010_11_42_Ilha_do_Fago_-_Cap-vert_-_Naurois.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg of Fea's petrel<br />(coll.<a href="/wiki/MHNT" class="mw-redirect" title="MHNT">MHNT</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> is strictly <a href="/wiki/Nocturnal" class="mw-redirect" title="Nocturnal">nocturnal</a> at the breeding sites to avoid predation by <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gulls</a>. Like most <a href="/wiki/Petrels" class="mw-redirect" title="Petrels">petrels</a>, its walking ability is limited to a short shuffle to the burrow. This <a href="/wiki/Endangered_species" title="Endangered species">endangered species</a> nests in colonies in <a href="/wiki/Burrow_nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Burrow nest">burrows</a> in spring and autumn. It lays a single white <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">egg</a>.
</p>
