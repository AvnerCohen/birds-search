<p>The <b>Graceful Prinia</b> (<i>Prinia gracilis</i>) is a small warbler (in some older works it is referred to as <b>Graceful Warbler</b>). This <a href="/wiki/Prinia" title="Prinia">prinia</a> is a resident breeder in Northeast Africa and <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia" title="Southwest Asia" class="mw-redirect">Southwest Asia</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> east to <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_India" title="North India">North India</a>, where it is sometimes called <b>Streaked Wren-Warbler</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-ali-daniel_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ali-daniel-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>This active <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is typically found in shrub or tall grass in a variety of habitats with thick undergrowth, <a href="/wiki/Tamarisk" title="Tamarisk" class="mw-redirect">tamarisks</a> or similar cover. Graceful Prinia builds its nest in a bush or grass and lays 3-5 eggs.</p>
<p>These 10–11&#160;cm long warblers have short rounded wings, and a long tapering tail with each feather tipped with black and white. In breeding plumage, adults are grey-brown above, with dark streaking. The underparts are whitish with buff flanks, and the bill is short and black.</p>
<p>The sexes are similar. In winter, adults are brighter sandy brown above with weaker streaking, there is more buff on the sides, and the bill is paler.</p>
<p>There are 12 subspecies, of which <i>P. g. akyildizi</i>, of southern <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> is the darkest, brownest, and most heavily streaked above, and has the brightest buff flanks.</p>
<p>The long tail is often cocked, and the flight of this species is weak. Like most warblers, Graceful Prinia is insectivorous. The call is a rolling trilled <i>breep</i>, and the song is a hard rolling repletion of <i>zerlip</i>.</p>
