<p>The <b>Green Warbler</b> alternatively named <b>Green Willow Warbler</b> or <b>Green Leaf Warbler</b> (<i><b>Phylloscopus nitidus</b></i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains" title="Caucasus Mountains">Caucasus Mountains</a> in southcentral <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.</p>
<p>Like all leaf warblers, it was formerly placed in the "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a>" assemblage, but now belongs to the new leaf-warbler <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Phylloscopidae" title="Phylloscopidae">Phylloscopidae</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> It is most closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Greenish_Warbler" title="Greenish Warbler">Greenish Warbler</a> but is brighter in color, and the underside is much more yellow. It has one strong and one faint wing bar, especially in young birds.</p>
