<p>The <b>green warbler</b> (<i>Phylloscopus nitidus</i>), also known as <b>green willow warbler</b> or <b>green leaf warbler</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/Leaf_warbler" title="Leaf warbler">leaf warbler</a> found in the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains" title="Caucasus Mountains">Caucasus Mountains</a> in southcentral Europe.
</p><p>Like all leaf warblers, it was formerly placed in the "<a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a>" assemblage, but now belongs to the new leaf-warbler <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Phylloscopidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Phylloscopidae">Phylloscopidae</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The genus name <i>Phylloscopus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>phullon</i>, "leaf", and <i>skopos</i>, "seeker" (from <i>skopeo</i>, "to watch"). The specific <i>nitidus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and means "shining".<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is most closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Greenish_warbler" title="Greenish warbler">greenish warbler</a> but is brighter in colour, and the underside is much more yellow. It has one strong and one faint wing bar, especially in young birds.
</p>
