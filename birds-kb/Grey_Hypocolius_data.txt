<p>The <b>grey hypocolius</b><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> or simply <b>hypocolius</b> (<i>Hypocolius ampelinus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>. It is the sole member of the genus <i><b>Hypocolius</b></i> and it is placed in a family of its own, the <b>Hypocoliidae</b>. This slender and long tailed bird is found in the dry semi-desert region of northern Africa, Arabia, Afghanistan, Pakistan, and western India. They fly in flocks and forage mainly on fruits, migrating south in winter.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The grey hypocolius is a slim bird with a long tail, slight crest and thick, short hook-tipped bill. Its shape and soft, satiny <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> resembles that of the <a href="/wiki/Waxwing" title="Waxwing">waxwing</a>. Birds are mainly a uniform grey or brownish-grey colour, with males having a black triangular mask around the eyes. They have white-tipped black primary wing feathers and a black terminal band on the tail. Adults are about 19–21&#160;cm in length.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The head feathers are raised when the bird is excited. They fly in a straight non-undulating style and when hopping in shrubbery, can appear like a babbler. The tarsus is short and sturdy with coarse scales. There are rictal bristles visible at the base of the bill and the nostrils are exposed, small and oval.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The relationships of the species are unclear. Earlier authors had suggested that they were related to the <a href="/wiki/Bulbul" title="Bulbul">bulbuls</a><sup id="cite_ref-BOA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> or <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrikes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> They appear to be related to the <a href="/wiki/Waxwing" title="Waxwing">waxwings</a>, and at least one study based on molecular sequences suggests that it belongs to the same group.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The grey hypocolius ranges through the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, breeding in the <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a> area,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and wintering mostly near the <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">Red Sea</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a> coasts of <a href="/wiki/Arabia" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabia">Arabia</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a> to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>. They are regular winter visitors to the <a href="/wiki/Kutch" class="mw-redirect" title="Kutch">Kutch</a> region of western India<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and vagrants have been noted as far south as Kihim near <a href="/wiki/Bombay" class="mw-redirect" title="Bombay">Bombay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It is found in woodland and scrub in arid and semiarid regions, especially river valleys near deserts, as well as in irrigated and cultivated areas with trees, such as palm groves and gardens.<sup id="cite_ref-BOA_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-King1997_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-King1997-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The grey hypocolius is a shy and unobtrusive, even cryptic, bird which will fly to thick cover when disturbed, where it will remain motionless until the perceived danger has passed. Its flight is strong and direct (without undulations).<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It will form flocks, especially in winter, and roost communally.<sup id="cite_ref-King1997_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-King1997-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The breeding season is June or July in Arabia. The nest is cup shaped and deep lined with hair and fluff. The nest is often placed on the leaves of a date palm at a height of about 3 to 5 feet. Both the male and female take part in nest building. The clutch is four eggs. The eggs are leaden white with blotches.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>  The incubation period is about 14–15 days.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hypocolius.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Hypocolius.svg/220px-Hypocolius.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Hypocolius.svg/330px-Hypocolius.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Hypocolius.svg/440px-Hypocolius.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="700" data-file-height="700" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hypocolius.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The male has a conspicuous black face mask</div></div></div>
<p>Grey hypocoliuses forage quietly and methodically in foliage, through clusters of trees, palm groves and orchards, rarely coming to the ground. Though they may eat some insects, their main diet consists of fruits and berries, including <a href="/wiki/Mulberry" class="mw-redirect" title="Mulberry">mulberries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ficus" title="Ficus">figs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_dactylifera" class="mw-redirect" title="Phoenix dactylifera">dates</a>. In captivity they have been known to readily take bread.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Calls">Calls</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Calls">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The most common <a href="/wiki/Bird_vocalization" title="Bird vocalization">vocalization</a> is a series of squealing descending notes or <i>kleeeu</i> whistles. Other calls include scolding chirps.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Grey_hypocolius&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Because the species has a large range, and because it does not meet the population size and decline criteria of the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a>, the conservation status is evaluated as being of "<a href="/wiki/Least_concern" class="mw-redirect" title="Least concern">least concern</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Because of its <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> family status it is much sought after by <a href="/wiki/Birdwatching" title="Birdwatching">birdwatchers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
