<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Puffinus_carneipes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Puffinus_carneipes.jpg/220px-Puffinus_carneipes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Puffinus_carneipes.jpg/330px-Puffinus_carneipes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Puffinus_carneipes.jpg/440px-Puffinus_carneipes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="2304" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Puffinus_carneipes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><i>Ardenna carneipes</i></div></div></div>
<p>The <b>flesh-footed shearwater</b> (<i>Ardenna carneipes</i>; formerly <i>Puffinus carneipes</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Shearwater" title="Shearwater">shearwater</a>. Its plumage is black. It has pale pinkish feet, and a pale bill with a distinct black tip. Together with the equally light-billed <a href="/wiki/Pink-footed_shearwater" title="Pink-footed shearwater">pink-footed shearwater</a>, it forms the <i>Hemipuffinus</i> group, a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a> which may or may not have an <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" class="mw-redirect" title="Atlantic">Atlantic</a> relative in the <a href="/wiki/Great_shearwater" title="Great shearwater">great shearwater</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> These large shearwaters are among those that have been separated into the genus <i>Ardenna</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Recent genetic analysis indicates evidence of strong divergence between Pacific colonies relative to those in South and Western Australia, thought to be explained by philopatry and differences in foraging strategies during the breeding season.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds in colonies, and has two main breeding areas; one in the southwest <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> includes <a href="/wiki/Lord_Howe_Island" title="Lord Howe Island">Lord Howe Island</a> (22,654 pairs <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>), South Australia (about 1,800 pairs breeding on two islands<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>) and northern <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> (13,000 pairs<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>); the other population comprises no more than 36,000 pairs breeding on 42 islands along the coast of <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Cape_Leeuwin" title="Cape Leeuwin">Cape Leeuwin</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Recherche_Archipelago" title="Recherche Archipelago">Recherche Archipelago</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Another 500 pairs breed on <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Saint-Paul" title="Île Saint-Paul">St Paul Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>.  A record of birds on <a href="/wiki/Astola_Island" title="Astola Island">Astola Island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a> is unconfirmed. Recent evidence suggests populations are declining across much of the species' range.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The species was recently listed as near threatened in Australia <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and nationally vulnerable in New Zealand,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and has been recommended for listing under the Agreement on the Conservation of Albatrosses and Petrels.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  At the state level, the species is listed as vulnerable in Western Australia<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and New South Wales and rare in South Australia.
</p><p>The species occurs as a (boreal) summer visitor in the <a href="/wiki/North_Pacific_Ocean" class="mw-redirect" title="North Pacific Ocean">North Pacific Ocean</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> where potentially large numbers are taken as bycatch in fisheries.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The species also suffers from climate related impacts <sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> and significant heavy metal contamination, the cause of which is not fully understood, but is likely due to the ingestion of significant quantities of <a href="/wiki/Plastic_pollution" title="Plastic pollution">plastic</a>, which the birds mistake for food floating on the ocean surface.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
