<p>The <b>Flesh-footed Shearwater</b>, <i>Puffinus carneipes</i>, is a small <a href="/wiki/Shearwater" title="Shearwater">shearwater</a>. Its plumage is black. It has pale pinkish feet, and a pale bill with a black tip. Together with the equally light-billed <a href="/wiki/Pink-footed_Shearwater" title="Pink-footed Shearwater">Pink-footed Shearwater</a>, it forms the <i>Hemipuffinus</i> group, a <a href="/wiki/Superspecies" title="Superspecies">superspecies</a> which may or may not have an <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic" class="mw-redirect">Atlantic</a> relative in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Shearwater" title="Great Shearwater">Great Shearwater</a> (Austin, 1996; Austin <i>et al.</i>, 2004). These are large shearwaters which are among those that could be separated in the genus <i>Ardenna</i> (Penhallurick &amp; Wink, 2004).</p>
<p>It breeds in colonies, and has two main breeding areas: one in the South West <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> includes <a href="/wiki/Lord_Howe_Island" title="Lord Howe Island">Lord Howe Island</a> (20,000 to 40,000 pairs) and northern <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a> (50,000 to 100,000 pairs); the other is along the coast of <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Cape_Leeuwin" title="Cape Leeuwin">Cape Leeuwin</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Recherche_Archipelago" title="Recherche Archipelago" class="mw-redirect">Recherche Archipelago</a>. Another 600 pairs breed on <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_Saint-Paul" title="Île Saint-Paul">St Paul Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, as well in the <a href="/wiki/Astola_Island" title="Astola Island">Astola Island</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">Arabian Sea</a>. It occurs as a summer visitor in the North <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a> as far north as <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia" title="British Columbia">British Columbia</a>. Flesh-footed shearwaters have been sighted in the Central-North Pacific, above the main Hawaiian Islands as well.</p>
