<p><b>Lichtenstein's sandgrouse</b> (<i>Pterocles lichtensteinii</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Pteroclidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Pteroclidae">Pteroclidae</a> family, which is named after <a href="/wiki/Martin_Lichtenstein" class="mw-redirect" title="Martin Lichtenstein">Martin Lichtenstein</a>. They are nomadic, mostly nocturnal birds, which drink before dawn and after dusk.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lichtenstein%27s_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The species is found over a wide region, from near the equator in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, through the Middle East to <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. In Africa it is besides found in <a href="/wiki/Algeria" title="Algeria">Algeria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chad" title="Chad">Chad</a>, <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">Morocco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>. In Asia it is also found in <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/State_of_Palestine" title="State of Palestine">Palestine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Races">Races</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lichtenstein%27s_sandgrouse&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Races">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_(1905)_(14747568674).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_%281905%29_%2814747568674%29.jpg/150px-Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_%281905%29_%2814747568674%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="150" height="258" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_%281905%29_%2814747568674%29.jpg/225px-Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_%281905%29_%2814747568674%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_%281905%29_%2814747568674%29.jpg/300px-Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_%281905%29_%2814747568674%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2446" data-file-height="4208" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Journal_f%C3%BCr_Ornithologie_(1905)_(14747568674).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><center><i>P. l. sukensis</i> ♂,♀<br /><i>P. l. lichtensteinii</i> ♂,♀<br /><i>Journal für Ornithologie</i></center></div></div></div>
<p>There are five accepted races:<sup id="cite_ref-dej_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dej-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>P. l. targius</i> <small>von Schweppenburg, 1916</small> – Sahara and Sahel</li>
<li><i>P. l. lichtensteinii</i> <small>Temminck, 1825</small> – Israel to Somalia and Socotra</li>
<li><i>P. l. sukensis</i> <small>Neumann, 1909</small> – South Sudan to central Kenya</li>
<li><i>P. l. ingramsi</i> <small>Bates &amp; Kinnear, 1937</small> – local in Yemen</li>
<li><i>P. l. arabicus</i> <small>Neumann, 1909</small> – Arabia to Pakistan</li></ul>
