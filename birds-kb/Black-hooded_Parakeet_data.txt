<p>The <b>Nanday Parakeet</b> (<i>Nandayus nenday</i>) is also known as the <b>Black-hooded Parakeet</b> or <b>Nanday Conure</b>. The bird is native to <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a> from southeast <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> to southwest <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, central <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>, from the region known as the <a href="/wiki/Pantanal" title="Pantanal">pantanal</a>. Caged birds have been released in some areas and the birds have established self-sustaining populations in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles,_California" title="Los Angeles, California" class="mw-redirect">Los Angeles, California</a>, and several areas of Florida (including <a href="/wiki/St._Petersburg,_Florida" title="St. Petersburg, Florida">St. Petersburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Broward_County,_Florida" title="Broward County, Florida">Broward County</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County,_Florida" title="Miami-Dade County, Florida">Miami-Dade County</a>).</p>
<p>A prehistoric relative, <i><a href="/wiki/Nandayus_vorohuensis" title="Nandayus vorohuensis">Nandayus vorohuensis</a></i>, was described from <a href="/wiki/Late_Pliocene" title="Late Pliocene" class="mw-redirect">Late Pliocene</a> fossils found in <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Nanday_Parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Nanday Parakeet is 32–37&#160;cm in length, and is mostly green in color. Its most distinguishing characteristic, for which it is named, is its black facial mask and beak. It also shows black trailing flight feathers on its wings and has a long tail edged at the end in blue. The upper chest is bluish-green and the lower chest is a paler green. Feathers covering the thighs are red.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Nanday_Parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Nanday_Parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span></h3>
<p>The bird feeds on <a href="/wiki/Seeds" title="Seeds" class="mw-redirect">seeds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a>, palm nuts, <a href="/wiki/Berries" title="Berries" class="mw-redirect">berries</a>, flowers and buds. <a href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">Feral</a> birds will also come to bird feeders. Wild birds primarily use scrub forest and forest clearings around settlements. It frequents open <a href="/wiki/Savannah" title="Savannah" class="mw-redirect">savannah</a>, pastures and stockyards in South America where it is considered a pest in some areas.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Nanday_Parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>Nanday Parakeets usually find holes in trees to nest in. It lays 3-4 eggs. After raising its young, all birds will form rather large communal roosts until the next breeding season.</p>

<p>In captivity they make wonderful pets if cared for and socialized properly. They all have distinct personalities and appreciate toys. They require a cage or enclosure that is a minimum of 24x24x30 inches as they are inclined to climb, flap their wings and have long tail feathers. They also like to be outside of their cage at least six hours a day. While they will accept most parrot seed mixes, seed-only diets are nutritionally inadequate, reduce their lifespan considerably and may cause them to become underweight. Pellet-based diets with fruits and vegetables offered daily are much preferable. These birds are extremely intelligent and skilled escape artists. Some of them will talk, others will not, depending on their personality. They are noted for having a very loud call and are not suited for apartments.</p>
<p>Fearing the birds may escape and become feral agricultural pests, the state of <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">Tennessee</a>, USA bans the keeping of Nanday Parakeets as well as <a href="/wiki/Monk_Parakeet" title="Monk Parakeet">Monk Parakeets</a>.</p>
