<p>The <b>Booted Eagle</b> (<i>Aquila pennata</i>) (formerly <i>Hieraaetus pennatus</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a>. It is about 46 centimetres (18&#160;in) in length and has a wingspan of 120 centimetres (47&#160;in). Like all <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagles</a>, it belongs to the family <i>Accipitridae</i>.</p>

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus_pennatus_sal.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Pale phase Booted Eagle wintering in India</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It breeds in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and across <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. This eagle lays 1-2 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> or crag <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a>.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Hieraaetus.jpg/220px-Hieraaetus.jpg" width="220" height="174" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Booted Eagle showing the "Landing Lights" white markings on the wings/shoulders. An aid to identify this raptor. Sighting: Bundala, Sri Lanka</div>

<p>This is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of wooded, often hilly countryside with some open areas. It hunts small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reptile" title="Reptile">reptiles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> up to 5 times its own weight.</p>
<p>The Booted is a small eagle, comparable to the <a href="/wiki/Common_Buzzard" title="Common Buzzard">Common Buzzard</a> in size though more eagle-like in shape. Males grow to about 510–770 grams (1.1–1.7 lb) in <a href="/wiki/Weight" title="Weight">weight</a>, with females about 950–1,000 grams (2.1–2.2 lb). There are two relatively distinct plumage <a href="/wiki/Forms_in_zoological_literature" title="Forms in zoological literature" class="mw-redirect">forms</a>. Pale birds are mainly light grey with a darker head and flight <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>. The other form has mid-brown <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> with dark grey <a href="/wiki/Flight_feather" title="Flight feather">flight feathers</a>.</p>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">call</a> is a shrill <i>kli-kli-kli</i>.</p>
<p>Recent <a href="/wiki/Genetics" title="Genetics">genetic</a> research<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from September 2007">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> resulted in the reclassification of this species to the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i>Aquila</i> from <i><a href="/wiki/Hieraaetus" title="Hieraaetus">Hieraaetus</a></i>. As it is the <a href="/wiki/Type_species" title="Type species">type species</a> of <i>Hieraaetus</i>, should any of the hawk-eagles be retained in a distinct genus a new name for that group would be necessary.</p>
<p>Along with the <a href="/wiki/Little_Eagle" title="Little Eagle">Little Eagle</a> this bird is one of the closest living relatives of the <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinct</a> <a href="/wiki/Haast%27s_Eagle" title="Haast's Eagle">Haast's Eagle</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.</p>
