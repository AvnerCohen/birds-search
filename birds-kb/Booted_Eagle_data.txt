<p>The <b>booted eagle</b> (<i>Hieraaetus pennatus</i>, also classified as <i>Aquila pennata</i>) is a medium-sized mostly migratory <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> with a wide distribution in the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> and southern Asia, wintering in the tropics of Africa and Asia, with a small, disjunct breeding population in south-western Africa. Like all <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagles</a>, it belongs to the family <i><a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The booted is a small eagle, comparable to the <a href="/wiki/Common_buzzard" title="Common buzzard">common buzzard</a> in size though more eagle-like in shape. Males grow to about 510–770&#160;g (1.12–1.70&#160;lb) in <a href="/wiki/Weight" title="Weight">weight</a>, with females about 840–1,025&#160;g (1.852–2.260&#160;lb) with a length of 40&#160;cm and a wingspan of 11–132&#160;cm. There are two relatively distinct plumage <a href="/wiki/Forms_in_zoological_literature" class="mw-redirect" title="Forms in zoological literature">forms</a>. Pale birds are mainly light grey with a darker head and flight <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">feathers</a>. The other form has mid-brown <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> with dark grey <a href="/wiki/Flight_feather" title="Flight feather">flight feathers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kemp_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kemp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AquilaPennata_nest.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/AquilaPennata_nest.jpg/220px-AquilaPennata_nest.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="175" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/AquilaPennata_nest.jpg/330px-AquilaPennata_nest.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/AquilaPennata_nest.jpg/440px-AquilaPennata_nest.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="954" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AquilaPennata_nest.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Booted eagle nest</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" class="mw-redirect" title="Bird song">call</a> is a shrill <i>kli-kli-kli</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kemp_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kemp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AquilaPennata.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/AquilaPennata.jpg/170px-AquilaPennata.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="277" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/AquilaPennata.jpg/255px-AquilaPennata.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/AquilaPennata.jpg/340px-AquilaPennata.jpg 2x" data-file-width="983" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AquilaPennata.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Light morph from below</div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a> and across <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, and also in western <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">Namibia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
The northern populations are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> spending November to February<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">Sub-Saharan Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>, while the small southern African populations is sedentary. This is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of wooded, often hilly countryside with some open areas, it breeds in rocky, broken terrain but migrants will use almost any type of habitat other than dense forest.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG/220px-Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG/330px-Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG/440px-Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3784" data-file-height="2695" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus_pennatus_MWNH_0846.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>This eagle lays 1–2 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> in a nest built from sticks and lined with green leaves in a <a href="/wiki/Tree" title="Tree">tree</a> or on a crag, or it takes over the disused nest of another large bird such as a <a href="/wiki/Black_kite" title="Black kite">black kite</a> or <a href="/wiki/Grey_heron" title="Grey heron">grey heron</a>. The female incubates the egg for around 45 days and is fed by the male, after hatching she guards the nest and the young while the male provides all the food. The chick fledges after 70–75 days.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Hieraaetus.jpg/220px-Hieraaetus.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="174" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Hieraaetus.jpg/330px-Hieraaetus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Hieraaetus.jpg/440px-Hieraaetus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="507" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hieraaetus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Showing the white marking on the wings termed as "landing lights"</div></div></div>
<p>It hunts small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reptile" title="Reptile">reptiles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Iziko_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Iziko-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Booted_eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Based on recent <a href="/wiki/Genetics" title="Genetics">genetic</a> research some authors reclassified this species to the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i>Aquila</i>, along with some<sup id="cite_ref-BB_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BB-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> or all<sup id="cite_ref-Clements_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Clements-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> other <i><a href="/wiki/Hieraaetus" title="Hieraaetus">Hieraaetus</a></i> species. As it is the <a href="/wiki/Type_species" title="Type species">type species</a> of <i>Hieraaetus</i>, should any of the hawk-eagles have been retained in a distinct genus then a new name for that group would have been necessary. However, <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> research has shown it forms a <a href="/wiki/Monophyletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monophyletic">monophyletic</a> <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> with <a href="/wiki/Ayres%27s_hawk_eagle" class="mw-redirect" title="Ayres&#39;s hawk eagle">Ayres's hawk eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wahlberg%27s_eagle" title="Wahlberg&#39;s eagle">Wahlberg's eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Little_eagle" title="Little eagle">little eagle</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Pygmy_eagle" title="Pygmy eagle">pygmy eagle</a> and this clade is often treated as forming the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Hieraeetus" class="mw-redirect" title="Hieraeetus">Hieraeetus</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-TED_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TED-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and most reference lists currently use <i>H. pennata</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-BOW-Raptors_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOW-Raptors-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ITIS-Hieraaetus_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITIS-Hieraaetus-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Along with the <a href="/wiki/Little_eagle" title="Little eagle">little eagle</a>, this bird is one of the closest living relatives of the <a href="/wiki/Extinction" title="Extinction">extinct</a> <a href="/wiki/Haast%27s_eagle" title="Haast&#39;s eagle">Haast's eagle</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bunce2005_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bunce2005-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although some authors name a number of <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> most now treat it as a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> species.<sup id="cite_ref-Avibase_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Avibase-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Aquila minut</i> described by Brehm (1831) is this bird. The <a href="/wiki/Fossil" title="Fossil">fossil</a> bird described under the same name by Milne-Edwards (1871) is preliminarily known as <i><a href="/wiki/Hieraaetus" title="Hieraaetus">Hieraaetus</a> edwardsi</i> but might belong in <i><a href="/wiki/Aquila_(genus)" title="Aquila (genus)">Aquila</a></i>.
</p>
