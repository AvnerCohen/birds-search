<p><b>MacQueen's Bustard</b> (<i>Chlamydotis macqueenii</i>) is a large <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bustard" title="Bustard">bustard</a> family. It breeds in southwestern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. It has recently been split as a separate species from the <a href="/wiki/Houbara_Bustard" title="Houbara Bustard">Houbara Bustard</a> (<i>Chlamydotis undulata</i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_Africa" title="North Africa">North Africa</a>. These two species are the only members of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chlamydotis" title="Chlamydotis">Chlamydotis</a></i> genus.</p>
<p>The dividing line between the two species is the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_peninsula" title="Sinai peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Sinai peninsula</a>. MacQueen's has a greater tendency to wander than the sedentary Houbara Bustard and a handful of <i>Chlamydotis</i> bustards have reached <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, mainly in the 19th century. All those attributable to a species have been the more geographically remote MacQueen's.</p>
<p>It is unlikely that any more of this species will reach western Europe, since it has been hunted to near-extinction in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> by Arab <a href="/wiki/Falcon" title="Falcon">falconers</a>. Conservation efforts by the late <a href="/wiki/Zayed_bin_Sultan_Al_Nahyan" title="Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan">Shaikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan</a> in the United Arab Emirates have given some hope for the future of this Bustard.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>This species breeds in deserts and other very arid sandy areas. It is 60 centimetres (24&#160;in) long with a 140 centimetres (55&#160;in) wingspan. It is brown above and white below, with a black stripe down the sides of its neck. In flight, the long wings show large areas of black and brown on the flight feathers. Sexes are similar, but the female is smaller and greyer above. Macqueen’s Bustard is vocally almost silent. Macqueen's is slightly larger and paler than Houbara.</p>
<p>Like other bustards, these have a flamboyant display raising the white feathers of the head and throat and withdrawing the head. 2–4&#160;eggs are laid on the ground.</p>
<p>This species is omnivorous taking seeds, insects and other small creatures. It is named after the British soldier and collector General <a href="/wiki/Thomas_MacQueen" title="Thomas MacQueen">Thomas MacQueen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
