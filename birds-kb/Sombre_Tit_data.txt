<p>The <b>Sombre Tit</b> (<i>Poecile lugubris</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Tit_(bird)" title="Tit (bird)">tit</a> family found in southeast <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and southwest <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.</p>
<p>Though it is traditionally placed in <i>Parus</i>, <a href="/wiki/MtDNA" title="MtDNA" class="mw-redirect">mtDNA</a> <a href="/wiki/Cytochrome_b" title="Cytochrome b">cytochrome <i>b</i></a> <a href="/wiki/DNA_sequence" title="DNA sequence" class="mw-redirect">sequence</a> data suggests that that <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> should be split, as already adopted by the <a href="/wiki/American_Ornithologists%27_Union" title="American Ornithologists' Union">American Ornithologists' Union</a>, for example. This species is part of the chickadee group, which would then make up the genus <i>Poecile</i>. In <i>Poecile</i>, this species forms part of a group of apparently rather <a href="/wiki/Basal_(phylogenetics)" title="Basal (phylogenetics)">basal</a> species, the relationships of which are ill-defined. They also include the <a href="/wiki/Varied_Tit" title="Varied Tit">Varied</a> and probably the <a href="/wiki/White-fronted_Tit" title="White-fronted Tit">White-fronted Tit</a> (which were sometimes separated in <i>Sittiparus</i>), and possibly the <a href="/wiki/White-browed_Tit" title="White-browed Tit">White-browed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Tit" title="Caspian Tit">Caspian Tits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
