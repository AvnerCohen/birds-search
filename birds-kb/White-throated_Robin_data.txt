<p>The <b>White-throated Robin</b> (<i>Irania gutturalis</i>)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> that was formerly classed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family Turdidae, but is now more generally considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a>, family Muscicapidae. It, and similar small <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, are often called <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chats</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_Robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>This species is larger than the <a href="/wiki/European_Robin" title="European Robin">European Robin</a>, having a length of 16 centimetres (6.3&#160;in) and a <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a> of 28 centimetres (11&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-BTO_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTO-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The breeding male has lead-grey upperparts, a black face with a white throat and supercilium, and orange underparts. The tail is black, as is the strong bill. The male's song is a fast twittering given from a bush or in flight. The call of this species is a <i>chis-it</i> like <a href="/wiki/Pied_Wagtail" title="Pied Wagtail" class="mw-redirect">Pied Wagtail</a>. Females are plainer, mainly grey apart from a black tail, hints of orange on the flanks, and some white throat streaks.</p>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" title="Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect">insectivorous</a> species breeding in south west <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> from <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> to <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and wintering in east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. This species is a very rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It will also eat <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> during the autumn.<sup id="cite_ref-BTO_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTO-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>It breeds in dry rocky slopes with some bushes, often at some altitude. The White-throated Robin nests in a shrub, laying 4-5 <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>.</p>
