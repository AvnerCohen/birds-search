<p>The <b>white-throated robin</b> (<i>Irania gutturalis</i>), or <b>irania</b>, is a small, <a href="/wiki/Sexual_dimorphism" title="Sexual dimorphism">sexually dimorphic</a>, migratory <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The vernacular and genus name <i><b>Irania</b></i> alludes to <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, its <a href="/wiki/Type_locality_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Type locality (biology)">type locality</a>, while the specific name <i>gutturalis</i> is <a href="/wiki/Medieval_Latin" title="Medieval Latin">Medieval Latin</a> for "of the throat".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It breeds in western Asia and overwinters in East Africa.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relationships">Relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was formerly classed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush</a> family, Turdidae, but is now more generally considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a>, family Muscicapidae. It, and similar passerine <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, are often called <a href="/wiki/Chat_(bird)" title="Chat (bird)">chats</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is larger than the <a href="/wiki/European_robin" title="European robin">European robin</a>, having a length of 16 centimetres (6.3&#160;in) and a <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a> of 28 centimetres (11&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-BTO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The breeding male has lead-grey upperparts, a black face with a white throat and <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, and orange underparts. The tail is black, as is the strong bill. Females are plainer, mainly grey apart from a black tail, hints of orange on the flanks, and some white throat streaks.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Song">Song</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Song">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The male's song is a fast twittering, given from a bush or in flight. The call of this species is a <i>chis-it</i> double note, like that of the <a href="/wiki/Pied_wagtail" class="mw-redirect" title="Pied wagtail">pied wagtail</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Though it is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a>, it will also eat <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> during autumn.<sup id="cite_ref-BTO_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range_and_habitat">Range and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Range and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> species, breeding from Turkey to Afghanistan in western Asia, and wintering in East Africa. In East Africa they are found in closed thicket in dry country, typically <i><a href="/wiki/Acacia_sensu_lato" title="Acacia sensu lato">Acacia</a></i>-<i><a href="/wiki/Commiphora" title="Commiphora">Commiphora</a></i> woodland on the dry central plateau. Small numbers reach the <a href="/wiki/Usangu_Plains" class="mw-redirect" title="Usangu Plains">Usangu Plains</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ruaha_National_Park" title="Ruaha National Park">Ruaha National Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>, which is normally their southern limit. In dry years however, some may move still further south, and reach moister uplands at 1,600 metres.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It is also a very rare vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_robin&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The white-throated robin's breeding habitat is dry rocky slopes with some bushes, often at some altitude. It nests in a shrub, laying 4-5 <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">eggs</a>.
</p>
