<p>The <b>Reed Bunting</b>, <i>Emberiza schoeniclus</i>, is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family Emberizidae, a group now separated by most modern authors from the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finches</a>, Fringillidae.</p>
<p>It breeds across <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and much of temperate and northern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. Most birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> south in winter, but those in the milder south and west of the range are resident. It is common in reedbeds and also breeds in drier open areas such as moorland and cultivation.</p>
<p>The familiar, if somewhat monotonous, song of the cock is a repetitive <i>zrip</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Reed_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_schoeniclus_-England_-male-8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Emberiza_schoeniclus_-England_-male-8.jpg/220px-Emberiza_schoeniclus_-England_-male-8.jpg" width="220" height="160" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_schoeniclus_-England_-male-8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A male in winter in England</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Reed Bunting is a medium sized bird, 13.5-15.5&#160;cm long, with a small but sturdy seed-eater's bill. The male has a black head and throat, white neck collar and underparts, and a heavily streaked brown back. The female is much duller, with a streaked brown head, and is more streaked below.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Reed_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span></h2>
<p>Its natural food consists of insects when feeding young, and otherwise seeds. The nest is in a bush or reed tussock. 4-7 eggs are laid, which show the hair-like markings characteristic of those of buntings.</p>
