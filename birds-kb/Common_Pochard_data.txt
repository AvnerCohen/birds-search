<p>The <b>Common Pochard</b> (<i>Aythya ferina</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Diving_duck" title="Diving duck">diving duck</a>.</p>
<p>The adult male has a long dark bill with a grey band, a red head and neck, a black breast, red eyes and a grey back. The adult female has a brown head and body and a narrower grey bill-band. The triangular head shape is distinctive. Pochards are superficially similar to the closely related <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> <a href="/wiki/Redhead_(duck)" title="Redhead (duck)" class="mw-redirect">Redhead</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canvasback" title="Canvasback">Canvasback</a>.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshes</a> and lakes with a metre or more water depth. Pochards breed in much of temperate and northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> into <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, and winter in the southern and west of Europe.</p>
<p>These are gregarious birds, forming large flocks in winter, often mixed with other diving ducks such as <a href="/wiki/Tufted_Duck" title="Tufted Duck">Tufted Duck</a>, with which they are known to hybridise.</p>
<p>These birds feed mainly by diving or dabbling. They eat aquatic plants with some <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" title="Mollusc" class="mw-redirect">molluscs</a>, aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and small <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>. They often feed at night, and will up-end for food as well as the more characteristic diving.</p>

<p>In the <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a>, birds breed in eastern <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and lowland <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, in small numbers in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> with numbers increasing gradually, and sporadically in the Republic of Ireland. Large numbers overwinter in <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, after the birds retreat from <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a>.</p>
<p>The Pochard is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
