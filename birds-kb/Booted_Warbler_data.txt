<p>The <b>Booted Warbler</b> (<i>Hippolais caligata</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> group. It was formerly considered to be conspecific with <a href="/wiki/Sykes%27s_Warbler" title="Sykes's Warbler">Sykes's Warbler</a>, but the two are now usually both afforded species status.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> Booted Warbler itself breeds from central <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> to western <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> to winter in the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> subcontinent as far south as <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>. It is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>, found in open country with bushes and other tall vegetation. 3-4 eggs are laid in a nest in a bush or vegetation. Like most warblers they are insectivorous.</p>
<p>These are small warblers, especially compared to others in their genus. They are pale brown (weak tea colour) above and whitish below with buff flanks. The outer tail feathers have pale edges. They have a short pale <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, and the bill is strong and pointed. Sykes's is larger and greyer than Booted, and most resembles an <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Olivaceous_Warbler" title="Eastern Olivaceous Warbler">Eastern Olivaceous Warbler</a>.</p>
