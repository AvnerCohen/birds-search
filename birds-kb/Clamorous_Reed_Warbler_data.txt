<p>The <b>clamorous reed warbler</b> (<i>Acrocephalus stentoreus</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Acrocephalus_(bird)" title="Acrocephalus (bird)">Acrocephalus</a></i>. It breeds from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> eastwards through <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and northernmost <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> to south <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, southeast <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and south to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>. <i>A. s. meridionalis</i> is an <a href="/wiki/Endemic_(ecology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Endemic (ecology)">endemic</a> race in <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clamorous_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Most populations are sedentary, but the breeding birds in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a> and north <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in peninsular India and Sri Lanka.
</p>

<p>This <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in large reed beds, often with some bushes. 3-6 <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid in a basket nest in reeds.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Clamorous_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Clamorous reed warbler is a large <a href="/wiki/Song_thrush" title="Song thrush">song thrush</a>-sized warbler at 18–20&#160;cm. The adult has an unstreaked brown back and whitish underparts. The forehead is flattened, and the bill is strong and pointed. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers. It is very like <a href="/wiki/Great_reed_warbler" title="Great reed warbler">great reed warbler</a>, but that species has richer coloured underparts.
</p><p>There are a number of races differing in <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">plumage</a> shades. The migratory northern race has the richest brown upperparts, and the endemic Sri Lanka subspecies is the darkest form.
</p><p>Like most warblers, clamorous reed warbler is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a>, but will take other small prey items.
</p><p>The song is loud and far carrying, but less raucous than great reed warbler. It is a slow, chattering <i>kereet-kereet-kereet</i> with typically acrocephaline whistles and mimicry added.
</p>
