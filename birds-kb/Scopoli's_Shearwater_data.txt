<p><b>Scopoli's shearwater</b> (<i>Calonectris diomedea</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Procellariidae" title="Procellariidae">Procellariidae</a> currently considered conspecific with <a href="/wiki/Cory%27s_shearwater" title="Cory&#39;s shearwater">Cory's shearwater</a> (<i>Calonectris borealis</i>). It has no <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The genus name <i>Calonectris</i> comes from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>kalos</i>, "good" and <i>nectris</i>, "swimmer". The specific <i>diomedea</i> refers to <a href="/wiki/Diomedes" title="Diomedes">Diomedes</a>, the mythical king. His wife was serially unfaithful while he fought at <a href="/wiki/Trojan_War" title="Trojan War">Troy</a>, so he left to found a city in Italy. After his death, his distraught friends were turned into white seabirds.<sup id="cite_ref-job90_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job90-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The English name is for Italian naturalist <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_Antonio_Scopoli" title="Giovanni Antonio Scopoli">Giovanni Antonio Scopoli</a> who first described the species.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scopoli%27s_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg/220px-Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg/330px-Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg/440px-Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="602" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Calonectris_diomedea_1zz.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Skull of a Scopoli's shearwater</div></div></div>
<p><i>Calonectris diomedea</i> breeds across the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>; on <a href="/wiki/Menorca" title="Menorca">Menorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ibiza" title="Ibiza">Ibiza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formentera" title="Formentera">Formentera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cabrera,_Balearic_Islands" title="Cabrera, Balearic Islands">Cabrera</a>, <a href="/wiki/Conillera" class="mw-redirect" title="Conillera">Conillera</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dragonera" title="Dragonera">Dragonera</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Balearics" class="mw-redirect" title="Balearics">Balearics</a>; on the <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Eles_d%27Hy%C3%A8res" title="Îles d&#39;Hyères">Îles d'Hyères</a> in France; on <a href="/wiki/Elba" title="Elba">Elba</a>, small islands off <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sicily" title="Sicily">Sicily</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pantelleria" title="Pantelleria">Pantelleria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lampedusa" title="Lampedusa">Lampedusa</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>; In <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>; on coastal <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatian</a> islands and parts of the mainland; on <a href="/wiki/Kythira" title="Kythira">Kythira</a>, Kolpos and the <a href="/wiki/Cyclades" title="Cyclades">Cyclades</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The majority of the population spends the non-breeding season in the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a>, including areas off the west coast of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and east coast of <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, as well as on the Greek islands.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Scopoli%27s_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Their diet is mostly <a href="/wiki/Squid" title="Squid">squid</a>, which are obtained mainly by surface-seizing. They are regularly attracted to trawlers to feed on <a href="/wiki/Offal" title="Offal">offal</a>. Sometimes they defecate on visitors who draw too near to their habitation.<sup id="cite_ref-IUCN_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IUCN-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
