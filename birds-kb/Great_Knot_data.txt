<p>The <b>Great Knot</b> (<i>Calidris tenuirostris</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a>. It is the largest of the <a href="/wiki/Calidrid" title="Calidrid">calidrid</a> species.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> in northeast <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>. They nest on the ground laying about four eggs in a ground scrape. They are strongly <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> wintering on coasts in southern Asia through to Australia. This species forms enormous flocks in winter. It is a rare <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrant</a> to western Europe.</p>
<p>This species has short dark legs and a medium-length thin dark bill. Breeding adults have mottled greyish upperparts with some rufous feathering. The face, throat and breast are heavily spotted black, and there are also some streaks on the rear belly. In winter the plumage becomes uniformly pale grey above.</p>
<p>This bird is closely related to the more widespread <a href="/wiki/Red_Knot" title="Red Knot">Red Knot</a>. In breeding plumage, the latter has a distinctive red face, throat and breast. In other plumages, the Great Knot can be identified by its larger size, longer bill, deeper chest, and the more streaked upperparts.</p>
<p>These birds forage on mudflats and beaches, probing or picking up food by sight. They mainly eat <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" title="Mollusc" class="mw-redirect">molluscs</a> and insects.</p>

<p><br style="clear:both" /></p>
<p>The Great Knot is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Knot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Conservation status">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_status">Conservation status</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Knot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Australia">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span></h3>
<p>Great Knot are not listed as threatened on the <a href="/wiki/Environment_Protection_and_Biodiversity_Conservation_Act_1999" title="Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999">Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Great_Knot&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: State of Victoria, Australia">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="State_of_Victoria.2C_Australia">State of Victoria, Australia</span></h3>
<ul>
<li>Great Knot are listed as <b>threatened</b> on the Victorian <a href="/wiki/Flora_and_Fauna_Guarantee_Act_(1988)" title="Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988)" class="mw-redirect">Flora and Fauna Guarantee Act (1988)</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Under this Act, an <i>Action Statement</i> for the recovery and future management of this species has not been prepared.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>On the 2007 advisory list of threatened vertebrate fauna in Victoria, this species is listed as <a href="/wiki/Endangered" title="Endangered" class="mw-redirect">endangered</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DSE200_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DSE200-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
</ul>
