<p>The <b>Levant Sparrowhawk</b> (<i>Accipiter brevipes</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a> which also includes many other diurnal <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">raptors</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Eagle" title="Eagle">eagles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buzzard" title="Buzzard">buzzards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harrier_(bird)" title="Harrier (bird)">harriers</a>.</p>
<p>It breeds in forests from <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a> east to southern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering from <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> across to southwestern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. It will migrate in large flocks, unlike the more widespread <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_Sparrowhawk" title="Eurasian Sparrowhawk">Eurasian Sparrowhawk</a></p>
<p>The Levant Sparrowhawk nests in trees, building a new nest, lined with green leaves, each year. The normal clutch is 3-5 eggs. It hunts small <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a> in woodland, relying on surprise as it flies from a perch to catch its prey unaware.</p>
<p>This bird is a small raptor with short broad wings and a longish tail, both adaptations to manoeuvring through trees. It is similar to the Eurasian Sparrowhawk, but its shorter tail and more pointed wings give it a more <a href="/wiki/Falcon" title="Falcon">falcon-like</a> appearance.</p>
<p>Levant Sparrowhawk is 30–37&#160;cm long with a 63–76&#160;cm wingspan. The female is larger than the male, but the difference is not as marked as with Eurasian Sparrowhawk. The adult male is blue-grey above, with dark wingtips, and barred reddish below.</p>
<p>The adult female is slate-grey above with darkish wingtips. She is barred reddish brown below, and may show a dark throat line. The juvenile is dark brown above and has dark-streaked underparts. It shows a dark throat line. The flight of this hawk is a characteristic "flap – flap – glide".</p>
<p>The call is a sharp "kee-wick".</p>
