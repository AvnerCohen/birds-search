<p>The <b>spur-winged lapwing</b> or <b>spur-winged plover</b> (<i>Vanellus spinosus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Lapwing" title="Lapwing">lapwing</a> <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>, one of a group of largish <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">waders</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Charadriidae" title="Charadriidae">Charadriidae</a>.
</p><p>It is one of several species of wader supposed to be the "trochilus" bird said by <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a> to have been involved in an unattested <a href="/wiki/Cleaning_symbiosis" title="Cleaning symbiosis">cleaning symbiosis</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Nile_crocodile" title="Nile crocodile">Nile crocodile</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spur-winged_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The spur-winged lapwing breeds around the eastern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, and in a wide band from sub-<a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Saharan</a> west <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> to <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkish</a> breeders are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, but other populations are resident. The species is declining in its northern range, but is abundant in much of tropical Africa, being seen at almost any wetland habitat in its range. The spur-winged lapwing is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spur-winged_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>These are conspicuous and unmistakable <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. They are medium-large waders with black crown, chest, foreneck stripe and tail. The face, the rest of the neck and belly are white and the wings and back are light brown. The bill and legs are black. Its striking appearance is supplemented by its noisy nature, with a loud <i>did-he-do-it</i> call. The bird's common name refers to a small <a href="/wiki/Claw" title="Claw">claw</a> or spur hidden in each of its wings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology_and_behaviour">Ecology and behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spur-winged_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology and behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species has a preference for marshes and similar freshwater wetland habitats. The food of the spur-winged lapwing is <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other invertebrates, which are picked from the ground.
</p><p>It lays four blotchy yellowish eggs on a ground scrape. The spur-winged lapwing is known to sometimes use the wing-claws in an attack on animals and, rarely, people, who get too close to the birds' exposed offspring.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Supposed_cleaning_symbiosis">Supposed cleaning symbiosis</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spur-winged_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Supposed cleaning symbiosis">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cleaning_symbiosis" title="Cleaning symbiosis">cleaning symbiosis</a></div>
<p>The "spur-winged plover" was identified by <a href="/wiki/Henry_Scherren" title="Henry Scherren">Henry Scherren</a> as the "trochilus" bird said by the Greek historian <a href="/wiki/Herodotus" title="Herodotus">Herodotus</a><sup id="cite_ref-Herodotus_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Herodotus-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> to be involved in what would now be called a <a href="/wiki/Cleaning_symbiosis" title="Cleaning symbiosis">cleaning symbiosis</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Nile_crocodile" title="Nile crocodile">Nile crocodile</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Scherren_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scherren-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, there is no reliable evidence that this or any other species in fact has such a relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-MacFarlandReeder_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MacFarlandReeder-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
