<p><b>Sykes's warbler</b> (<i>Iduna rama</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tree_warbler" title="Tree warbler">tree warbler</a> family. It was formerly considered a subspecies of the <a href="/wiki/Booted_warbler" title="Booted warbler">booted warbler</a>, but is now considered a full species. Its breeding <a href="/wiki/Range_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Range (biology)">range</a> is from northeast <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Turkestan" title="Turkestan">Turkestan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Western_China" title="Western China">West China</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>. Like the booted warbler, many populations of the species <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> in winter to the <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">Indian</a> subcontinent as far south as <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sykes%27s_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sykes%27s_Warbler_(Hippolais_rama)_W_IMG_6807.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Sykes%27s_Warbler_%28Hippolais_rama%29_W_IMG_6807.jpg/220px-Sykes%27s_Warbler_%28Hippolais_rama%29_W_IMG_6807.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="236" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Sykes%27s_Warbler_%28Hippolais_rama%29_W_IMG_6807.jpg/330px-Sykes%27s_Warbler_%28Hippolais_rama%29_W_IMG_6807.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Sykes%27s_Warbler_%28Hippolais_rama%29_W_IMG_6807.jpg/440px-Sykes%27s_Warbler_%28Hippolais_rama%29_W_IMG_6807.jpg 2x" data-file-width="559" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sykes%27s_Warbler_(Hippolais_rama)_W_IMG_6807.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>in <a href="/wiki/Krishna_Wildlife_Sanctuary" title="Krishna Wildlife Sanctuary">Krishna Wildlife Sanctuary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">Andhra Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The English name commemorates the Colonel <a href="/wiki/William_Henry_Sykes" title="William Henry Sykes">William Henry Sykes</a> who served in the British military in India.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Keyserling" class="mw-redirect" title="Alexander Keyserling">Keyserling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johann_Heinrich_Blasius" title="Johann Heinrich Blasius">Blasius</a> gave no explanation of the genus name <i>Iduna</i>, though in <a href="/wiki/Norse_mythology" title="Norse mythology">Norse mythology</a> <a href="/wiki/I%C3%B0unn" title="Iðunn">Iðunn</a>, or Iduna, is the goddess of spring and fertility who was changed into a sparrow to enable her rescue by <a href="/wiki/Loki" title="Loki">Loki</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The specific epithet <i>rama</i> refers to the <a href="/wiki/Hindu" class="mw-redirect" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> god <a href="/wiki/Rama" title="Rama">Rama</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Incarnation" title="Incarnation">incarnation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vishnu" title="Vishnu">Vishnu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sykes%27s_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are differences in the nesting and egg morphology between <i>rama</i> and <i>caligata</i>. It has been suggested that the <i>rama</i> group can be separated from <i>Hippolais</i> and the older genus name of <i>Iduna</i> was proposed<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and molecular data supporting the <i>Iduna</i> clade was found in 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sykes%27s_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg/220px-Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg/330px-Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg/440px-Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1366" data-file-height="720" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Territory_protecting_from_Lesser_Whitethroat.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Territory protecting from Lesser Whitethroat at Kutch</div></div></div>
<p>It is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> found in open country with bushes and other tall vegetation. 3-4 eggs are laid in a nest in a bush or vegetation.  Like most warblers they are insectivorous.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sykes%27s_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is a small warbler, especially compared to others in their genus. They are pale brown (weak tea colour) above and whitish below with buff flanks. The outer tail feathers have pale edges. They have a short pale <a href="/wiki/Supercilium" title="Supercilium">supercilium</a>, and the bill is strong and pointed. Sykes's is larger and greyer than booted, and most resembles an <a href="/wiki/Eastern_olivaceous_warbler" title="Eastern olivaceous warbler">eastern olivaceous warbler</a>.
</p>
