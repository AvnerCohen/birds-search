<p>The <b>Black-bellied Sandgrouse</b> (<i>Pterocles orientalis</i>) is a medium large <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sandgrouse" title="Sandgrouse">sandgrouse</a> family.</p>
<p>The nominate race breeds in <a href="/wiki/Iberian_peninsula" title="Iberian peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Iberia</a>, northwest <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>. The eastern form <i>P. o. arenarius</i> (<a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">Pallas</a>, 1775) is found in <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, western <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>. It is a partial <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, with central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asian</a> birds moving to the Pakistan and northern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> in winter.</p>
<p>This gregarious species breeds on dry open plains and similar habitats, but unlike the <a href="/wiki/Pin-tailed_Sandgrouse" title="Pin-tailed Sandgrouse">Pin-tailed Sandgrouse</a>, it avoids areas completely lacking in vegetation. Its nest is a ground scrape into which three greenish eggs with cryptic markings are laid. Both sexes incubate, but only the male brings water.</p>
<p>The Black-bellied Sandgrouse is 33–39 cm (13–15 in) long and weighs 300–615 g (11–21.7 oz).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The male has a grey head, neck, and breast. The underparts are black and the upperparts are golden-brown with darker markings. There is a thin black border around the lower breast, and a chestnut throat patch.</p>
<p>The female has browner, more finely marked upperparts, including the head and the breast. The underparts and breast band are identical to the male.</p>
<p>The eastern race is paler and heavier than <i>orientalis</i>. Males have yellower upperparts and greyer underparts than the western form. Females are whiter below, but often inseparable.</p>
<p>This sandgrouse has a small, pigeon like head and neck, but a stocky compact body. It has long pointed wings and a fast direct flight. The white underwings and black belly make this species easy to identify while in flight. Flocks fly to watering holes at dawn.</p>
<p>The call is a soft <i>chowrrr rrrr-rrrr</i>.</p>
