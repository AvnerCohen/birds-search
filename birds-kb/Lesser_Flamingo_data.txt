<p>The <b>lesser flamingo</b> (<i>Phoeniconaias minor</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Flamingo" title="Flamingo">flamingo</a> occurring in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan" class="mw-redirect" title="Sub-Saharan">sub-Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and northwestern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Manakadan2020_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Manakadan2020-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Birds are occasionally reported from further north, but these are generally considered <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrants</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The lesser flamingo is the smallest species of flamingo, though it is a tall and large bird by most standards. The species can weigh from 1.2 to 2.7&#160;kg (2.6 to 6.0&#160;lb).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The standing height is around 80 to 90&#160;cm (31 to 35&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-Seaworld_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seaworld-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The total length (from <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">beak</a> to tail) and wingspan are in the same range of measurements, from 90 to 105&#160;cm (35 to 41&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-Seaworld_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seaworld-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Most of the plumage is pinkish white. The clearest difference between this species and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_flamingo" title="Greater flamingo">greater flamingo</a>, the only other Old World species of flamingo, is the much more extensive black on the bill. Size is less helpful unless the species are together, since the sexes of each species also differ in height.
</p><p>The lesser flamingo may be the most numerous species of flamingo, with a population that (at its peak) probably numbered up to two million individual birds. This species feeds primarily on <i><a href="/wiki/Spirulina_(genus)" title="Spirulina (genus)">Spirulina</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Algae" title="Algae">algae</a> which grow only in very <a href="/wiki/Soda_lake" title="Soda lake">alkaline lakes</a>. Presence of flamingo groups near water bodies is indication of <a href="/wiki/Alkali_soils" class="mw-redirect" title="Alkali soils">sodic alkaline</a> water which is not suitable for irrigation use.  Although blue-green in colour, the algae contain the <a href="/wiki/Photosynthetic_pigment" title="Photosynthetic pigment">photosynthetic pigments</a> that give the birds their pink colour. Their deep bill is specialised for filtering tiny food items.
</p><p>Lesser flamingos are prey to a variety of species, including <a href="/wiki/Marabou_stork" title="Marabou stork">marabou stork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baboon" title="Baboon">baboon</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_fish_eagle" title="African fish eagle">African fish eagle</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_wildcat" title="African wildcat">African wildcat</a>, and <a href="/wiki/African_golden_wolf" title="African golden wolf">African golden wolf</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg/220px-Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="193" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg/330px-Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg/440px-Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4894" data-file-height="4287" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phoeniconaias_minor_MHNT_226_RdN_Aftout_Es_Saheli_Mauritanie.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9um_de_Toulouse" title="Muséum de Toulouse">Muséum de Toulouse</a></div></div></div>
<p>In Africa, where they are most numerous, the lesser flamingos breed principally on the highly <a href="/wiki/Corrosive_substance" title="Corrosive substance">caustic</a> <a href="/wiki/Lake_Natron" title="Lake Natron">Lake Natron</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>. Their other African breeding sites are at <a href="/wiki/Etosha_Pan" class="mw-redirect" title="Etosha Pan">Etosha Pan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Makgadikgadi_Pan" title="Makgadikgadi Pan">Makgadikgadi Pan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kamfers_Dam" title="Kamfers Dam">Kamfers Dam</a>. The last confirmed breeding at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aftout_es_Saheli&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aftout es Saheli (page does not exist)">Aftout es Saheli</a> in coastal <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> was in 1965. Breeding occurred at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Magadi" title="Lake Magadi">Lake Magadi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> in 1962 when Lake Natron was unsuitable due to flooding. In the early 20th century, breeding was also observed at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Nakuru" title="Lake Nakuru">Lake Nakuru</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABB_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABB-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The species also breeds in southwestern and southern Asia. In 1974, they bred at the <a href="/wiki/Rann_of_Kutch" title="Rann of Kutch">Rann of Kutch</a>, but since then, only at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zinzuwadia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zinzuwadia (page does not exist)">Zinzuwadia</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Purabcheria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Purabcheria (page does not exist)">Purabcheria</a> <a href="/wiki/Salt_pan_(geology)" title="Salt pan (geology)">salt pans</a> in northwestern India.<sup id="cite_ref-ABB_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABB-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Manakadan2020_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Manakadan2020-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Some movement of individuals occurs between Africa and India.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Like all flamingos, they lay a single chalky-white <a href="/wiki/Bird_egg" title="Bird egg">egg</a> on a mound they build of mud. Chicks join <a href="/wiki/Creche_(zoology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Creche (zoology)">creches</a> soon after hatching, sometimes numbering over 100,000 individuals. The creches are marshalled by a few adult birds that lead them by foot to fresh water, a journey that can reach over 20&#160;mi (32&#160;km).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite being the most numerous species of flamingo, it is classified as <a href="/wiki/Near_threatened" class="mw-redirect" title="Near threatened">near threatened</a> due to its declining population and the low number of breeding sites, some of which are threatened by human activities.<sup id="cite_ref-Save_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Save-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The population in the two key East African lakes, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Nakuru" title="Lake Nakuru">Nakuru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Bogoria" title="Lake Bogoria">Bogoria</a>, have been adversely affected in recent years by suspected  <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metals" title="Heavy metals">heavy metal</a> <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoning</a>, while its primary African breeding area in Lake Natron is currently under threat by a proposed soda ash plant by <a href="/wiki/Tata_Chemicals" title="Tata Chemicals">Tata Chemicals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABB2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABB2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The only breeding site in South Africa, situated at <a href="/wiki/Kamfers_Dam" title="Kamfers Dam">Kamfers Dam</a>, is threatened by pollution and encroaching development.<sup id="cite_ref-MG_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MG-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The lesser flamingo is one of the species to which the <a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</a> applies.
</p>
