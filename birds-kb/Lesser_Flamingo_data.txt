<p>The <b>Lesser Flamingo</b> (<i>Phoenicopterus minor</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Flamingo" title="Flamingo">flamingo</a> occurring in <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan" title="Sub-Saharan" class="mw-redirect">sub-Saharan</a> <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> with another population in India. Birds are occasionally reported from further north, but these are generally considered vagrants.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span></h2>
<p>The Lesser Flamingo is the smallest species of flamingo, though it is a tall and largish bird by most standards. The species can weigh from 1.2 to 2.7 kg (2.6 to 6.0 lb).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> The standing height is around 80 to 90 cm (31 to 35 in).<sup id="cite_ref-Seaworld_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seaworld-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The total length (from <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">beak</a> to tail) and wingspan are in the same range of measurements, from 90 to 105 cm (35 to 41 in).<sup id="cite_ref-Seaworld_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seaworld-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg/220px-Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg/330px-Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg/440px-Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lesser_Flamingos_I3_IMG_9297.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
In <a href="/wiki/Chilika" title="Chilika" class="mw-redirect">Chilika</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orissa" title="Orissa">Orissa</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Most of the plumage is pinkish white. The clearest difference between this species and the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Flamingo" title="Greater Flamingo">Greater Flamingo</a>, the only other Old World species of flamingo, is the much more extensive black on the bill. Size is less helpful unless the species are together, since the sexes of each species also differ in height.</p>
<p>The Lesser Flamingo may be the most numerous species of flamingo, with a population that (at its peak) probably numbers up to two million individual birds. This species feeds primarily on <i><a href="/wiki/Spirulina_(dietary_supplement)" title="Spirulina (dietary supplement)">Spirulina</a></i>, algae which grow only in very alkaline lakes. Although blue-green in colour, the algae contain the <a href="/wiki/Photosynthetic_pigment" title="Photosynthetic pigment">photosynthetic pigments</a> that give the birds their pink colour. Their deep bill is specialised for filtering tiny food items. The lesser flamingo also feeds on shrimp.</p>
<p>Lesser Flamingos are prey to a variety of species, including <a href="/wiki/Marabou_Stork" title="Marabou Stork">Marabou Storks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baboon" title="Baboon">Baboons</a>, <a href="/wiki/African_Fish_Eagle" title="African Fish Eagle">African Fish Eagles</a> and Wildcats.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Threats">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Threats">Threats</span></h2>
<p>In the Rann of Kutch salt marsh of India and Pakistan, Greater Flamingos are occasionally electrocuted when they sit on 1000 watt electric cables near their breeding areas. Recently 49 deaths were officially recorded in the region.</p>
<p>People are killing them for food in the salt marshes Rann of Kutch in India and Pakistan.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h2>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, where they are most numerous, the Lesser Flamingos breed principally on the highly <a href="/wiki/Corrosive_substance" title="Corrosive substance">caustic</a> <a href="/wiki/Lake_Natron" title="Lake Natron">Lake Natron</a> in northern <a href="/wiki/Tanzania" title="Tanzania">Tanzania</a>. Their other African breeding sites are at <a href="/wiki/Etosha_Pan" title="Etosha Pan" class="mw-redirect">Etosha Pan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Makgadikgadi_Pan" title="Makgadikgadi Pan">Sua Pan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamfers_Dam" title="Kamfers Dam">Kamfers Dam</a>. The last confirmed breeding at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Aftout_es_Saheli&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Aftout es Saheli (page does not exist)">Aftout es Saheli</a> in coastal <a href="/wiki/Mauritania" title="Mauritania">Mauritania</a> was in 1965. Breeding occurred at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Magadi" title="Lake Magadi">Lake Magadi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a> in 1962 when Lake Natron was unsuitable due to flooding. In the early 20th century, breeding was also observed at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Nakuru" title="Lake Nakuru">Lake Nakuru</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABB_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABB-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The species also breeds in southwestern and southern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. In 1974, they bred at the <a href="/wiki/Rann_of_Kutch" title="Rann of Kutch">Rann of Kutch</a>, but since then only at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zinzuwadia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Zinzuwadia (page does not exist)">Zinzuwadia</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Purabcheria&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Purabcheria (page does not exist)">Purabcheria</a> salt pans in north-western <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABB_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABB-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Like all flamingos, they lay a single chalky white egg on a mound they build of mud. Chicks join creches soon after hatching, sometimes numbering over a hundred thousand individuals. The creches are marshalled by a few adult birds who lead them by foot to fresh water, a journey that can reach over 20 miles (32&#160;km).</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Lesser_Flamingo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria,_Kenya-8a.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria%2C_Kenya-8a.jpg/220px-Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria%2C_Kenya-8a.jpg" width="220" height="148" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria%2C_Kenya-8a.jpg/330px-Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria%2C_Kenya-8a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria%2C_Kenya-8a.jpg/440px-Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria%2C_Kenya-8a.jpg 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Phoenicopterus_minor_-Lake_Bogoria,_Kenya-8a.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A huge flock of Lesser Flamingoes at <a href="/wiki/Lake_Bogoria" title="Lake Bogoria">Lake Bogoria</a>, Kenya</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Despite being the most numerous species of flamingo, it is classified as near-<a href="/wiki/Threatened" title="Threatened" class="mw-redirect">threatened</a> due to its declining population and the low number of breeding sites, some of which are threatened by human activities.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The population in the two key east African lakes, <a href="/wiki/Lake_Nakuru" title="Lake Nakuru">Nakuru</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lake_Bogoria" title="Lake Bogoria">Bogoria</a>, have been adversely affected in recent years by suspected <a href="/wiki/Heavy_metals" title="Heavy metals" class="mw-redirect">heavy metal</a> <a href="/wiki/Poison" title="Poison">poisoning</a>, while its primary African breeding area in <a href="/wiki/Lake_Natron" title="Lake Natron">Lake Natron</a> is currently under threat by a proposed soda ash plant by <a href="/wiki/Tata_Chemicals" title="Tata Chemicals">Tata Chemicals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ABB2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABB2-7"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The only breeding site in South Africa, situated at <a href="/wiki/Kamfers_Dam" title="Kamfers Dam">Kamfers Dam</a>, is threatened by pollution and encroaching development.<sup id="cite_ref-MG_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MG-8"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The Lesser Flamingo is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
