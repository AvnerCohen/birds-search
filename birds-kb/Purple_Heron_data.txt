<p>The <b>Purple Heron</b> (<i>Ardea purpurea</i>) is a wading <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a> family <a href="/wiki/Ardeidae" title="Ardeidae" class="mw-redirect">Ardeidae</a>, breeding in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, central and southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, and southern and eastern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. The European populations are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in tropical Africa; the more northerly Asian populations also migrate further south within Asia. It is a rare but regular wanderer north of its breeding range.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Purple_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Purple Heron is a large bird, 78–97 cm (31–38 in) in length with a standing height from to 70 to 94 cm (28 to 37 in) and a 120–152 cm (47–60 in) wingspan.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> However, it is slender for its size, weighing only 0.5–1.35 kg (1.1–3.0 lb).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> It is somewhat smaller than the <a href="/wiki/Grey_Heron" title="Grey Heron">Grey Heron</a>, from which it can be distinguished by its darker reddish-brown <a href="/wiki/Feather" title="Feather">plumage</a>, and, in adults, darker grey back. It has a narrower yellow bill, which is brighter in breeding adults. The most similarly-colored heron is the <a href="/wiki/Goliath_Heron" title="Goliath Heron">Goliath Heron</a>, which is considerably larger.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Purple_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>There are three or four <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a>:</p>
<ul>
<li><i>Ardea purpurea purpurea</i> Linnaeus, 1766. Africa, Europe north to the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>, and southwestern Asia east to <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>.</li>
<li><i>Ardea purpurea bournei</i> (<a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_de_Naurois" title="René de Naurois">de Naurois</a>, 1966), <a href="/wiki/Bourne%27s_Heron" title="Bourne's Heron">Bourne's Heron</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands" class="mw-redirect">Cape Verde Islands</a> (included in <i>purpurea</i> by some authors, but treated as a distinct species <i>Ardea bournei</i> by some others).</li>
<li><i>Ardea purpurea madagascariensis</i> Oort, 1910. <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>.</li>
<li><i>Ardea purpurea manilensis</i> Meyen, 1834. Asia from <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> east to the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>, and north to <a href="/wiki/Primorsky_Krai" title="Primorsky Krai">Primorsky Krai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Purple_Heron&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>The Purple Heron breeds in <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonies</a> in reed beds or trees close to large lakes or other extensive wetlands. It builds a bulky stick nest.</p>
<p>It feeds in shallow water, spearing <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Frogs" title="Frogs" class="mw-redirect">frogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insects" title="Insects" class="mw-redirect">insects</a> and small <a href="/wiki/Mammals" title="Mammals" class="mw-redirect">mammals</a>. It will often wait motionless for prey, or slowly stalk its victim. It tends to keep within reedbeds more than the <a href="/wiki/Grey_Heron" title="Grey Heron">Grey Heron</a>, and is often inconspicuous, despite its size.</p>
<p>It has a slow flight, with its neck retracted. This is characteristic of herons and <a href="/wiki/Bittern" title="Bittern">bitterns</a>, and distinguishes them from <a href="/wiki/Stork" title="Stork">storks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crane_(bird)" title="Crane (bird)">cranes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spoonbill" title="Spoonbill">spoonbills</a>, which extend their necks. The long neck of Purple Heron looks particularly snake-like, with more of an S-shape in flight. The call is a loud croaking "krek".</p>
<p>The Purple Heron is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
