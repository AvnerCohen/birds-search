<p>The <b>Pallid Scops Owl</b> (<i>Otus brucei</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Scops_Owl" title="Scops Owl" class="mw-redirect">Scops Owl</a> ranging from the Middle East to West and Central Asia, sometimes called the <b>Striated Scops Owl</b>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Pallid Scops Owl is a small eared owl similar in appearance to the <a href="/wiki/Common_Scops_Owl" title="Common Scops Owl">Common Scops Owl</a><sup class="plainlinks noprint Inline-Template" style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="//toolserver.org/%7Edispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py?page=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn"><span title="Link needs disambiguation from October 2012">disambiguation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> but with more distinct streaks on the back and less intricate markings.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>The Pallid Scops Owl ranges from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> to West and Central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, with some populations <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">moving</a> as far as the Arabian Peninsula, Egypt, and Pakistan in the winter. It inhabits semi-open country with trees and bushes and has an estimated range of one to ten million kilometers.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span></h3>
<p>Primarily an insectivore, the Pallid Scops Owl's diet includes <a href="/wiki/Insects" title="Insects" class="mw-redirect">insects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lizards" title="Lizards" class="mw-redirect">lizards</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spiders" title="Spiders" class="mw-redirect">spiders</a>, and small <a href="/wiki/Mammals" title="Mammals" class="mw-redirect">mammals</a>. It occasionally hunts during the day, and has been known to take bats and insects on the wing.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nesting">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Nesting">Nesting</span></h3>
<p>Breeding takes place any time between April and June, where 4 to 6 eggs are laid in a tree cavity, such as a woodpecker hole. Incubation takes approximately 27 days, and the juveniles are fledged at 30 days.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallid_Scops_Owl&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Voice">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Voice">Voice</span></h3>
<p>The Pallid Scops Owl's call is a series of low, hollow, dove-like notes.</p>
