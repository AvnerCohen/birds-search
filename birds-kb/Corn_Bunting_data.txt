<p>The <b>Corn Bunting</b> (<i>Miliaria calandra</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family Emberizidae, a group now separated by most modern authors from the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finches</a>, Fringillidae. It is the sole member of the genus <i>Miliaria</i>, although a few authorities place it in the large genus <i>Emberiza</i>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>This is an unusual bunting because the sexes appear similar in plumage, although the males are approximately 20% larger than females. This large bulky bunting is 16–19&#160;cm long, has male and female plumages similar, and lacks the showy male colours, especially on the head, common in the genus <i>Emberiza</i>. Both sexes look something like <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">larks</a>, with streaked grey-brown above, and whitish underparts.</p>
<p>The song of the male is a repetitive metallic sound, usually likened to jangling keys, which is given from a low bush, fence post or <a href="/wiki/Telephone" title="Telephone">telephone</a> wires.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>It breeds across southern and central <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> across to <a href="/wiki/Kazahkstan" title="Kazahkstan" class="mw-redirect">Kazahkstan</a>. It is mainly resident, but some birds from colder regions of central Europe and Asia <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> southwards in winter.</p>
<p>The Corn Bunting is a bird of open country with trees, such as farmland and weedy wasteland. It has declined greatly in northwest Europe due to intensive agricultural practices depriving it of its food supply of weed seeds and insects, the latter especially when feeding young.It has recently become extinct in Ireland, where it was previously common.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span></h3>
<p>Its natural food consists of insects when feeding young, and otherwise seeds.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_calandra.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Emberiza calandra.jpg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Emberiza_calandra.jpg/220px-Emberiza_calandra.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_calandra.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Males defend territories in the breeding season and can be <a href="/wiki/Polygynous" title="Polygynous" class="mw-redirect">polygynous</a>, with up to three females per breeding male. The population sex ratio is generally 1:1, which means some males remain unmated during a season. Males play only a small role in parental care; they are not involved in nest building or incubation, and only feed the chicks when they are over half grown.</p>
<p>The nest is made of grass, lined with hair or fine grass, and is usually built on the ground. Average clutch size is 4, but commonly varies from 3 to 5, occasionally 6.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_Bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Environmental conservation">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental_conservation">Environmental conservation</span></h2>
