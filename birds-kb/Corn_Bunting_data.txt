<p>The <b>corn bunting</b> (<i>Emberiza calandra</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bunting_(bird)" title="Bunting (bird)">bunting</a> family Emberizidae, a group now separated by most modern authors from the <a href="/wiki/Finch" title="Finch">finches</a>, Fringillidae. This is a large bunting with heavily steaked buff-brown plumage. The sexes are similar but the male is slightly larger than the female. Its range extends from Western Europe and North Africa across to northwestern China. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The corn bunting was <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">formally described</a> by the Swedish naturalist <a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> in 1758 in the <a href="/wiki/10th_edition_of_Systema_Naturae" title="10th edition of Systema Naturae">tenth edition</a> of his <i><a href="/wiki/Systema_Naturae" title="Systema Naturae">Systema Naturae</a></i> and retains its original <a href="/wiki/Binomial_nomenclature" title="Binomial nomenclature">binomial</a> name of <i>Emberiza calandra</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Type_locality_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Type locality (biology)">type locality</a> is Sweden.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  The genus name <i>Emberiza</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Old_High_German" title="Old High German">Old German</a> <i>Embritz</i>, a bunting. The specific <i>calandra</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>kalandros</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Calandra_lark" title="Calandra lark">calandra lark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The corn bunting has sometimes been placed in its own <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> genus <i>Miliaria</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTECrampPerrins1994323_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrampPerrins1994323-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognised:<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>E. c. calandra</i> Linnaeus, 1758 – northwest Africa, Canary Islands and Europe to Turkey, the Caucasus and north Iran</li>
<li><i>E. c. buturlini</i> <a href="/wiki/Hans_Johansen" title="Hans Johansen">Johansen, HE</a>, 1907  –  Middle East to northwest China</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting,_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting%2C_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg/220px-Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting%2C_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="157" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting%2C_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg/330px-Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting%2C_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting%2C_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg/440px-Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting%2C_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2050" data-file-height="1460" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_calandra_-_Corn_Bunting,_Kahramanmara%C5%9F_2016-11-18_03-4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In Turkey</div></div></div>
<p>This is an unusual bunting because the plumages of the sexes are similar in appearance, though the male is approximately 20% larger than the female. This large bulky bunting is 16–19&#160;cm long, with a conspicuously dark eye and yellowish mandibles. Males lack any showy colours, especially on the head, which is otherwise typical of genus <i>Emberiza</i>. Both sexes look something like <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">larks</a>, being streaked grey-brown above with whitish underparts. The underparts are streaked over the flanks and breast, and the streaking forms gorget around the throat. The lesser wing coverts are distinctively dark and white-tipped. The tail is plain brown.<sup id="cite_ref-bfg_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bfg-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The song of the male is a repetitive metallic sound, usually likened to jangling keys, which is given from a low bush, fence post or telephone wires. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It breeds across southern and central <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Asia</a> across to <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>. It is mainly resident, but some birds from colder regions of central Europe and Asia <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> southwards in winter.
</p><p>The corn bunting is a bird of open country with trees, such as farmland and weedy wasteland. It has declined greatly in north-west Europe due to intensive agricultural practices depriving it of its food supply of weed seeds and insects, the latter especially  vital when feeding  the young. It has recently become extinct in <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, where it was previously common.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Its natural food consists mainly of seeds but also includes insects such as crickets, especially when feeding young.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg/220px-Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="215" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg/330px-Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg/440px-Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3546" data-file-height="3468" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Emberiza_calandra_MHNT.ZOO.2010.11.217_Chiach_Maroc-Naurois.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, <a href="/wiki/Mus%C3%A9um_de_Toulouse" title="Muséum de Toulouse">Muséum de Toulouse</a></div></div></div> 
<p>Males defend territories in the breeding season and can be <a href="/wiki/Polygynous" class="mw-redirect" title="Polygynous">polygynous</a>, with up to three females per breeding male. The population sex ratio is generally 1:1, which means some males remain unmated during a season. Males play only a small role in parental care; they are not involved in nest building or incubation, and only feed the chicks when they are over half grown.
</p><p>The nest is made of grass, lined with hair or fine grass, and is usually built on the ground. Average clutch size is four, but commonly varies from three to five, occasionally six.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status_and_conservation">Status and conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Corn_bunting&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Status and conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In England, the government's environmental organisation <a href="/wiki/Natural_England" title="Natural England">Natural England</a> offers grants towards implementing measures to conserve this species, under the <a href="/wiki/Environmental_stewardship_(England)" title="Environmental stewardship (England)">environmental stewardship</a> scheme.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
