<p>The <b>Ferruginous Duck</b>, also <b>Ferruginous Pochard</b> (<i>Aythya nyroca</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Diving_duck" title="Diving duck">diving duck</a> from <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. The species is known colloquially by birders as "Fudge Duck".</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshes</a> and lakes with a metre or more water depth. These <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">ducks</a> breed in southern and eastern Europe and southern and western Asia. They are somewhat <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, and winter farther south and into north Africa.</p>
<p>The adult male is a rich chestnut colour with a darker back and a yellow eye. The pure white undertail helps to distinguish this species from the somewhat similar <a href="/wiki/Tufted_Duck" title="Tufted Duck">Tufted Duck</a>. The female is similar but duller, and with a dark eye.</p>
<p>These are gregarious birds, forming large flocks in winter, often mixed with other diving ducks, such as <a href="/wiki/Tufted_Duck" title="Tufted Duck">Tufted Ducks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Common_Pochard" title="Common Pochard">Pochards</a>.</p>
<p>These birds feed mainly by diving or dabbling. They eat <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_plants" title="Aquatic plants" class="mw-redirect">aquatic plants</a> with some <a href="/wiki/Molluscs" title="Molluscs" class="mw-redirect">molluscs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aquatic_insects" title="Aquatic insects">aquatic insects</a> and small fish. They often feed at night, and will upend (dabble) for food as well as the more characteristic diving.</p>
<p>It is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
<p>Hybrids between this species and the Common Pochard are sometimes referred to as "Paget's Pochard".</p>
