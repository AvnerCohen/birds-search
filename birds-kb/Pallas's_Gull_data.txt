<p><b>Pallas's gull</b> (<i>Ichthyaetus ichthyaetus</i>), also known as the <b>great black-headed gull</b>, is a large <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> species. As is the case with many gulls, it has traditionally been placed in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Larus" title="Larus">Larus</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pons2005_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pons2005-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The scientific name is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a>. <i>Ichthyaetus</i> is from <i>ikhthus</i>, "fish", and <i>aetos</i>, "eagle".<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg/220px-Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg/330px-Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg/440px-Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1148" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pallas%27s_Gull_Assuming_breeding_plumage.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>at Kutch</div></div></div>
<p>This species breeds in colonies in marshes and islands from southern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. This gull nests on the ground, laying between two and four eggs.
</p><p>It occurs in western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> only as a rare vagrant. In <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> a recent review left a single occurrence in 1859 as the only acceptable record of this bird. The species also occurs as a vagrant in differing parts of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, south of its normal range, and along the northern and eastern coasts of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, where it visits annually on an irregular basis.<sup id="cite_ref-Stevenson2001_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stevenson2001-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg/220px-Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg/330px-Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg/440px-Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2602" data-file-height="1758" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A juvenile</div></div></div>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a very large gull, being easily the world's largest black-headed gull and the third largest species of gull in the world, after the <a href="/wiki/Great_black-backed_gull" title="Great black-backed gull">great black-backed gull</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Glaucous_gull" title="Glaucous gull">glaucous gull</a>. It measures 55–72&#160;cm (22–28&#160;in) in length with a 142 to 170&#160;cm (56 to 67&#160;in) wingspan.<sup id="cite_ref-Panayotov_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Panayotov-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Olsen_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsen-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Harrison_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrison-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Weight can vary from 0.96–2.1&#160;kg (2.1–4.6&#160;lb), with an average of 1.6&#160;kg (3.5&#160;lb) in males and 1.22&#160;kg (2.7&#160;lb) in females.<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Among standard measurements, the <a href="/wiki/Wing_chord_(biology)" title="Wing chord (biology)">wing chord</a> is 43.5 to 52&#160;cm (17.1 to 20.5&#160;in), the bill is 4.7 to 7.3&#160;cm (1.9 to 2.9&#160;in) and the <a href="/wiki/Tarsus_(skeleton)" title="Tarsus (skeleton)">tarsus</a> is 6.5 to 8.4&#160;cm (2.6 to 3.3&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-Olsen_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsen-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Summer adults are unmistakable, since no other gull of this size has a black hood. The adults have grey wings and back, with conspicuous white "mirrors" at the wing tips. The legs are yellow and the bill is orangey-yellow with a red tip.
</p><p>In all other plumages, a dark mask through the eye indicates the vestiges of the hood. The call is a deep <i>aargh</i> cry. Young birds attain largely grey upperparts quite rapidly, but they take four years to reach maturity.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Pallas%27s_gull&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird has a deep, rather nasal flight-call which resembles the call of the <a href="/wiki/Lesser_black-backed_gull" title="Lesser black-backed gull">lesser black-backed gull</a>. Although they are noisy at colonies, Pallas's gulls are mostly silent when breeding.
</p><p>These birds are predatory, taking fish, crustaceans, insects and even small mammals.
</p><p>The Pallas's gull is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
