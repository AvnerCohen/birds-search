<p>The <b>Pallas's Gull</b> or <b>Great Black-headed Gull</b>, <i>Ichthyaetus ichthyaetus</i>, is a large <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gull</a>. As is the case with many gulls, it has traditionally been placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Larus" title="Larus">Larus</a></i>.</p>
<p>This species breeds in colonies in marshes and islands from southern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mongolia" title="Mongolia">Mongolia</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in the eastern <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabia</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. This gull nests on the ground, laying between two and four eggs.</p>
<p>It occurs in western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> only as a rare vagrant. In <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> a recent review left a single occurrence in 1859 as the only acceptable record of this <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a>. The species also occurs as a vagrant in differing parts of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>, south of its normal range, and along the northern and eastern coasts of <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, where it visits annually on an irregular basis.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Larus_ichthyaetus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Juvenile Great Black-headed Gull</div>

<p>This is a very large gull, being easily the world's largest black-headed gull and the third largest species of gull in the world, after the <a href="/wiki/Great_Black-backed_Gull" title="Great Black-backed Gull">Great Black-backed Gull</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Glaucous_Gull" title="Glaucous Gull">Glaucous Gull</a>. It measures 55–72 cm (22–28 in) in length with a 142 to 170 cm (56 to 67 in) wingspan.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Olsen_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsen-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Harrison_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Harrison-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> Weight can vary from 0.96–2.1 kg (2.1–4.6 lb), with an average of 1.6&#160;kg (3.5&#160;lb) in males and 1.22&#160;kg (2.7&#160;lb) in females.<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> Among standard measurements, the <a href="/wiki/Wing_chord_(biology)" title="Wing chord (biology)">wing chord</a> is 43.5 to 52 cm (17.1 to 20 in), the <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is 4.7 to 7.3 cm (1.9 to 2.9 in) and the <a href="/wiki/Tarsus_(skeleton)" title="Tarsus (skeleton)">tarsus</a> is 6.5 to 8.4 cm (2.6 to 3.3 in).<sup id="cite_ref-Olsen_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Olsen-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> Summer adults are unmistakable, since no other gull of this size has a black hood. The adults have grey wings and back, with conspicuous white "mirrors" at the wing tips. The legs are yellow and the bill is red.</p>
<p>In all other plumages, a dark mask through the eye indicates the vestiges of the hood. The call is a deep <i>aargh</i> cry. Young birds attain largely grey upperparts quite rapidly, but they take four years to reach maturity.</p>
<p>These birds are predatory, taking <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crustacean" title="Crustacean">crustaceans</a>, <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and even small <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>.</p>
<p>The Pallas's Gull is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
