<p>The <b>Woodchat Shrike</b> (<i>Lanius senator</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family Laniidae.</p>
<p>The Woodchat breeds in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and northwest <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and winters in tropical Africa. It breeds in open cultivated country, preferably with orchard trees and some bare or sandy ground.</p>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Perching_bird" title="Perching bird" class="mw-redirect">passerine</a> eats large <a href="/wiki/Insects" title="Insects" class="mw-redirect">insects</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Birds" title="Birds" class="mw-redirect">birds</a> and small amphibians. Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a "larder".</p>
<p>The male is a striking bird with black and white plumage and a chestnut crown. The race <i>L. s. badius</i> of the western <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> lacks the large white wing patches.</p>
<p>In the female and young birds the upperparts are brown and vermiculated. Underparts are buff and also vermiculated.</p>
<p>This species often overshoots its breeding range on spring migration, and is a rare, but annual, visitor to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. The Balearic race <i>badius</i> has occurred in Britain around four times as a vagrant, and has also been recorded once in Ireland.</p>
