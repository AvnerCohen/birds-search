<p>The <b>woodchat shrike</b> (<i>Lanius senator</i>) is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family Laniidae. The genus name, <i><a href="/wiki/Lanius" title="Lanius">Lanius</a></i>, is derived from the <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> word for "<a href="/wiki/Butcher" title="Butcher">butcher</a>", and some shrikes are also known as "butcher birds" because of their feeding habits. The specific <i>senator</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "<a href="/wiki/Roman_Senate" title="Roman Senate">senator</a>", so-named because its chestnut cap recalled the colour of the stripe on the <a href="/wiki/Toga" title="Toga">toga</a> of a Roman senator.<sup id="cite_ref-job_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The common name "Woodchat" is an Anglicisation of German <i>waldkatze</i>, literally "woodcat",<sup id="cite_ref-OED2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OED2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and  "shrike" is from <a href="/wiki/Old_English" title="Old English">Old English</a> <i>scríc</i>, "shriek", referring to the shrill call.<sup id="cite_ref-OED_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OED-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The woodchat shrike breeds in <a href="/wiki/Southern_Europe" title="Southern Europe">southern Europe</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maghreb" title="Maghreb">northwest Africa</a>, and winters in tropical Africa. It breeds in open cultivated country, preferably with orchard trees and some bare or sandy ground.<sup id="cite_ref-Lefranc_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lefranc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woodchat_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The male is a striking bird with black and white upper parts, a chestnut crown and pure white underparts. The race <i>L. s. badius</i> of the western <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect" title="Mediterranean">Mediterranean</a> lacks the large white wing patches.  In the female and young birds, the upperparts are brown and white and vermiculated. Underparts are buff and also vermiculated.<sup id="cite_ref-Lefranc_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lefranc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woodchat_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The breeding range of the woodchat shrike is in southern Europe, northern Africa and the Middle East. The range extends from Portugal to Greece, Turkey, Iraq and Iran, and from Mauritania and Western Sahara in northern Africa to Libya. This bird overwinters in tropical central Africa, its winter range extending from Senegal to Sudan and Ethiopia in the east and southwards to Gabon.<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Woodchat_shrike&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Perching_bird" class="mw-redirect" title="Perching bird">passerine</a> eats large <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amphibian" title="Amphibian">amphibians</a>. Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a "larder". This species often overshoots its breeding range on spring migration, and is a rare, but annual, visitor to <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>. The Balearic race <i>badius</i> has occurred in Britain around four times as a vagrant, and has also been recorded once in Ireland.<sup id="cite_ref-Lefranc_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lefranc-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
