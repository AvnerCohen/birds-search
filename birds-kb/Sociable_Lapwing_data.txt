<p>The <b>sociable lapwing</b> or <b>sociable plover</b> (<i>Vanellus gregarius</i>) is a critically endangered <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lapwing" title="Lapwing">lapwing</a> family of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">birds</a>. The genus name is <a href="/wiki/Medieval_Latin" title="Medieval Latin">Medieval Latin</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Northern_lapwing" title="Northern lapwing">lapwing</a> and derives from <i>vannus</i> a <a href="/wiki/Winnowing#In_Greek_culture" title="Winnowing">winnowing fan</a>. The specific <i>gregarius</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "sociable" from <i>grex, gregis</i>, "flock".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sociable_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This medium-sized lapwing has longish black legs and a short black bill. Summer adults have grey backs and breast, dark belly and white undertail. The head has a striking pattern, with a black crown and eyestripe, the latter being bordered above and below with white. The upper neck is ochre. Its longish black legs, white tail with a black terminal band and distinctive brown, white and grey wings make it almost unmistakable in flight. Length is 27–30&#160;cm (11–12&#160;in). The call is a harsh <i>kereck</i>.
</p><p>Winter adults have a less distinct head pattern, slightly browner back and breast, and white belly. Young birds have a scaly back and only traces of the head pattern.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sociable_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species breeds on open grassland in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>. Three to four eggs are laid in a ground nest. These birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> south through <a href="/wiki/Kyrgyzstan" title="Kyrgyzstan">Kyrgyzstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tajikistan" title="Tajikistan">Tajikistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uzbekistan" title="Uzbekistan">Uzbekistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkmenistan" title="Turkmenistan">Turkmenistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, to key wintering sites in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eritrea" title="Eritrea">Eritrea</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> and north-west <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>. Birds winter occasionally in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>. It is a very rare vagrant in western and northern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, where this gregarious bird is usually found with <a href="/wiki/Northern_lapwing" title="Northern lapwing">northern lapwings</a>. It feeds in a similar way, picking <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other small prey mainly from grassland or arable land.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sociable_lapwing&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2004, <a href="/wiki/BirdLife_International" title="BirdLife International">BirdLife International</a> categorised this bird as critically endangered, due to a very rapid population decline for poorly understood reasons. The main decline took place between 1960 and 1987, when the known population halved; it is some 20–25% of the 1930s population levels.<sup id="cite_ref-BirdLifeFactsheet_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLifeFactsheet-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The current population was estimated to be between 600 and 1,800 mature birds in 2006,<sup id="cite_ref-BirdLifeFactsheet_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLifeFactsheet-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> but is being revised to the upward end of that scale, possibly more, following the discovery of the species' previously unknown main wintering grounds in <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, where 1500 birds of all ages were encountered.<sup id="cite_ref-BirdLife_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BirdLife-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, in October 2007, a superflock of approximately 3,200 sociable lapwings were discovered in Turkey, according to Guven Eken, director of the Turkish Nature Association.<sup id="cite_ref-npr_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-npr-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The current <a href="/wiki/IUCN" class="mw-redirect" title="IUCN">IUCN</a> classification is <b>CR A3bc</b>—meaning that the population is expected to decline in the next decade or so by 80%, but based on theoretical considerations and the known <a href="/wiki/Habitat_destruction" title="Habitat destruction">habitat destruction</a> rather than direct observation of the birds. Thus, the new discoveries might mean that as more data becomes available, the species could be downlisted to <a href="/wiki/Endangered" class="mw-redirect" title="Endangered">Endangered</a>.
</p>
