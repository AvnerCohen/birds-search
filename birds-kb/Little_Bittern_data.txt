<p>The <b>little bittern</b> or <b>common little bittern</b> (<i>Ixobrychus minutus</i>) is a wading <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Heron" title="Heron">heron</a> family, <a href="/wiki/Ardeidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Ardeidae">Ardeidae</a>. <i>Ixobrychus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>ixias</i>, a reed-like plant and <i>brukhomai</i>, to bellow, and <i>minutus</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for "small".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bittern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The little bittern is native to the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>, breeding in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, central and southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, western and southern <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Asia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Madagascar" title="Madagascar">Madagascar</a>. Birds from temperate regions in Europe and western Asia are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in Africa and further south in Asia, while those nesting in the tropics are sedentary. It is rare north of its breeding range.<sup id="cite_ref-Rasmussen2005_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rasmussen2005-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a>  there were intermittent reports of breeding in the nineteenth century,  and again in 1946 and 1957, but none of these records were proven. The first proven British breeding record is from <a href="/wiki/Yorkshire" title="Yorkshire">Yorkshire</a> in 1984,<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the second from the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Avalon_Marshes&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Avalon Marshes (page does not exist)">Avalon Marshes</a> in <a href="/wiki/Somerset" title="Somerset">Somerset</a>  in  2010, by 2017 this species had been present in this area for nine consecutive years.<sup id="cite_ref-BTO_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTO-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bittern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Carl Linnaeus</a> described the little bittern in 1766. The three <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are:
</p>
<ul><li><i>I. m. minutus</i> – <small>(<a href="/wiki/Carl_Linnaeus" title="Carl Linnaeus">Linnaeus</a>, 1766)</small>: <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies#Nominotypical_subspecies_and_subspecies_autonyms" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate</a>, found in Europe, Asia, northern Africa; winters in sub-Saharan Africa and southern Asia</li>
<li><i>I. m. payesii</i> – <small>(Hartlaub, 1858)</small>: found in sub-Saharan Africa, resident</li>
<li><i>I. m. podiceps</i> – <small>(Bonaparte, 1855)</small>: found in Madagascar, resident</li></ul>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Australian_little_bittern" class="mw-redirect" title="Australian little bittern">Australian little bittern</a> (<i>I. dubius</i>) and the extinct <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand_little_bittern" class="mw-redirect" title="New Zealand little bittern">New Zealand little bittern</a> (<i>I. novaezelandiae</i>) were formerly considered subspecies of the little bittern.<sup id="cite_ref-Christidis2008_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Christidis2008-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bittern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG/220px-Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="126" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG/330px-Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG/440px-Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2365" data-file-height="1353" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ixobrychus_minutus_MWNH_0907.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Egg, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ixobrychus_minutus_3_(Marek_Szczepanek).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Ixobrychus_minutus_3_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/170px-Ixobrychus_minutus_3_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Ixobrychus_minutus_3_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/255px-Ixobrychus_minutus_3_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Ixobrychus_minutus_3_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg/340px-Ixobrychus_minutus_3_%28Marek_Szczepanek%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="366" data-file-height="550" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ixobrychus_minutus_3_(Marek_Szczepanek).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Immature</div></div></div>
<p>The little bittern has a length of 33–38 centimetres (13–15&#160;in) and a wing span of 52–58 centimetres (20–23&#160;in). It is the smallest of the breeding herons of Europe and is characterised by its tiny size, long and sharp bull and thick neck. The male's are distinctively patterned and both sexes show pale forewing panels. The males have black with a faint green sheen on the crown, nape, back, tail and scapulars. The underparts are pale buff and the wing has a pinkish buff oval shaped panel which contrasts with the otherwise black wings and is formed by the inner wing coverts. The underwing is completely whiteish in colour. The female is duller than the male and has brownish black upperparts with paler feather margins visible at close range. The underparts of the female are not as clean as those of the male and are streaked with dark buff and brown. The female's wing panel is less obvious than the male's. The juveniles are duller and more rufous than the females and are more heavily streaked on both their upperparts and underparts, including their wing coverts.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bittern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The little bittern is one of the species to which the <a href="/wiki/Agreement_on_the_Conservation_of_African-Eurasian_Migratory_Waterbirds" title="Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds">Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</a> applies.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_bittern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The little bittern is <a href="/wiki/Crepuscular" class="mw-redirect" title="Crepuscular">crepuscular</a>, skulking and normally solitary. It feeds on fishes, amphibians and insects which are caught within reedbeds or at their edges by the bird slowly stalking the prey. The male claims a <a href="/wiki/Territoriality" class="mw-redirect" title="Territoriality">territory</a> in the Spring, advertising his presence with a deep  barking or croaking call and the <a href="/wiki/Monogamy" title="Monogamy">monogamous</a> pair remain together for at least one breeding season. Eggs are laid in a nest situated in dense reedbeds, rushes or bushes above the water from the middle of May and there is a single brood which is normally 5-6 eggs. These are incubated for 17–19 days and the chicks are fledged after 25–30 days.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Europe the little bittern is a migratory species, crossing the Mediterranean from Africa in the early Spring and arriving in their breeding wetlands from mid April onwards. The return to Africa occurs in August and September and there are normally only a few juveniles left in Europe by October. The European breeders migrate as far south as the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Cape" title="Eastern Cape">Eastern Cape</a> and <a href="/wiki/Transvaal_Province" class="mw-redirect" title="Transvaal Province">Transvaal</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BWP_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BWP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
