<p>The <b>Black Wheatear</b> (<i>Oenanthe leucura</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wheatear" title="Wheatear">wheatear</a>, a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> that was formerly classed as a member of the <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">Thrush</a> family Turdidae, but is now more generally considered to be an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_flycatcher" title="Old World flycatcher">Old World flycatcher</a>, Muscicapidae.</p>
<p>This large 16–18&#160;cm long wheatear breeds on cliffs and rocky slopes in western north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iberian_peninsula" title="Iberian peninsula" class="mw-redirect">Iberia</a>. It is largely resident and nests in crevices in rocks laying 3-6 eggs.</p>
<p>The male of this species is all black except a white rump and mainly white tail. The female is similar, but dark brown rather than black.</p>
<p>The similar <a href="/wiki/White-crowned_Wheatear" title="White-crowned Wheatear">White-crowned Wheatear</a>, <i>Oenanthe leucopyga,</i> also breeds in the African part of the Black Wheatear's range, but the Black Wheatear has a black inverted "T" on its white tail, whereas White-crowned has only a black centre to its tail. The Black Wheatear never has a white crown, but young White-crowned Wheatears also lack this feature.</p>
<p>The food of this wheatear is mainly insects. It has a loud <a href="/wiki/Thrush_(bird)" title="Thrush (bird)">thrush-like</a> song.</p>
