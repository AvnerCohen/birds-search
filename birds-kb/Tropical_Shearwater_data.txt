<p>The <b>tropical shearwater</b> (<i>Puffinus bailloni</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Procellariidae" title="Procellariidae">Procellariidae</a> formerly considered conspecific with <a href="/wiki/Audubon%27s_shearwater" title="Audubon&#39;s shearwater">Audubon's shearwater</a> (<i>Puffinus lherminieri</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are five listed <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> of the tropical shearwater:<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>Puffinus bailloni nicolae</i> – <small>(<a href="/wiki/Christian_Jouanin" title="Christian Jouanin">Jouanin</a>, 1971)</small>: breeds on islands in the northwest <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">Indian Ocean</a>.</li>
<li><i>Puffinus bailloni colstoni</i> – <small>(<a href="/wiki/Hadoram_Shirihai" title="Hadoram Shirihai">Shirihai</a> &amp; Christie, 1996)</small>: breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Aldabra_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Aldabra Islands">Aldabra Islands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Seychelles" title="Seychelles">Seychelles</a>.</li>
<li><i>Puffinus bailloni bailloni</i> – <small>(<a href="/wiki/Charles_Lucien_Bonaparte" title="Charles Lucien Bonaparte">Bonaparte</a>, 1857)</small>: <a href="/wiki/Mauritius" title="Mauritius">Mauritius</a>, <a href="/wiki/R%C3%A9union" title="Réunion">Réunion</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Europa_Island" title="Europa Island">Europa Island</a>.</li>
<li><i>Puffinus bailloni dichrous</i> – <small>(<a href="/wiki/Otto_Finsch" title="Otto Finsch">Finsch</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Hartlaub" title="Gustav Hartlaub">Hartlaub</a>, 1867)</small>: Central <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific</a> islands.</li>
<li><i>Puffinus bailloni gunax</i> – <small>(<a href="/wiki/Gregory_Mathews" title="Gregory Mathews">Mathews</a>, 1930)</small>: <a href="/wiki/Vanuatu" title="Vanuatu">Vanuatu</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Range">Range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The tropical shearwater is found in the tropical parts of the western Indian Ocean from <a href="/wiki/East_Africa" title="East Africa">east Africa</a> to southern <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and in similar regions of the Pacific from just to the southeast of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> to <a href="/wiki/French_Polynesia" title="French Polynesia">French Polynesia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Redman_Stevenson_Fanshawe_2016_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Redman_Stevenson_Fanshawe_2016-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Population">Population</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Population">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The total population has not been definitively quantified. Subspecies <i>P. b. dichrous</i> is estimated to number 1,000–10,000 pairs on the <a href="/wiki/Line_Islands" title="Line Islands">Line Islands</a> and 10,000–100,000 pairs on the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_Islands" title="Phoenix Islands">Phoenix Islands</a>, with the nominate subspecies thought to number 3,000–5,000 pairs on Réunion and fewer than 100 individuals on Europa (reviewed by Brooke 2004), though it is thought there are many more breeding colonies on other islands in the Pacific. The population seems to be stable.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
