<p>The <b>whiskered tern</b> (<i>Chlidonias hybrida</i>) is a  <a href="/wiki/Tern" title="Tern">tern</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Laridae" title="Laridae">Laridae</a>. The genus name is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>khelidonios</i>, "swallow-like", from <i>khelidon</i>, "<a href="/wiki/Swallow" title="Swallow">swallow</a>". The specific <i>hybridus</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for <i>hybrid</i>; <a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">Pallas</a> thought it might be a hybrid of <a href="/wiki/White-winged_black_tern" class="mw-redirect" title="White-winged black tern">white-winged black tern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Common_tern" title="Common tern">common tern</a>, writing "<i>Sterna fissipes [</i>Chlidonias leucopterus<i>] et Hirundine [</i>Sterna hirundo<i>] natam</i>”.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> has a number of geographical races, differing mainly in size and minor plumage details.
</p><p><i>C. h. hybrida</i> breeds in warmer parts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> (northwestern Africa and central and southern Europe to southeastern Siberia, eastern China and south to Pakistan and northern India). The smaller-billed and darker <i>C. h. delalandii</i> is found in east and south <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and the paler <i>C. h. javanicus</i> from <a href="/wiki/Java_(island)" class="mw-redirect" title="Java (island)">Java</a> to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>.
</p><p>The tropical forms are resident, but European and Asian birds <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">winter</a> south to Africa and the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Subcontinent">Indian Subcontinent</a>.
</p><p>This species breeds in colonies on inland marshes, sometimes amongst <a href="/wiki/Black-headed_gull" title="Black-headed gull">black-headed gulls</a>, which provide some protection. The scientific name arises from the fact that this, the largest <a href="/wiki/Marsh_tern" title="Marsh tern">marsh tern</a>, show similarities in appearance to both the white <i>Sterna</i> terns and to <a href="/wiki/Black_tern" title="Black tern">black tern</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Whiskered_tern&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg/220px-Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg/330px-Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg/440px-Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2029" data-file-height="1352" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Whiskered_tern_flying.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Whiskered tern in flight</div></div></div>
<p>The size, black cap, strong bill (29–34&#160;mm in males, 25–27&#160;mm and stubbier in females, with a pronounced gonys) and more positive flight recall common or <a href="/wiki/Arctic_tern" title="Arctic tern">Arctic tern</a>, but the short, forked-looking tail and dark grey breeding plumage above and below are typically marsh tern characteristics. The summer adult has white cheeks and red legs and bill.  The crown is flecked with white in the juvenile, and the hindcrown is more uniformly blackish, though in the winter adult this too is flecked with white.  The black ear-coverts are joined to the black of the hindcrown, and the space above is mottled with white, causing the black to appear as a C-shaped band.  The sides of the neck are white; this sometimes continues across the nape.  The collar is less sharply defined.  All through the year the rump is pale grey.  In the juvenile, the mantle (279&#160;mm) has a variegated pattern.  The feathers of the back and scapulars are dark brown, with prominent broad buff edgings and often subterminal buff bars or centers.  There is usually an admixture of new gray feathers, especially on the mantle, quite early in the fall.  The mantle is silvery-gray in the adult.
The call is a characteristic <i>krekk</i>.
</p><p>In winter, the forehead becomes white and the body plumage a much paler grey. Juvenile whiskered terns have a ginger scaly back, and otherwise look much like winter adults. The first winter plumage is intermediate between juvenile and adult winter, with patchy ginger on the back.
</p><p>The whiskered tern eats small fish, amphibians, insects and crustaceans.
</p>
