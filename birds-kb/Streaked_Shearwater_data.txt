<p>The <b>streaked shearwater</b> (<i>Calonectris leucomelas</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> of <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a>.  The adult bird averages 48&#160;cm (19&#160;in) in length, with a 122&#160;cm (48&#160;in) wingspan.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The streaked shearwater feeds mainly on <a href="/wiki/Fish" title="Fish">fish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Squid" title="Squid">squid</a>.  It follows fishing boats, attracted to <a href="/wiki/Anchovy" title="Anchovy">anchovy</a> crawls off Japan and have been known to be taken as by-catch in nets or drowned when ingesting the bait on long-line fishing lines.
</p><p>The streaked shearwater nests in <a href="/wiki/Burrow_nest" class="mw-redirect" title="Burrow nest">burrows</a>.  It prefers forested hills.
</p><p>This bird is abundant and widespread, however some mortality occurs through becoming entangled in <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">fishing</a> nets, and from some predation by <a href="/wiki/Cat" title="Cat">cats</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rats</a>.  In addition, it is harvested by some traditional endemic human cultures.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Streaked_shearwater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This species is <a href="/wiki/Pelagic" class="mw-redirect" title="Pelagic">pelagic</a>, but is also found in inshore waters.  It occurs in the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">Pacific Ocean</a>, nesting in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Peninsula" title="Korean Peninsula">Korean Peninsula</a>, predominately on their offshore islands.<sup id="cite_ref-Yamamoto_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yamamoto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Sugawa_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sugawa-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hart_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hart-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;note 2&#93;</a></sup> After breeding, the streaked shearwater migrate south, feeding in the seas off northern <a href="/wiki/New_Guinea" title="New Guinea">New Guinea</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Arafura_Sea" title="Arafura Sea">Arafura Sea</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">South China Sea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Yamamoto_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yamamoto-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  <i>Calonectris leucomelas</i> have also been reported well off the west coast of the United States,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> from the southern coast of <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Indiacoast_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Indiacoast-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and from <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
