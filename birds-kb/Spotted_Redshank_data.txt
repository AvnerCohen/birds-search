<p>The <b>spotted redshank</b> (<i>Tringa erythropus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> (shorebird) in the large <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> family <a href="/wiki/Scolopacidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Scolopacidae">Scolopacidae</a>. The genus name <i>Tringa</i> is the <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> name given to the <a href="/wiki/Green_sandpiper" title="Green sandpiper">green sandpiper</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ulisse_Aldrovandi" title="Ulisse Aldrovandi">Aldrovandus</a> in 1599 based on <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>trungas</i>, a thrush-sized, white-rumped, tail-bobbing wading bird mentioned by <a href="/wiki/Aristotle" title="Aristotle">Aristotle</a>. The specific <i>erythropus</i> is from Ancient Greek <i>eruthros</i>, "red", and <i>pous</i>, "foot".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It breeds across northern Scandinavia and the northern <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> south to the Mediterranean, the southern British Isles, France, tropical Africa, and tropical Asia for the winter. It is an occasional vagrant to Australia and North America.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The spotted redshank was described by German zoologist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">Peter Simon Pallas</a> in 1764 and given the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Scolopax erythropus</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
It is a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a> species, with no recognised subspecies.<sup id="cite_ref-ShoreGuide357_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShoreGuide357-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Taxonomically, it forms a close-knit group with several other large <i>Tringa</i> species, with <a href="/wiki/Molecular_sequencing" class="mw-redirect" title="Molecular sequencing">molecular sequencing</a> showing it to be a <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">sister clade</a> to that containing the <a href="/wiki/Greater_yellowlegs" title="Greater yellowlegs">greater yellowlegs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Common_greenshank" title="Common greenshank">common greenshank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Parkin_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parkin-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This is a large wader (shorebird), measuring 29–31&#160;cm (11–12&#160;in) long,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;nb 1&#93;</a></sup> with a <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a> of 61–67&#160;cm (24–26&#160;in) and a weight ranging from 121 to 205&#160;g (4.3 to 7.2&#160;oz).<sup id="cite_ref-ShoreGuide254_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShoreGuide254-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It is black in breeding plumage, and very pale in winter. It has a red legs and bill, and shows a white oval on the back in flight. Juveniles are grey-brown finely speckled white above, and have pale, finely barred underparts. 
Adults moult completely between July and October. In spring, the body plumage is moulted between March and May. Juveniles have a partial moult between August and February.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> 
The call is a creaking whistle <i>teu-it</i> (somewhat similar to the call of a <a href="/wiki/Roseate_tern" title="Roseate tern">roseate tern</a>), the alarm call a <i>kyip-kyip-kyip</i>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_range">Habitat and range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat and range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The spotted redshank breeds in the Arctic across much of the Palearctic, from <a href="/wiki/Lapland_(region)" class="mw-redirect" title="Lapland (region)">Lapland</a> in the west to <a href="/wiki/Chukotka_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Chukotka Autonomous Okrug">Chukotskaya</a> in the east.<sup id="cite_ref-Parkin_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parkin-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like most waders, it feeds on small invertebrates.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg/220px-Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="188" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg/330px-Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg/440px-Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="554" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Spotted_Redshank_Breeding_Plumage.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Spotted redshank - breeding plumage</div></div></div>
<p>It nests on open boggy <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a>, laying four eggs in a ground scrape. For breeding the bird moults to a black to dark grey with white spots. During breeding plumage the legs also turn a dark grey. See image alongside.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_and_threats">Conservation and threats</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation and threats">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The spotted redshank is one of the species to which the <a href="/wiki/Agreement_on_the_Conservation_of_African-Eurasian_Migratory_Waterbirds" title="Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds">Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</a> (AEWA) applies.
</p>
