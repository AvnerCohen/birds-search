<p>The <b>Spotted Redshank</b> (<i>Tringa erythropus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> (shorebird) in the large <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> family Scolopacidae. It breeds across northern <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrates</a> south to the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>, the southern <a href="/wiki/British_Isles" title="British Isles">British Isles</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, tropical <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and tropical <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> for the winter. It is an occasional vagrant to <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span></h2>
<p>The Spotted Redshank was first described by German zoologist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Simon_Pallas" title="Peter Simon Pallas">Peter Simon Pallas</a> in 1764. It is a <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" title="Monotypic" class="mw-redirect">monotypic</a> species, with no recognised subspecies.<sup id="cite_ref-ShoreGuide357_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ShoreGuide357-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Taxonomically, it forms a close-knit group with several other large <i>Tringa</i> species, with <a href="/wiki/Molecular_sequencing" title="Molecular sequencing" class="mw-redirect">molecular sequencing</a> showing it to be a <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">sister clade</a> to that containing the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Yellowlegs" title="Greater Yellowlegs">Greater Yellowlegs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Common_Greenshank" title="Common Greenshank">Common Greenshank</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Parkin_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parkin-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>This is a large wader (shorebird), measuring 29–31 cm (11–12 in) long,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>nb 1<span>]</span></a></sup> with a <a href="/wiki/Wingspan" title="Wingspan">wingspan</a> of 61–67 cm (24–26 in) and a weight ranging from 121 to 205 g (4.3 to 7.2 oz).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> It is black in breeding plumage, and very pale in winter. It has a red legs and bill, and shows a white oval on the back in flight. Juveniles are grey-brown finely speckled white above, and have pale, finely barred underparts. The call is a creaking whistle <i>teu-it</i> (somewhat similar to the call of a <a href="/wiki/Roseate_Tern" title="Roseate Tern">Roseate Tern</a>), the alarm call a <i>kyip-kyip-kyip</i>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat and range">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_range">Habitat and range</span></h2>
<p>The Spotted Redshank breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a> across much of <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Lapland" title="Lapland">Lapland</a> in the west to <a href="/wiki/Chukotka_Autonomous_Okrug" title="Chukotka Autonomous Okrug">Chukotskaya</a> in the east.<sup id="cite_ref-Parkin_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Parkin-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Food and feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food_and_feeding">Food and feeding</span></h3>
<p>Like most waders, it feeds on small invertebrates.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span></h3>
<p>It nests on open boggy <a href="/wiki/Taiga" title="Taiga">taiga</a>, laying four eggs in a ground scrape.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Spotted_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Conservation and threats">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation_and_threats">Conservation and threats</span></h2>
<p>The Spotted Redshank is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
