<p><b>Menetries's Warbler</b> or <b>Ménétries's Warbler</b> (<i>Sylvia mystacea</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of south-west <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> belonging to the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">Sylvia</a></i>. The name of the species commemorates <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_M%C3%A9n%C3%A9tries" title="Édouard Ménétries">Édouard Ménétries</a>, the <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> zoologist who described the species in 1832. It is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Sardinian_Warbler" title="Sardinian Warbler">Sardinian Warbler</a> (<i>Sylvia melanocephala</i>) of the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> basin and is similar to it in appearance.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Menetries%27s_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Menetries's Warbler is 12 to 14&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 15 to 19&#160;cm and weighs about 9-11 grams. Its fairly long tail is blackish with white on the outer-feathers and is often held cocked. It is frequently wagged up and down or from side to side. The <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is fairly heavy and is dark with a pinkish patch at the base. There is a pale bare ring around the eye.</p>
<p>The male of the nominate <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>S. m. mystacea</i> is dark greyish above and whitish below with a white submoustachial stripe and a pink throat and breast. It has a dark cap which is dull black at the front but becomes paler further back and merges into the grey nape unlike the Sardininan Warbler which has an entirely jet-black cap. Menetries's Warblers have rather plain <a href="/wiki/Flight_feather#Tertials" title="Flight feather">tertial</a> feathers unlike the Sardinian Warbler which has tertials with more obvious dark centres and pale edges. The western subspecies <i>S. m. rubescens</i> is paler grey above than the nominate and the pink on the underparts is paler or absent entirely. The eastern subspecies <i>S. m. turcmenica</i> is paler grey above and paler pink below than the nominate and has longer wings than both the other subspecies. Male Menetries's Warblers become browner outside the breeding season with a less dark cap.</p>
<p>The female is sandy grey-brown above and buff-white below. It is similar to female Sardinian and <a href="/wiki/Subalpine_Warbler" title="Subalpine Warbler">Subalpine Warblers</a> but has plainer tertial feathers and more contrast between the pale back and dark tail. First-winter males are similar to females but may have some pink on the throat and breast.</p>
<p>The species has a harsh buzzing call as well as a sparrow-like chattering call. The <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> of the male is a quiet chattering which mixes musical and harsh notes and is often given in flight.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Menetries%27s_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p>It is a migratory bird, nesting in south-west Asia and wintering southwards as far as north-east <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The subspecies <i>S. m. mystacea</i> breeds from the <a href="/wiki/Caspian_Sea" title="Caspian Sea">Caspian Sea</a> region of <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> down through <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> and easternmost <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> to northern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. <i>S. m. rubescens</i> breeds locally in south-east Turkey, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>, western Iran and possibly in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>. <i>S. m. turcmenica</i> occurs from north-east <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> eastward to the <a href="/wiki/Syr_Darya" title="Syr Darya">Syr Darya</a> valley in <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Balochistan_(Pakistan)" title="Balochistan (Pakistan)" class="mw-redirect">Balochistan</a> region in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>.</p>
<p>The winter range covers southern <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arabia" title="Arabia" class="mw-redirect">Arabia</a> and Northeast Africa from <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> to <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a>. A few birds pass through <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> on migration and <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_in_birds" title="Vagrancy in birds" class="mw-redirect">vagrants</a> have been recorded from <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">Nigeria</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niger" title="Niger">Niger</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Menetries%27s_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span></h2>
<p>It occurs in <a href="/wiki/Scrubland" title="Scrubland" class="mw-redirect">scrub</a>, thickets, open woodland, cultivated areas and gardens in areas with dry, <a href="/wiki/Continental_climate" title="Continental climate">continental</a> climates. It is typically found in <a href="/wiki/Steppe" title="Steppe">steppe</a> regions, on mountain slopes and along rivers and is often associated with <a href="/wiki/Tamarisk" title="Tamarisk" class="mw-redirect">tamarisk</a> bushes. It forages among leaves and branches for <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>, starting at the top of a bush and moving downwards. It also feeds on <a href="/wiki/Fruit" title="Fruit">fruit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Seed" title="Seed">seeds</a>.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Menetries%27s_Warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span></h3>
<p>The cup-shaped <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nest</a> is built in low vegetation, up to 90&#160;cm above the ground. It is made of twigs, stems and grass leaves with feathers and hair used in the lining. Four or five <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid and are incubated for 11 to 13 days. They are glossy white with brown spots. The young birds <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a> after 10 or 11 days. Both sexes are involved in incubating the eggs and caring for the young. They usually raise two broods during the breeding season.</p>
