<p>The <b>Olive-backed Pipit</b> (<i>Anthus hodgsoni</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipit</a> (<i>Anthus</i>) genus, which breeds across <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South</a>, north <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">East Asia</a>, as well as in the northeast of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>. It is a long-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> moving in winter to southern Asia and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. Sometimes it is also called <b>Indian</b>, or <b>Hodgson's</b>, <b>Tree Pipit</b>, owing to the resemblance with the <a href="/wiki/Tree_Pipit" title="Tree Pipit">Tree Pipit</a>. However, its back is more olive-toned and less streaked than that species, and its head pattern is different with a better-marked supercilium.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Olive-backed_Pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9872.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9872.jpg/220px-Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9872.jpg" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9872.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
at Bracebridge in Kolkata, India.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Summer: from <a href="/wiki/Himalaya" title="Himalaya" class="mw-redirect">Himalayan</a> <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, westward through <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>, into <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>, north to <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> province, and eastwards through <a href="/wiki/Korea" title="Korea">Korea</a> to <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, and north through north <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">Central Asia</a> into north-eastern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (European <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>). Occasionally a rare vagrant in western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. Breeds up to 4500m in eastern <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">Nepal</a>.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Winter: Broad southern region across <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, from peninsular <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, east to <a href="/wiki/Southeast_Asia" title="Southeast Asia">southeast Asia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a>.</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>Habitat: Affects open country. Wintering in evergreen woodland, Summers in groves and wooded biotope.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Olive-backed_Pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9911.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0f/Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9911.jpg/220px-Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9911.jpg" width="220" height="197" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive-backed_Pipit-_Kolkata_I_IMG_9911.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
at Bracebridge in Kolkata, India.</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Size: Sparrow+ (ca. 15&#160;cm)</li>
<li>Appearance: Greenish brown streaked with darker brown above. Supercilium, double wingbar and outer rectrices whitish. Whitish to buff below streaked with dark brown on breast and flanks. Sexes alike.<sup id="cite_ref-ali-ripley_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ali-ripley-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></li>
<li>Habits: Seen singly or pairs. Runs about on the ground in search of food and flies up into trees when disturbed. Flight jerky and undulating.</li>
<li>Call: Song lark-like and uttered on the wing, similar to the Tree Pipit, but faster and higher pitched. A single <i>tseep</i> or <i>spek</i>, also similar to the Tree Pipit.</li>
<li>Food: Insects, grass and weed seeds.</li>
<li>Food: Largely insects, but will also take seeds.</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Olive-backed_Pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nesting">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Nesting">Nesting</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg/220px-Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg" width="220" height="154" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Breeding at Mailee Thaatch (10000 ft.) in Kullu - Manali District of Himachal Pradesh, India</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li>Season: May to July.</li>
<li>Nest: a cup of moss and grass placed on the ground under a tuft of grass or boulder. open woodland and scrub.</li>
<li>Eggs: 3-5, usu. 4, dark brown, spotted darker. Usually two broods are raised.</li>
</ul>
<p>The scientific name of this bird commemorates the British ornithologist <a href="/wiki/Brian_Houghton_Hodgson" title="Brian Houghton Hodgson">Brian Houghton Hodgson</a>.</p>
