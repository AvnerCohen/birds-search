<p>The <b>olive-backed pipit</b> (<i>Anthus hodgsoni</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipit</a> (<i>Anthus</i>) genus, which breeds across southern, north central and eastern Asia, as well as in the north-eastern European Russia. It is a long-distance <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a> moving in winter to southern Asia and <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a>. Sometimes it is also called <b>Indian pipit</b> or <b>Hodgson's pipit</b>, as well as <b>tree pipit</b> owing to its resemblance with the <a href="/wiki/Tree_pipit" title="Tree pipit">tree pipit</a>. However, its back is more olive-toned and less streaked than that species, and its head pattern is different with a better-marked supercilium.
</p><p>The genus name <i>Anthus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and is the name for a small bird of grasslands. The specific <i>hodgsoni</i> commemorates English diplomat and collector <a href="/wiki/Brian_Houghton_Hodgson" title="Brian Houghton Hodgson">Brian Houghton Hodgson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Olive-backed_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Summer: from <a href="/wiki/Himalaya" class="mw-redirect" title="Himalaya">Himalayan</a> Pakistan and India, westward through Nepal, into China, north to <a href="/wiki/Gansu" title="Gansu">Gansu</a> province, and eastwards through Korea to Japan, and north through north central Asia into north-eastern Europe (European Russia). Occasionally a rare vagrant in western Europe. Breeds up to 4500m in eastern Nepal.</li>
<li>Winter: Broad southern region across Asia, from peninsular India, east to Southeast Asia and the Philippines.</li>
<li>Habitat: Affects open country. Wintering in evergreen woodland, Summers in groves and wooded biotope.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Olive-backed_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Size: Sparrow+ (ca. 15&#160;cm)</li>
<li>Appearance: Greenish brown streaked with darker brown above. Supercilium, double wingbar and outer rectrices whitish. Whitish to buff below streaked with dark brown on breast and flanks. Sexes alike.<sup id="cite_ref-ali-ripley_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ali-ripley-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Habits: Seen singly or pairs. Runs about on the ground in search of food and flies up into trees when disturbed. Flight jerky and undulating.</li>
<li>Call: Song lark-like and uttered on the wing, similar to the tree pipit, but faster and higher pitched. A single <i>tseep</i> or <i>spek</i>, also similar to the tree pipit.</li>
<li>Food: Insects, grass and weed seeds.</li>
<li>Food: Largely insects, but will also take seeds.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nesting">Nesting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Olive-backed_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nesting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg/220px-Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg/330px-Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg/440px-Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Olive_backed_Pipit_I_IMG_3859.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Breeding at Mailee Thaatch (10000 ft.) in Kullu - Manali District of Himachal Pradesh, India</div></div></div>
<ul><li>Season: May to July.</li>
<li>Nest: a cup of moss and grass placed on the ground under a tuft of grass or boulder. open woodland and scrub.</li>
<li>Eggs: 3–5, usu. 4, dark brown, spotted darker. Usually two broods are raised.</li></ul>
