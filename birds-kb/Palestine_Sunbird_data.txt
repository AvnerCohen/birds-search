<p>The <b>Palestine Sunbird</b> or <b>Northern Orange-tufted Sunbird</b> (<i>Cinnyris oseus</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sunbird" title="Sunbird">sunbird</a> family which is found in parts of the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a> and sub-Saharan <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is also known as <b>Orange-tufted Sunbird</b>, a name which is also used for the similar <a href="/wiki/Orange-tufted_Sunbird" title="Orange-tufted Sunbird">Orange-tufted Sunbird</a> (<i>Cinnyris bouvieri</i>), found further south in Africa.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Palestine_Sunbird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nectarinia_osea_(fledgling).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Nectarinia_osea_%28fledgling%29.JPG/220px-Nectarinia_osea_%28fledgling%29.JPG" width="220" height="214" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nectarinia_osea_(fledgling).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A juvenile bird in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The Palestine Sunbird is 8 to 12&#160;cm long with a wingspan of 14 to 16&#160;cm. Males have an average weight of 7.6&#160;g and females weigh around 6.8&#160;g. The <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> is fairly long, black and curves downwards. The <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> of breeding males is mostly dark but appears glossy blue or green in the light. There are orange tufts at the sides of the breast which are hard to see except at close range. Females and <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_(organism)" title="Juvenile (organism)">juveniles</a> are grey-brown above with pale underparts. Non-breeding males are similar but may retain some dark feathers.</p>
<p>It has a high, fast, jingling <a href="/wiki/Bird_song" title="Bird song" class="mw-redirect">song</a> and various calls including a harsh alarm call.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Palestine_Sunbird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitat and range">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat_and_range">Habitat and range</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Palestine_Sunbird_Hovering.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Palestine_Sunbird_Hovering.jpg/220px-Palestine_Sunbird_Hovering.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Palestine_Sunbird_Hovering.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
A male hovering.</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>It occurs in areas with high temperatures and dry climate from sea-level up to an altitude of 3200&#160;m. It inhabits dry woodland, <a href="/wiki/Scrubland" title="Scrubland" class="mw-redirect">scrub</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wadi" title="Wadi">wadis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Savanna" title="Savanna">savannas</a>, orchards and gardens and is common in towns in some areas: it is a familiar garden bird in <a href="/wiki/Tel_Aviv" title="Tel Aviv">Tel Aviv</a>.</p>
<p>The Middle Eastern <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>C. o. oseus</i> breeds from <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Palestinian_territories" title="Palestinian territories">Palestinian territories</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a> in the north, down through western <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a> in the south. In recent decades it has colonized the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a> in <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>. Some birds reach <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a> in winter and it has bred in <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a>. It has been suggested as a possible <a href="/wiki/National_bird" title="National bird" class="mw-redirect">national bird</a> of <a href="/wiki/State_of_Palestine" title="State of Palestine">Palestine</a> in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The African subspecies <i>C. o. decorsei</i> is found very locally in parts of <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a>, north-west <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Central_African_Republic" title="Central African Republic">Central African Republic</a>, north-east <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Republic_of_Congo" title="Democratic Republic of Congo" class="mw-redirect">Democratic Republic of Congo</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">Cameroon</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Palestine_Sunbird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Feeding">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Feeding">Feeding</span></h2>
<p>The diet consists mainly of <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nectar" title="Nectar">nectar</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Tongue" title="Tongue">tongue</a> is long and brush-tipped to extract nectar from flowers. The birds usually feed while perched beside a flower but they are also capable of hovering.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Palestine_Sunbird&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reproduction">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Reproduction">Reproduction</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nectarinia_osea_Female_in_Nest.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Nectarinia_osea_Female_in_Nest.JPG/220px-Nectarinia_osea_Female_in_Nest.JPG" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nectarinia_osea_Female_in_Nest.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Female bird inside nest.</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:PalestineSunbird1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/PalestineSunbird1.jpg/220px-PalestineSunbird1.jpg" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:PalestineSunbird1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Male Palestine Sunbird in <a href="/wiki/Tzofit" title="Tzofit">Tzofit</a>, Israel</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>The purse-like <a href="/wiki/Nest" title="Nest">nest</a> hangs from a branch in a tree or bush. It is 18&#160;cm long and 8&#160;cm wide at the base. It is made of leaves, grass and other plant material which is bound together with hair and <a href="/wiki/Spider_web" title="Spider web">spider webs</a> and lined with wool and feathers. One to three smooth, glossy <a href="/wiki/Egg_(biology)" title="Egg (biology)">eggs</a> are laid. These are somewhat variable in colour; often white or grey with faint markings at the broader end. They are <a href="/wiki/Avian_incubation" title="Avian incubation" class="mw-redirect">incubated</a> for 13 to 14 days. The young are downy with an orange-red mouth and <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">fledge</a> after 14 to 21 days.</p>
