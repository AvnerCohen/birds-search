<p>The <b>white-throated bee-eater</b> (<i>Merops albicollis</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Near_passerine" title="Near passerine">near passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Bee-eater" title="Bee-eater">bee-eater</a> <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a>, <a href="/wiki/Meropidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Meropidae">Meropidae</a>. It breeds in semi-desert along the southern edge of the <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. The white-throated bee-eater is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in a completely different habitat in the equatorial rain forests of Africa from southern <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">Senegal</a> to <a href="/wiki/Uganda" title="Uganda">Uganda</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_bee-eater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:White_Throated_Bee_Eater_(149086841),_crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/White_Throated_Bee_Eater_%28149086841%29%2C_crop.jpg/220px-White_Throated_Bee_Eater_%28149086841%29%2C_crop.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/White_Throated_Bee_Eater_%28149086841%29%2C_crop.jpg/330px-White_Throated_Bee_Eater_%28149086841%29%2C_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/White_Throated_Bee_Eater_%28149086841%29%2C_crop.jpg/440px-White_Throated_Bee_Eater_%28149086841%29%2C_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="971" data-file-height="679" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:White_Throated_Bee_Eater_(149086841),_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Male in flight</div></div></div>
<p>This species, like other bee-eaters, is a richly coloured, slender bird. It is predominantly green, but its face and throat are white with a black crown, eye stripe, and neckband. The underparts are pale green shading to blue on the breast. The eye is red and the beak is black.
</p><p>The white-throated bee-eater can reach a length of 19–21&#160;cm, excluding the two very elongated central tail feathers, which can exceed an additional length of 12&#160;cm. They weigh between 20 and 28 grams. Sexes are alike, except that the male has longer tail feathers. The call is similar to <a href="/wiki/European_bee-eater" title="European bee-eater">European bee-eater</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habits">Habits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_bee-eater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>White-throated bee-eaters also feed and roost communally. As the name suggests, bee-eaters predominantly eat  <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a>, especially <a href="/wiki/Bee" title="Bee">bees</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wasp" title="Wasp">wasps</a> and  <a href="/wiki/Hornet" title="Hornet">hornets</a>, which are caught in the air by sorties from an open perch.  However, this species probably takes mainly flying <a href="/wiki/Ant" title="Ant">ants</a> and <a href="/wiki/Beetle" title="Beetle">beetles</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nesting">Nesting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_bee-eater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Nesting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The white-throated bee-eater is a bird which breeds in dry sandy open country, such thorn scrub and near-desert. These abundant bee-eaters are gregarious, nesting <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonially</a> in sandy banks or open flat areas. They make a relatively long 1–2 m tunnel in which the 6 to 7 spherical white eggs are laid. Both the male and the female take care of the eggs, but up to five helpers also assist with caring for the young.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Status">Status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=White-throated_bee-eater&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Widespread and common throughout its large range, the white-throated bee-eater is evaluated as Least Concern on the <a href="/wiki/IUCN_Red_List" title="IUCN Red List">IUCN Red List</a> of Threatened Species.
</p>
