<p>The <b>Southern Grey Shrike</b>, <i>Lanius meridionalis</i>, is a member of the <a href="/wiki/Shrike" title="Shrike">shrike</a> family.</p>
<p>It is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Great_Grey_Shrike" title="Great Grey Shrike">Great Grey Shrike</a>, <i>Lanius excubitor</i>, with which it used to be considered conspecific; where they co-occur, they do not interbreed and are separated by choice of <a href="/wiki/Habitat_(ecology)" title="Habitat (ecology)" class="mw-redirect">habitat</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The race <i>L. m. meridionalis</i> is resident in southern <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and north <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. It is slightly smaller and darker than the Great Grey Shrike, and prefers dry open country.</p>
<p>The race <i>L. m. pallidirostis</i> breeds in central <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and winters in the tropics. It is much paler than Southern Grey or Great Grey, and is sometimes split as a separate species, the <b>Steppe Grey Shrike</b>, <i>L. pallidirostis</i>. It too prefers more arid habitat with sparse vegetation.</p>
<p>This medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> eats large <a href="/wiki/Insects" title="Insects" class="mw-redirect">insects</a>, small <a href="/wiki/Birds" title="Birds" class="mw-redirect">birds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rodents" title="Rodents" class="mw-redirect">rodents</a>. Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a "larder".</p>
<p>The plumage is generally similar to Great Grey Shrike apart from the differences noted above.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> eastern form is a scarce vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, usually in autumn.</p>
