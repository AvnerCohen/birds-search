<p>The <b>meadow pipit</b> (<i>Anthus pratensis</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> which breeds in much of the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a>, from southeastern Greenland and Iceland east to just east of the <a href="/wiki/Ural_Mountains" title="Ural Mountains">Ural Mountains</a> in Russia, and south to central France and Romania; there is also an isolated population in the <a href="/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains" title="Caucasus Mountains">Caucasus Mountains</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> over most of its range, wintering in southern Europe, North Africa and south-western Asia, but is resident year-round in western Europe. However, even here, many birds move to the coast or lowlands in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meadow_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anthus_pratensis.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Anthus_pratensis.jpg/220px-Anthus_pratensis.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Anthus_pratensis.jpg/330px-Anthus_pratensis.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Anthus_pratensis.jpg/440px-Anthus_pratensis.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anthus_pratensis.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A meadow pipit perched on a fishing net</div></div></div>
<p>This is a widespread and often abundant small <a href="/wiki/Pipit" title="Pipit">pipit</a>, 14.5–15&#160;cm long and 15–22 g weight. It is an undistinguished looking species on the ground, mainly brown above and buff below, with darker streaking on most of its plumage; the tail is brown, with narrow white side edges. It has a thin bill and pale pinkish-yellow legs; the hind claw is notably long, longer than the rest of the hind toe. The call is a weak <i>tsi-tsi</i>. The simple repetitive song is given in a short song flight.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Birds breeding in <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> are slightly darker coloured than those in other areas, and are often distinguished as the <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> <i>Anthus pratensis whistleri</i>, though it intergrades clinally with nominate <i>Anthus pratensis pratensis</i> found in the rest of the species' range.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is similar to the <a href="/wiki/Red-throated_pipit" title="Red-throated pipit">red-throated pipit</a> <i>Anthus cervinus</i>, which is more heavily streaked and (in summer only) has an orange-red throat, and to the <a href="/wiki/Tree_pipit" title="Tree pipit">tree pipit</a> <i>Anthus trivialis</i>, which is slightly larger, less heavily streaked, and has stronger facial markings and a shorter hind claw. The song of the meadow pipit accelerates towards the end while that of the tree pipit slows down.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meadow_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg/220px-Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg/330px-Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg/440px-Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anthus_pratensis_nest_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nest with eggs</div></div></div>
<p>It is primarily a species of open habitats, either uncultivated or low-intensity agriculture, such as pasture, bogs, and moorland, but also occurs in low numbers in arable croplands. In winter, it also uses saltmarshes and sometimes open woodlands. It is a fairly terrestrial pipit, always feeding on the ground, but will use elevated perches such as shrubs, fence lines or electricity wires as vantage points to watch for predators.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ebcc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebcc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The estimated total population is 12 million pairs. It is an abundant species in the north of its range, and generally the commonest breeding bird in most of upland Britain, but less common further south. Breeding densities range from 80 pairs per square kilometre in northern Scandinavia, to 5–20 pairs per square kilometre in grassland in the south of the breeding range, and just one pair per square kilometre in arable farmland.<sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ebcc_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ebcc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> There are a small number of isolated breeding records from south of the main range, in the mountains of <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, and the northern <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">Balkans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There has been a general decline in the population over the past 17 years, most notable in French farmland, with a 68% drop.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meadow_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG/220px-Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="138" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG/330px-Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG/440px-Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3528" data-file-height="2208" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anthus_pratensis_MWNH_1590.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<p>Its food is primarily <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>, mostly small items less than 5&#160;mm long. It also eats the seeds of <a href="/wiki/Poaceae" title="Poaceae">grasses</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyperaceae" title="Cyperaceae">sedges</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juncaceae" title="Juncaceae">rushes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Calluna" title="Calluna">heather</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Empetrum" title="Empetrum">crowberry</a> berries, mainly in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meadow_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The nest is on the ground hidden in dense vegetation, with 2–7 (most often 3–5) eggs; the eggs hatch after 11–15 days, with the chicks fledging 10–14 days after hatching. Two broods are commonly raised each year. This species is one of the most important nest hosts of the <a href="/wiki/Common_cuckoo" title="Common cuckoo">cuckoo</a>, and it is also an important prey species for <a href="/wiki/Merlin_(bird)" title="Merlin (bird)">merlins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hen_harrier" title="Hen harrier">hen harriers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Snow_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Snow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hoyo_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hoyo-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Meadow_pipit&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The generic name 'pipit', first documented by <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pennant" title="Thomas Pennant">Thomas Pennant</a> in 1768, is <a href="/wiki/Onomatopoeia" title="Onomatopoeia">onomatopoeic</a>, from the call note of this species.<sup id="cite_ref-Lockwood_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lockwood-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In colloquial use by <a href="/wiki/Birdwatching" title="Birdwatching">birders</a> in the UK the name meadow pipit is often abbreviated to "mippit".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Old citation no longer available and needs updating (April 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Old folk names, no longer used, include "chit lark", "peet lark", "tit lark" and "titling"; these refer to its small size and superficial similarity to a <a href="/wiki/Lark" title="Lark">lark</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Lockwood_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lockwood-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The scientific name is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a>. <i>Anthus</i> is the name for a small bird of grasslands, and the specific <i>pratensis</i> means "of a meadow ", from <i>pratum</i>,  "meadow".<sup id="cite_ref-job_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
