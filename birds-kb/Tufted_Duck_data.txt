<p>The <b>Tufted Duck</b> (<i>Aythya fuligula</i>) is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Diving_duck" title="Diving duck">diving duck</a> with a population of close to one million birds.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Tufted_Duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The adult male is all black except for white flanks and a blue-grey bill. It has an obvious head tuft that gives the species its name. The adult female is brown with paler flanks, and is more easily confused with other diving ducks. In particular, some have white around the bill base which resembles the <a href="/wiki/Scaup" title="Scaup">scaup</a> species, although the white is never as extensive as in those <a href="/wiki/Duck" title="Duck">ducks</a>.</p>
<p>The only duck which is at all similar is the drake <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> <a href="/wiki/Ring-necked_Duck" title="Ring-necked Duck">Ring-necked Duck</a>, which however has a different head shape, no tuft and greyish flanks.</p>
<p>The Tufted Duck is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Tufted_Duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p>The Tufted Duck breeds widely throughout temperate and northern <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. It occasionally can be found as a winter visitor along both coasts of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It is believed to have expanded its traditional range with the increased availability of open water due to gravel extraction, and the spread of freshwater <a href="/wiki/Mussel" title="Mussel">mussels</a>, a favourite food. These ducks are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> in most of their range, and winter in the milder south and west of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, southern <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a> and all year in most of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. They will form large flocks on open water in winter.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Tufted_Duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span></h2>
<p>Their breeding habitat is close to <a href="/wiki/Marsh" title="Marsh">marshes</a> and lakes with plenty of vegetation to conceal the nest. They are also found on coastal <a href="/wiki/Lagoons" title="Lagoons" class="mw-redirect">lagoons</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Seashore" title="Seashore">seashore</a>, and sheltered ponds.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Tufted_Duck&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Food">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Food">Food</span></h2>
<p>These birds feed mainly by diving, but they will dabble. They eat <a href="/wiki/Mollusc" title="Mollusc" class="mw-redirect">molluscs</a>, aquatic <a href="/wiki/Insect" title="Insect">insects</a> and some plants and sometimes feed at night.</p>
