<p><b>Blyth's reed warbler</b> (<i>Acrocephalus dumetorum</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World_warbler" title="Old World warbler">Old World warbler</a> in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Acrocephalus_(bird)" title="Acrocephalus (bird)">Acrocephalus</a></i>. It breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> and easternmost <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>. It is <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a>, wintering in <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">Sri Lanka</a>. It is one of the most common winter warblers in those countries. It is a rare vagrant to western <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blyth%27s_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This bird is named after the British zoologist <a href="/wiki/Edward_Blyth" title="Edward Blyth">Edward Blyth</a>. The genus name <i>Acrocephalus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>akros</i>, "highest", and <i>kephale</i>, "head". It is possible that <a href="/wiki/Johann_Andreas_Naumann" title="Johann Andreas Naumann">Naumann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Naumann" title="Johann Friedrich Naumann">Naumann</a> thought <i>akros</i> meant "sharp-pointed". The specific <i>dumetorum</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>dumetum</i>, "thicket".<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blyth%27s_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This small <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> is a species found in scrub or clearings, often near water, but it is not found in marshes. 4-6 eggs are laid in a nest in a bush. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg/220px-Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg/330px-Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg/440px-Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Blyth%27s_Reed_Warbler_Im-_Kolkata_IMG_9405.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>At New Alipore in <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">Kolkata</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>This is a medium-sized warbler, <span class="nowrap">12.5-14 cm</span> in length. The adult has a plain brown back and pale underparts. It can easily be confused with <a href="/wiki/Reed_warbler" class="mw-redirect" title="Reed warbler">reed warbler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marsh_warbler" title="Marsh warbler">marsh warbler</a> and some of the <i><a href="/wiki/Hippolais" title="Hippolais">Hippolais</a></i> warblers. It is most like reed warbler but is greyer on the back, the forehead is less flattened and the bill is less strong and pointed. The sexes are identical, as with most warblers, but young birds are yellower below.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitat">Habitat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blyth%27s_reed_warbler&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Habitat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The habitat is different from the reed beds favoured by reed warbler or the rank vegetation of marsh warbler, this species choosing trees or bushes as songposts. Like most warblers, Blyth's reed warbler is <a href="/wiki/Insectivorous" class="mw-redirect" title="Insectivorous">insectivorous</a>, but will take other small food items, including berries.
</p><p>In the breeding season, the best identification feature is the song, which is slow and repetitive, with much mimicry of other birds, punctuated with scales and typically acrocephaline whistles.
</p>
