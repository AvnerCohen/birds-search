<p>The <b>little ringed plover</b> (<i>Charadrius dubius</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a>. The genus name <i>Charadrius</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">Late Latin</a> word for a yellowish bird mentioned in the fourth-century <a href="/wiki/Vulgate" title="Vulgate">Vulgate</a>. It derives from <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>kharadrios</i> a bird found in river valleys (<i>kharadra</i>, "ravine"). The specific <i>dubius</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> for doubtful, since <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Sonnerat" title="Pierre Sonnerat">Sonnerat</a>, writing in 1776, thought this bird might be just a variant of <a href="/wiki/Common_ringed_plover" title="Common ringed plover">common ringed plover</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-job_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_ringed_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Adult little ringed plovers have a grey-brown back and wings, a white belly and a white breast with one black neckband. They have a brown cap, a white forehead, a black mask around the eyes with white above and a short dark bill. The legs are flesh-coloured and the toes are all webbed.
</p><p>This species differs from the larger <a href="/wiki/Ringed_plover" class="mw-redirect" title="Ringed plover">ringed plover</a> in leg colour, the head pattern, and the presence of a clear yellow eye-ring.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Habitats_and_range">Habitats and range</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_ringed_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Habitats and range">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Their breeding habitat is open gravel areas near freshwater, including gravel pits, islands and river edges across the <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Palearctic</a> including northwestern <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. They nest on the ground on stones with little or no plant growth. Both males and females take turns incubating the eggs.
</p><p>They are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and winter in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. These birds forage for food on muddy areas, usually by sight. They eat insects and worms.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_ringed_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three subspecies recognized.
</p>
<ul><li><i>C. d. curonicus</i> <small>J. F. Gmelin, 1789</small> - from Eurasia, Russian Fareast, Japan, Korea, China to North Africa. Winter spends in Sahara, Sri Lanka, to Indonesia and China.</li>
<li><i>C. d. jerdoni</i> <small>(Legge, 1880)</small> - from India, Sri Lanka, Pakistan to South East Asia.</li>
<li><i>C. d. dubius</i> <small>Scopoli, 1786</small> - from the Philippines, New Guinea to Bismarck Archipelago.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conservation">Conservation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Little_ringed_plover&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Conservation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The little ringed plover is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" class="mw-redirect" title="AEWA">AEWA</a>) applies.
</p>
