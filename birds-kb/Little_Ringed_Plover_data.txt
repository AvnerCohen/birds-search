<p>The <b>Little Ringed Plover</b> (<i>Charadrius dubius</i>) is a small <a href="/wiki/Plover" title="Plover">plover</a>. Adults have a grey-brown back and wings, a white belly, and a white breast with one black neckband. They have a brown cap, a white forehead, a black mask around the eyes with white above and a short dark bill. The legs are flesh-coloured and the toes are all webbed.</p>

<p>This species differs from the larger <a href="/wiki/Ringed_Plover" title="Ringed Plover" class="mw-redirect">Ringed Plover</a> in leg colour, the head pattern, and the presence of a clear yellow eye-ring.</p>
<p>Their breeding habitat is open gravel areas near freshwater, including gravel pits, islands and river edges in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> and western <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>. They nest on the ground on stones with little or no plant growth. Both male and female take turn to incubate the eggs.</p>
<p>They are <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> and winter in <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>. These birds forage for food on muddy areas, usually by sight. They eat insects and worms.</p>
<p>The Little Ringed Plover is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
