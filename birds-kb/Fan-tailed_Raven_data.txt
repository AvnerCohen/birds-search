<p>The <b>Fan-tailed Raven</b> (<i>Corvus rhipidurus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passerine" title="Passerine">passerine</a> bird of the <a href="/wiki/Corvidae" title="Corvidae">crow family</a> native to Eastern Africa and the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">Arabian Peninsula</a>. Like the <a href="/wiki/Chihuahuan_Raven" title="Chihuahuan Raven">Chihuahuan Raven</a> it is one of the smaller species (47&#160;cm in length), in fact it is about the same size as a <a href="/wiki/Carrion_Crow" title="Carrion Crow">Carrion Crow</a> but with a much thicker bill, shorter tail and much larger wings.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Fan-tailed_Raven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span></h2>
<p>The Fan-tailed Raven is completely black including bill, legs and feet and the plumage has a purplish-blue gloss in good light. Worn plumage is slightly coppery-brown. The base of the feathers on the upper neck are white and only seen if the bird is inspected or a strong gust blows them the wrong way. The throat hackles are shorter than in most other ravens.</p>
<p>The voice is described as guttural croaks mixed with the sound of <a href="/wiki/Frog" title="Frog">frog</a>-call.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Fan-tailed_Raven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Distribution and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_habitat">Distribution and habitat</span></h2>
<p>It occurs in the Middle East, North Africa, Arabia, and south to <a href="/wiki/Sudan" title="Sudan">Sudan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>. It also ranges across the <a href="/wiki/A%C3%AFr_Massif" title="Aïr Massif" class="mw-redirect">Aïr Massif</a> in the southern <a href="/wiki/Sahara" title="Sahara">Sahara</a>. It lives in desert or open dry country that includes crags for nesting. It is one of the most aerial of birds traveling huge distances in search of food, its large wings being adapted to gliding on thermals in a rather <a href="/wiki/Vulture" title="Vulture">vulture</a>-like way.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Fan-tailed_Raven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Fan-tailed_Raven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Diet">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Diet">Diet</span></h3>
<p>Food is invariably taken on the ground and includes all manner of insects and other <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>, grain taken from animal dung, carrion and scraps of human food. It has also been seen taking skin parasites from <a href="/wiki/Camel" title="Camel">camels</a> and, where not persecuted, scavenges around rubbish dumps and camp sites. Fruits of all types are eaten readily. It soars and plays in <a href="/wiki/Thermal" title="Thermal">thermals</a> even more so than other Raven species and often associates with the <a href="/wiki/Brown-necked_Raven" title="Brown-necked Raven">Brown-necked Raven</a> roosting in the same tree as it.</p>
<h3><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Fan-tailed_Raven&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nesting">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Nesting">Nesting</span></h3>
<p>This species nests on rock ledges and in cavities in cliffs though very rarely in <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">Somalia</a> it has been known to nest in trees. There are usually 2-4 eggs laid. The <a href="/wiki/Great-spotted_Cuckoo" title="Great-spotted Cuckoo" class="mw-redirect">Great-spotted Cuckoo</a> (<i>Clamator glandarius</i>) sometimes uses this species as a brood host.</p>
