<p>The <b>monk parakeet</b> (<i>Myiopsitta monachus</i>), also known as the <b>Quaker parrot</b>, is a species of <a href="/wiki/True_parrot" title="True parrot">true parrot</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Psittacidae" title="Psittacidae">Psittacidae</a>. It is a small, bright-green <a href="/wiki/Parrot" title="Parrot">parrot</a> with a greyish breast and greenish-yellow abdomen. Its average lifespan is 20–30 years. It originates from the temperate to <a href="/wiki/Subtropical" class="mw-redirect" title="Subtropical">subtropical</a> areas of <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and the surrounding countries in <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>. Self-sustaining <a href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">feral</a> populations occur in many places, mainly in <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The monk parakeet was described by the French polymath <a href="/wiki/Georges-Louis_Leclerc,_Comte_de_Buffon" title="Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon">Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon</a> in 1780 in his <i>Histoire Naturelle des Oiseaux</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-buffon_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buffon-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The bird was also illustrated in a hand-coloured plate engraved by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois-Nicolas_Martinet" title="François-Nicolas Martinet">François-Nicolas Martinet</a> in the <i>Planches Enluminées D'Histoire Naturelle</i>, which was produced under the supervision of <a href="/wiki/Edme-Louis_Daubenton" title="Edme-Louis Daubenton">Edme-Louis Daubenton</a> to accompany Buffon's text.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Neither the plate caption nor Buffon's description included a scientific name, but in 1783 the Dutch naturalist <a href="/wiki/Pieter_Boddaert" title="Pieter Boddaert">Pieter Boddaert</a> coined the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Psittacus monachus</i> in his catalogue of the <i>Planches Enluminées</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> As Buffon did not specify the origin of his specimen, in 1937 the American ornithologist <a href="/wiki/James_L._Peters" title="James L. Peters">James Peters</a> assigned the <a href="/wiki/Type_location_(biology)" class="mw-redirect" title="Type location (biology)">type location</a> as <a href="/wiki/Montevideo" title="Montevideo">Montevideo</a>, Uruguay.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The monk parakeet is now placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Myiopsitta" title="Myiopsitta">Myiopsitta</a></i> that was introduced by French naturalist <a href="/wiki/Charles_Lucien_Bonaparte" title="Charles Lucien Bonaparte">Charles Lucien Bonaparte</a> in 1854.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ioc_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The genus name combines the <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Greek" title="Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek</a> <i>mus, muos</i> meaning "mouse" and the <a href="/wiki/New_Latin" title="New Latin">New Latin</a> <i>psitta</i> meaning "parrot", alluding to the mouse-grey face and underparts. The specific epithet <i>monachus</i> is <a href="/wiki/Late_Latin" title="Late Latin">Late Latin</a> for a "monk".<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The monk parakeet is one of two species in the genus <i>Myiopsitta</i>, the other being the <a href="/wiki/Cliff_parakeet" title="Cliff parakeet">cliff parakeet</a> (<i>Myiopsitta luchsi</i>). The two parakeets were previously considered to be a single species. However, due to morphological and behavioral differences, as well as dissimilarities geographically, the cliff parakeet has been elevated to species status.<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The cliff parakeet's altitudinal range apparently does not overlap, so is thus entirely, but just barely, <a href="/wiki/Allopatric" class="mw-redirect" title="Allopatric">allopatric</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Juniper_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Juniper-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/American_Ornithological_Society" title="American Ornithological Society">American Ornithological Society</a> has deferred recognizing the cliff parakeet as distinct "because of insufficient published data".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-hbw_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbw-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Juniper_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Juniper-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>M. m. monachus</i> <small>(Boddaert, 1783)</small> – <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> from southeastern <a href="/wiki/Santiago_del_Estero_Province" title="Santiago del Estero Province">Santiago del Estero Province</a> throughout the <a href="/wiki/Salado_River,_Argentina" class="mw-redirect" title="Salado River, Argentina">Río Salado</a> and lower <a href="/wiki/Paran%C3%A1_River" title="Paraná River">Paraná</a> basins to <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires_Province" title="Buenos Aires Province">Buenos Aires Province</a> and <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">Uruguay</a></li></ul>
<dl><dd>The largest subspecies</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. m. calita</i> <small>(Boddaert, 1783)</small> – <a href="/wiki/Andean" class="mw-redirect" title="Andean">Andean</a> foothills up to 1,000&#160;m <a href="/wiki/Above_mean_sea_level" class="mw-redirect" title="Above mean sea level">ASL</a>, from southeastern <a href="/wiki/Bolivia" title="Bolivia">Bolivia</a> (<a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_Department_(Bolivia)" title="Santa Cruz Department (Bolivia)">Santa Cruz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tarija_department" class="mw-redirect" title="Tarija department">Tarija departments</a>) to <a href="/wiki/Paraguay" title="Paraguay">Paraguay</a> and northwestern Argentina, then west of the range of <i>monachus</i>, extending into the lowlands again in <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Negro_Province" title="Río Negro Province">Río Negro</a> and possibly <a href="/wiki/Chubut_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Chubut province">Chubut provinces</a>.</li></ul>
<dl><dd>Smaller than <i>monachus</i>, wings more prominently blue, gray of head darker.</dd></dl>
<ul><li><i>M. m. cotorra</i> <small>(Finsch, 1868)</small> – southwestern <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso" title="Mato Grosso">Mato Grosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mato_Grosso_do_Sul" title="Mato Grosso do Sul">Mato Grosso do Sul</a>, possibly <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_do_Sul" title="Rio Grande do Sul">Rio Grande do Sul</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-adqp2006_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adqp2006-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> throughout the <a href="/wiki/R%C3%ADo_Paraguay" class="mw-redirect" title="Río Paraguay">Río Paraguay</a> and middle Paraná basins as well as the <a href="/wiki/Gran_Chaco" title="Gran Chaco">Gran Chaco</a>.</li></ul>
<dl><dd>Essentially identical to <i>calita</i> but reported as less yellow below and brighter overall.</dd></dl>
<p>The subspecies' ranges meet in the general area of Paraguay, and there they are insufficiently delimited. The distinctness and delimitation of <i>M. m. calita</i> and <i>M. m. cotorra</i> especially require further study.
</p><p>Like the other <a href="/wiki/Neotropical_parrots" class="mw-redirect" title="Neotropical parrots">neotropical parrots</a>, the monk parakeet is usually placed in the <a href="/wiki/Tribe_(biology)" title="Tribe (biology)">tribe</a> <a href="/wiki/Arini_(tribe)" title="Arini (tribe)">Arini</a>, which might warrant elevation to <a href="/wiki/Subfamily" title="Subfamily">subfamily</a> rank as the <a href="/wiki/Arinae" class="mw-redirect" title="Arinae">Arinae</a>. <i>M. monachus</i> belongs to the long-tailed <a href="/wiki/Clade" title="Clade">clade</a> of these – <a href="/wiki/Macaw" title="Macaw">macaws</a> and <a href="/wiki/Conure" title="Conure">conures</a>, essentially, which would retain the name Arini/Arinae if this <a href="/wiki/Polyphyletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Polyphyletic">polyphyletic</a> group were split.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Quaker_Parrot.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Quaker_Parrot.jpg/220px-Quaker_Parrot.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="181" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Quaker_Parrot.jpg/330px-Quaker_Parrot.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Quaker_Parrot.jpg/440px-Quaker_Parrot.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1359" data-file-height="1116" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Quaker_Parrot.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Female pet monk parakeet</div></div></div>
<table class="infobox" style="width:22em;float: right; clear: right; margin:0 0 1.5em 1.5em"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" style="text-align:center;font-size:125%;font-weight:bold;font-size:115%">External audio</th></tr><tr><td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;text-align: left"><img alt="audio icon" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/16px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/24px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Nuvola_apps_arts.svg/32px-Nuvola_apps_arts.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="128" data-file-height="128" /> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.xeno-canto.org/sounds/uploaded/OOECIWCSWV/XC272943-Monk%20Parakeet%20calls%20A.mp3">Monk Parakeet bird call may be heard here</a>, from <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.xeno-canto.org/explore?query=Myiopsitta+monachus&amp;dir=0&amp;order=loc&amp;pg=1">xeno-canto.org</a>, <i>retrieved 1.08.2018</i></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate subspecies</a> of this parakeet is 29&#160;cm (11&#160;in) long on average, with a 48&#160;cm (19&#160;in) wingspan, and weighs 100&#160;g (3.5&#160;oz). Females tend to be 10–20% smaller, but can only be reliably sexed by DNA or feather testing. It has bright-green upperparts. The forehead and breast are pale gray with darker scalloping and the rest of the underparts are very light-green to yellow. The <a href="/wiki/Remiges" class="mw-redirect" title="Remiges">remiges</a> are dark blue, and the tail is long and tapering. The bill is orange. The call is a loud and throaty <i>chape(-yee)</i> or <i>quak quaki quak-wi quarr</i>, and screeches <i>skveet</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-hbw_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbw-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Juniper_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Juniper-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Domestic breeds in colors other than the natural plumage have been produced. These include birds with white, blue, and yellow in place of green. As such coloration provides less camouflage, feral birds are usually of wild-type coloration.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg/220px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg/330px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg/440px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4272" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Myiopsitta_monachus_-nests_-Zaragoza_-Spain-8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Nests in <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, Spain</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago,_Chile_-nest-8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago%2C_Chile_-nest-8.jpg/220px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago%2C_Chile_-nest-8.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago%2C_Chile_-nest-8.jpg/330px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago%2C_Chile_-nest-8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago%2C_Chile_-nest-8.jpg/440px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago%2C_Chile_-nest-8.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4320" data-file-height="3240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Myiopsitta_monachus_-Santiago,_Chile_-nest-8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Birds and their nest in Santiago, Chile</div></div></div>
<p>The monk parakeet is the only parrot that builds a stick <a href="/wiki/Bird_nest" title="Bird nest">nest</a>, in a tree or on a man-made structure, rather than using a hole in a tree. This <a href="/wiki/Social_animal" class="mw-redirect" title="Social animal">gregarious</a> species often breeds colonially, building a single large nest with separate entrances for each pair. In the wild, the colonies can become quite large, with pairs occupying separate "apartments" in nests that can reach the size of a small <a href="/wiki/Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Automobile">automobile</a>. These nests can attract many other tenants including birds of prey such as the <a href="/wiki/Spot-winged_falconet" title="Spot-winged falconet">spot-winged falconet</a> (<i>Spiziapteryx circumcincta</i>), ducks such as the <a href="/wiki/Yellow-billed_teal" title="Yellow-billed teal">yellow-billed teal</a> (<i>Anas flavirostris</i>), and even <a href="/wiki/Mammal" title="Mammal">mammals</a>. Their five to 12 white eggs hatch in about 24 days.
</p><p>Unusually for a parrot, monk parakeet pairs occasionally have helper individuals, often grown offspring, which assist with feeding the young (see <a href="/wiki/Kin_selection" title="Kin selection">kin selection</a>).
</p><p>The lifespan of monk parakeets has been given as 15–20 years<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> or as much as 25–30 years;<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> the former might refer to typical lifespans in captivity and/or in the wild, while the latter is in the range of maximum lifespans recorded for parakeets.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Distribution">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The monk parakeet is globally very common.<sup id="cite_ref-iucn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iucn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In Argentina, Brazil, and Uruguay, monk parakeets are regarded as major agricultural pests (as noted by <a href="/wiki/Charles_Darwin" title="Charles Darwin">Charles Darwin</a>, among others). Their population explosion in South American rural areas seems to be associated with the expansion of <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">eucalyptus</a> <a href="/wiki/Forestry" title="Forestry">forestry</a> for paper pulp production, which offers the bird the opportunity to build protected nests in artificial forests where ecological competition from other species is limited.<sup id="cite_ref-hbw_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbw-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_an_introduced_species">As an introduced species</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: As an introduced species">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Lovebirds_A.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/A_Lovebirds_A.JPG/220px-A_Lovebirds_A.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="75" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/A_Lovebirds_A.JPG/330px-A_Lovebirds_A.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/A_Lovebirds_A.JPG/440px-A_Lovebirds_A.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4116" data-file-height="1398" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:A_Lovebirds_A.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monk parakeets in <a href="/wiki/Parc_de_la_Ciutadella" title="Parc de la Ciutadella">Parc de la Ciutadella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a></div></div></div>
<p>Self-sustaining <a href="/wiki/Feral" title="Feral">feral</a> populations have been recorded in several U.S. states and various regions of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> (namely <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azores" title="Azores">Azores</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madeira" title="Madeira">Madeira</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gibraltar" title="Gibraltar">Gibraltar</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">Corsica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malta" title="Malta">Malta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_Islands" title="Channel Islands">Channel Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>), as well as in <a href="/wiki/British_Columbia,_Canada" class="mw-redirect" title="British Columbia, Canada">British Columbia, Canada</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahamas" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">Puerto Rico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Easter_Island" title="Easter Island">Easter Island</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. As it is an open-woodlands species, it adapts readily to urban areas.
</p><p>In areas where they have been introduced, some fear they will harm <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">crops</a> and native species, while others dispute evidence of harm caused by feral colonies, and oppose exterminating these birds. However, local bans and eradication programs exist in some areas of the U.S. Outside the U.S., introduced populations do not appear to raise similar controversy, presumably because of smaller numbers of birds, or because their settlement in urban areas does not pose a threat to agricultural production. The U.K. appears to have changed its view on its feral populations and the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a> is planning to remove monk parakeets from the wild, as it believes that they threaten local wildlife and crops.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Feral populations are often descended from very small founder populations. Being as social and intelligent as they are, monk parakeets develop some cultural <a href="/wiki/Tradition" title="Tradition">traditions</a>, namely vocal <a href="/wiki/Dialect" title="Dialect">dialects</a> that differ between groups. In populations descended from a large number of birds, a range of "dialects" will exist. If the founder population is small, however, a process similar to <a href="/wiki/Genetic_drift" title="Genetic drift">genetic drift</a> may occur if prominent founders vocalize in an unusual "dialect", with this particular way of vocalizing becoming established in the resulting feral colony. For example, no fewer than three different "dialects" occur among the feral monk parrots of the <a href="/wiki/Milford,_Connecticut" title="Milford, Connecticut">Milford, Connecticut</a>, metropolitan area.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Brazil">Brazil</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Brazil">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The species has in recent years expanded its range in Brazil, where a self-sustaining population occurs in the downtown area of <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a>. Since this population occurs far from the bird's original range in Brazil – it was only found in the far south and southwest – it is most probably a consequence of escapees from the <a href="/wiki/Pet_trade" class="mw-redirect" title="Pet trade">pet trade</a>. In Rio de Janeiro, the bird can be easily seen at the <a href="/wiki/Aterro_do_Flamengo" class="mw-redirect" title="Aterro do Flamengo">Aterro do Flamengo</a> gardens – where it nests on <a href="/wiki/Palm_trees" class="mw-redirect" title="Palm trees">palm trees</a> and feeds on their fruit; the Rio birds seem to favor nesting amid the leaves of <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">coconut</a> palm trees, as well as in the vicinity of the neighboring domestic flight terminal, the <a href="/wiki/Santos_Dumont_Airport" title="Santos Dumont Airport">Santos Dumont Airport</a> and in the gardens of <a href="/wiki/Quinta_da_Boa_Vista" title="Quinta da Boa Vista">Quinta da Boa Vista</a>, where communal nests roughly 1 m in diameter have been seen.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Santa_Catarina_State" class="mw-redirect" title="Santa Catarina State">Santa Catarina State</a>, probable escapees have been reported on occasion for quite some time, and a feral population seems to have established itself in <a href="/wiki/Florian%C3%B3polis" title="Florianópolis">Florianópolis</a> early in the first decade of the 21st century when birds were observed feeding right next to the highway in the Rio Vermelho-Vargem Grande area.<sup id="cite_ref-adqp2006_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adqp2006-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">Mexico</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mexico">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua,_Chihuahua,_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua%2C_Chihuahua%2C_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg/220px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua%2C_Chihuahua%2C_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="177" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua%2C_Chihuahua%2C_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg/330px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua%2C_Chihuahua%2C_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ef/Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua%2C_Chihuahua%2C_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg/440px-Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua%2C_Chihuahua%2C_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1001" data-file-height="807" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Myiopsitta_monachus_-_Chihuahua,_Chihuahua,_M%C3%A9xico.3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monk parakeets in <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_City" title="Chihuahua City">Chihuahua City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">Chihuahua</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9xico" class="mw-redirect" title="México">México</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The monk parakeet was first recorded in Mexico City in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-ibiologia.unam.mx_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ibiologia.unam.mx-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Records exist from seven other locations, including the cities of Puebla, Morelia, Celaya, Oaxaca, Tuxtla Gutiérrez, Hermosillo, and Mexicali, and the mouth of the Loreto River in Baja California Sur.
</p><p>Nesting populations are known in Mexico City and Oaxaca. A small but growing population has also been established in the southern part of the city of <a href="/wiki/Puebla,_Puebla" class="mw-redirect" title="Puebla, Puebla">Puebla, Puebla</a>, in the surroundings of the city's aviary, which they are known to visit frequently, and where they can often be seen clinging to the outer side of its mesh walls. No studies have been made to assess the impact they might have on the relict populations of <a href="/wiki/Green_parakeet" title="Green parakeet">green parakeet</a> that live in the same area and other well-wooded zones of the city.
</p><p>Following the ban on the trade of native parrot species, local traditional bird sellers have now switched to the monk parakeet as their staple parrot, and that might have increased the number of escapees. Sometimes, the head and breast feathers of monk parakeets are dyed yellow to deceive uninformed buyers, mimicking the endangered <a href="/wiki/Yellow-headed_amazon" title="Yellow-headed amazon">yellow-headed amazon</a>. The presence of this species in seven geographically distant and independent locations in Mexico indicates that the source of these individuals is most likely the pet trade.<sup id="cite_ref-ibiologia.unam.mx_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ibiologia.unam.mx-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">United States</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: United States">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Thousands of monk parakeets were imported to the United States between the 1960s and the 1980s as pets.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Many escaped or were intentionally released, and populations were allowed to proliferate. By the early 1970s, <i>M. monachus</i> was established in seven states, and by 1995, it had spread to eight more. In <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> alone, estimates range from 150,000 to 500,000.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As one of the few temperate-zone parrots, the monk parakeet is more able than most to survive cold climates (partly because they build communal nests about heat-producing electrical equipment atop utility poles), and <a href="/wiki/Bird_colony" title="Bird colony">colonies</a> exist as far north as <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallas" title="Dallas">Dallas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austin" class="mw-redirect" title="Austin">Austin</a>, Wisconsin, <a href="/wiki/Cincinnati" title="Cincinnati">Cincinnati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">Louisville</a>, coastal Rhode Island, Massachusetts, Connecticut, and southwestern <a href="/wiki/Washington_(U.S._state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington (U.S. state)">Washington</a>. <a href="/wiki/Edgewater,_New_Jersey#Edgewater_parrots" title="Edgewater, New Jersey">Edgewater, New Jersey</a> has had a colony since 1980.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> This hardiness makes this species second only to the <a href="/wiki/Rose-ringed_parakeet" title="Rose-ringed parakeet">rose-ringed parakeet</a> among parrots as a successful <a href="/wiki/Introduced_species" title="Introduced species">introduced species</a>.
</p><p>In 2012, a pair of monk parakeets attempted nesting in <a href="/wiki/Watervliet,_New_York" title="Watervliet, New York">Watervliet, New York</a> (about 240&#160;km (150&#160;mi) north of New York City, near <a href="/wiki/Albany,_New_York" title="Albany, New York">Albany, New York</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Prior to egg-laying, one bird was captured and the nest eventually was removed due to concerns that the nest built adjacent to an electrical transformer created a fire hazard.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They have also found a home in <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn, New York</a>, after an accidental release decades ago of what appear to have been black-market birds<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> within <a href="/wiki/Green-Wood_Cemetery" title="Green-Wood Cemetery">Green-Wood Cemetery</a>. The grounds crew initially tried to destroy the unsightly nests at the entrance gate, but no longer do so because the presence of the parrots has reduced the number of <a href="/wiki/Feral_pigeon" title="Feral pigeon">pigeons</a> nesting within it. The management's decision was based on a comparative chemical analysis of pigeon feces (which destroy <a href="/wiki/Brownstone" title="Brownstone">brownstone</a> structures) and monk parakeet feces (which have no ill effect). The monk parakeets are in effect preserving this historic structure. <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn_College" title="Brooklyn College">Brooklyn College</a> has a monk parakeet as an "unofficial" mascot in reference to the colony of the species that lives in its campus grounds. It is featured on the masthead of the student magazine. They have also made their homes in the lamp posts in <a href="/wiki/Pelham_Bay_Park" title="Pelham Bay Park">Pelham Bay Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>. Most of these monk parakeet populations can be traced to shipments of captured birds from Argentina.<sup id="cite_ref-QuakeLegal_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QuakeLegal-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Chicago, parrot origin theories include a <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a> experiment gone awry,<sup id="cite_ref-Bobeda_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bobeda-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> an overturned truck on its way to a pet store,<sup id="cite_ref-Bobeda_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bobeda-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> escaped birds from a holding pen at <a href="/wiki/O%27Hare_International_Airport" title="O&#39;Hare International Airport">O'Hare Airport</a><sup id="cite_ref-chicago_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicago-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> or discarded/escaped pets.<sup id="cite_ref-chicago_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicago-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> According to University of Chicago ornithologist Dr. Stephen Pruett-Jones, "They got here through the pet trade and the pet trade really peaked in the mid to late 1960s."<sup id="cite_ref-Bobeda_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bobeda-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The first documented parrot nest in Chicago dates to 1973.<sup id="cite_ref-Bobeda_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bobeda-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The species continues to thrive despite several unusually harsh winters that occurred during the 1980s and in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-chicago_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicago-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Various attempts to remove them were made over the years, most of which were resisted by a group of <a href="/wiki/Hyde_Park,_Chicago" title="Hyde Park, Chicago">Hyde Park</a> residents, including Mayor <a href="/wiki/Harold_Washington" title="Harold Washington">Harold Washington</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Brotman_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brotman-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The birds are generally welcomed in the city, especially by bird watchers, and were the subject of a 2012 ornithological study.<sup id="cite_ref-chicago_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicago-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The population is estimated to be at 1,000 birds, with healthy colonies located in several of the city's parks.<sup id="cite_ref-chicago_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicago-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Houston,_Texas" class="mw-redirect" title="Houston, Texas">Houston, Texas</a> also have thriving monk parakeet populations.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Europe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg/220px-MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg/330px-MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg/440px-MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:MonkParakeetsSantaPonsa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Monk parakeets in Santa Ponsa, <a href="/wiki/Majorca" class="mw-redirect" title="Majorca">Majorca</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a></div></div></div>
<p>Monk parakeets can be seen in <a href="/wiki/Madrid" title="Madrid">Madrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cadiz" class="mw-redirect" title="Cadiz">Cadiz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seville" title="Seville">Seville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Torremolinos" title="Torremolinos">Torremolinos</a>, <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A1laga" title="Málaga">Málaga</a>, Nerja, <a href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarragona" title="Tarragona">Tarragona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roquetas_de_Mar" title="Roquetas de Mar">Roquetas de Mar</a> (<a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">Andalusia</a>), <a href="/wiki/Zaragoza" title="Zaragoza">Zaragoza</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">Canary Islands</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Majorca" class="mw-redirect" title="Majorca">Majorca</a> in <a href="/wiki/Balearic_Islands" title="Balearic Islands">Balearic Islands</a>. They were first seen around 1975. In Madrid, they especially frequent the <a href="/wiki/Complutense_University_of_Madrid" title="Complutense University of Madrid">Ciudad Universitaria</a> (Complutense university campus) and <a href="/wiki/Casa_de_Campo" title="Casa de Campo">Casa de Campo</a> park. They are a common sight in Barcelona parks, often as numerous as pigeons. They form substantial colonies in <a href="/wiki/Parc_de_la_Ciutadella" title="Parc de la Ciutadella">Parc de la Ciutadella</a>, Parc de la <a href="/wiki/Barceloneta,_Barcelona" class="mw-redirect" title="Barceloneta, Barcelona">Barceloneta</a>, and in smaller city parks such as Jardins <a href="/wiki/Josep_Trueta" title="Josep Trueta">Josep Trueta</a> in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Poble_Nou&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Poble Nou (page does not exist)">Poble Nou</a>, with a colony as far north as <a href="/wiki/Empuriabrava" title="Empuriabrava">Empuriabrava</a>. They are more frequent in watered urban parks with grass areas and palm trees, near to a river or the sea. The monk parakeet, as an invasive species, has become a problem to local fauna such as pigeons and sparrows, but not yet so harmful to <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_magpie" title="Eurasian magpie">magpies</a>. Parakeets have also caused trouble to agriculture near the cities.
</p><p>Spain has outlawed the possession, selling, breeding and trafficking of monk parakeets since 2013.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Madrid has the greatest population of monk parakeets in Europe with 10,800 parakeets as of June 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> As of 2015, the estimated population of monk parakeets in Barcelona was 6,248.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Greece" title="Greece">Greece</a>, they have established breeding colonies in the <a href="/wiki/National_Garden,_Athens" title="National Garden, Athens">National Garden, Athens</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> population in 2011 is believed to be around 150, in the <a href="/wiki/Home_Counties" class="mw-redirect" title="Home Counties">Home Counties</a> region. The <a href="/wiki/Department_for_the_Environment,_Food_and_Rural_Affairs" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs">Department for the Environment, Food and Rural Affairs</a> announced plans in 2011 to control them, countering the threat to infrastructure, crops, and native British wildlife by trapping and rehoming, removing nests, and shooting when necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-gray_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gray-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Groups of monk parakeets can be found in the <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgian</a> capital city <a href="/wiki/Brussels" title="Brussels">Brussels</a> and its surrounding areas. They have been living in the wild at least since the 1970s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="As_pets">As pets</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Monk_parakeet&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: As pets">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Monk parakeets are highly intelligent, social birds. Those kept as pets routinely develop <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">vocabularies</a> of scores of words and phrases.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Due to this early speaking ability, it is overtaking the <a href="/wiki/Cockatiel" title="Cockatiel">cockatiel</a> as the favorite bird to teach to talk. Another contributing factor to growing popularity is that this bird has a shorter lifespan and lower price than African grey parrots.
</p><p>Because of monk parakeets' listing as an agricultural pest, the U.S. states of California, Georgia, Kansas, Kentucky, Hawaii, Maine, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Tennessee, and Wyoming,<sup id="cite_ref-QuakeLegal_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QuakeLegal-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> as well as <a href="/wiki/Western_Australia" title="Western Australia">Western Australia</a><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> outlaw sale and ownership. In Connecticut, one can own a monk parakeet, but cannot sell or breed them. In New York and Virginia, one can own a monk parakeet with banding and registration. In Ohio, owning one is legal if the bird's flight feathers are clipped or it is incapable of free flight.<sup id="cite_ref-QuakeLegal_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QuakeLegal-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
