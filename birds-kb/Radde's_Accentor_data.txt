<p><b>Radde's accentor</b> (<i>Prunella ocularis</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Prunellidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Prunellidae">Prunellidae</a>. It is found in mountainous parts of <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a> and northern <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="Southwest Asia">Southwest Asia</a>.
</p><p>Its natural <a href="/wiki/Habitat" title="Habitat">habitat</a> is temperate <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">grassland</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy">Taxonomy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Radde%27s_accentor&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Radde's accentor was <a href="/wiki/Species_description" title="Species description">described</a> by the German naturalist <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Radde" title="Gustav Radde">Gustav Radde</a> in 1884 from a specimen collected in the <a href="/wiki/Talysh_Mountains" title="Talysh Mountains">Talysh Mountains</a> near the Azerbaijan-Iran border. He coined the <a href="/wiki/Binomial_name" class="mw-redirect" title="Binomial name">binomial name</a> <i>Accentor ocularis</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It is now placed in the <a href="/wiki/Genus" title="Genus">genus</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Prunella_(bird)" class="mw-redirect" title="Prunella (bird)">Prunella</a></i> that was introduced by the French ornithologist <a href="/wiki/Louis_Jean_Pierre_Vieillot" title="Louis Jean Pierre Vieillot">Louis Vieillot</a> in 1816.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The species is <a href="/wiki/Monotypic" class="mw-redirect" title="Monotypic">monotypic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ioc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ioc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
