<p><b>Tristram's starling</b> or <b>Tristram's grackle</b> (<i>Onychognathus tristramii</i>) is a species of <a href="/wiki/Starling" title="Starling">starling</a> native to the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>. Its territory is in the areas of <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan" title="Jordan">Jordan</a>, northeastern <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a>), western <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yemen" title="Yemen">Yemen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">Oman</a>, nesting mainly on rocky cliff faces.<sup id="cite_ref-bwpc_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwpc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The species is named after Reverend <a href="/wiki/Henry_Baker_Tristram" title="Henry Baker Tristram">Henry Baker Tristram</a>, who also collected natural history specimens.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tristram%27s_starling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A member of the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Onychognathus" title="Onychognathus">Onychognathus</a></i>, it is 25&#160;cm long (including a 9&#160;cm tail), with a wingspan of 44–45&#160;cm, and a weight of 100–140 g. The males have glossy iridescent black plumage with orange patches on the outer wing, which are particularly noticeable in flight. The bill and legs are black. Females and young birds are similar but duller and with a greyish head, lacking the plumage gloss.<sup id="cite_ref-bwpc_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwpc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-birdguide_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-birdguide-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behavior">Behavior</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Tristram%27s_starling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Behavior">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It is gregarious and noisy, with a call that resembles a <a href="/wiki/Wolf_whistle" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolf whistle">wolf whistle</a>. They are <a href="/wiki/Omnivore" title="Omnivore">omnivorous</a>, feeding on fruit and invertebrates, and can also be observed grooming <a href="/wiki/Nubian_ibex" title="Nubian ibex">Nubian ibex</a> and domestic <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">livestock</a> for <a href="/wiki/Parasite" class="mw-redirect" title="Parasite">parasites</a>. They are becoming increasingly commensal with man, feeding in towns and villages; this has enabled a recent northward spread in their distribution.<sup id="cite_ref-bwpc_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bwpc-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
