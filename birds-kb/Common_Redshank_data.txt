<p>The <b>Common Redshank</b> or simply <b>Redshank</b> (<i>Tringa totanus</i>) is an <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasian</a> <a href="/wiki/Wader" title="Wader">wader</a> in the large <a href="/wiki/Family_(biology)" title="Family (biology)">family</a> <a href="/wiki/Scolopacidae" title="Scolopacidae" class="mw-redirect">Scolopacidae</a>.</p>

Winter plumage, at <a href="/wiki/Sungei_Buloh_Wetland_Reserve" title="Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve">Sungei Buloh Wetland Reserve</a> (<a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>)</div>
</div>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description and systematics">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Description_and_systematics">Description and systematics</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tringa_totanus-pjt.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Tringa_totanus-pjt.jpg/220px-Tringa_totanus-pjt.jpg" width="220" height="152" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tringa_totanus-pjt.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Bird in flight (Laguna di Venezia, Italy)</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Common Redshanks in breeding <a href="/wiki/Plumage" title="Plumage">plumage</a> are a marbled brown color, slightly lighter below. In winter plumage they become somewhat lighter-toned and less patterned, being rather plain greyish-brown above and whitish below. They have red legs and a black-tipped red bill, and show white up the back and on the wings in flight.</p>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Spotted_Redshank" title="Spotted Redshank">Spotted Redshank</a> (<i>T. erythropus</i>), which breeds in the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a>, has a longer <a href="/wiki/Beak" title="Beak">bill</a> and legs; it is almost entirely black in breeding plumage and very pale in winter. It is not a particularly close relative of the Common Redshank, but rather belongs to a high-<a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">latitude</a> lineage of largish shanks. <i>T. totanus</i> on the other hand is closely related to the <a href="/wiki/Marsh_Sandpiper" title="Marsh Sandpiper">Marsh Sandpiper</a> (<i>T. stagnatilis</i>), and closer still to the small <a href="/wiki/Wood_Sandpiper" title="Wood Sandpiper">Wood Sandpiper</a> (<i>T. glareola</i>). The ancestors of the latter and the Common Redshank seem to have diverged around the <a href="/wiki/Miocene" title="Miocene">Miocene</a>-<a href="/wiki/Pliocene" title="Pliocene">Pliocene</a> boundary, about 5-6 <a href="/wiki/Million_years_ago" title="Million years ago" class="mw-redirect">million years ago</a>. These three <a href="/wiki/Subarctic" title="Subarctic">subarctic</a>- to <a href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate" class="mw-redirect">temperate</a>-region <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a> form a group of smallish shanks with have red or yellowish legs, and in breeding plumage are generally a subdued light brown above with some darker mottling, and have somewhat diffuse small brownish spots on the breast and neck.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Common_Redshank&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Ecology">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Ecology">Ecology</span></h2>
<p>It is a widespread breeding <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> across <a href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate" class="mw-redirect">temperate</a> <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">Eurasia</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migratory</a> species, wintering on coasts around the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a>, on the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic" class="mw-redirect">Atlantic</a> coast of Europe from <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">Great Britain</a> southwards, and in <a href="/wiki/South_Asia" title="South Asia">South Asia</a>. They are uncommon <a href="/wiki/Vagrancy_(biology)" title="Vagrancy (biology)">vagrants</a> outside these areas; on <a href="/wiki/Palau" title="Palau">Palau</a> in <a href="/wiki/Micronesia" title="Micronesia">Micronesia</a> for example, the species was recorded in the mid-1970s and in 2000<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup>.</p>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Redshanks_on_stone_pillar.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Redshanks_on_stone_pillar.jpg/220px-Redshanks_on_stone_pillar.jpg" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Redshanks_on_stone_pillar.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf1/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
Two Redshanks on a stone pillar</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>They are wary and noisy birds which will alert everything else with their loud piping call. Like most <a href="/wiki/Wade" title="Wade">waders</a>, they feed on small <a href="/wiki/Invertebrate" title="Invertebrate">invertebrates</a>. Redshanks will nest in any wetland, from damp meadows to saltmarsh, often at high densities. They lay 3-5 eggs.</p>
<p>The Common Redshank is one of the species to which the <i>Agreement on the Conservation of African-Eurasian Migratory Waterbirds</i> (<a href="/wiki/AEWA" title="AEWA" class="mw-redirect">AEWA</a>) applies.</p>
<p>It is widely distributed and quite plentiful in some regions, and thus not considered a <a href="/wiki/Threatened_species" title="Threatened species">threatened species</a> by the <a href="/wiki/IUCN" title="IUCN" class="mw-redirect">IUCN</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
