<p>The <b>long-tailed skua</b> or <b>long-tailed jaeger</b> (<i>Stercorarius longicaudus</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Seabird" title="Seabird">seabird</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Skua" title="Skua">skua</a> family <a href="/wiki/Stercorariidae" class="mw-redirect" title="Stercorariidae">Stercorariidae</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long-tailed_jaeger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Etymology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The word "jaeger" is derived from the <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a> word <i>Jäger</i>, meaning "hunter".<sup id="cite_ref-OEDjaeger_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OEDjaeger-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The English word "skua" comes from the <a href="/wiki/Faroese_language" title="Faroese language">Faroese</a> name <i>skúgvur</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[ˈskɪkvʊər]</a></span> for the <a href="/wiki/Great_skua" title="Great skua">great skua</a>, with the island of <a href="/wiki/Sk%C3%BAvoy" title="Skúvoy">Skúvoy</a> known for its colony of that bird. The general Faroese term for skuas is <i>kjógvi</i> <small></small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA" title="Help:IPA">[ˈtʃɛkvə]</a></span>.<sup id="cite_ref-OEDskua_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OEDskua-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The genus name <i>Stercorarius</i> is <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> and means "of dung"; the food disgorged by other birds when pursued by skuas was once thought to be excrement. The specific <i>longicaudus</i> is from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>longus</i>, "long", and <i>cauda</i>, "tail".<sup id="cite_ref-job_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-job-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG/220px-Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG/330px-Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG/440px-Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Stercorarius_longicaudus_MWNH_0305.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Eggs, Collection <a href="/wiki/Museum_Wiesbaden" title="Museum Wiesbaden">Museum Wiesbaden</a></div></div></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long-tailed_jaeger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg/220px-Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="148" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg/330px-Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg/440px-Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1050" data-file-height="707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Long-tailed_Skua_in_flight.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Long-tailed jaeger in flight</div></div></div>
<p>This species is unmistakable as an adult, with grey back, dark primary wing feathers without a white "flash", black cap and very long tail. Adults often hover over their breeding territories. Juveniles are much more problematic, and are difficult to separate from <a href="/wiki/Parasitic_jaeger" title="Parasitic jaeger">parasitic jaeger</a> over the sea. They are slimmer, longer-winged and more tern-like than that species, but show the same wide range of plumage variation. However, they are usually colder toned than Arctic, with greyer shades, rather than brown.
</p><p>This is the smallest of the skua family at 38–58&#160;cm (15–23&#160;in), depending on season and age. However up to 29&#160;cm (11&#160;in) of its length can be made up by the tail which may include the 15&#160;cm (5.9&#160;in) tail streamers of the summer adult. The wingspan of this species ranges from 102 to 117&#160;cm (40 to 46&#160;in) and the body mass is 230–444&#160;g (8.1–15.7&#160;oz).<sup id="cite_ref-CRC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CRC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-arkive_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-arkive-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long-tailed_jaeger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are described:<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i>S. l. longicaudus</i> – <small><a href="/wiki/Louis_Jean_Pierre_Vieillot" title="Louis Jean Pierre Vieillot">Vieillot</a>, 1819</small>:  <a href="/wiki/Nominate_subspecies#Nominotypical_subspecies_and_subspecies_autonyms" class="mw-redirect" title="Nominate subspecies">nominate</a>, found in northern <a href="/wiki/Scandinavia" title="Scandinavia">Scandinavia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.</li>
<li><i>S. l. pallescens</i> – <small>Løppenthin, 1932</small>: found in eastern <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">Siberia</a>, Arctic <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a>.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Breeding">Breeding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Long-tailed_jaeger&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Breeding">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg/220px-Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="74" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg/330px-Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg/440px-Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="168" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chaluha_mal%C3%A1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Long-tailed jaeger illustration by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Naumann" title="Johann Friedrich Naumann">Johann Friedrich Naumann</a></div></div></div>
<p>This species breeds in the high Arctic of <a href="/wiki/Palearctic" class="mw-redirect" title="Palearctic">Eurasia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>, with major populations in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> and smaller populations around the rest of the <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">Arctic</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrant</a>, wintering in the south <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pacific" class="mw-redirect" title="Pacific">Pacific</a>. Passage juvenile birds sometimes hunt small prey in ploughed fields or golf-courses, and are typically quite fearless of humans.
</p><p>They nest on dry <a href="/wiki/Tundra" title="Tundra">tundra</a> or higher fells laying two spotted olive-brown eggs. On the breeding grounds they can be heard making yelping and rattling sounds. Outside of the breeding season they spend most of their time over open ocean and have a harsh <i>kreeah</i> cry. This bird feeds on fish (mainly caught from other seabirds), smaller <a href="/wiki/Birds" class="mw-redirect" title="Birds">birds</a>, food scraps, small mammals, fruit and carrion. On migration, long-tailed jaegers are more likely to catch their own food, and less likely to steal from <a href="/wiki/Gull" title="Gull">gulls</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tern" title="Tern">terns</a> than larger <a href="/wiki/Species" title="Species">species</a>.
</p>
