<p>The <b>Short-toed Snake Eagle</b> (<i>Circaetus gallicus</i>) also known as <b>Short-toed Eagle</b>, is a medium-sized <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">bird of prey</a> in the family <a href="/wiki/Accipitridae" title="Accipitridae">Accipitridae</a> which also includes many other diurnal <a href="/wiki/Bird_of_prey" title="Bird of prey">raptors</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Kite_(bird)" title="Kite (bird)">kites</a>, <a href="/wiki/Buzzard" title="Buzzard">buzzards</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harrier_(bird)" title="Harrier (bird)">harriers</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Short-toed_Snake_Eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Range and habitat">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Range_and_habitat">Range and habitat</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CircaetusFoot.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/CircaetusFoot.png/220px-CircaetusFoot.png" width="220" height="258" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/CircaetusFoot.png/330px-CircaetusFoot.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/CircaetusFoot.png/440px-CircaetusFoot.png 2x" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CircaetusFoot.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"><img src="//bits.wikimedia.org/static-1.21wmf2/skins/common/images/magnify-clip.png" width="15" height="11" alt="" /></a></div>
The short toes that give the name</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>This is an <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a> species spread throughout the <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean" title="Mediterranean" class="mw-redirect">Mediterranean</a> basin and into <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, and into parts of <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, mainly in the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Subcontinent" title="Indian Subcontinent" class="mw-redirect">Indian Subcontinent</a> and also further east in some <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesian</a> islands).</p>
<p>Those present on the northern edge of the Mediterranean and other parts of <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">migrate</a> mainly to <a href="/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa" title="Sub-Saharan Africa">sub-Saharan Africa</a> north of the <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">equator</a>, leaving in September/October and returning in April/May. In the Middle and Far East the populations are resident. In Europe it is most numerous in <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> where it is fairly common but elsewhere it is rare in many parts of its range. A bird on the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a>, in October 1999 was the first confirmed record for that country.</p>
<p>The Short-toed Snake Eagle is found in open cultivated plains, arid stony deciduous scrub areas and foothills and semi-desert areas. It requires trees for nesting.</p>

<p>Adults are 62–67 cm (2&#160;ft 0&#160;in–2&#160;ft 2&#160;in) long with a 170–185 cm (5&#160;ft 7&#160;in–6&#160;ft 1&#160;in) wingspan and weigh 1.2–2.3 kg (2.6–5.1 lb).<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> They can be recognised in the field by their predominantly white underside, the upper parts being greyish brown. The chin, throat and upper breast are a pale, earthy brown. The tail has 3 or 4 bars. Additional indications are an <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>-like rounded head, brightly yellow eyes and lightly barred under wing.</p>

<p>The Short-toed Snake Eagle is an accomplished flyer and spends more time on the wing than do most members of its genus. It favours soaring over hill slopes and hilltops on updraughts, and it does much of its hunting from this position at heights of up to 500&#160;meters (550 yards). When quartering open country it frequently hovers like a <a href="/wiki/Common_Kestrel" title="Common Kestrel">Kestrel</a>. When it soars it does so on flattish wings.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Short-toed_Snake_Eagle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Behaviour">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour">Behaviour</span></h2>
<p>Its prey is mostly reptiles, mainly <a href="/wiki/Snake" title="Snake">snakes</a>, but also some <a href="/wiki/Lizard" title="Lizard">lizards</a>. Sometimes they become entangled with larger snakes and battle on the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Occasionally, they prey on small mammals up to the size of a rabbit, and rarely birds and large insects.</p>
<p>This eagle is generally very silent. On occasions, it emits a variety of musical whistling notes. When breeding, it lays only one egg; it can live up to 17 years.</p>
<p>The Short-toed Snake Eagle has suffered a steep decline in numbers and range in Europe and is now rare and still decreasing in several countries due to changes in agriculture and land use. It needs protection.<sup class="Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources from February 2010">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> In the middle and far eastern part of its range, this species is not yet threatened.</p>
